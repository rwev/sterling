## Risk Flag Responses

The Risk Manager raised six issues. Each is addressed below with the PM's decision and rationale.

### Flag 1: Gross Exposure Breach at 103%

**Risk finding:** The evening draft proposed 103% gross, breaching the 100% ceiling by 3 percentage points. Risk recommended Option B -- trim VST an additional 1.5% (to 30.5%) and CEG an additional 1.5% (to 25.5%) to bring gross back to 100%.

**Decision: ACCEPTED. Trims applied. Gross exposure returns to 100.0%.**

The Risk Manager is correct that formal limit breaches should not be waived casually, particularly this early in the fund's life when we have no track record to justify exceptions. The 3% margin exposure in the draft was a convenience -- it avoided further diluting the power infrastructure thesis. On reflection, 30.5% VST and 25.5% CEG remain appropriately sized for the conviction level, and the VaR and diversification improvements from adding NOW and TASK are preserved. The discipline of respecting the gross exposure ceiling outweighs the marginal conviction cost of trimming the two longs by 1.5% each.

This decision also further reduces the sector concentration breach (Utilities/Power from 59% to 56%), the single-name concentration on VST (from 32% to 30.5%), and the VST-CEG covariance contribution to portfolio variance.

### Flag 2: TASK Post-Dividend Risk/Reward Is Thin

**Risk finding:** After the March 25 ex-dividend adjustment, the TASK cover target of $6.50-$7.50 provides only approximately 5% downside from the adjusted price of ~$6.86. The post-dividend risk/reward is approximately 1.0-1.2:1 (inverted -- the stop loss represents more potential loss than the cover target represents potential gain on a post-dividend-adjusted basis).

**Decision: APPROVED at 3% with a conditional review. The position proceeds, but with a mandatory reassessment after the ex-dividend date.**

The Risk Manager's arithmetic on the post-dividend economics is correct and I flagged this concern in the draft. The thesis on TASK is structural -- it is not primarily a trade on the $3.65 dividend adjustment. The real profit potential comes from fundamental deterioration in H1-H2 2026: the largest client accelerating AI-driven volume reductions, legacy BPO revenue declining, and interest expense on the new $500M debt consuming free cash flow. These are catalysts that play out over Q1-Q2 earnings (May and August), not on the ex-dividend date.

However, I acknowledge that the near-term risk/reward around the ex-date is unfavorable. The following conditions apply:

- TASK remains at 3% through the ex-dividend date.
- After the ex-dividend adjustment, the position will be reassessed at the next IC (March 7 or an ad hoc session). If the post-dividend stock price settles above $8.50 (adjusted), the position will be cut to 2% or exited.
- The cover target is revised lower to $5.00-$6.50 (from $6.50-$7.50) to reflect a more meaningful post-dividend profit objective. This revised target is contingent on Q1 2026 earnings disappointing in May.
- The stop loss remains at $13.50 pre-dividend. Post-dividend, this translates to approximately $9.85 adjusted. Maximum position loss at stop: ~0.9% of NAV (unchanged at 3% sizing).

### Flag 3: AI Theme Concentration at 84% of Gross

**Risk finding:** Total AI-linked gross exposure (power demand 56%, software 8%, MSFT short 14%, TASK short 3%, with the evening final trims) is approximately 81% of NAV. Risk warned that a broad reassessment of AI's economic impact would affect the majority of the portfolio.

**Decision: ACCEPTED as a known risk. No allocation change.**

This is a deliberate, thesis-driven concentration. The fund's investment thesis is built around the AI infrastructure and adoption cycle. The positions are on different sides of the AI trade:

- VST/CEG: long AI power demand (infrastructure layer)
- NOW: long AI workflow automation (application layer)
- MSFT: short AI capex without ROI (investment/spending layer)
- TASK: short AI labor displacement victim (disruption layer)

These four AI sub-themes have meaningfully different drivers. A scenario where "AI disappoints broadly" does not affect them uniformly: if hyperscalers cut capex (bad for VST/CEG), that does not mean enterprises stop adopting AI workflow tools (NOW) or that BPO companies suddenly become safe from displacement (TASK). The correlation matrix in the risk report confirms this -- NOW-VST correlation is only 0.20, and TASK has near-zero correlation with the power names.

That said, a true "AI winter" narrative -- where the entire AI theme is abandoned simultaneously -- would be a severe tail risk for this portfolio. The mitigation is:

1. NKE short (19%) is entirely uncorrelated to the AI theme (tariff/China/market-share driven). This provides 19% of gross exposure as a non-AI hedge.
2. The European defense thesis (RNMBY/BAESY), expected for the March 7 IC, would add a non-AI long and further diversify. This is the highest-priority directive for the Long Analyst.
3. If AI sentiment deteriorates broadly (measured by a >15% drawdown in the AI/data center ETF complex over any 2-week period), the PM will convene an emergency IC to reassess the AI concentration.

### Flag 4: Net Exposure Arithmetic Discrepancy (+34% Draft vs. +31% Risk Calculation)

**Risk finding:** The draft stated net exposure of +34%. Risk calculated +31% (Long 67% - Short 36% = +31%). The discrepancy appeared to stem from a netting convention error in the draft.

**Decision: CORRECTED. The Risk Manager's arithmetic was correct for the draft allocations. The final portfolio net exposure is +28.0%.**

The draft contained an arithmetic error. Long 67% minus Short 36% equals +31%, not +34%. I am correcting this in the final memo. With the additional trims to VST and CEG (1.5% each), the final allocations produce: Long 64.0% - Short 36.0% = Net +28.0%.

This lower net exposure is a beneficial side effect of the gross exposure correction. A +28% net long bias is more defensible in the current stagflationary regime than +31% or +34%.

### Flag 5: Stagflation Regime and Net Exposure Level

**Risk finding:** Core PCE at 3.0%, first rate cut pushed to July 2026. The portfolio is net long rate sensitivity. Risk recommended considering a reduction to +20-25% net if inflation persists, with a monitoring trigger at the March 13 January PCE release.

**Decision: The net exposure of +28% (after the gross exposure correction) is within the range Risk considers defensible. Accept with monitoring triggers.**

The correction from +31-34% to +28% partially addresses this concern. At +28% net, the portfolio is modestly long-biased -- consistent with the structural conviction on power infrastructure (which has contracted, inflation-protected PPA revenue) while maintaining substantial short-side hedging (36% short).

Specific responses to the stagflation risk:

1. VST and CEG PPAs are contracted at fixed or inflation-escalated prices. Higher inflation actually increases the value of uncontracted generation (merchant power benefits from higher energy prices). The rate sensitivity is real through the leverage channel, but the revenue visibility from signed PPAs provides a floor that generic "rate-sensitive" equities lack.
2. NOW at 25x forward P/E is the most rate-sensitive position on the long side. The 8% sizing reflects this concern. I am not willing to go larger on NOW until the rate environment clarifies.
3. The MSFT short (14%) and NKE short (19%) both benefit from a stagflationary environment -- higher rates compress MSFT's growth multiple, and slowing consumer spending pressures NKE. The short book is well-aligned with the macro regime.

**Monitoring triggers adopted from Risk:**
- If January Core PCE (March 13 release) prints above 3.0%: convene ad hoc IC to evaluate reducing net exposure to +20-22% by trimming VST/CEG further or adding a new short.
- If the 10-year yield rises above 4.25%: same review.
- If both triggers fire simultaneously: reduce net exposure to +20% or below.

### Flag 6: Sector Concentration Breach -- Utilities/Power at 56%

**Risk finding:** Net Utilities/Power Infrastructure exposure is now 56% of NAV (down from 65% morning, 59% draft). Still exceeds the 25% sector limit by 31 percentage points.

**Decision: ACCEPTED as a continuing, documented waiver. The sector limit breach is the defining feature of this fund's thesis and will persist until the power infrastructure positions are exited or materially reduced.**

This has been flagged and accepted since the morning IC. The rationale has not changed: the fund's highest-conviction thesis is that AI-driven electricity demand is structural, contracted, and mispriced. Concentrating in this sector is the bet, not an accident. The trims from 65% to 56% over the course of the day represent meaningful progress -- 9 percentage points of reduction -- driven by the addition of NOW and the gross exposure correction.

Further reduction from here requires either: (a) exiting one of VST/CEG entirely, which is not warranted by the fundamentals; or (b) adding multiple non-utility longs to dilute the sector weight. The European defense thesis (expected March 7) and any additional long ideas will help address this over time.

Risk will continue to flag this breach each cycle. The PM will continue to accept it with documented rationale until the thesis changes or the positions are exited.
