## Macro Update Summary (Evening)

The evening macro update materially shifts the regime assessment. Three developments:

1. **Core PCE at 3.0% YoY (December)** -- inflation is re-accelerating, not decelerating. The "disinflation intact" framing from the morning is now incorrect. First rate cut pushed to July 2026. This is a mild stagflationary setup: slowing growth plus sticky inflation is the worst combination for equity multiples.

2. **NVDA beat-and-dump (-5.5%)** -- confirms AI capex fatigue is structural. The market demands ROI proof, not demand proof. This reinforces the MSFT short and introduces nuance on VST/CEG (if AI capex enthusiasm fades broadly, does power demand follow? Not near-term given signed PPAs, but sentiment risk is real).

3. **Block (XYZ) cuts 40% of workforce citing AI, stock +24%** -- the market is aggressively rewarding AI-driven labor displacement. This creates two investable themes: long the AI workflow automation enablers (NOW, PATH), short the labor-intensive BPO companies vulnerable to displacement (TASK and peers).

The evening update adds a new sector call: **AI Workflow / Automation Software -- Overweight.** This is distinct from the AI infrastructure trade that is fatiguing. These are application-layer companies whose products enable enterprises to replace workers with AI agents.

**Portfolio implication:** The stagflationary shift argues for modestly lower net exposure and more emphasis on idiosyncratic, catalyst-driven positions. The AI labor displacement theme provides a paired trade opportunity (long NOW / short TASK) that is partially market-neutral and directly aligned with the macro's strongest emergent signal.

## Pitch Reviews

### NOW (ServiceNow) -- Long

**Presented by:** Long Analyst
**Current Price:** ~$107 (2026-02-28 close)
**Analyst Entry Range:** $98 - $110
**Analyst Target:** $137 (12-month, probability-weighted)
**Analyst Stop:** $88
**Upside/Downside:** ~2.7:1 from midpoint

**Decision: APPROVED**
**Proposed Allocation: 8% of NAV**

**Rationale:**

ServiceNow earns a slot in the book. This is a high-quality business at a cyclically depressed valuation, and the thesis is directly aligned with the evening macro's strongest new signal -- AI labor displacement. Four considerations drive the approval:

1. **The thesis is right on the structural positioning.** The market is pricing NOW as an AI victim (seat compression). The analyst makes a compelling case that NOW is instead the enabler of AI-driven workforce reduction -- the "picks and shovels" of the labor displacement cycle. Block's 40% headcount cut and 24% stock surge validate that enterprises are executing on this thesis. ServiceNow's Autonomous Workforce product, launched February 26, is the enterprise-grade tooling that makes these cuts possible. Now Assist at $600M ACV doubling YoY is real product-market fit, not a roadmap aspiration.

2. **The valuation is genuinely compressed.** At 25x forward P/E and ~7x forward revenue, this is half ServiceNow's historical multiple. A 98% renewal rate, 20% guided revenue growth, and 36% FCF margins at this multiple create asymmetric risk/reward. The PEG ratio of 1.25x is reasonable for a Rule of 56 (growth + FCF margin) business.

3. **The entry timing is favorable.** The SaaSpocalypse selloff has created the dislocation. The stock is trading within the analyst's entry range at ~$107. The next catalyst is Q1 earnings in late April, which will provide the first look at Autonomous Workforce traction and Now Assist trajectory. There is a 6-8 week window to accumulate before the catalyst.

4. **Portfolio diversification benefit.** The current book is 65% power infrastructure longs and 35% shorts (consumer discretionary + mega-cap tech). Adding NOW introduces enterprise software exposure -- a different sector, different factor exposure, and a different catalyst calendar. This reduces portfolio concentration risk.

**Why 8% and not larger:**

- **Stagflationary macro headwind.** Core PCE at 3.0% and rate cuts pushed to July 2026 are headwinds for growth/software multiples. A 25x P/E could compress further to 20-22x if inflation stays hot and the Fed stays on hold. The macro argues against sizing aggressively into growth names right now.
- **The SaaSpocalypse may not be over.** The seat compression narrative has not been disproven -- it has been paused. If a major enterprise publicly reports ServiceNow seat reductions alongside AI adoption, the stock revisits the lows. The thesis depends on NOW successfully shifting to consumption-based pricing faster than seats compress. That is a bet on execution during a transition, not a proven outcome.
- **Armis acquisition risk.** The $7.75B cash deal for Armis is expensive and unproven. Integration risk on a cybersecurity bolt-on is non-trivial.
- **Correlation with existing positions.** NOW has some positive correlation with the power infrastructure longs (both benefit from AI buildout narratives) and some negative correlation with the MSFT short (both are enterprise software; if the SaaS selloff deepens, NOW and MSFT both decline, creating a drag on the long and reducing the short's profitability). I want to keep this position sized to avoid amplifying factor concentration.

**Entry range:** $98 - $110 (current ~$107 is acceptable)
**12-month target:** $137
**Stop loss:** $88
**Maximum position loss at stop:** 1.5% of NAV

### TASK (TaskUs) -- Short

**Presented by:** Short Analyst
**Current Price:** ~$10.50 (2026-02-28)
**Analyst Entry Range:** $10.50 - $11.50
**Analyst Cover Target:** $6.50 - $7.50
**Analyst Stop:** $13.50
**Upside/Downside:** ~1.4:1 from current (accounting for post-dividend adjustment)

**Decision: APPROVED**
**Proposed Allocation: 3% of NAV**

**Rationale:**

TaskUs earns a small slot in the book as a thematic short paired with the NOW long. The thesis is structurally sound but the position demands conservative sizing due to liquidity constraints and near-term event risk. Three factors drive the approval:

1. **The leveraged recapitalization is a bearish signal the market is misreading.** The analyst correctly identifies the $333M special dividend funded by $500M in new debt as financial extraction, not capital return. When a board in a structurally challenged industry borrows against future cash flows to pay a special dividend, it signals awareness that the equity value today exceeds the present value of future cash flows. Post-recap, leverage jumps from 0.1x to 2.0x+, and interest expense consumes 35-40% of adjusted FCF. The balance sheet cushion is gone precisely when the business faces secular headwinds.

2. **The largest client concentration (27% of revenue) is an acute risk.** This client is explicitly reducing volumes due to AI. The 2026 revenue guidance of 3.5% growth already incorporates "anticipated reductions" -- but the pace of enterprise AI adoption is accelerating (Block, ServiceNow's own internal data, 49,000+ tech layoffs in early 2026). If the largest client accelerates reductions beyond what is guided, the 3.5% growth guide becomes negative growth, and the thesis accelerates.

3. **The AI services revenue is a fragile pillar.** The bull case for TASK rests on AI services doubling in 2026. But AI services (data annotation, AI training, content moderation) is itself vulnerable to automation as models improve. The analyst rightly flags this as potentially transitional revenue. The market is extrapolating a durable growth engine from what may be a temporary bridge.

**Why only 3%:**

- **Liquidity risk.** TASK has a market cap under $1B with limited float. The analyst's own recommendation is 1-2% of NAV. I am sizing at 3% because the thesis conviction is high, but this is the upper bound for a name with this liquidity profile. Exiting a 3% position in a sub-$1B name during a disorderly selloff could be costly.
- **Special dividend timing complexity.** The March 25 ex-dividend date creates a near-term event that complicates the short. On the ex-date, the stock adjusts down by $3.65 (~34% of the current price). The short seller owes the dividend. This means we pay $3.65/share on the short position -- a ~34% immediate cash cost against a ~30% cover target. The math still works (cover target of $6.50-$7.50 from a dividend-adjusted ~$7.00 implies the stock needs to fall further post-dividend to reach the cover range), but the margin of safety on the short is thinner than the headline numbers suggest.
- **Takeout risk.** At sub-$1B market cap with new leverage, TASK could attract PE take-private interest. This is a tail risk that could produce a 30-50% loss on a short position.
- **Short interest is low (0.74%).** This means we are not crowded, but it also means there is no base of short sellers to validate the thesis. We could be early.

**Entry range:** $10.50 - $11.50 (current ~$10.50 is at the low end; acceptable)
**Cover target:** $6.50 - $7.50 (post-dividend adjusted)
**Stop loss:** $13.50
**Maximum position loss at stop:** 0.9% of NAV

**Timing:** Initiate the short before the March 25 ex-dividend date. Be aware of the dividend obligation. Add on any rally toward $11.50 or on weak Q1 2026 earnings in May.

### NOW / TASK as a Paired Theme

The NOW long and TASK short form a natural pair trade around the AI labor displacement theme:

- NOW is the company providing the tools that enable enterprises to replace human workers with AI agents.
- TASK is the labor-intensive outsourcer whose workers are being replaced.

This pairing isolates the AI displacement alpha from broad market beta. If the macro deteriorates and all equities sell off, the pair should be partially insulated -- NOW may fall, but TASK should fall more because its fundamental exposure to AI displacement is negative, not positive.

Combined, the pair adds 11% gross exposure (8% long + 3% short) with only 5% net long exposure. This is modest and well-calibrated to the stage of the theme (early, with Q1 earnings in April-May as the key validation point).
