## Thesis Summary

Merck trades at a decade-low forward P/E of approximately 12x on 2026 guidance of $5.00-$5.15 adjusted EPS, reflecting the market's assumption that Keytruda's 2028 patent cliff will crater the business. This assumption is wrong -- or at minimum, drastically overstated. Merck is executing a multi-layered defense-and-growth strategy that the current valuation ignores: (1) subcutaneous Keytruda Qlex extends franchise protection to 2030+, (2) Winrevair is ramping toward $6-8B in peak sales, (3) 20+ late-stage pipeline programs represent a $70B+ commercial opportunity by mid-2030s, and (4) $3B in cost cuts by end-2027 protect margins through the transition. Meanwhile, Merck offers the defensive characteristics the macro environment demands -- domestic revenue insulation from tariffs, inelastic demand, and a 2.85% dividend yield growing for 16 consecutive years. The stock trades at a 37-46% discount to pharma sector multiples. The market is pricing catastrophe; the company is delivering transition.
