# Sterling — Portfolio Update

The portfolio has shifted from pure infrastructure conviction to infrastructure-plus-healthcare. We trimmed our second-largest position for risk management and added two defensively-oriented healthcare plays that improve the portfolio's drawdown mechanics without diluting our structural AI-demand thesis.

## What Changed

**Constellation Energy (CEG):** Resized from 20% to 18%. The position had approached its $350 target at current prices, offering modest 0.2:1 risk/reward at the larger sizing. The 2% reduction accomplishes three objectives simultaneously: it de-risks a maturing position, it improves our overall cash buffer by freeing liquidity, and it brings our power sector concentration down from 43% to a more sustainable 41%—still elevated under our existing waiver, but materially better positioned for margin-of-safety investors.

**Merck (MRK) & Argenx (ARGX):** Added two healthcare positions representing 9% combined allocation. MRK is a large-cap pharma dividend-yielder with near-zero portfolio correlation; ARGX is a biotech growth name with a defined binary catalyst in May. Neither position has meaningful co-movement with our power/software cluster. Together, they introduce genuine diversification and reduce portfolio beta below 1.0 for the first time.

**Cash:** Increased from 12% to 14% via the CEG trim. Still below our preferred 15–20% range in volatile markets, but adequate for managing single-position risk events. A drawdown contingency plan is in place to raise cash further if needed.

## Where We Stand

Nine of ten positions held. The portfolio remains architecturally biased toward AI infrastructure buildout—power generation and enterprise software together account for 63% of capital—but the healthcare addition signals a deliberate shift toward durable secular themes beyond pure compute demand.

Vistra (VST), our largest position at 23%, has declined sharply on tariff-driven broad market selling. The decline is macro-driven; the fundamental PPAs with AWS and Meta remain intact. We have a hard stop in place at $140 and active monitoring protocols to manage tail risk.

Our software holdings (ServiceNow, Salesforce) confirmed their theses through recent earnings; we see them as positioned in multi-year structural upgrades. The 22% combined allocation is justified by the conviction level and by recent multiple compression in the SaaSpocalypse selloff.

## Looking Ahead

The March 4 tariff event will likely create stress across the portfolio, with healthcare names potentially benefiting from relative demand insulation. We view near-term volatility as opportunity to scale into the two new healthcare positions. The portfolio's real test is whether power generation sustains its 20-year PPA anchors through a potential recession; Constellation Energy's March 31 earnings call is our decision point for whether the position remains at 18% or contracts further. We remain highly concentrated by design—this portfolio does not survive a GFC-magnitude event without material loss—but we are confident in the quality of our thesis bets and in the risk tools we've deployed.
