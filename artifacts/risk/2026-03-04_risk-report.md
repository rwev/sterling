**Assessment**: VaR has improved dramatically despite higher deployed capital (83% vs. 80%). This is driven by three factors:

1. **VIX collapse from 25.0 to 14.90.** This is the dominant driver. The 40% decline in VIX reduces implied volatilities across the board. Every position's standalone VaR contribution has declined.

2. **AVGO post-earnings IV crush.** AVGO's implied volatility declined from 72% (pre-earnings) to approximately 45% (post-earnings), as the event uncertainty has resolved. This reduces AVGO's standalone VaR contribution from 0.37% (at 5% weight, 72% vol) to 0.37% (at 8% weight, 45% vol) -- essentially flat despite the 60% increase in position size.

3. **Diversification benefit increased from 48% to 64%.** This is a meaningful structural improvement. In risk-off regimes, correlations spike (the "correlations go to 1" phenomenon). As markets normalize, correlations return to their structural levels, restoring the diversification benefit. The portfolio's natural diversifiers (FRO negative correlations, AEM/LHX independence) are functioning as designed.

**VaR 95% at 1.42% is well within the 2.0% guideline for the first time since the Iran conflict began.** VaR 99% at 2.01% is also within the 3.0% guideline. Both exceedances flagged in Cycles IV and V are resolved. **Severity: OK.** No action required.

**Caution**: VaR is a function of current implied vol. If VIX spikes back to 25+, VaR would return to the 2.2-2.4% range (above guideline). The current VaR of 1.42% assumes the low-vol regime persists. This desk does not treat VaR at 14.9 VIX as permanently settled -- it is a snapshot.
