## Marginal Risk Contribution of FRO

### FRO (Frontline) -- 7% Allocation

**Standalone risk**: At 7% NAV and 55% estimated annualized vol, FRO's standalone VaR 95% contribution is 0.40% of NAV. Stop-out at $33 (16.7% below $39.60) costs 1.17% of NAV.

**Marginal risk contribution**: Because FRO has negative correlation with the dominant portfolio cluster (AI/power at -0.10 to -0.15), its marginal contribution to portfolio VaR is approximately 0.20-0.25% -- roughly half its standalone contribution. This is the best marginal risk profile of any position added in the portfolio's history, including AEM.

For comparison:
- AEM (Cycle II): 5% allocation, marginal VaR contribution ~0.05-0.08% (very low due to small size and near-zero correlation)
- CEG (Cycle III): 10% allocation, marginal VaR contribution ~0.30-0.35% (correlated with existing cluster)
- VST (Cycle III): 10% allocation, marginal VaR contribution ~0.35-0.40% (highest correlated risk added)
- **FRO (Cycle IV): 7% allocation, marginal VaR contribution ~0.20-0.25% (negative correlation offset)**

**Qualitative risk factors**:

1. **Binary Hormuz risk**: This is the dominant risk. A ceasefire, diplomatic resolution, or Hormuz reopening could collapse VLCC spot rates from the current record $424K/day to $40-60K/day within 48-72 hours. FRO has predominantly spot exposure (not long-term charters), meaning revenue impact is immediate. The PM correctly identifies this as the primary risk and has sized accordingly (7%, not 12-15%).

2. **Rate mean-reversion**: Even without a ceasefire, VLCC rates at $424K/day are 8-10x the long-term average. Rates will normalize. The question is when and how fast. The PM's 1-3 month tactical horizon is appropriate.

3. **Dividend sustainability**: The $1.03/quarter dividend ($4.12 annualized, ~10.4% yield at $39.60) is funded by current spot rates. If rates normalize, the dividend could be cut 50-70%. FRO has no debt maturities until 2030, so the balance sheet is not at risk, but the income component of the thesis is rate-dependent.

4. **Fleet exposure**: FRO's 41-VLCC fleet is the largest independent position. A maritime incident involving an FRO vessel in or near the Strait of Hormuz would be both an operational and stock-specific event. This is a low-probability but high-impact idiosyncratic risk.

5. **Liquidity**: FRO has a market cap of ~$8.2B and average daily volume of ~3-4M shares (~$120-160M daily dollar volume). At 7% of NAV, the position is liquid but not deep-pool liquid. Days-to-liquidate at 25% of ADV: approximately 1-2 days depending on fund AUM. Adequate but not as frictionless as large-cap holdings.

**Risk-adjusted assessment**: FRO at 7% is a well-constructed tactical position. The sizing limits maximum stop-out loss to 1.17% of NAV. The negative correlation with the dominant portfolio cluster provides a structural diversification benefit that makes the portfolio measurably safer despite adding risk exposure. The binary Hormuz risk is real but priced into the sizing. **The 7% allocation is appropriate. No change recommended.**
