The Long Analyst built a two-pillar case: (1) the Grasberg Block Cave mine restart in Q2 2026, which would restore approximately 60% of district copper and 75% of gold production after the September 2025 mud rush incident, dramatically improving EBITDA from ~$10B to an estimated $14–16B on normalized production; and (2) structural copper demand from the AI/electrification supercycle. FCX was characterized as trading at 11.5x EV/EBITDA on depressed 2025 earnings but only 7–8x on normalized Grasberg production — a valuation compression story tied to a defined operational catalyst.

- **Entry Range**: $58 – $64 (requiring a 6–15% pullback from the $68 thesis price)

## Lessons Learned

The FCX thesis correctly identified the Grasberg restart as the most important near-term catalyst in the copper mining sector, correctly framed the valuation compression opportunity from depressed production to normalized production, and correctly stipulated that entry discipline was critical given the stock's 61.5% prior-quarter rally. The thesis's explicit acknowledgment — "much of the Grasberg restart and copper supercycle thesis is priced in" at $68 — was an accurate and honest assessment that the analyst's own entry parameters reflected. The fundamental lesson is that the thesis was written for a price level ($58–$64) that the market never offered during the evaluation window. This is not an analytical failure; it is a market outcome. However, the persistence of the stock above the entry range for multiple IC cycles without any plan to either adjust the entry parameters upward or officially close the thesis created conditional pipeline clutter. Future long theses submitted when the stock is already above the entry range should either include an explicit expiry condition ("drop if no pullback to entry within 30 days") or a revised entry range justified by updated fundamental analysis.

**Analyst Grade: A-** — The FCX thesis was analytically thorough, the valuation work was multi-scenario and realistic, and the entry discipline was exemplary. The analyst correctly refused to chase the stock above the entry range and explicitly told the PM to wait for a pullback. Copper and gold prices have moved in the thesis's favor since submission. The Grasberg restart catalyst remains on track. The grade falls short of A because the thesis was submitted when the stock was already 6–15% above the stated entry range, creating an immediate question about deployment feasibility that was never fully resolved before the drop.
