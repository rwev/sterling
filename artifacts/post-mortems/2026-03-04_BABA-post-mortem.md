## Lessons Learned

The ADR prohibition was a policy decision that had nothing to do with the quality of the BABA thesis — and the thesis itself demonstrates credible fundamental analysis. The Contrarian Analyst correctly identified the Pentagon list episode as a non-event with no sanctions mechanism, correctly flagged the PCAOB audit compliance as the actual delisting gating factor, and correctly distinguished investment-driven FCF compression from structural deterioration. The primary lesson is operational rather than analytical: ADR compliance screening must precede thesis development. Writing a 130-line fundamental thesis for a security that fails the first compliance test (Form 20-F / depositary structure) wastes analytical resources that could be allocated to eligible securities.

**Analyst Grade: B+** — The fundamental analysis was disciplined and the bear case rebuttals were well-constructed. The R/R at 1.6:1 was on the lower end for a speculative contrarian position that requires a geopolitical discount to compress, which is a judgment call with high uncertainty. Deducted for the compliance oversight in pitching an ADR, though this appears to be a policy that was introduced concurrent with the thesis, not a pre-existing requirement the analyst ignored.
