## Observations and Patterns

**1. Pipeline entry discipline needs improvement.** Two of the four closed theses (CF and FCX) were submitted for stocks already above the stated entry range. Both required either a pullback that never materialized or a deliberate expansion of the entry range. Analysts should not submit conditional theses unless the entry range is currently achievable or within a plausible 10-day window.

**2. Compliance screening must precede thesis development.** The BABA thesis was a significant analytical effort that was disqualified in the same IC session by a compliance rule that should have screened the security before work began. The ADR prohibition test is a one-minute compliance check (Form 20-F / depositary bank structure) that must be performed before initiating any research.

**3. Conditional thesis expiry conditions are missing.** None of the four theses included an expiry condition specifying the conditions under which the thesis should be dropped if the entry trigger is not met within a defined window. FCX sat in the conditional pipeline for multiple cycles above its entry range. A standard expiry mechanism (e.g., "drop if price does not reach entry range within 45 days") would improve pipeline hygiene.

**4. Contrarian Analyst has the highest volume but the most mixed discipline.** Three theses submitted in two days is a high output rate. The quality of fundamental analysis is strong, but the entry timing on CF was poor and the BABA compliance failure was avoidable. The SMR thesis was the best-calibrated of the three — appropriate sizing, honest about speculative character, and the primary predicted catalyst (Romania FID) proved accurate.

**5. Long Analyst demonstrates the best entry discipline.** The FCX thesis explicitly acknowledged the entry problem and refused to compromise on parameters. This is the right behavior even if it means the thesis never deploys — a forced entry at a high R/R is worse than no entry at all.
