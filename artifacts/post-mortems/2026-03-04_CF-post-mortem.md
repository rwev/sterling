## Thesis Recap

CF Industries was pitched as a structural beneficiary of the Strait of Hormuz closure and Qatar LNG production halt, which widened the gap between US Henry Hub natural gas prices (~$4/MMBtu) and European TTF prices (over 60 EUR/MWh), directly expanding margins for the largest US nitrogen fertilizer producer. The thesis argued that consensus models had not incorporated this structural cost advantage, and that the Yazoo City plant outage (temporarily depressing production by ~6%) was being mispriced as a permanent impairment rather than a recoverable event covered by business interruption insurance.

## Lessons Learned

The CF thesis identified a real and well-documented structural advantage — US nitrogen producers benefit materially when international gas prices spike. The analysis of the Yazoo City outage as recoverable rather than permanent was accurate and well-supported. The problem was timing: the thesis was written when the stock was at a 52-week high, the primary energy spread catalyst was already moderating, and the R/R at current prices was below the portfolio's minimum threshold. The thesis would have been far more actionable if submitted six to eight weeks earlier, when CF was trading at $78–$85 and the Hormuz disruption was beginning rather than peaking. The lesson is that contrarian thesis value is highly entry-price-dependent — a fundamentally sound idea submitted at the wrong point in the price cycle generates an unacceptable R/R. Future pitches for commodity producers in active geopolitical upswings should be submitted at the inflection point, not after a 38% year-to-date move.

**Analyst Grade: B-** — The fundamental analysis of CF's cost structure, Yazoo City optionality, and nitrogen market dynamics was competent and well-sourced. The bear case rebuttals were balanced. However, submitting a thesis for a stock at its 52-week high with an explicit R/R below 1.5:1 at current prices and a stated dependence on a pullback that had not occurred reflects poor entry discipline. The Barclays target raise on the same day as the thesis submission suggests the analyst was writing into a momentum headline rather than a contrarian dislocation.
