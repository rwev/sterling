But beneath the surface, the market is bifurcating violently: energy is the best-performing sector in 2026 (XLE +22% YTD), while everything exposed to the consumer or to borrowing costs is getting repriced.

The March 17-18 FOMC will hold at 3.50-3.75% (94.1% probability per CME FedWatch), but the forward curve is where the real action will be. The market needs to decide whether this is a temporary oil shock that the Fed can look through, or a structural shift that forces the Fed to choose between jobs and prices. We believe it is the latter.

**Regime classification: Early stagflation. The oil-driven surge in the 10Y yield is directly threatening the housing recovery thesis. If Brent sustains above $90, expect mortgage rates to push toward 6.25-6.50%, which would stall the nascent spring housing recovery.
