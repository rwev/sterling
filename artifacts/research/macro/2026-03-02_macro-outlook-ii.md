## Theme 1: Photonics — The Optical Backbone of the AI Buildout

### The Setup

NVIDIA announced today (March 2, 2026) a $4 billion strategic investment split equally between Coherent Corp (COHR) and Lumentum Holdings (LITE), each receiving $2 billion plus multibillion-dollar purchase commitments for advanced laser and optical networking products. This is the clearest signal yet that photonics has moved from a niche technology subsector to a critical bottleneck in the AI infrastructure stack.

### Why It Matters Now

The AI buildout documented in the first outlook ($690B in hyperscaler capex) creates a massive demand pull for data movement between chips, racks, and data centers. Traditional copper interconnects are hitting physical limits at the speeds and distances required by next-generation AI clusters. Silicon photonics — embedding optical data transmission directly into silicon — is the solution to this interconnect bottleneck.

### Macro Drivers

- **Interconnect bottleneck**: As GPU cluster sizes scale from thousands to hundreds of thousands of accelerators, the data movement problem becomes as important as the compute problem. Optical interconnects solve bandwidth density and power efficiency challenges that copper cannot.
- **Market size trajectory**: The global silicon photonics market was $2.86B in 2025, is projected at $3.69B in 2026, and is expected to reach $28.75B by 2034 — a 29.25% CAGR. This is a market in the early innings of exponential growth.
- **Co-packaged optics (CPO) commercialization**: Broadcom's third-generation CPO with 200G/lane capability is entering commercial deployment in 2026. Meta has tested Broadcom's CPO solutions for one million link hours without a single link flap. The CPO era is dawning and will reshape data center interconnect architecture.
- **NVIDIA validation**: The $4B investment is not charity — NVIDIA is locking up supply chain access for advanced laser components and optical networking products critical to its next-generation platforms. This is analogous to hyperscalers locking up TSMC capacity.
- **1.6T networking supercycle**: The transition from 800G to 1.6T optical networking is underway, driving a replacement cycle across the entire optical networking stack. Ciena projects $5.7-6.1B in FY2026 revenue (~24% growth) with a $5B backlog; the company is essentially sold out for Q1.

## Theme 2: International Exposure — The Non-US Equity Opportunity

### The Setup

Emerging markets returned 33% in 2025 — more than double the S&P 500 — and are continuing to outperform in 2026 with low-teens returns year-to-date. The iShares MSCI Emerging Markets ETF (EEM) attracted over $4B in January 2026, its strongest monthly inflow since 2015. Meanwhile, European equities opened 2026 at record highs, with the STOXX 600 reaching 621 and the DAX pushing above 25,000. This is not a one-month anomaly; it represents a structural rotation that the team needs to engage with.

### Macro Drivers

- **Valuation gap**: Emerging markets entered 2026 at sizeable discounts to developed peers, particularly the US. After years of US exceptionalism, the valuation compression creates a mathematical tailwind for EM returns even with modest earnings growth.
- **Dollar weakness**: The DXY has declined 7.56% over the past 12 months (currently 98.51). A weaker dollar eases EM financing conditions, improves dollar-denominated returns on foreign assets, and reduces pressure on EM current accounts. The structural direction of the dollar is lower (see Theme 3).
- **China stimulus pivot**: Beijing adopted "moderately loose" monetary policy for the first time since 2010, with a 4% budget deficit target and domestic demand as the top priority for 2026. Goldman Sachs raised its 2026 China GDP forecast to 4.8%; the IMF expects 4.5%. The 15th Five-Year Plan emphasizes technological self-reliance.
- **Hardware geography**: The physical supply chain of AI is overwhelmingly Asian — TSMC in Taiwan (70% global foundry share, 90%+ advanced nodes), Samsung in Korea, and the entire optical/electronics supply chain across Asia. Investing in AI without international exposure means missing the manufacturing backbone.
- **European defense and industrial renaissance**: The Iran conflict and broader geopolitical tensions are accelerating European defense spending. Germany's DAX at 25,000+ reflects industrial strength, ASML's monopoly in EUV lithography, and the beginning of European fiscal expansion.
- **Monetary policy divergence**: The ECB has more room to cut than the Fed; the Bank of Japan is cautiously tightening (rates still near zero). This divergence creates FX-driven investment opportunities.

### Regional Views

**Asia-Pacific**: The AI hardware supply chain is the primary draw. TSMC controls the most critical chokepoint in the global technology stack. Chinese equities offer catch-up potential driven by stimulus, AI investment (Alibaba's cloud revenue +34% y/y), and valuation re-rating. However, the Pentagon's updated military-linked entity list creates regulatory overhang for US-listed Chinese ADRs.

**Japan**: The yen at 157/USD creates a structural competitiveness boost for Japanese exporters. The BOJ is cautiously tightening but remains the most dovish major central bank. Japanese equities offer a combination of corporate governance reform, cheap currency, and world-class industrial franchises.

## Theme 3: Currency Devaluation — Dollar Weakness, Gold, and Real Asset Hedges

### The Setup

Gold has shattered records, trading at $5,278/oz as of March 2, 2026, after breaching $5,300 intraday on the Iran conflict safe-haven bid. The DXY has fallen 7.56% over the past year to 98.51. The US fiscal deficit is projected at $1.9 trillion for FY2026 (5.8% of GDP), with CBO projecting deficits averaging over 6% of GDP for the next decade. Federal debt is on track to reach 120% of GDP by 2036. The structural case for dollar weakness and real asset appreciation is strengthening.

### Macro Drivers

- **Fiscal deterioration**: The $1.9T FY2026 deficit comes during economic expansion, not recession. The reconciliation bill includes $152B in additional Pentagon spending plus potential tax cut extensions. There is no political constituency for fiscal discipline in either party. The twin deficits (fiscal + trade) historically correlate with dollar weakness.
- **Central bank de-dollarization**: Central banks added 863 tonnes of gold to reserves in 2025 (following 1,000+ tonnes in each of the three prior years). Nearly 70% of central banks plan to increase gold's share of reserves over the next five years. The USD's share of global FX reserves has fallen below 60% for 11 consecutive quarters, hitting a 30-year low at 56.32%.
- **Geopolitical safe-haven bid**: The Iran conflict is providing an acute catalyst for gold and commodities, but the structural trend predates the conflict. Sanctions weaponization of the dollar (Russia, Iran) is accelerating reserve diversification away from USD-denominated assets.
- **Real interest rate dynamics**: With the Fed at 3.50-3.75% and CPI at 2.7%, real rates are positive but narrowing. If the ISM Prices Paid at 70.5 signals resurgent inflation, real rates could compress or turn negative — historically the most bullish environment for gold and commodities.
- **Copper as electrification play**: Copper prices surged to $13,238/tonne ($6/lb) in January 2026, a historical LME high. JP Morgan projects $12,500/mt in Q2 2026. Copper benefits from the same AI/electrification themes (data center power infrastructure, grid modernization, EV buildout) while also serving as an inflation hedge — historically, for every 1% rise in CPI, copper prices rise 18%.

### The Gold Miner Opportunity

Gold miners are experiencing a "super-margin" environment. With gold above $5,000/oz and all-in sustaining costs (AISC) generally in the $1,200-1,800/oz range, margins are at historical extremes. Barrick is reporting 70% margins at Tier One assets. Newmont generated a record $7.3B in free cash flow. These are not speculative junior miners — they are massive free cash flow machines at current gold prices, and they trade at significant discounts to the underlying commodity move.

The key risk is production guidance: both Newmont and Barrick have guided for lower 2026 production due to mine sequencing. This creates a potential entry point if shares pull back on volume disappointments despite margin expansion.

### What Needs Monitoring

- Gold price trajectory relative to Iran conflict duration — a ceasefire could trigger a 5-10% pullback in gold, which would be a buying opportunity if the structural thesis holds
- Fed policy path — any move toward rate cuts accelerates the dollar weakness thesis; a surprise hawkish pivot on inflation data would temporarily strengthen the dollar
- Congressional fiscal trajectory — reconciliation bill details, tax cut extensions, defense supplementals all add to deficit
- Central bank gold purchase data (quarterly from WGC) — any acceleration above 1,000 tonnes/year would be extremely bullish
- Copper supply/demand balance — mine supply growth constrained at 1-2% annually while demand accelerates from electrification

## Cross-Theme Interactions

These three themes are not independent — they reinforce each other:

- **Photonics + International**: The optical component supply chain is global. Coherent and Lumentum are building US manufacturing (partly funded by NVIDIA), but much of the photonics supply chain runs through Asia. TSMC and ASML are essential enablers of silicon photonics chip production.
- **International + Currency**: Dollar weakness is the single most important macro variable for international equity returns. A sustained move below DXY 95 would turbocharge EM and developed international returns. The 7.56% DXY decline over the past year has already been a tailwind.
- **Currency + Photonics/AI**: The massive AI capex cycle ($690B) requires enormous fiscal and monetary accommodation. The resulting fiscal deficits and potential inflationary pressures feed the structural dollar weakness thesis, which supports gold and commodity prices.

## Risks to These Themes

1. **Dollar reversal on safe-haven demand**: The Iran conflict is temporarily strengthening the dollar (DXY up 1.1% over the past month). A major escalation could trigger a USD surge that pressures EM equities and gold simultaneously — though historically, extended conflicts eventually weaken the currencies of the belligerent nations.

2. **China regulatory crackdown / US-China decoupling**: Pentagon military-linked entity designations for Alibaba and Baidu demonstrate that US-China investment risks remain acute. Forced delisting of Chinese ADRs remains a tail risk.

3. **Photonics adoption slower than expected**: CPO and silicon photonics could face integration challenges that delay the transition from pluggable optics. This would slow the revenue ramp for COHR and LITE while keeping Ciena's traditional business intact longer.

4. **Gold mean reversion**: Gold at $5,278 is far above any historical norm. A sudden de-escalation in geopolitical tensions, combined with a hawkish Fed pivot, could trigger a sharp correction. Miners would amplify the downside.

5. **Fed hawkish surprise**: If ISM Prices Paid at 70.5 translates into a CPI reacceleration, the Fed could signal tightening rather than easing, strengthening the dollar and pressuring all three themes simultaneously.
