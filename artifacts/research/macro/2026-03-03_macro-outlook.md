## Theme 1: Tanker Shipping — Hormuz Crisis Windfall

### The Setup (one sentence)

The effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz has driven VLCC tanker day rates to an all-time record of $424,000/day, creating a windfall profit opportunity for tanker operators with spot market exposure.

### Why It Is Interesting

This is the most direct, near-term trade on the Iran conflict that Sterling does not currently own. Tanker stocks are structurally uncorrelated with the AI/power/defense book. The Hormuz closure forces crude oil rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope, adding 10-15 sailing days per voyage and effectively reducing global tanker supply by absorbing vessels into longer routes. War-risk insurance premiums have doubled to tripled, but operators pass these through to charterers. The math is straightforward: every additional week of Hormuz disruption generates outsized cash flow for VLCC and Suezmax operators.

### What I Know

- VLCC spot rates hit all-time high of $424K/day on March 3 (source: CNBC)
- Hormuz traffic down 80% per maritime intelligence data
- War-risk insurance premiums up from 0.125% to 0.2-0.4% of vessel value per transit
- Frontline (FRO) hit 12-month high of $39.89 on March 3; BTIG raised target to $42; quarterly dividend of $1.03/share ($4.12 annualized, ~10.4% yield at current price)
- International Seaways (INSW) at $76.50, up 4.8%; analysts raised target to $90; $1B+ returned to shareholders since 2020; Q4 dividend $2.15/share
- Scorpio Tankers (STNG) at $78.28; Deutsche Bank raised target to $83
- Container shipping rates also surging: Asia-North Europe at $4,000/FEU, 3x pre-conflict levels
- Maersk, MSC, Hapag-Lloyd, CMA CGM all issued emergency freight surcharges

### What Needs Verification

- Duration of Hormuz disruption -- this is the critical variable; a rapid ceasefire collapses the thesis
- Tanker fleet positioning and current utilization rates by vessel class
- Charter vs. spot exposure mix for each operator (higher spot = more upside but more reversal risk)
- Whether strategic petroleum reserve releases could moderate tanker demand
- Insurance market capacity for continued war-risk coverage at current premiums

### Suggested Direction

This is a catalyst-driven trade with binary characteristics. If Hormuz remains closed for weeks, tanker operators will generate extraordinary cash flow. If a ceasefire emerges rapidly, rates normalize and the trade reverses. The right sizing approach is a moderate position with tight monitoring. Frontline offers the most direct VLCC exposure with an attractive dividend. International Seaways offers better diversification across vessel classes.

## Theme 2: Obesity Therapeutics — The GLP-1 Platform Expansion

### The Setup (one sentence)

The GLP-1 obesity drug market is entering a new phase of oral formulation approvals, Medicare coverage expansion (mid-2026), and competitive broadening that creates investment opportunities beyond the Lilly/Novo duopoly.

### Why It Is Interesting

Healthcare/biotech is genuinely uncorrelated with Sterling's AI/power/defense concentration. The GLP-1 market is now projected to reach $100B by decade-end. Two structural catalysts are converging in 2026: (1) oral GLP-1 formulations are reaching market -- Novo's oral Wegovy was FDA-approved December 2025, and Lilly's orforglipron has Phase 3 data with FDA action expected Q2 2026; (2) Medicare coverage of GLP-1s for obesity begins mid-2026, dramatically expanding the addressable patient population. This is not a speculative biotech bet -- it is a massive, validated market entering its next growth inflection.

### What I Know

- Eli Lilly (LLY) at $1,001; orforglipron Phase 3 successful across three trials (12.4% weight loss at 36mg); FDA submission filed; US action expected Q2 2026; $1T+ market cap
- Novo Nordisk (NVO) at $37.76 (down from $91.90 52-week high, a 59% drawdown); oral Wegovy FDA-approved Dec 2025; semaglutide franchise generated $24.8B in first 9 months of 2025
- Viking Therapeutics (VKTX) -- pre-revenue clinical-stage; VK2735 dual GLP-1/GIP agonist showed 14.7% weight loss in 13 weeks (Phase 2); VANQUISH Phase 3 trials over-enrolled; oral formulation entering Phase 3 in Q3 2026; analyst consensus target ~$87 (155% upside implied)
- Medicare GLP-1 coverage for obesity begins mid-2026 -- the largest single payer expansion in the drug class history
- WHO issued first global guideline recommending long-term GLP-1 use for obesity (Dec 2025)

### What Needs Verification

- Orforglipron FDA timeline precision -- Q2 2026 action date needs confirmation
- Novo Nordisk's competitive positioning with oral Wegovy vs. orforglipron (head-to-head data shows orforglipron superior on blood sugar and weight)
- Viking's partnership/acquisition prospects -- a $5B+ market cap company with no revenue is either an acquisition target or a dilution risk
- GLP-1 supply chain constraints and manufacturing scale-up timelines
- Impact of compounding pharmacy litigation on branded GLP-1 pricing

### Suggested Direction

Novo Nordisk at $37.76 is the contrarian opportunity -- down 59% from highs despite owning the first approved oral GLP-1 for obesity, holding the largest installed patient base, and generating massive cash flow from the semaglutide franchise. The market is pricing in competitive fears from Lilly's orforglipron, but the obesity market is large enough for multiple winners. Lilly is the quality compounder at $1,001 but trades at a premium. Viking is the high-risk/high-reward small-cap bet on next-generation dual agonist efficacy.

## Theme 3: Housing and Mortgage Rate Beneficiaries — Sub-6% Unlock

### The Setup (one sentence)

The 30-year mortgage rate has fallen below 6% for the first time since September 2022, unlocking pent-up housing demand and relieving margin pressure on homebuilders that have been spending heavily on rate buydowns.

### Why It Is Interesting

Housing is inversely correlated with rates and largely uncorrelated with Sterling's tech/defense/commodity tilt. The rate move is structural, not noise: the 10Y has held in the 4.0-4.1% range while the spread compression is driven by credit easing and flight-to-quality demand from the Iran conflict driving Treasury buying. Homebuilders have been artificially subsidizing buyer mortgage rates for two years at the cost of 200-400bps of gross margin. With market rates now naturally below 6%, that margin headwind becomes a tailwind. D.R. Horton is up 12% YTD and Lennar up 10.3% vs. the S&P 500 down 1% -- the rotation is already underway but has further to run if rates stay here or decline further.

### What I Know

- 30Y fixed mortgage averaged 5.98% week of Feb 26 (Freddie Mac) -- first sub-6% reading since Sept 2022
- January existing home sales dropped 8.4% m/m (largest since 2022), but this was pre-rate-drop; spring selling season is the real test
- Housing permits "shattered expectations" in early 2026 data
- D.R. Horton (DHI): up 12% YTD but reported 22% EPS decline from rate-buydown costs; reaffirmed 2026 guidance; rate-buydown expense should decline materially at sub-6% market rates
- Lennar (LEN): up 10.3% YTD; pivoting to asset-light model; benefits from reduced buydown costs
- PulteGroup (PHM): up 17.4% YTD; outperforming builder peers
- NAR forecasts 14% increase in existing home sales for 2026; Zillow predicts continued rate improvement
- Regional divergence: Dallas and Phoenix oversupplied with rising vacancy; supply-constrained Northeast/Midwest markets healthier
- Rocket Companies (RKT) -- mortgage refinancing beneficiary if rates continue declining

### What Needs Verification

- Whether the Iran oil shock reverses the rate decline (oil-driven inflation could push yields back up)
- March and April housing activity data -- the spring selling season will validate or invalidate the thesis
- Builder inventory levels and pricing power by region
- Refinancing wave potential -- how many existing mortgages are above 6.5% and eligible for refi?
- DOGE-related federal workforce layoffs impact on housing demand in Washington DC metro

### Suggested Direction

PulteGroup has been the strongest performer and offers the best combination of quality execution and margin recovery. D.R. Horton is the volume leader and most levered to the rate-buydown margin recovery. The thesis strengthens if rates stay at or below 6% through the spring selling season.

## Theme 4: Cybersecurity — Structural Spend Growth With Geopolitical Kicker

Cybersecurity spending is growing 14% annually toward a $563B market by 2032, with the Iran conflict and escalating state-sponsored cyber threats providing a near-term spending acceleration catalyst.

The Iran conflict creates an acute catalyst: every major US military operation in the past decade has been accompanied by retaliatory cyber attacks on US critical infrastructure. The portfolio currently has no exposure to software-as-a-service or recurring-revenue cybersecurity models. CrowdStrike just reported its best year ever with ARR hitting $5.25B (+24% y/y) and net new ARR surpassing $1B for the first time. ## Theme 5: Homebuilding Materials and Building Products — Second-Derivative Housing Play

### The Setup (one sentence)

If the sub-6% mortgage rate environment drives a housing recovery, building products and materials companies offer leveraged upside with less direct exposure to the volatile rate-buydown dynamics affecting homebuilder earnings.

### Why It Is Interesting

This is the "picks and shovels" of a housing recovery -- companies that sell into every home regardless of which builder constructs it. Building products companies typically carry higher margins than homebuilders, have more diversified revenue streams (new construction + repair/remodel), and benefit from both volume recovery and pricing power in a supply-constrained materials environment. The ISM Manufacturing PMI returning to expansion (52.4) supports the industrial/materials thesis. These names are uncorrelated with Sterling's tech/defense book.

### What I Know

- 30Y mortgage below 6% for first time since Sept 2022 (Freddie Mac)
- Housing permits "shattered expectations" in early 2026
- ISM Manufacturing PMI at 52.4 (second consecutive expansion); new orders at 55.8
- ISM Prices Paid at 70.5 suggests pricing power for materials producers (tariffs on steel/aluminum feed through)
- 10% universal tariff + 25% on Canadian lumber creates import substitution demand for domestic materials producers
- Repair/remodel market supported by aging housing stock and home equity appreciation

### What Needs Verification

- Specific stock prices and recent earnings for building products names (Builders FirstSource, Fortune Brands, Installed Building Products)
- Volume vs. price dynamics in building materials -- are units recovering or is growth purely price-driven?
- Canadian lumber tariff impact on US timber/lumber producers
- Labor availability in residential construction trades
- Regional divergence in building activity vs. national aggregates

### Suggested Direction

The analyst team should screen for high-quality building products companies with (a) exposure to both new construction and repair/remodel, (b) pricing power in a tariff-supported environment, and (c) above-average margins relative to homebuilders. This is best suited as a Long Analyst or Small-Cap Analyst screen rather than specific name recommendations at this stage.

## Risks to These Themes

1. **Hormuz reopening collapses tanker trade**: A rapid ceasefire would normalize shipping rates within days, creating significant downside for tanker positions sized for sustained disruption. This is a position-sizing and monitoring discipline issue.

2. **Oil shock reverses rate decline**: If Brent sustains above $90 and CPI reaccelerates, the 10Y yield could rise back above 4.5%, pushing mortgage rates above 6.5% and invalidating the housing thesis. The tanker and housing themes are partially offsetting -- oil shock helps tankers but hurts housing.

3. **GLP-1 pricing pressure**: Compounding pharmacies, government price negotiation, and competitive entry could compress GLP-1 margins. Novo's 59% stock decline partially reflects this risk.

4. **Tariff escalation disrupts housing materials**: While 10% universal tariffs support domestic producers, a further escalation (to 15% or higher) could raise construction costs enough to offset the mortgage rate benefit for buyers.

5. **Cyber spending cuts in government austerity**: DOGE-related federal spending cuts could reduce government cybersecurity procurement, though private-sector spending is the primary growth driver.
