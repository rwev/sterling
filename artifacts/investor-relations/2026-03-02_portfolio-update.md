# Sterling — Portfolio Update

**AI infrastructure is still the only game in town, but we've trimmed the players closest to the tariff line.**

## What Changed

We resized Dell from 8% to 5% of the portfolio, freeing 3% for cash ahead of March 4's tariff event. The reason is tactical, not philosophical: the stock has drifted below its entry range, and with 112 as the hard stop, we were carrying excess risk for a thesis we already own more comfortably elsewhere. Vistra — our largest position at 15% — is executing flawlessly (record EBITDA, AWS and Meta contracts locked in), and we prefer to stand behind that rather than absorb additional tariff friction in a lower-conviction semiconductor server name.

We've also conditionally approved two new positions: **Celestica** (AI hardware assembly) at 5%, contingent on Marvell disappointing in earnings on March 5, and **Limbach Holdings** (HVAC/plumbing for AI data centers) at 2%, pending acceptable earnings and a slot opening. These represent our first measured steps to diversify the portfolio beyond pure semiconductor/chip supply chain, though we remain structurally overweight AI infrastructure.

**Broadcom** and **Coherent** are held at deployed weights pending near-term catalysts (Broadcom reports March 4; Coherent has an industry conference on March 17). We are **pausing** all new capital deployment — the remaining 8% authorization for Broadcom and 4% for Coherent will stay in cash until volatility normalizes or these earnings events prove conclusive. VIX is at 23.6; we are waiting for a durable drop below 22.

## Where We Stand

The portfolio is 74% deployed, 14% cash (target weight). We hold ten positions across semiconductors (25% of the portfolio), power generation (15%, with Vistra), infrastructure services (14%, EMCOR and Vertiv), and software (10%, ServiceNow). The semiconductor concentration is deliberate: Broadcom, NVIDIA, Marvell, and Tower Semiconductor all sit on different points of the AI compute stack.

Our largest theme — data center infrastructure for AI — now accounts for 76% of the portfolio. This is the structuring risk we document and accept. We are positioned for a multi-year buildout that is already visible in record backlogs, capacity constraints, and hyperscaler capex guidance. The tariff event on March 4 is a near-term scare; it does not change the long-arc thesis.

Drawdown from our high-water mark is 2.5%, well within tolerance. If both Broadcom *and* Marvell disappoint on March 4-5, compounded with tariff escalation and a VIX spike, we estimate a drawdown of 8-10%, which would test our risk limit but remain within it.

## Looking Ahead

March 4-5 is a compound event: Broadcom earnings, tariff announcements, and Marvell earnings in rapid succession. We have pre-committed contingency paths for all four scenarios (both beat, both miss, mixed outcomes). The portfolio will rebalance based on earnings results and volatility conditions. If Broadcom beats decisively and Marvell disappoints, Celestica enters at 5%. If volatility normalizes, we resume tranche execution into Broadcom. If tariffs prove less severe than priced, we have dry powder to deploy.

The strategic arc remains unchanged: AI infrastructure is capital-intensive and secular. Narrow down to the best-positioned companies, hold through volatility, trim around execution risk, and let the earnings calendar drive rebalancing.
