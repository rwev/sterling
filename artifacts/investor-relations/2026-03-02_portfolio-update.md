# Sterling — Portfolio Update

The portfolio has crossed a structural threshold: we are now fully positioned in the power generation and AI infrastructure build-out, betting decisively on the confluence of geopolitical pressure and technological demand. Three new positions have been approved and are deploying at this week's open. We remain concentrated, deliberate, and positioned for an extended multi-year tailwind.

## What Changed

**New Positions (Cycle III)**

Three new positions entered the portfolio this week, all tied to the power generation and AI infrastructure themes:

- **Constellation Energy (CEG)**: A 10% anchor position in diversified US baseload generation. CEG owns 33 GW of nuclear capacity and stands to benefit immediately from the integration of Calpine's natural gas fleet. The Microsoft 20-year power purchase agreement anchors long-term demand visibility.

- **Vistra Corp (VST)**: An 8% position in thermal and nuclear power generation. VST has stumbled through three consecutive quarterly earnings misses—creating a rare valuation discount in a structural growth story. The 2026 EBITDA guidance of $6.8-7.6 billion remains intact, and Meta's 20-year nuclear PPA provides multi-decade demand certainty. This is the most asymmetric risk/reward in the portfolio.

- **NuScale Power (SMR)**: A 3% calculated option on the nuclear renaissance. NuScale is the only NRC-approved small modular reactor design and is the sole licensed player in this emerging category. At current price, maximum loss is immaterial to NAV; upside, if regulatory and customer tailwinds accelerate, is substantial.

**Allocation Adjustments**

Vistra was trimmed from the initial 10% draft to 8% in response to risk management feedback on correlated power generation exposure. The freed 2% moved to cash, preserving portfolio buffer.

**All Existing Holdings: Hold**

Vertiv, L3Harris, Intel, Broadcom, IPG Photonics, Agnico Eagle, and Innodata remain in the portfolio at current allocations. Broadcom's deployment will phase to 8% pending March 4 earnings confirmation (AI segment doubling, margins above 77%).

## Where We Stand

**Thematic Positioning**

The portfolio is now explicitly overweight to the intersection of three macroeconomic realities:

1. **The Power Deficit**: US electricity demand is rising faster than generation capacity, driven by hyperscaler compute buildout. Hyperscalers are committing $690 billion to capex in 2026 alone. This is confirmed by public announcements from Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta. The portfolio holds this theme through diversified power generation (18% in CEG and VST) and the supporting infrastructure layer (13% in Vertiv's cooling and power delivery systems, 5-8% in Broadcom's custom AI semiconductors). Collectively, this "AI electrification chain" represents 36-39% of NAV—a deliberate concentration accepted on the basis of genuine sub-theme diversification. The four names occupy genuinely different links in the power value chain and carry differentiated risk exposures.

2. **Defense Spending and Autonomous Warfare**: The Iran conflict has accelerated validation of the autonomous systems thesis. L3Harris (12% of NAV) owns missile solutions, night vision, and next-generation autonomous platforms. The company is on the cusp of spinning off its missiles business, unlocking significant valuation upside.

3. **Contrarian Alpha in Semiconductors and Industrial**: Intel (8%) benefits from the 18A process node launch and the Panther Lake architecture. The consensus median target is below current price—the contrarian setup persists. IPG Photonics (7%) saw earnings beat consensus by 156% in Q4 and is benefiting from a broad industrial recovery (ISM Manufacturing at 52.4). Both names have been marked down by the market relative to their catalysts.

**Diversification and Risk**

The portfolio retains meaningful hedges against the tech and power cluster concentration. Agnico Eagle (5% of NAV) is the strongest negative-correlation diversifier; gold has surged to record highs on Middle East escalation fears, and the company's all-in production cost of $1,339/oz against gold at $5,400/oz generates extraordinary margins. L3Harris also provides low-correlation diversification on the defense theme. Together, these positions and smaller uncorrelated holdings provide real portfolio resilience.

Cash represents 21-24% of NAV, preserving dry powder for opportunistic entries and managing downside scenarios.
