# Sterling — Portfolio Update

We have deployed our inaugural portfolio. Our thesis is straightforward: the artificial intelligence capital expenditure cycle represents the most significant private infrastructure buildout in history, and the companies enabling that buildout represent asymmetric opportunity. We are constructing a portfolio positioned for this capex super-cycle while maintaining dry powder to add on weakness.

## What Changed

This is Sterling's first investment committee cycle. We began with 100% cash and have approved five positions totaling 51% of net asset value, with two additional positions conditionally approved for deployment on price weakness. The portfolio reflects our conviction in the AI infrastructure narrative, balanced against disciplined risk-sizing and thematic diversification.

**Core positions deployed immediately:**
- **AI Infrastructure positions**: A meaningful allocation across the thermal management and custom silicon segments of the AI infrastructure buildout, with exposure to both emerging and established competitors. Entry prices currently at or near fair value; we are comfortable deploying now rather than waiting for pullbacks.
- **Contrarian turnaround**: A concentrated position in a semiconductor legacy player undertaking a fundamental restructuring, with government support backing the transition. This position carries elevated execution risk but offers asymmetric upside if the turnaround succeeds.
- **Defense diversifier**: A high-conviction defense position offering low correlation to the broader portfolio and meaningful exposure to a missile solutions catalyst in the second half of 2026. This position is structurally uncorrelated with our AI exposures.
- **Small-cap asymmetry**: An under-followed AI data engineering pure-play with the most favorable risk-reward ratio in the portfolio, sized appropriately for its liquidity profile.

**Phased entry:**
- **Custom silicon secondary tranche**: Our largest single position in custom silicon will deploy in two phases. The first tranche deploys immediately; the second tranche (representing the majority of the position) deploys after earnings confirmation on March 4. This structure manages binary event risk.

**Conditional positions held in reserve:**
- **Renewable energy infrastructure**: A high-quality industrial business with excellent foundational thesis but current valuation above our entry range. Approved for deployment on a pullback to $800–$850 or on positive earnings catalyst confirmation in April. If this position is promoted, we have committed to maintaining our AI infrastructure theme allocation at or below 30% by trimming an existing position.
- **Infrastructure services play**: A levered utility-like business that we view as a secondary beneficiary of energy infrastructure buildout. Approved at entry range of $510–$540 but currently above that level. Held for pullback.

## Where We Stand

**Portfolio composition at full Phase 1 and Phase 2 deployment:**
- **AI Infrastructure (26% combined)**: Our largest thematic exposure, representing both semiconductor supply and thermal management segments. This allocation sits modestly above our 25% guideline, a decision we have made deliberately given the scale and durability of the underlying capex cycle.
- **Defense / Aerospace (12%)**: Non-correlated diversifier with structural tailwinds and a near-term catalyst that could re-rate the entire subsector.
- **Contrarian Semiconductors (8%)**: Binary turnaround with government backstop and improving cash flow dynamics.
- **AI Services / Data Engineering (5%)**: Asymmetric small-cap with highest upside/downside ratio, sized conservatively pending customer diversification improvement.
- **Cash (49%)**: Elevated cash position is intentional. We are not chasing valuations at all-time highs; we are waiting for the conditional positions to reach our entry ranges, and we are maintaining firepower to add on portfolio stress events. The 49% cash buffer also means our worst-case drawdown in a severe AI/tech rotation scenario remains within acceptable bounds.

**Risk framework:**
- Single-name concentration is below our 15% guideline.
- Correlation between our two largest positions has been acknowledged and accepted, given the thesis quality and risk-reward ratios involved. Our defense position provides genuine diversification.
- We have estimated Value-at-Risk at 1.64% (95% confidence), within our 2.0% guideline.

## Looking Ahead

We are entering this portfolio with conviction in the secular tailwinds driving AI infrastructure capex, confidence in our entry prices and risk-reward ratios, and discipline around cash deployment. Our next major decision point arrives after AVGO earnings on March 4, which will determine whether we complete our second-largest position. Over the next 2–3 cycles, we expect to deploy the conditional positions and reduce cash below 30% through a combination of pullback deployments and new analyst pitches in defensive sectors. We are long the AI buildout, prudent about execution risk, and patient about valuation. The portfolio is positioned accordingly.
