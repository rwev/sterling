# Sterling — Portfolio Update

The AI infrastructure supercycle is real. We're rotating into it.

## What Changed

We've made a material strategic shift: six positions exited (Constellation Energy, Salesforce, Merck, Lockheed Martin, Argenx, Willdan), three positions resized down (Vistra, ServiceNow), and six new positions added (Broadcom, Coherent, NVIDIA, Tower Semiconductor, Vertiv, Marvell). This is not a tactical shuffle. It's a portfolio reordering.

The thesis is straightforward: hyperscaler capex commitments exceed $625 billion in 2026. That capital flows into silicon, power, cooling, and optical interconnect. Every GPU needs electricity, silicon, and the infrastructure to move data at scale. We're positioning across the full stack.

We are overweighting photonics — a conviction bet on 1.6 Tbps optical interconnect technology that moves data between AI clusters. This is supply-constrained, high-margin, and not yet priced in by consensus.

## Where We Stand

**Sector tilts:**
- Semiconductors dominate the portfolio (custom silicon at Broadcom, standardized GPU compute at NVIDIA, optical interconnect platforms).
- Nuclear power remains our highest-conviction single position; it's the only renewable energy source compatible with 24/7 hyperscale loads.
- Photonics (optical transceiver and silicon photonics foundries) is now a material portfolio theme.
- Enterprise software (ServiceNow) provides the only meaningful diversification — down-weighted but retained as a non-correlated position.
- Physical infrastructure (EMCOR's data center construction, Vertiv's cooling systems) captures the picks-and-shovels angle on AI deployment.

**Positioning:**
The portfolio is concentrated. We're at our maximum holding count. We've accepted higher volatility as a structural feature of maximum AI exposure. Risk management is active: we've defined contingencies for drawdown scenarios and position-specific stop-loss protocols. Risk limits have been reviewed and accepted by the committee.

**Cash:** We're beginning the rotation with a meaningful cash buffer that will deploy over the next 2–3 weeks as phased accumulations complete and market conditions confirm thesis catalysts.

## Looking Ahead

March brings two critical catalysts: Broadcom earnings (March 4) and the Optical Fiber Communication conference (March 17). Broadcom is our highest-conviction new position; the earnings report will validate or stress the AI backlog thesis. OFC will confirm photonics demand signals from hyperscalers. If both confirm, our thesis is on track. If either disappoints, we'll reassess positioning at our March 7 investment committee meeting.

The risk/reward for AI infrastructure is favorable if capex guidance holds. The risk is execution — can hyperscalers actually deploy at the projected pace? We believe the answer is yes. We're betting accordingly.
