# Sterling — Portfolio Update

The portfolio added three new positions and modestly trimmed two existing ones to resolve a single-month consolidation of directional risk and capitalize on AI-infrastructure dislocations. We are long power, long software resilience, and long infrastructure at exactly the moment when electricity demand meets capex constraints.

## What Changed

We added **Salesforce** at a deep discount to our target, rewarding its resilience through the AI demand disappointment and its ability to sell AI-native agents to enterprises faster than the market assumes. We added **EMCOR**, an infrastructure services compounder that is directly exposed to data center buildout with a fortress balance sheet and backlog growth that contradicts the selloff. We added **Willdan**, a micro-cap discovery play driven by insider conviction and a temporary tax-driven mispricing.

We trimmed **Vistra** by 2 percentage points and reduced **EMCOR's** initial allocation by 1 percentage point to maintain portfolio beta within tolerance while keeping both core theses intact. Cash increased to 21%, providing a buffer for downside volatility and future opportunistic adds.

Two pitches were rejected outright: **Materion** (mining/metals) failed the risk/reward test at current prices, and **Pathward** (fintech) was flagged for binary event risk with confirmation pending.

## Where We Stand

The portfolio is now 7 holdings across 4 thematic clusters: **power generation** dominates at 43% (Vistra + Constellation), both names riding AI-driven electricity demand; **enterprise software** sits at 22% (ServiceNow + Salesforce), a both-ways bet on AI adoption and franchise resilience; **infrastructure services** comprises 9% (EMCOR + Willdan), leveraging data center capex concentration; and **AI hardware** at 5% (Dell), a validation check on demand.

Portfolio beta is 0.98 — slightly below market — despite overweight power. Enterprise software concentration warrants active monitoring through March's BDC earnings; Salesforce's 7.9% FCF yield and $50B buyback program provide a structural floor. Vistra's 33% upside to target remains intact. Constellation's near-term decision point is March 31's outlook call; we are awaiting updated targets from our analyst.

The structure reflects conviction that AI capex will consume more electricity than consensus assumes, that software franchise value will consolidate toward best-in-class execution (not burn), and that infrastructure buildout lags demand by 12-18 months, creating a multi-quarter window for operational compounders.

## Looking Ahead

Near-term catalysts are dense: Constellation's March 31 guidance, Salesforce's guidance revision, EMCOR's data center RPO progression. We have directed analysts to surface non-AI-correlated ideas across healthcare, consumer staples, financials, and defense. The three open portfolio slots and 21% cash provide dry powder if volatility creates better entry points. Tail risk is concentrated in simultaneous power drawdowns; we are sized for that tail and prefer that risk to the alternative of being under-positioned into a capex supercycle.
