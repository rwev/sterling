## New Pitch Review

### Venture Global (VG) -- Growth Analyst Thesis

**Source**: Growth Analyst (2026-03-04) | **Current Price**: ~$10.96 | **Entry Range**: $10.00-$12.00 | **Target**: $15.50 | **Stop**: $8.50

**Thesis Summary**: VG is the second-largest US LNG exporter at the steepest phase of its capacity ramp. FY2025 revenue surged 177% to $13.8B, Q4 revenue +192% YoY to $4.45B, cargo volumes quadrupled. Plaquemines LNG Phase 1 targeting COD Q4 2026 with all 36 trains started. CP2 ($15.1B, 28 MTPA) construction on schedule. Structural tailwind from Hormuz closure removing ~20% of global LNG supply. Henry Hub/TTF spread at 5-6x normal. Goldman Sachs Buy rating, $15 PT.

**Fundamental Assessment**:

The growth story is legitimate. VG's capacity ramp is among the most aggressive in US energy infrastructure history -- from zero exports in 2022 to 380 cargoes in FY2025, with 486-527 guided for 2026. The Hormuz closure provides a structural demand tailwind that extends the duration of elevated pricing.

However, several concerns temper my enthusiasm:

1. **2026 EBITDA guidance ($5.2-5.8B) is BELOW FY2025 ($6.3B)**. This is a function of commissioning cargo recognition -- VG booked commissioning cargoes at full market value in 2025, inflating that year's EBITDA. The transition to commercial operations normalizes but lowers near-term EBITDA. This creates an optics problem where the "growth company" appears to be shrinking on the key profitability metric. Sophisticated investors understand this; the market broadly does not.

2. **$30.1B in total debt (288.8% debt-to-equity)** is staggering even for project-financed infrastructure. While non-recourse at the facility level, any impairment triggers contagion risk to equity sentiment. This is the highest leverage in our portfolio or conditional pipeline by a significant margin.

3. **The stock is down 6.5% on March 4 and trading near its 52-week low**. Put option volume surged 284% on March 3. Institutional sentiment is bearish near-term. This could be a contrarian entry signal, but it could also be smart money positioning ahead of lockup-related selling (January 2025 IPO).

4. **Shell/BP litigation**: While the NY court ruled in VG's favor on March 3 (Shell's challenge rejected), BP won its own arbitration case in October. Further legal challenges are possible and create an overhang.

5. **Overlap with existing conditional (LNG -- Cheniere Energy)**: Cheniere at $249 within its $245-260 entry range offers LNG exposure through a more mature, higher-quality platform with proven cash generation and $10B buyback. Cheniere is the blue-chip US LNG vehicle; VG is the higher-beta, earlier-stage version with more execution risk.

**R/R Calculation at $10.96**: Upside to $15.50 = $4.54 (41%). Downside to $8.50 = $2.46 (22%). R/R = 1.8:1.

**Displacement Analysis**: At 1.8:1 R/R, VG is superior to IPGP (1.4:1) and AEM (1.5:1), comparable to CEG (1.8:1) and LHX (1.6:1). However:
- IPGP provides non-correlated industrial/defense exposure with a fortress balance sheet (net cash $917M vs. VG's $30.1B debt). Retain IPGP.
- AEM provides gold hedge with zero correlation to the AI/energy chain that dominates the portfolio. At 5%, it is already undersized. Retain AEM.
- CEG has higher earnings quality, longer operational track record, lower execution risk. Retain CEG.
- LHX has active conflict validation and Missile Solutions IPO catalyst. Retain LHX.

None of the existing positions should be displaced for VG at current levels. The portfolio already has indirect LNG exposure via the energy chain (FRO benefits from the same Hormuz dynamics) and Cheniere (LNG) sits in the conditional pipeline as a cleaner LNG vehicle.

**Risk Manager considerations for VG**:
- At 7% allocation, VG would add to Hormuz-dependent concentration (already 7% via FRO). Combined 14% Hormuz exposure would exceed the Risk Manager's geopolitical concentration limit.
- VG's $30.1B debt makes it the highest-leverage name in the portfolio by far, adding credit risk in a rising HY OAS environment (>4.0% per Risk Manager Cycle VII).
- Correlation with FRO is high (both benefit from Hormuz closure). Adding VG while holding FRO violates the principle of diversifying against correlated blow-up risk.

**Decision: CONDITIONALLY APPROVED.**

VG has genuine merit. The capacity ramp is real, the Hormuz tailwind is durable (war powers vote failure extends the timeline), and the R/R at 1.8:1 is acceptable. But this is not the right moment for deployment:

- Portfolio full at 10/10 with no clear displacement candidate
- Cheniere (LNG) in the conditional pipeline is the superior LNG vehicle at comparable R/R with lower execution risk
- VG + FRO would double Hormuz-dependent concentration (14% vs. 7% limit)
- $30.1B debt is a significant risk factor in a widening credit spread environment
- VIX at 24.19 argues against aggressive new deployment

**Deployment conditions**: (1) FRO exits (stop or pre-stop trigger) AND Cheniere is deployed at 7% to replace FRO, freeing the "Hormuz-dependent" label for LNG. At that point, VG could be considered as a second LNG play at 5% if portfolio capacity permits. OR (2) The stock pulls back to $9.00-$10.00 AND the R/R improves to 2.5:1+, making the risk/reward compelling enough to justify the leverage and execution risk as a standalone deployment.

Entry range: $9.00-$11.00 (tightened from analyst's $10.00-$12.00 to reflect the leverage risk). Target: $15.50. Stop: $8.50. Max sizing: 5% (reduced from standard to account for leverage).
