# Long Thesis: Salesforce (CRM)

## Investment Summary

Salesforce is the world's largest enterprise CRM platform, generating $41.5 billion in FY2026 revenue with $12.4 billion in free cash flow, now trading at $196 -- down 34% from its 52-week high of $298 and caught in the SaaS selloff. At a forward P/E of ~15x and EV/EBITDA of ~14x, Salesforce trades at roughly half its 5-year average multiple for a business that grew revenue 10% in FY2026, guided FY2027 revenue to $45.8-46.2B (+10-11%), and built Agentforce/Data 360 into a $1.5 billion ARR business in its first full year. The company returned over $14 billion to shareholders in FY2026 through buybacks and dividends. While the FY2027 revenue guide failed to excite AI re-acceleration bulls, the quality of the underlying business -- $12B+ FCF, 35% non-GAAP operating margins, and 150,000+ enterprise customers -- at a mid-teens earnings multiple represents a compelling risk/reward for the long book.

## Business Overview

Salesforce is the dominant CRM platform globally, with approximately 23% market share -- more than the next four competitors combined. The business operates across five clouds:

- **Sales Cloud**: Core CRM functionality for sales teams. The original product and still the largest revenue contributor.
- **Service Cloud**: Customer service and support platform. Growing as companies consolidate service workflows.
- **Platform/Other (includes Agentforce)**: The Salesforce platform, MuleSoft integration, and the new Agentforce AI agent product. Agentforce and Data 360 reached $1.5B ARR by end of FY2026, growing over 200% y/y.
- **Marketing & Commerce Cloud**: Digital marketing automation, e-commerce, and customer data platform.
- **Data Cloud / Tableau / Slack**: Analytics, data integration, and collaboration tools. Includes the Informatica acquisition ($399M Q4 revenue contribution).

The company has 150,000+ customers ranging from SMBs to the world's largest enterprises. The platform's breadth creates deep ecosystem lock-in: once a company builds its customer data, workflows, and integrations on Salesforce, switching costs are enormous.

## Investment Case

**1. Platform moat is wide and deepening.** Salesforce is not a point solution vulnerable to AI replacement. It is the system of record for customer data, the orchestration layer for go-to-market workflows, and the integration hub connecting dozens of enterprise applications. AI agents (Agentforce) run on top of Salesforce data and workflows -- they do not replace the platform, they extend it. BofA called the notion that AI replaces Salesforce "illogical" and a "paradox."

**2. Agentforce is a real growth vector.** The $1.5B ARR in Agentforce/Data 360 (up 200%+ y/y) demonstrates that Salesforce's AI strategy is gaining traction. The Formula 1 partnership announced March 3 showcases Agentforce's ability to personalize engagement at scale, reducing customer service response times by 80%. Unlike many SaaS companies claiming AI revenue, Salesforce has measurable, disclosed ARR from AI products.

**3. Margin expansion story is intact.** Non-GAAP operating margins reached 35.5% in Q3 FY2026, guided to 34% for the full year. GAAP operating margins expanded from 14.4% to 21.6% in FY2026. This margin expansion is structural, driven by platform scale, reduced hiring pace, and AI-driven internal productivity. Management has a path to 40%+ non-GAAP margins by FY2030.

**4. Massive free cash flow generation.** FCF grew from $9.5B in FY2024 to $12.4B in FY2025, with further expansion expected. At $196, the FCF yield is approximately 6.5% on FY2025 FCF -- extraordinary for a business growing 10%+ with 35% operating margins.

**5. Shareholder returns are accelerating.** $14B+ returned in FY2026 through buybacks and dividends. The company initiated a dividend and has been aggressively repurchasing shares, reducing share count. At current depressed prices, buybacks are highly accretive.

**6. Valuation is at a decade low.** The forward P/E of ~15x is lower than the S&P 500's. Salesforce grew revenue 10% with 35% non-GAAP margins and $12B+ FCF -- this is not a slow-growth value trap. The market is pricing Salesforce as if AI will disrupt its business when AI is actually expanding it.

## Earnings Quality

- **FCF conversion is best-in-class.** Salesforce converts over 95% of EBITDA to free cash flow, driven by subscription-based revenue recognition, negative working capital (deferred revenue exceeds receivables), and modest capex requirements (~4-5% of revenue).

- **GAAP/non-GAAP convergence improving.** The gap between GAAP and non-GAAP operating margins has narrowed from ~20 points to ~13 points as SBC as a percentage of revenue declines. Management is committed to further convergence.

- **Revenue quality is high.** Over 95% of revenue is recurring (subscription + support). Remaining performance obligation (RPO) provides forward visibility. Current RPO growth of ~14% y/y in Q4 FY2026 supports the FY2027 guidance.

- **Informatica integration.** The $399M Q4 revenue contribution from Informatica is an inorganic addition. Excluding Informatica, organic growth was approximately 8% in Q4. This is a modest headwind to the organic growth narrative but the integration is strategically sound (data management for AI workloads).

## Risk Factors

1. **AI disruption to CRM workflows.** If AI agents can autonomously manage customer relationships without a centralized CRM platform, Salesforce's moat weakens. Counter: Salesforce's data moat (150,000+ customer implementations, years of proprietary customer data) is the foundation AI agents need. The platform is the data layer AI runs on, not a function AI replaces.

2. **Revenue growth deceleration.** FY2027 guidance of 10-11% is steady but did not show the AI-driven re-acceleration bulls hoped for. If growth slips to mid-single digits, the quality premium erodes. Counter: The current FY2027 guidance is intentionally conservative (Salesforce has beaten guidance 90%+ of quarters). Agentforce ARR of $1.5B growing 200%+ is not yet meaningfully reflected in revenue guidance.

3. **SaaS selloff persists.** With the IGV ETF down 30% and sector RSI at 18, the selling pressure may continue regardless of fundamentals. Counter: At a forward P/E below the S&P 500, much of the selling pressure is already reflected. Institutional buyers (Warren Buffett's Berkshire added CRM in 2024) provide buying support at these valuations.

4. **Informatica integration risk.** Large acquisitions carry execution risk. If Informatica underperforms or distracts management, it could weigh on results. Counter: Salesforce has a strong track record integrating acquisitions (Slack, Tableau, MuleSoft) and Informatica's data management capabilities are directly complementary to the AI/Data Cloud strategy.

5. **Competition from Microsoft Dynamics/Copilot.** Microsoft's bundling of CRM with its productivity suite and AI copilot could pressure Salesforce in the mid-market. Counter: Salesforce has defended its enterprise position against Microsoft for over a decade. The switching cost moat protects existing customers.
