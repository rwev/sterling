# Contrarian Long Thesis: Atlassian (TEAM)

## Investment Summary

Atlassian has been destroyed -- down 83% from its all-time high and 49% year-to-date -- yet the business is growing 23% year-over-year, just delivered its first billion-dollar cloud revenue quarter, and maintains net revenue retention above 120%. The market cap has collapsed from $136 billion to approximately $15 billion, pricing in a scenario where developer workflow software becomes obsolete. The forensic evidence says the opposite: Atlassian's Q2 FY2026 results showed record RPO growth of 44% year-over-year to $3.8 billion, non-GAAP EPS of $1.22 (67% above consensus), and a clear path to 25%+ non-GAAP operating margins by FY2027. At $76, this is a 20%+ compounder trading at 2.5x revenue -- a valuation that would be cheap for a company growing at half this rate. With 25 analysts rating it Buy and a median price target of $175-$222 (130-190% upside), the asymmetry is extraordinary.

## Why the Market Is Wrong

Atlassian is a casualty of the SaaSPocalypse, but the selloff has no basis in the company's actual performance. The market is making three errors:

1. **Conflating task-level AI automation with platform displacement.** AI can automate individual tasks -- writing code, summarizing tickets, triaging bugs. But Atlassian is not a task tool; it is the workflow orchestration platform for software development teams. Jira, Confluence, and Bitbucket form the connective tissue of how engineering organizations plan, track, build, and ship software. AI makes these platforms more valuable by automating work within them -- which is exactly what Atlassian's "agents in Jira" feature does, assigning AI agents to tasks alongside human engineers.

2. **Ignoring the cloud migration tailwind.** Atlassian's cloud migration (from server/data center to cloud) is a multi-year revenue accelerator that has nothing to do with AI disruption fears. Cloud revenue hit $1 billion for the first time in Q2 FY2026, growing 26% year-over-year. This migration drives higher per-user revenue, better retention, and expanding margins. The market is pricing in disruption while the company is executing a structural revenue quality upgrade.

3. **Punishing GAAP losses while ignoring FCF reality.** Atlassian generated $1.4 billion in free cash flow in FY2025 -- a company trading at $15 billion market cap is generating nearly 10% FCF yield. The GAAP losses are driven by stock-based compensation and strategic investments, not operational weakness. Non-GAAP operating income swung from negative $32 million to positive $323 million in recent quarters. The path to profitability is not a hope -- it is happening.

## Business Overview

Atlassian provides collaboration and project management software for software development and IT teams. Its core products -- Jira (project tracking), Confluence (knowledge management), Bitbucket (source code management), and Jira Service Management (ITSM) -- serve over 350,000 customers, including more than 80% of the Fortune 500. The company operates a distinctive low-touch, product-led growth model with minimal direct sales force, resulting in industry-leading efficiency.

Atlassian's fiscal year ends June 30. The company is mid-stream in a multi-year cloud migration that is transforming its revenue profile from lower-margin server/data center licenses to higher-margin, stickier cloud subscriptions. Jira Service Management has passed 65,000 customers with enterprise growth exceeding 60% year-over-year, representing a significant expansion into the ITSM market traditionally dominated by ServiceNow.

## Earnings Quality & Hidden Value

**FCF yield of approximately 10% at a 23% grower.** Atlassian generated $1.42 billion in free cash flow in FY2025 on a market cap of approximately $15 billion. This 9.5% FCF yield is remarkable for a company growing revenue at 23%. The FCF is real, recurring, and growing -- it is not inflated by working capital timing or one-time items.

**RPO acceleration tells the forward story.** Remaining performance obligations grew 44% year-over-year to $3.8 billion in Q2 FY2026. RPO is contracted future revenue -- it provides visibility that revenue growth will sustain at 20%+ through FY2027. The market is pricing in deceleration that the backlog directly contradicts.

**The GAAP loss is misleading.** The operating loss on a GAAP basis is driven by stock-based compensation. Non-GAAP operating income reached $323 million in Q2 FY2026, and management is targeting 25%+ non-GAAP operating margins by FY2027. The company is not unprofitable -- it is a highly profitable business that compensates employees with equity, and the market is punishing the GAAP optics while ignoring the cash flow reality.

**Cloud migration is a hidden earnings accelerator.** As customers migrate from server/data center to cloud, Atlassian captures higher per-user revenue and better retention. Cloud revenue of $1 billion per quarter (growing 26%) is displacing lower-margin legacy revenue. This mix shift will drive margin expansion for years.

**New CFO appointment (March 30, 2026).** James Chuong, a veteran finance leader, takes over as CFO. Fresh financial leadership often signals an inflection toward shareholder-friendly capital allocation and clearer profitability messaging -- both of which the stock desperately needs.

## Margin of Safety

At $76, Atlassian trades at:
- P/S of approximately 2.5x LTM revenue -- compared to its 5-year average of 15-20x
- EV/FCF of approximately 10x -- compared to its 5-year average of 60-80x
- P/FCF of approximately 10.5x -- compared to SaaS peers at 25-40x

The margin of safety is derived from: (1) a valuation at 83% below peak that prices in near-zero terminal growth, (2) $1.4 billion in annual FCF providing a hard floor of intrinsic value, (3) RPO of $3.8 billion providing contracted revenue visibility, and (4) 120%+ NRR ensuring the existing customer base continues expanding.

Even in a bear scenario where revenue growth slows to 12% and FCF margins compress to 20%, Atlassian would generate approximately $1.5 billion in FCF on $7.5 billion of revenue by FY2028. At a conservative 15x FCF, that implies a stock price of approximately $90 -- still above current levels. The downside is limited; the upside, if multiples normalize even partially, is 130-190%.

## Bear Case Rebuttal

**"AI will replace developer tools."** AI coding assistants (GitHub Copilot, Cursor) automate code writing, not project management. Software teams still need to plan sprints, track bugs, manage releases, and coordinate across functions. Atlassian's "agents in Jira" feature demonstrates the correct framing: AI agents work within Atlassian's platform, making it more indispensable, not less. The 7.2% stock pop on the agents-in-Jira announcement shows the market is beginning to understand this.

**"Atlassian is not profitable."** On a GAAP basis, correct. On a cash basis, the company generates $1.4 billion in annual FCF. Non-GAAP operating income is positive and expanding. The path to 25%+ non-GAAP operating margins by FY2027 is credible given the cloud migration mix shift and operating leverage inherent in the platform model.

**"The stock could fall further."** Possible -- sentiment can remain irrational. But at 2.5x revenue and 10x FCF for a 23% grower, the valuation already embeds an extreme bear case. The risk/reward is asymmetric: limited additional downside versus 130%+ upside on any normalization.

**"Competition from ServiceNow, Monday.com, Linear."** Atlassian's developer-centric moat is deep. Jira is the standard in enterprise software development, embedded in CI/CD pipelines, integrated with GitHub/GitLab, and used by 80% of the Fortune 500. Switching costs are enormous. The competitive threat is real for point solutions, but Atlassian's platform breadth (Jira + Confluence + Bitbucket + JSM) creates a bundle that competitors cannot replicate.

## Risk Factors

1. **Extended SaaS bear market.** The sector could remain out of favor for multiple quarters. Atlassian's beta to the IGV ETF means it could continue to fall even if fundamentals improve.
2. **AI disruption risk to point features.** While the platform thesis is sound, individual features (basic project tracking, documentation) could see usage erosion from AI-native alternatives. Monitor monthly active user growth and product-level engagement.
3. **Stock-based compensation dilution.** SBC remains elevated. Without a buyback program, share count dilution will continue to weigh on per-share value creation.
4. **CFO transition risk.** Leadership transitions create uncertainty. The outgoing CFO's departure and Chuong's onboarding could introduce a quarter of operational noise.
5. **Cloud migration slowdown.** If the server-to-cloud migration decelerates, the margin expansion story weakens. Monitor cloud revenue growth rate versus data center revenue.
