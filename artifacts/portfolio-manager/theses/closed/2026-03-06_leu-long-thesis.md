# Growth Long Thesis: Centrus Energy (LEU)

## Investment Summary

Centrus Energy is the only US-licensed uranium enrichment company, holding a monopoly position on domestic HALEU (High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium) production at precisely the moment when three structural forces are converging: the Section 232 designation of uranium as a national security asset (January 2026), the ban on Russian enriched uranium imports (phasing out by 2028), and the Hormuz crisis accelerating the energy security imperative for nuclear power. The company has secured $900M in DOE HALEU contracts, announced a $560M Oak Ridge centrifuge manufacturing expansion, and holds a $3.8B backlog extending to 2040. While 2026 revenue guidance of $425-475M appears flat, the business is transforming from a uranium trading intermediary to a domestic enrichment producer -- a shift that will unlock structurally higher margins and a valuation re-rating over the next 2-3 years. At $196, the stock trades at 50x trailing earnings, but on a PEG basis against the backlog conversion trajectory, the growth is not priced in.

## Growth Inflection Case

The growth inflection for Centrus Energy is not visible in current quarterly revenue trends, which are lumpy and driven by contract delivery timing. The inflection is in the business model itself -- a transformation from middleman to monopoly producer that is being funded by the US government and mandated by national security policy.

1. **Business model transformation from trader to producer.** Centrus has historically operated as a uranium enrichment services intermediary, purchasing SWU (separative work units) from foreign suppliers and reselling to US utilities. This is a low-margin, working-capital-intensive business. The company is now transitioning to domestic enrichment production at its Piketon, Ohio facility. In December 2025, Centrus began domestic centrifuge manufacturing -- the first US centrifuge production in decades. The first new cascade is expected online in 2029, with commercial-scale HALEU production by end of decade and 12 metric tons/year thereafter. This transition from trading to production will drive gross margins from the current 26% toward 40-50%+ over a 3-5 year period.

2. **Government-funded capacity buildout creates a cost-free growth engine.** The $900M DOE HALEU task order (with $170M additional options, potentially exceeding $1B) funds the Piketon expansion. The $560M Oak Ridge centrifuge manufacturing expansion creates the supply chain for thousands of advanced centrifuges. This capex is largely government-funded or government-backstopped, meaning Centrus is building a multi-billion-dollar enrichment capability with minimal equity dilution. The company's $2.0B cash position (bolstered by an $805M convertible note issuance in August 2025) provides ample runway.

3. **Russian supply elimination creates a structural demand gap.** Russia currently provides approximately 20% of US enriched uranium. The 2024 ban phases out Russian deliveries by 2028. There is no other Western HALEU producer. Centrus is literally the only entity in the Western world that can produce HALEU for advanced reactors. This is a policy-created monopoly with multi-decade duration.

4. **Hormuz crisis accelerates the nuclear energy security narrative.** With Brent at $93 and gas-fired electricity costs rising, nuclear power's zero fuel-cost sensitivity to fossil fuels becomes a strategic advantage. The Hormuz crisis has shifted the nuclear conversation from "clean energy option" to "energy security imperative." This strengthens the political and regulatory tailwind for uranium enrichment investment, including potential Section 232 import restrictions that would further benefit domestic producers.

## Business Overview & TAM

Centrus Energy operates in two segments:

**LEU Segment ($346M revenue, 2025):** Sells low-enriched uranium and SWU to domestic and international nuclear utilities under long-term contracts. The segment holds a $2.3B contingent backlog of LEU sales extending to 2040.

**Technical Solutions Segment ($103M revenue, 2025):** Provides engineering, manufacturing, and technical services, primarily to the US government. Includes the HALEU production operations at Piketon and the Oak Ridge centrifuge manufacturing. Revenue grew 11% in 2025, driven by HALEU contract expansion.

**Total Addressable Market:** The global uranium enrichment services market is approximately $10-12B annually. The US consumes approximately 40 million SWU per year, with domestic enrichment currently at zero (all imported). The DOE's goal to triple US nuclear energy production by mid-century would roughly triple domestic SWU demand. The HALEU market specifically is nascent but projected to reach $2-3B annually by 2035 as advanced reactor designs (X-energy, TerraPower, Kairos) move to commercial deployment. Centrus is the sole Western producer.

**Backlog as a revenue bridge:** Total backlog of $3.8B extending to 2040 provides revenue visibility far exceeding typical growth companies. The LEU segment backlog of $2.3B (contingent on Centrus achieving domestic production capability) represents the growth option embedded in the business model transformation.

## Business Drivers

1. **$900M DOE HALEU award (January 2026).** The single largest catalyst. This funds commercial-scale HALEU production at Piketon. The contract could grow to $1B+ with options. Centrus delivered 900 kg of HALEU UF6 to DOE in 2025 and produced over 1 metric ton by year-end, demonstrating production capability. The award validates Centrus as the DOE's primary domestic enrichment partner.

2. **$560M Oak Ridge centrifuge manufacturing expansion (January 2026).** Creates nearly 430 new jobs and transitions the Oak Ridge facility to a high-rate manufacturing plant producing thousands of advanced centrifuges. First centrifuges expected online at Piketon in 2029. This is the physical infrastructure that converts the enrichment license into production capability.

3. **Russian uranium ban phasing out by 2028.** Eliminates approximately 20% of US enriched uranium supply. Utilities are already contracting with Western suppliers for replacement volumes. Centrus's $2.3B contingent LEU backlog directly benefits from this supply chain restructuring.

4. **Section 232 national security designation (January 2026).** Designating uranium as a national security asset opens the door to import restrictions, price floors, and government equity stakes in domestic production. If the administration imposes tariffs on imported enriched uranium, Centrus as the sole domestic enrichment licensee would be the primary beneficiary.

5. **Uranium spot price above $101/lb.** Up 24% in January 2026 alone. Elevated uranium prices increase the value of Centrus's inventory, improve contract economics on new bookings, and create urgency for utility customers to secure long-term supply from Western producers.

6. **Hormuz crisis strengthens nuclear energy security narrative.** With oil at $93 and gas costs rising, the political case for expanding nuclear capacity becomes compelling across party lines. Nuclear fuel costs are approximately $5-7/MWh regardless of fossil fuel prices. Every dollar higher in natural gas prices widens the economic advantage of nuclear power and strengthens the case for domestic uranium enrichment.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

The margin path for Centrus is the single most important variable in the growth thesis. Today's margin profile reflects a trading business: buy SWU from foreign suppliers, mark up, resell to utilities. Gross margins are 26% and operating margins are 11%.

The transformation to domestic production fundamentally changes the cost structure:

- **Current model (trading):** COGS is driven by purchased SWU costs. Margins are thin and dependent on spread between purchase and sale prices. Working capital requirements are high.
- **Future model (production):** COGS shifts to operating costs of centrifuge cascades (electricity, maintenance, labor). Once centrifuges are online, marginal production cost per SWU drops significantly. Gross margins for domestic enrichment are estimated at 40-55% based on historical USEC operations and international comparators (Urenco reports 30-40% operating margins).

**Timeline:** First commercial cascade online in 2029. Margin inflection begins 2029-2030. Full production scale (12 MT HALEU/year) post-2030. The stock must be owned before the margin inflection becomes visible in reported numbers.

**Near-term margin risk:** Q3 2025 showed -22% operating margin due to timing of SWU deliveries and pricing. This quarterly volatility will persist until the production transition is complete. Investors must look through quarterly noise to the structural trajectory.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Centrus Energy's moat is arguably the widest of any company in Sterling's coverage universe:

1. **Sole US enrichment license.** Centrus holds the only active NRC license for uranium enrichment in the United States. Obtaining a new license requires years of regulatory review and billions of dollars in capital. No competitor is close to challenging this position.

2. **HALEU monopoly.** Centrus is the only entity in the Western world producing HALEU. Advanced reactor designs from X-energy, TerraPower, and Kairos Power require HALEU fuel that cannot be sourced from any other non-Russian supplier. This is a policy-mandated monopoly.

3. **$3.8B backlog.** Multi-decade contracts with utilities and the DOE provide revenue visibility and reduce business risk. The contingent nature of $2.3B in LEU contracts (contingent on achieving domestic production) creates a powerful incentive to execute the enrichment buildout.

4. **Government alignment.** The DOE, DOD, and bipartisan Congressional support for domestic uranium enrichment create a regulatory moat that protects Centrus from foreign competition. The Section 232 designation elevates uranium to the same national security status as steel and aluminum.

5. **Centrifuge technology.** Centrus's American Centrifuge technology was developed with $5B+ in cumulative DOE/USEC investment over decades. Replicating this technology would require similar investment and decades of development.

## Risk Factors

1. **Execution risk on enrichment buildout.** The transition from demonstration-scale HALEU production (1 metric ton) to commercial-scale enrichment (12 MT/year) is a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar engineering challenge. Delays, cost overruns, or technical failures would defer the margin inflection indefinitely.

2. **2026 guidance appears flat.** Revenue guidance of $425-475M versus $449M in 2025 suggests no near-term growth acceleration. The stock could underperform while investors wait for the 2029+ production inflection.

3. **Quarterly earnings volatility.** Revenue swings from $73M to $155M quarter-to-quarter based on contract delivery timing. Q3 2025 posted a -22% operating margin. This volatility can create sharp sell-offs on "miss" quarters even when the full-year trajectory is intact.

4. **Nuclear policy reversal risk.** A change in administration or political priorities could reduce government support for domestic enrichment. However, the bipartisan nature of energy security and the DOE's multi-year contract commitments reduce this risk.

5. **Uranium price sensitivity.** If uranium prices decline from $101/lb to $70-$80/lb, the urgency for domestic enrichment capacity diminishes, and Centrus's inventory values decline. However, the Russian supply ban creates a structural floor for Western uranium prices.

6. **Convertible note dilution.** The $805M in 0% convertible notes (due 2032) represent potential dilution. Conversion terms would need to be monitored relative to share price trajectory.
