# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Ducommun Incorporated (DCO)

## Investment Summary

Ducommun is a $1.9 billion defense and aerospace sub-supplier generating record revenue, record margins, and a record $1.2 billion backlog driven by missile programs that are at the center of the Iran conflict munition replacement cycle. The stock trades at 29.8x forward earnings -- a steep discount to peers HEICO (55x) and TransDigm (30x+) -- despite delivering four consecutive quarters of double-digit military/space growth and a clear path to its VISION 2027 targets of $950M-$1B revenue at 18% adjusted EBITDA margins. With only 7 analysts covering the name, 8.9% insider ownership, and the company presenting at the Sidoti Small Cap Conference on March 18, DCO is a textbook discovery play hiding behind the larger defense primes.

## Business Overview

Ducommun Incorporated is a Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier of advanced electronic systems and structural systems to the aerospace, defense, and industrial markets. Founded in 1849 and headquartered in Santa Ana, California, the company is one of the oldest continuously operating companies in the United States. It operates through two segments:

- **Electronic Systems**: Designs, manufactures, and integrates electronic assemblies, interconnect systems, lightning diversion systems, and printed circuit board assemblies for military and commercial platforms. This segment produces mission-critical components for the F-35, missile guidance systems, radar, and space applications.
- **Structural Systems**: Manufactures large, complex contoured aerostructures, advanced composite structures, and metal bond assemblies. Products appear on virtually every major commercial and military aircraft platform, including the Boeing 737 MAX, Airbus A220, F-35, and various missile platforms.

Key defense programs include: F-35 (Lockheed Martin), Tomahawk cruise missiles (RTX), AMRAAM air-to-air missiles, Standard Missiles (RTX), THAAD ballistic missile defense (Lockheed Martin), and the classified MIR program (awarded $80M+ in 2025). Defense and military/space revenue reached $480M in FY2025, approximately 58% of total revenue, and has been the primary growth engine for four consecutive quarters.

Commercial aerospace accounts for approximately 35% of revenue and is recovering post-COVID, with increasing rates on the 737 MAX and A220. The remaining ~7% comes from industrial applications.

- **Founder involvement**: While not founder-led, Ducommun was founded in 1849 and retains the family name. The Ducommun family still holds a legacy interest. CEO Oswald has led the company since 2017 and has driven the transformation from a commoditized aerostructures supplier to a value-added defense electronics and missile systems company.
- **Capital allocation track record**: Oswald has executed a disciplined strategy of bolt-on acquisitions (BLR Aerospace, Magnetic Seal), organic investment in defense capabilities, and debt reduction. Total debt has been reduced from $378M (2019) to $304M (2025) while revenue grew from $636M to $825M. The company does not pay dividends, reinvesting all cash flow into growth and debt reduction -- appropriate at this stage of the compounding cycle.
- **Incentive alignment**: Executive compensation is heavily weighted toward adjusted EBITDA growth and adjusted EPS targets, directly aligned with shareholder value creation. The VISION 2027 framework ($950M-$1B revenue, 18% adjusted EBITDA margin) provides clear, measurable accountability.
- **Recent insider activity**: Net insider selling of ~9,864 shares over the past 18 months, primarily from CEO Oswald (3,200 shares). This is routine portfolio management for a CEO with $43M in stock holdings -- a ~7% trim of total position. No cluster selling or unusual activity.

## Risk Factors

1. **Ceasefire or diplomatic resolution with Iran**: A rapid de-escalation would remove the urgency behind munition replacement spending. However, the structural NATO rearmament cycle (3% GDP targets) and the depleted US munition inventory provide a multi-year floor for defense spending regardless of the Iran outcome.

2. **Defense budget sequestration or DOGE-driven cuts**: The current political environment prioritizes defense spending, but a shift toward fiscal austerity could slow procurement timelines. DCO's backlog of $1.2B provides 1.5 years of revenue visibility even in a slowdown scenario.

3. **Single-program concentration risk**: The MIR program ($80M+) and missile franchise concentration means a program cancellation or delay could materially impact revenue. Mitigation: DCO serves across 20+ defense platforms and both electronic and structural systems.

4. **Margin expansion execution risk**: VISION 2027 targets 18% adjusted EBITDA margin vs. 16.4% currently. Failure to achieve this through facility consolidation and mix shift would leave the stock at its current discount. However, the 530 bps gross margin expansion over two years demonstrates management's execution capability.

5. **Illiquidity**: Average daily volume of ~43,000 shares ($5.5M/day) means larger institutional investors face execution risk. This illiquidity also amplifies downside moves on negative surprises.

6. **ROIC below cost of capital**: At ~5.3% ROIC vs. ~10.8% WACC, DCO is not currently earning above its cost of capital. The VISION 2027 margin expansion and revenue growth are required to close this gap. If they fail, the stock may not rerate.
