# Contrarian Long Thesis: NuScale Power Corporation (SMR)

## Investment Summary

NuScale Power, the only company with an NRC-approved small modular reactor design, trades at $13.00 -- down 77% from its 52-week high of $57.42 -- despite a strengthening commercial pipeline that includes a landmark 6 GW ENTRA1/TVA deployment agreement (the largest planned SMR program in US history), Romania's final investment decision for a 462 MW six-module plant, and 12 modules already in production with Doosan. The stock carries 21.6% short interest and a "Hold" consensus rating, reflecting deep market skepticism about commercialization timelines. With $1.3B in liquidity providing 6+ years of runway at current burn rates, and a structural demand environment where hyperscalers are signing multi-billion-dollar nuclear PPAs, NuScale has the funding, the technology certification, and the demand tailwind to reach commercial deployment. At $13, the market is pricing NuScale as a likely failure -- but the risk/reward is asymmetric: the downside is bounded by the cash position, while the upside in a successful commercialization scenario is multiples of the current price.

**Important caveat**: This is a speculative, pre-revenue company with binary risk. Position sizing must reflect that reality. This is not a core value holding -- it is a calculated option on the nuclear renaissance with defined risk parameters.

## Why the Market Is Wrong

The market's primary concern -- that NuScale cannot convert its NRC approval into commercial revenue -- was reasonable in 2023 when the UAMPS Carbon Free Power Project was cancelled. But the landscape has shifted materially since then:

1. **The ENTRA1/TVA 6 GW agreement (September 2025)** is not a science project. TVA is a $12B-revenue federal utility that operates the nation's third-largest nuclear fleet. The collaborative agreement to deploy six NuScale-powered plants across TVA's seven-state region -- enough to power 4.5 million homes or 60 data centers -- represents a credible path to multi-GW-scale deployment. NuScale recognized $507.4M in milestone contributions under its Partnership Milestone Agreement with ENTRA1, demonstrating that this partnership has financial substance.

2. **Romania's Doicesti project reached Final Investment Decision (February 2026)**. Nuclearelectrica's shareholders approved the 462 MW six-module project at the former Doicesti coal site, with a budget estimated at EUR 4.9B. Pre-EPC work is underway with geotechnical investigations, licensing, and long-lead equipment procurement expected by May 2026. This is no longer a memorandum of understanding -- it is an approved capital project.

3. **The demand environment has fundamentally changed.** When UAMPS collapsed in November 2023, data center power demand was a theoretical future concern. Today, Microsoft has signed a $1.6B nuclear restart PPA with Constellation, Amazon signed an $18B nuclear PPA with Talen, and Meta signed 6.6 GW of nuclear deals with Oklo, Vistra, and TerraPower. The hyperscaler commitment to nuclear is real and accelerating. NuScale's modular design -- deployable in smaller increments, on brownfield sites, behind the meter -- addresses the exact pain point these customers face.

4. **The $1.3B cash position eliminates near-term survival risk.** After raising $750M through ATM issuance in Q4 2025, NuScale has sufficient liquidity for 6+ years of operations at current burn rates (~$200M/year). The market's implicit assumption that NuScale will run out of money before reaching commercialization is incorrect on the math.

The 21.6% short interest (vs. 9.0% peer average) reflects crowded bearish positioning. Any positive commercial milestone -- a binding contract, a DOE loan guarantee, or a confirmed deployment timeline -- could trigger a significant short squeeze.

## Business Overview

NuScale Power is a pre-revenue nuclear technology company focused on small modular reactor design, licensing, and commercialization:

- **Technology**: NuScale Power Module (NPM) -- 77 MWe integral pressurized water reactor; scalable from single module (77 MWe) to 12-module plants (924 MWe)
- **Regulatory status**: Only SMR with NRC Standard Design Approval (77 MWe approved ahead of schedule)
- **Commercialization partner**: ENTRA1 Energy (exclusive global commercialization partner)
- **Manufacturing**: 12 modules in production with Doosan; capacity ramping to ~20 modules/year
- **Key projects**: ENTRA1/TVA 6 GW program (US), RoPower Doicesti 462 MW (Romania)
- **Employees**: ~500 (post-resource optimization)
- **Market cap**: ~$4.0B

## Earnings Quality & Hidden Value

**Revenue is misleading as a performance indicator.** NuScale's revenue is driven by licensing fees and engineering milestone payments, primarily from the Romania FEED 2 contract ($63.1M recognized over 18 months ending December 2025). As FEED 2 wound down, Q4 2025 revenue plummeted. This is not demand destruction -- it is the lumpy nature of pre-commercial project revenue. The transition to production-phase revenue (module manufacturing, licensing fees from ENTRA1 deployments) will create a step-change in the revenue profile, but not until 2028-2029 at the earliest.

**The $507.4M ENTRA1 milestone contribution is the hidden value.** Under the Partnership Milestone Agreement, NuScale receives milestone payments as ENTRA1 hits commercialization targets. The $507.4M recognized from Milestone Contribution 1 (triggered by the TVA agreement) demonstrates that the ENTRA1 relationship has concrete economic value. Future milestones tied to plant construction starts and commercial operation dates could generate additional hundreds of millions.

**Operating expense discipline is real.** The January 2024 resource optimization plan reduced annualized expenses by $50-60M, and opex has stabilized in the $172-200M range. For a company developing first-of-a-kind nuclear technology, this is a lean operation.

**The cash position is the floor.** At $1.3B in cash against a $4.0B market cap, roughly 33% of the equity value is cash. Net of cash, the market is valuing NuScale's technology, NRC approval, ENTRA1 partnership, Romania project, and 12 modules in production at approximately $2.7B.

## Margin of Safety

The margin of safety in NuScale is unconventional -- it comes not from earnings coverage or asset value in the traditional sense, but from:

1. **Cash floor**: $1.3B in cash represents ~$4.28/share, providing a partial floor at 33% of the current stock price
2. **Technology monopoly**: NuScale holds the only NRC-approved SMR design. This regulatory moat took over a decade and hundreds of millions to build. No competitor is close -- Oklo's Aurora has not received design certification, and GE-Hitachi's BWRX-300 is still in review
3. **Demand validation**: Hyperscalers have committed over $20B in nuclear PPAs in the past 18 months. The question is no longer "will there be demand for SMRs?" but "can NuScale execute?"
4. **Short squeeze potential**: 21.6% short interest with 1.2 days to cover creates asymmetric upside on any positive catalyst

The honest assessment: this is a speculative position. There is no earnings margin of safety, no dividend cushion, no asset liquidation floor (beyond cash). The margin of safety is the gap between the market's implied probability of failure (~80-90% based on valuation) and our assessed probability of failure (~50-60%). That gap, combined with the asymmetric payoff in the success scenario, creates a positive expected value bet.

## Bear Case Rebuttal

**"UAMPS project cancellation proves NuScale cannot commercialize."**
Rebuttal: UAMPS was a consortium of small municipal utilities that could not aggregate sufficient demand to justify the project economics. TVA is a $12B-revenue federal utility with existing nuclear operating experience. The customer quality is categorically different. Romania's Nuclearelectrica is a sovereign-backed nuclear operator. The failure mode of UAMPS does not apply to these counterparties.

**"Revenue is declining -- the business is shrinking."**
Rebuttal: Revenue declined because the Romania FEED 2 contract completed. This is contract roll-off, not demand destruction. The next revenue phase comes from module manufacturing and deployment, which requires construction starts that are still 2-3 years away. Judging a pre-commercial nuclear company by quarterly revenue is like judging a pharmaceutical company by Phase 2 revenue.

**"Dilution from ATM issuance destroys shareholder value."**
Rebuttal: The $750M ATM raise in Q4 2025 was dilutive (39.3M shares at ~$19 average price), but it extended the runway from ~3 years to 6+ years. The alternative -- running low on cash and negotiating from a position of weakness -- would have been far more destructive. With $1.3B in cash, NuScale can negotiate commercial terms from strength rather than desperation.

**"21.6% short interest means smart money is bearish."**
Rebuttal: High short interest is a lagging indicator of sentiment, not a forward predictor of value. The same "smart money" was short Tesla at $30 pre-split. When the shorts are crowded (21.6% vs. 9% peer average) and catalysts are approaching, the risk/reward favors the long side. Any binding contract announcement could force rapid covering.

## Risk Factors

1. **Commercialization timeline risk**: If no binding commercial contract materializes by 2028, the thesis weakens materially regardless of technology merit
2. **Continued cash burn**: While the runway is 6+ years, sustained $200M+/year burn without revenue inflection erodes the cash floor and may necessitate additional dilutive raises
3. **Regulatory risk**: Any NRC design modification requirements or safety concerns could delay deployment timelines by years
4. **Cost overruns**: Romania's budget estimate of EUR 4.9B carries a +50% / -30% uncertainty range. Nuclear construction has a history of catastrophic cost overruns (Vogtle, Hinkley Point C)
5. **Competition from other nuclear technologies**: GE-Hitachi BWRX-300, Oklo Aurora, TerraPower Natrium, X-energy Xe-100 are all pursuing the same market. NuScale's NRC approval is a moat, but not an impenetrable one
6. **ENTRA1/TVA agreement is non-binding**: The 6 GW program could be scaled back or abandoned if economics do not pencil or political winds shift
7. **Share dilution**: At 304M shares (vs. ~195M in 2023), further ATM issuance or equity raises are possible if cash burn accelerates
