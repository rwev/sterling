# Long Thesis: Intuit (INTU)

## Investment Summary

Intuit is a high-quality software compounder with dominant market positions in tax preparation (TurboTax), small business accounting (QuickBooks), and consumer finance (Credit Karma) that has been caught in the indiscriminate SaaS selloff -- down 50% from its 52-week high of $813.70 to ~$419. The company just reported Q2 FY2026 earnings that beat estimates on both revenue ($4.7B, +17% y/y) and EPS ($4.15 non-GAAP vs. $3.68 consensus), raised its dividend 15%, and reiterated full-year guidance of $21.0-21.2B in revenue. At a forward P/E of ~16x and a PEG of 1.1x, Intuit is trading at its cheapest valuation in a decade for a business that is accelerating, not decelerating. The SaaSPocalypse has created a mispricing event in one of software's widest-moat franchises.

## Business Overview

Intuit operates four platform businesses that serve as financial operating systems for their respective customer segments:

- **Small Business & Self-Employed (QuickBooks)**: The largest segment, generating approximately 60% of revenue. QuickBooks Online revenue grew ~24% y/y in Q2 FY2026, driven by customer growth, pricing power, and mix improvements. QuickBooks is the de facto standard for SMB accounting, payroll, and payments in North America, with deep ecosystem lock-in through years of customer financial data.

- **Consumer (TurboTax)**: The dominant consumer tax preparation platform with ~40% market share in self-prepared returns. Revenue grew 12% y/y in Q2. TurboTax benefits from an unassailable regulatory moat: tax complexity creates recurring demand, and switching costs are high because TurboTax retains prior-year return data.

- **Credit Karma**: Consumer finance marketplace that monetizes through financial product referrals. Revenue grew 23% y/y in Q2, the fastest-growing segment, benefiting from AI-driven personalization of credit offers.

- **ProTax**: Professional tax preparer tools (Lacerte, ProConnect). Smaller but high-margin and deeply embedded in accountant workflows.

The company is investing aggressively in AI through a multi-year partnership with Anthropic to develop custom AI agents for businesses. Rather than being disrupted by AI, Intuit is integrating AI into its platform to expand its total addressable market -- automating bookkeeping, tax optimization, and financial advisory functions that were previously manual.

## Investment Case

**1. Widest moat in consumer and SMB software.** TurboTax and QuickBooks are two of the stickiest software products in existence. Switching costs are enormous: customers entrust years of financial data, tax history, payroll records, and banking integrations to these platforms. Net revenue retention is structurally above 100% because customers add services (payroll, payments, lending) over time. The tax code itself is a permanent tailwind -- complexity ensures demand.

**2. AI is additive, not destructive.** The SaaSPocalypse thesis assumes AI replaces SaaS. For Intuit, AI enhances the platform: automated categorization, proactive tax optimization, AI-powered financial advice for SMBs. The Anthropic partnership positions Intuit to deliver AI agents within its existing platform rather than being displaced by standalone AI tools. Management's Q2 commentary emphasized that AI is driving higher engagement and average revenue per user.

**3. Accelerating growth at a decade-low valuation.** Revenue growth of 17% in Q2 is the strongest in recent quarters. Non-GAAP operating income grew 23% y/y, demonstrating operating leverage. Yet the stock trades at ~16x forward earnings -- a level last seen when Intuit was growing at single-digit rates. The valuation dislocation is driven by sector-wide selling, not company-specific deterioration.

**4. Shareholder-friendly capital allocation.** The 15% dividend increase signals management confidence. The company generates over $6B in annual free cash flow with modest net debt (~$0.2B). FCF yield at current market cap (~$116B) is approximately 5.2%, exceptional for a high-quality software compounder.

**5. Secular tailwinds intact.** Tax complexity is increasing (not decreasing) with new regulations. SMB digitization is still early innings globally. The gig economy is expanding the addressable market for self-employed tax and accounting tools. Credit Karma benefits from consumer credit activity cycles.

## Earnings Quality

Intuit's earnings quality is strong. Key observations:

- **GAAP to non-GAAP divergence is manageable.** The primary adjustment is stock-based compensation. GAAP diluted EPS guidance of $15.49-15.69 vs. non-GAAP of $22.98-23.18 implies ~$7.50/share in SBC and amortization. This is material but consistent and declining as a percentage of revenue.

- **FCF conversion is excellent.** Free cash flow of $6.1B on EBITDA of $5.7B in FY2025 implies FCF/EBITDA conversion above 100%, driven by favorable working capital dynamics (deferred revenue from annual subscriptions collected upfront). This is a hallmark of high-quality recurring revenue.

- **Working capital trends are favorable.** Deferred revenue provides a natural cash flow tailwind. The Q2 FY2026 results showed strong working capital contributions.

- **No aggressive accounting red flags.** Revenue recognition is straightforward subscription-based. No major restructuring charges or one-time items distorting the trend.

**DCF sanity check**: At $7.0B estimated FY2026 FCF growing at 12% for 5 years and 4% terminal, with an 8.5% discount rate, the intrinsic value is approximately $600-650 per share. The current price of $419 implies the market is pricing in either significant deceleration or structural disruption -- neither of which is supported by the Q2 earnings beat and reiterated guidance.

## Risk Factors

1. **Tax policy simplification.** If tax filing is radically simplified (unlikely given historical complexity trend), TurboTax's moat narrows. Counter: Every administration since Reagan has added tax complexity, not reduced it. The IRS Direct File program remains limited in scope.

2. **AI-native competitors.** Standalone AI accounting or tax tools could emerge. Counter: Financial data is deeply personal and regulated; trust and data lock-in favor incumbents. Intuit is integrating AI faster than startups can build the regulatory compliance infrastructure.

3. **SMB economic sensitivity.** QuickBooks revenue correlates with SMB formation and survival. A recession would slow customer acquisition. Counter: QuickBooks is also counter-cyclical in that SMBs need accounting software more, not less, during difficult economic periods to manage cash flow.

4. **SaaS selloff continues.** The sector RSI of 18 could go lower before mean-reverting. Counter: Intuit's fundamentals (beat-and-raise quarter, 15% dividend hike) differentiate it from the sector.

5. **Near-term seasonality.** Management guided for a softer Q3 with higher marketing costs during peak tax season. This is seasonal and expected but could create a near-term trading dip.
