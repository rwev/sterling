# Long Thesis: Equinix (EQIX)

## Investment Summary

Equinix is the world's largest data center REIT with 280+ facilities across 72 metros globally, and it is entering its first $10 billion revenue year in 2026. The company guided FY2026 revenue of $10.1-$10.2 billion (+9-10% normalized growth), AFFO per share of $41.93-$42.74 (+9-12% YoY), and adjusted EBITDA margin of 51% (200bps expansion). Q4 2025 delivered record annualized bookings of $474 million (+42% QoQ), with 60% of the largest deals driven by AI workloads, and full-year 2025 bookings of $1.6 billion (+27% YoY). The company has a 2% customer churn rate, passes power costs through to tenants, and has increased its dividend for 11 consecutive years since REIT conversion. At $937, EQIX trades at approximately 22x forward AFFO -- reasonable for a quality compounder growing AFFO 9-12% annually with visible AI-driven demand acceleration. The stock sits 5% below the consensus analyst target of $985 and 15% below JPMorgan's $1,100 target. For Sterling, EQIX fills the missing layer of the AI infrastructure stack: the fund owns the chips (AVGO, INTC) and the power (CEG, VST, VRT) but not the physical buildings where AI compute runs. EQIX also introduces defensive income characteristics through its 2.2% dividend yield and rate-cut optionality, providing portfolio ballast in the current stagflationary macro environment.

## Investment Case

### 1. Equinix Completes Sterling's AI Infrastructure Stack

Sterling's portfolio holds semiconductors (AVGO 8%, INTC 8%) that design AI chips, and power generation (CEG 10%, VST 8%, VRT 13%) that supplies electricity to data centers. But the fund owns nothing in between -- the physical data center infrastructure where the chips actually run and the power is consumed. This is a structural gap.

Equinix operates 280+ data centers across 72 metros in 33 countries. It is the largest operator of network-dense, interconnection-rich colocation facilities in the world, hosting more than 300 Fortune 500 companies. When a hyperscaler deploys AI training clusters, it needs three things: chips (AVGO/INTC), power (CEG/VST/VRT), and physical space with connectivity (EQIX). Adding EQIX at 2-3% completes the value chain and ensures Sterling participates in the full economics of AI infrastructure buildout, not just the component and energy layers.

### 2. Record AI-Driven Bookings Signal Multi-Year Demand Acceleration

The most important data point from Q4 2025 is the bookings number: $474 million in annualized new bookings, a record, up 42% from Q3 and 27% YoY for the full year ($1.6B total). Sixty percent of the largest deals in Q4 were driven by AI workloads. This is not guidance or management commentary -- it is signed contracts that will convert to revenue over the next 12-18 months as facilities are built out and customers deploy.

Equinix surpassed 500,000 total interconnections globally during Q4, an important metric because each interconnection is a recurring monthly revenue stream with extremely low churn. The interconnection business is the strategic moat: once a customer deploys in an Equinix facility and establishes cross-connects to cloud providers, network carriers, and enterprise partners, switching costs become prohibitive. The customer would have to replicate its entire connectivity ecosystem at a competing facility.

Hyperscaler AI/data center capex is expected to exceed $430 billion in 2026, up from $414 billion in 2025. Equinix's xScale program (12 AI-ready wholesale facilities under construction) is purpose-built to capture this demand. The company plans to invest $4-5 billion annually in capital expenditures from 2026-2029, up from $3.5-3.9 billion in 2025.

### 3. Structural Supply Constraints Protect Pricing Power

Data centers take 18-36 months to build. Power availability in key markets (Northern Virginia, Dallas, Amsterdam, Singapore) is severely constrained. Equinix's Q4 MRR grew 10% YoY with customer churn of approximately 2% -- evidence of strong pricing power in a supply-constrained market.

The same grid bottleneck that drives Sterling's CEG/VST/VRT thesis also protects Equinix's competitive position: new competitors cannot simply build data centers without securing grid connections, which are increasingly scarce and subject to multi-year permitting delays. Equinix's existing land bank, pre-approved power capacity, and construction pipeline represent a barrier to entry that grows stronger as grid constraints intensify.

Lease renewal spreads are positive and accelerating. Unlike commercial office REITs where vacancy and rent concessions are compressing returns, data center REITs are experiencing secular demand growth that exceeds supply additions.

### 4. Power Cost Pass-Through Eliminates Oil Price Risk

A critical concern for data center operators in the current $92+ Brent environment is energy cost exposure. Equinix addresses this structurally: the company invoices each client an energy bill alongside the colocation rent, effectively passing through actual power costs to tenants. This means rising electricity prices (driven by oil, natural gas, or grid congestion) flow through to customers, not to Equinix's margins.

The adjusted EBITDA margin guidance of 51% for FY2026 (200bps expansion over 2025) confirms that energy cost pass-through is working as designed. Equinix is not absorbing the energy cost inflation that is pressuring other capital-intensive businesses.

Additionally, Equinix has invested in alternative energy procurement, including fuel cell-powered data centers and long-term renewable energy PPAs, which reduce exposure to volatile wholesale power markets over time.

### 5. Rate-Cut Optionality Provides Asymmetric Upside

REITs are duration-sensitive assets that benefit from falling interest rates. The 10-year Treasury yield settled at 4.12% on March 6, and the bond market is beginning to price rate cuts over the next 6-12 months as the labor market deteriorates (NFP -92K, unemployment 4.4%). If the Fed cuts rates in response to economic weakness, EQIX benefits from a double tailwind: (a) lower discount rates on its stable cash flows, which raises the present value of future AFFO, and (b) lower financing costs for its $3.7-4.2 billion annual capex program.

Moody's upgraded Equinix's senior unsecured rating to Baa1 with stable outlook on March 5, 2026, reflecting the company's improving credit profile. The company just closed a $1.5 billion senior notes offering, pre-funding its capital program at attractive rates. If 10Y yields decline toward 3.5-3.75% as the growth scare intensifies, EQIX could re-rate from 22x AFFO to 25-27x AFFO, implying 15-25% upside from the multiple expansion alone.

### 6. Defensive Income in a Stagflationary Environment

EQIX pays a quarterly dividend of $5.16 per share ($20.64 annually), yielding 2.2%. The company has increased its dividend for 11 consecutive years since REIT conversion, with the most recent increase (from $4.69 to $5.16, +10%) announced in February 2026. While the yield is modest, the combination of dividend income + 9-12% AFFO growth provides a total return profile that is attractive relative to zero-yield growth stocks in a stagflationary environment.

For Sterling, which holds significant exposure to high-beta, zero-yield names (VRT, AVGO, INTC), EQIX introduces a quality income component that improves portfolio resilience during drawdowns.

## Business Overview

Equinix, Inc. is a global digital infrastructure company and the world's largest data center REIT, headquartered in Redwood City, California. The company provides colocation, interconnection, and related services across three geographic segments:

- **Americas (50% of revenue):** 117 data centers across 27 metros in the US, Canada, and Brazil. Key markets include Northern Virginia (Ashburn), Silicon Valley, New York, and Dallas.
- **EMEA (30%):** 93 data centers across 26 metros including London, Amsterdam, Frankfurt, and Paris.
- **Asia-Pacific (20%):** 70+ data centers across 19 metros including Singapore, Tokyo, Hong Kong, and Sydney.

Equinix's business model centers on "interconnection" -- connecting enterprises, cloud providers, network carriers, and SaaS companies within its facilities through low-latency cross-connects. This creates a network effect: each new customer that deploys in an Equinix facility increases the value of the facility for every other customer. The company hosts 10,000+ customers globally, including all major cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud), all major network carriers, and 300+ Fortune 500 companies.

The xScale program is Equinix's wholesale/hyperscale offering, designed specifically for AI training clusters and large cloud deployments. Twelve xScale facilities are currently under construction, with the program structured as a joint venture to limit balance sheet exposure.

## Earnings Quality

- **GAAP vs. Adjusted divergence:** GAAP EPS of $13.76 in FY2025 versus AFFO per share of $38.33 -- the standard REIT divergence driven by depreciation and amortization of real estate assets. GAAP depreciation significantly exceeds economic depreciation because data center assets appreciate with demand. AFFO is the correct cash flow metric for REITs, and Equinix's AFFO calculation has been validated by the audit committee following the Hindenburg Research allegations (March 2024). The SEC and US Attorney's Office investigated and did not pursue enforcement action. Equinix settled a shareholder class action for $41.5M without admitting wrongdoing.
- **FCF Conversion:** AFFO of $3.76B on adjusted EBITDA of approximately $4.7B implies approximately 80% conversion after maintenance capex. This is strong for a REIT. The remaining 20% is maintenance capex and working capital.
- **Accruals and Working Capital:** Data center REITs have predictable working capital profiles. Monthly recurring revenue is billed in advance. Receivables are short-duration (30-60 days). No inventory concerns.
- **Balance Sheet Durability:** Net debt of $19.6B is substantial but well-structured: 99% fixed-rate, weighted average maturity of 7+ years, no significant near-term maturities. The Moody's Baa1 upgrade and $1.5B notes offering demonstrate market confidence. Available liquidity exceeds $5B (cash + revolver).
- **Conservative Accounting:** The Hindenburg allegations of maintenance capex misclassification were investigated and found to be without merit by the audit committee and external auditors. No restatements were required. The $41.5M settlement was a nuisance resolution, not an admission of accounting issues. Equinix's financial reporting is now more scrutinized than any REIT peer, which paradoxically increases confidence in the numbers.

## Risk Factors

1. **Hyperscaler In-Sourcing.** Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and Meta are all building their own data centers. If hyperscalers shift meaningfully from leasing Equinix/DLR space to building entirely in-house, the demand growth thesis weakens. Mitigant: hyperscalers still need Equinix's interconnection ecosystem for network-dense workloads, customer-facing deployments, and multi-cloud connectivity. The xScale program is purpose-built for hyperscaler wholesale needs.

2. **Elevated Leverage.** Net debt/EBITDA of approximately 4.0x is high relative to non-REIT companies. If rates remain elevated and Equinix cannot refinance at favorable terms, the cost of capital could compress returns on new investments. Mitigant: 99% fixed-rate debt, 7+ year average maturity, Baa1 rating, and $5B+ available liquidity.

3. **Hindenburg Residual Risk.** Although the SEC investigation concluded without enforcement action and the audit committee validated accounting practices, the Hindenburg report created lasting reputational damage and a $41.5M settlement cost. Any new short-seller report or accounting allegations could pressure the stock. Mitigant: the post-Hindenburg scrutiny has effectively validated Equinix's numbers; a re-run of the same allegations would have diminishing impact.

4. **Data Center Supply Overshoot.** If the AI capex cycle brings on massive new data center supply in 2027-2028, lease renewal rates and occupancy could soften. The 18-36 month build cycle provides visibility into supply additions, and current power constraints limit where new supply can be built. But a supply overshoot in key markets (Northern Virginia, Dallas) would pressure pricing.

5. **Stagflation Impact on Enterprise IT Budgets.** While hyperscaler demand is secular, enterprise colocation demand (which represents the majority of Equinix's customer base) is partially GDP-sensitive. If the NFP -92K signals a genuine recession, enterprise IT budget cuts could slow new bookings. Mitigant: existing contracts are multi-year with 2% churn; the impact would be on new bookings, not existing revenue.

6. **Currency Risk.** With 50% of revenue outside the Americas, Equinix faces meaningful FX translation exposure. A strong dollar (DXY at 99.34) reduces reported revenue from EMEA and APAC. Mitigant: Equinix guides on a constant-currency basis and uses natural hedges through local-currency debt.
