# Long Thesis: GE Vernova (GEV)

## Investment Summary

GE Vernova is the indispensable infrastructure backbone of the AI power super-cycle. With $150B in backlog, $44-45B in 2026 revenue guidance, and a path to 20% EBITDA margins by 2028, the company sits at the intersection of every major energy theme: data center power demand, grid modernization, the nuclear renaissance, and now geopolitical-driven energy security. The balance sheet is pristine with $7.7B net cash. The stock trades at a premium (33x 2026E EV/EBITDA) that reflects high expectations, but the 2028 earnings trajectory ($22+ EPS) and cumulative FCF generation ($24B+ through 2028) justify the valuation on a two-year view. We recommend building a position on pullbacks toward the $800 level, targeting $1,050 on a 12-month horizon.

## Business Overview

GE Vernova was spun out of General Electric in April 2024 as a pure-play energy company. The business operates across three segments:

- **Power** (~55% of revenue): Gas turbines, steam turbines, nuclear services, and power conversion. GE Vernova holds a dominant installed base of 7,000+ gas turbines globally, creating a recurring services revenue stream.
- **Wind** (~20% of revenue): Onshore and offshore wind turbine manufacturing and services. This segment has been a margin drag but is being restructured aggressively.
- **Electrification** (~25% of revenue): Grid solutions, power conversion, solar/storage. This is the fastest-growing segment, driven by grid modernization demand.

The company serves utilities, independent power producers, and increasingly hyperscaler data center operators directly. Its products are mission-critical and often sole-sourced due to installed base lock-in and multi-year lead times.

## Investment Case

### 1. Structural Power Deficit Creates Multi-Year Demand Visibility

US data center demand is projected to reach 74GW by 2028 against a 49GW shortfall. Hyperscaler capex of $690B in 2026 requires massive new power generation and grid infrastructure. GE Vernova's gas turbines, grid equipment, and nuclear services address this deficit directly. Orders surged 34% organically to $59.3B in 2025, with Q4 alone delivering $22.2B in orders — a 65% year-over-year increase. Backlog stands at $150.2B, up $31.2B from prior year.

### 2. Margin Expansion Story Is Inflecting

GE Vernova's margin trajectory is the core of the investment case. Adjusted EBITDA margins expanded from approximately 6% in 2024 to 8.4% in 2025, with guidance for 11-13% in 2026 and a 2028 target of 20%. This progression is driven by:
- Mix shift toward higher-margin services and electrification
- Wind segment restructuring (losses narrowing toward breakeven)
- Operating leverage on growing revenue base
- Prolec GE acquisition accretion ($600M+ revenue, accretive margins)

### 3. Capital Return Acceleration

The company doubled its quarterly dividend to $0.50/share (effective Q2 2026, $2.00 annualized), increased its buyback authorization, and generated $3.7B in FCF in 2025 — more than doubling year-over-year. 2026 FCF guidance of $5.0-5.5B represents a 3.5-4x increase from just two years ago. Cumulative FCF through 2028 is targeted at $24B+.

### 4. Geopolitical Tailwind

The US-Iran conflict and Strait of Hormuz disruption reinforce the case for energy security and domestic power generation. GE Vernova's gas turbines, nuclear services, and grid infrastructure all benefit from accelerated investment in energy independence. Defense-grade reliability of power supply is now a national security priority.

## Earnings Quality

GE Vernova's earnings quality is high and improving:

- **FCF conversion**: Free cash flow of $3.7B vs. adjusted EBITDA of $3.2B represents over 100% conversion, indicating conservative accounting and favorable working capital dynamics from advance payments on long-cycle equipment.
- **Backlog visibility**: $150B backlog provides 3-4 years of revenue visibility, reducing earnings volatility.
- **Order-to-revenue spread**: 2025 book-to-bill of 1.56x (orders of $59.3B vs. revenue of $38.1B) means the backlog is growing, not just sustaining.
- **Segment profitability**: Wind losses are narrowing and Power/Electrification margins are expanding organically, not through one-time items.
- **Balance sheet**: $7.7B net cash with minimal debt ($328M) eliminates financial risk and provides strategic flexibility.

The Proficy software divestiture ($600M) and Prolec GE acquisition demonstrate disciplined portfolio management — selling non-core assets and acquiring accretive industrial businesses.

## Risk Factors

1. **Wind segment execution**: Offshore wind remains structurally challenged. Continued losses or write-downs would delay the margin expansion story.
2. **Valuation premium**: At 33x 2026E EV/EBITDA, any earnings miss or guidance reduction would trigger a sharp de-rating. The stock has tripled from its 52-week low of $252.
3. **Hyperscaler capex deceleration**: If the $690B AI capex cycle slows, power demand forecasts compress and GEV's order momentum weakens.
4. **Tariff/input cost inflation**: ISM Prices Paid at 70.5 signals rising input costs. Steel and aluminum tariffs directly impact GEV's manufacturing cost base.
5. **Execution risk on margin targets**: The leap from 8.4% to 20% EBITDA margin by 2028 is aggressive. Procurement, labor, and supply chain bottlenecks could slow progress.
6. **Customer concentration**: Growing reliance on hyperscaler data center customers creates concentration risk.
