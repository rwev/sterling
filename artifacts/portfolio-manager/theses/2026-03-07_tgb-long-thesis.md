# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Taseko Mines (TGB)

## Investment Summary

Taseko Mines is a $2.6 billion copper producer undergoing a transformational shift from a single-mine operator to a two-mine company with the March 2, 2026 commencement of copper cathode production at Florence Copper in Arizona -- the first new greenfield copper facility in the United States since 2008. At $7.20 per share and approximately 14x forward earnings, TGB offers direct exposure to the copper supercycle thesis with a unique US domestic production angle that maximizes Section 232 tariff benefit, a ramp-up catalyst that will drive earnings inflection through 2026 and into 2027, and a third asset (Yellowhead, $2B NPV) that provides massive long-term optionality currently assigned zero value by the market. With only four analysts covering the stock and institutional ownership below 20%, the information asymmetry is significant -- the market has not yet priced the earnings transformation that Florence Copper's ramp to 85 million pounds per year will deliver.

## Discovery Edge

- **Analyst coverage count**: 4 analysts (3 Buy, 1 Hold); consensus price target ranges from $4.77 to $7.34 depending on source, with most targets stale and pre-dating the Florence commissioning catalyst
- **Institutional ownership**: approximately 18% -- far below the 50-70% typical of mid-cap miners, indicating the institutional discovery cycle has barely begun
- **Information asymmetry**: The market is pricing TGB on trailing financials that reflect a single-mine company with negative net income (-$30M in FY2025). The forward picture -- two producing mines generating an estimated 140-150M lbs of copper in 2026 at elevated US-premium pricing -- is fundamentally different. Most sell-side targets were set before the March 2 first cathode harvest and do not incorporate the Florence contribution at scale. The Yellowhead project ($2B NPV per the July 2025 technical report) receives zero market value in any published estimate.

## Business Overview

Taseko Mines is a Canadian copper producer (headquartered in Vancouver, BC) operating two mines:

**Gibraltar Mine (British Columbia)** -- An open-pit copper-molybdenum mine in south-central BC, one of North America's largest. Taseko owns 87.5% (increased from 75% in March 2023). Gibraltar produced 98 million pounds of copper and 1.9 million pounds of molybdenum in 2025, with Q4 C1 cash costs of US$2.47/lb. The mine has been operating since 1972 and is a reliable cash flow generator. 2026 guidance is 110-115 million pounds of copper at improving costs as mining transitions fully into the Connector pit.

**Florence Copper (Arizona)** -- A greenfield in-situ copper recovery (ISCR) operation in Pinal County, Arizona. This is the first commercial-scale ISCR copper mine globally and the first new US greenfield copper facility since 2008. Construction was completed in Q4 2025 at approximately $275 million, largely on budget. The electrowinning plant commenced operations in late February 2026, and the first copper cathodes were harvested on March 2, 2026. The operation produces LME Grade A copper cathode directly -- no smelting or refining required -- at what management expects will be materially lower operating costs than Gibraltar. 2026 production guidance is 30-35 million pounds as the operation ramps toward nameplate capacity of 85 million pounds per year. At full capacity, Taseko will become the third-largest copper cathode producer in the United States. The mine life exceeds 22 years with over 1.5 billion pounds of recoverable copper.

**Yellowhead Project (British Columbia)** -- A 100%-owned, fully permitted copper porphyry deposit located 150 km northeast of Kamloops. The July 2025 updated technical report established a $2 billion NPV for the project, with 4.4 billion pounds of copper in reserves (817 million tonnes at 0.29% Cu equivalent) and a 25-year mine life at 90,000 tonnes per day throughput. Taseko targets deleveraging to gross debt below $400M (under 1x leverage) before advancing Yellowhead to a development decision. This asset provides generational optionality but is not included in any near-term valuation.

**Key financial observations**:

1. **Revenue inflection**: FY2025 revenue of $673M was a record, driven by 99M lbs copper sold at $4.61/lb average realized price. In 2026, combined production of 140-150M lbs at current COMEX prices ($5.80-5.90/lb) would represent a step-change in revenue, potentially exceeding $800M.

2. **Florence Copper's margin profile**: ISCR technology eliminates mining, crushing, grinding, and flotation -- the costliest steps in conventional copper production. Florence Copper's expected operating costs are materially below Gibraltar's C1 of $2.47-2.87/lb. As Florence ramps, blended margins should improve significantly.

3. **Deleveraging trajectory**: Total debt stands at approximately C$730M with net debt of approximately C$780M (as of September 2025). The October 2025 equity raise ($173M gross proceeds from 42.7M shares at US$4) was used to pay down the revolving credit facility. Pro forma liquidity is approximately C$300M. Management targets gross debt below C$400M before advancing Yellowhead.

4. **Cash flow from operations**: Q4 2025 operating cash flow was $101M, the strongest quarter of the year. Full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA of $230M was achieved largely from Gibraltar alone. Adding Florence production at low incremental cost should drive substantial free cash flow generation beginning mid-2026.

## Risk Factors

1. **Florence Copper ramp-up execution risk**: ISCR technology has never been deployed at this commercial scale. Wellfield performance, leach rates, and SX/EW plant throughput are all unproven at the 85M lbs/year target. Early results are positive (initial flow rates exceeded expectations), but any technical setback -- wellfield complications, acid consumption issues, or permitting challenges -- would delay the production ramp and the earnings inflection. This is the primary risk.

2. **Copper price cyclicality**: At current prices ($5.80/lb COMEX), TGB's economics are attractive. A decline to $4.00/lb (as occurred in 2023) would compress Gibraltar margins significantly (C1 costs of ~$2.50/lb) and make the Florence ramp period more financially stressful. The 52-week range of $1.67-$9.25 illustrates the stock's extreme copper price sensitivity (beta of 1.85).

3. **Leverage and dilution risk**: Net debt of approximately C$780M on C$230M of 2025 EBITDA represents ~3.4x leverage. While the trajectory is toward deleveraging, any operational setback that reduces cash flow could force additional equity raises at dilutive terms, as occurred in October 2025.

4. **Permitting and regulatory risk**: Florence Copper operates under EPA and Arizona regulatory oversight. Any changes to environmental regulations regarding in-situ recovery or water usage in Arizona could create operational uncertainty. The Yellowhead project in BC faces its own permitting timeline.

5. **Recession and demand destruction**: The current macro environment (NFP -92K, rising recession probability) could weigh on copper demand and prices in the near term, creating headwinds for the stock even as the Florence ramp proceeds. TGB's high beta means it would sell off sharply in a broad risk-off environment.

6. **Concentration risk**: Until Florence is fully ramped, Gibraltar represents the majority of Taseko's cash flow. Any production disruption at Gibraltar (pit wall instability, equipment failure, severe weather) would disproportionately impact results.
