# Long Thesis: Coherent Corp. (COHR)

## Investment Summary

Coherent is the volume leader in optical transceivers with approximately 25% market share, positioned at the center of the 800G-to-1.6T networking supercycle that is critical to scaling AI infrastructure. The company's proprietary 6-inch Indium Phosphide wafer fabrication line -- the only one of its kind at scale -- provides a durable manufacturing cost advantage over competitors still running 4-inch wafers. With datacenter revenue growing 36% year-over-year and now exceeding $1 billion per quarter, four consecutive earnings beats, and the 1.6T transceiver ramp beginning in H2 2026, Coherent is the highest-conviction pure-play on photonic interconnects. At $259, the stock trades at approximately 28x forward non-GAAP EPS, which is elevated but justified by the multi-year growth runway. The macro team identifies this as the top photonics name, and the PM has flagged photonics as a priority. My independent work confirms the thesis.

## Business Overview

Coherent operates across three segments:

**Networking (~45% of revenue, growing fastest):** Optical transceivers (800G today, 1.6T in development), laser components, modulators, and photonic integrated circuits for data center interconnects. This is the AI-driven growth engine. Coherent supplies transceivers to all major hyperscalers and cloud service providers, and manufactures the laser chips that go into its own and competitors' transceiver modules.

**Materials (~30% of revenue):** Engineered materials including Silicon Carbide (SiC) substrates for EVs and industrial applications, and specialty compound semiconductors. This segment is mature but provides diversification.

**Lasers (~25% of revenue):** Industrial, scientific, and defense laser systems. Stable but not a growth driver.

**Competitive Position:** Coherent's moat is vertical integration in photonics. The company designs, fabricates, and assembles the entire optical transceiver stack: from InP laser chips to modulators to packaged transceiver modules. The 6-inch InP wafer line in Sherman, Texas, which ramped in late 2025, is a structural cost advantage. A 6-inch wafer yields approximately 2.25x more die than a 4-inch wafer, translating to 30-40% lower per-unit laser chip costs. No competitor has matched this capability at production scale. This is a manufacturing moat, not a design moat -- it is harder to replicate and more durable.

## Investment Case

1. **The 800G-to-1.6T transceiver transition is a multi-year supercycle.** TrendForce projects 800G+ transceiver shipments will leap from 24 million units in 2025 to 63 million in 2026 -- a 2.6x increase. The 1.6T standard, tied to NVIDIA's Rubin platform and Spectrum-X Ethernet switches launching H2 2026, extends the cycle through 2028-2029. Coherent is the volume leader and will capture outsized share of this ramp.

2. **The 6-inch InP line is a cost moat that compounds over time.** As transceiver volumes scale, the cost advantage widens. Coherent can price competitively while maintaining higher gross margins than peers, or it can match peer margins while winning share on price. Either outcome is favorable.

3. **Datacenter revenue is inflecting.** Q2 FY2026 datacenter revenue reached $1.09 billion, up 14% sequentially and 36% year-over-year. This segment is now the largest revenue contributor and its growth rate is accelerating, not decelerating. The mix shift toward higher-margin datacenter products is driving gross margin expansion (non-GAAP gross margin improved from 36.0% in FY2024 to 39.0% in Q2 FY2026).

4. **Four consecutive earnings beats signal execution quality.** Q2 FY2026 non-GAAP EPS of $1.29 vs. $1.14 expected (13% beat). Q1 FY2026 EPS of $0.94 vs. $0.85 expected. Management is under-promising and over-delivering -- a hallmark of companies in the early innings of a product cycle ramp.

5. **OFC 2026 catalyst on March 17.** Coherent will host a Technology Innovation Briefing at OFC 2026 with CEO Jim Anderson and senior leaders. This event typically features product roadmap updates and customer engagement announcements. Any 1.6T design win disclosures or capacity expansion announcements would be catalytic.

## Earnings Quality

**GAAP vs. Adjusted Divergence:** The gap between GAAP and non-GAAP is significant but narrowing. Primary adjustments include: (1) amortization of intangibles from the II-VI/Coherent merger (~$200M annually), (2) stock-based compensation, and (3) restructuring charges. The GAAP loss in FY2024 was driven entirely by non-cash amortization; the underlying business was profitable. The convergence toward GAAP profitability ($0.76 GAAP EPS in Q2 FY2026) is a positive signal.

**FCF Conversion:** Mixed. Free cash flow has been lumpy due to significant capital expenditure for the 6-inch InP line and datacenter capacity expansion. Capex consumed ~70% of operating cash flow in some quarters. This is growth capex, not maintenance -- it will generate returns as volumes ramp. Expect FCF to improve materially in FY2027 as capex intensity normalizes.

**Balance Sheet:** Improving. Net leverage at 1.7x is within comfortable territory. The $875M+ cash position provides adequate liquidity. No near-term refinancing risk.

**Working Capital:** Datacenter customers (hyperscalers) pay reliably. Inventory build is intentional -- Coherent is pre-building transceiver inventory ahead of the 1.6T ramp. This is not a red flag; it is a rational commercial decision given supply chain lead times.

**Bull Case Valuation:** If datacenter revenue reaches $5B+ annually by FY2028 (plausible given the 1.6T ramp), total revenue exceeds $8B, and non-GAAP EPS reaches $7-8, a 25x P/E yields $175-$200. At 30x forward P/E (appropriate for a 20%+ grower), the stock is worth $240-$300 in 12 months, discounting FY2028 earnings. The $300 high-end analyst target reflects this scenario.

## Risk Factors

1. **Valuation is not cheap.** At $259, much of the near-term upside is priced. A miss on Q3 FY2026 earnings or a broader tech selloff could send the stock back to $200-$220 quickly. The all-time high of $276.50 was set just days ago.

2. **Customer concentration in datacenter.** A handful of hyperscalers drive the majority of networking revenue. Any single customer pausing orders would be immediately visible in quarterly results.

3. **Technology risk.** Co-packaged optics (CPO) could disrupt the pluggable transceiver market in the medium term. If CPO becomes the standard architecture by 2028-2029, Coherent's transceiver business model needs to evolve. Management is investing in CPO, but the competitive landscape could shift.

4. **Tariff exposure.** Coherent manufactures transceivers in Asia (primarily Thailand and China). The March 4 tariff escalation (20% on China) could compress margins if costs cannot be passed through. The Sherman, Texas InP line partially mitigates this for laser chips, but final assembly remains offshore.

5. **Capital intensity.** The 6-inch InP line and capacity expansions require sustained capex investment. If the 1.6T ramp is slower than expected, Coherent could face a period of depressed free cash flow with limited return.

6. **Materials and Lasers segments dragging blended growth.** Approximately 55% of revenue comes from slower-growth businesses. A pure-play datacenter optics company would command higher multiples.
