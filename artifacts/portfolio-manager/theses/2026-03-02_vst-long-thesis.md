# Contrarian Long Thesis: Vistra Corp (VST)

## Investment Summary

Vistra Corp is a 44 GW diversified power generation company trading at $173 -- a 27% discount to the $234 consensus analyst target -- after two consecutive quarterly earnings misses driven by idiosyncratic plant outages, not structural business deterioration. The market is punishing a top-of-class power generation fleet at the exact moment when US electricity demand is entering its strongest growth cycle in two decades. Vistra's 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $6.8-7.6B (a 15-29% increase over 2025), a transformational 20-year Meta nuclear PPA covering 2,609 MW, the pending $4B Cogentrix acquisition adding 5,500 MW of gas capacity, and an aggressive share buyback program that has retired 30% of shares since 2021 together create a compelling margin of safety at current levels. On a forward EV/EBITDA basis, the stock trades at roughly 10-11x the midpoint of 2026 guidance -- a discount to the replacement cost of its generation fleet.

## Why the Market Is Wrong

The market is extrapolating two quarters of plant-level operational noise into a structural earnings concern. The reality is simpler: Vistra's Q3 2025 miss ($1.75 vs. $3.50 consensus) was driven by the Moss Landing battery storage fire and unplanned nuclear outages, and Q4 2025 ($2.18 vs. $2.51 consensus) was hit by the Martin Lake coal unit outage in Texas and continued Moss Landing downtime. These are discrete, non-recurring events at specific facilities. They say nothing about the company's 44 GW fleet or its contracted earnings trajectory.

Meanwhile, the market is underweighting three transformational developments:

1. **Meta 20-year nuclear PPA (January 2026)**: 2,609 MW across Perry, Davis-Besse, and Beaver Valley nuclear plants in PJM, beginning deliveries late 2026 through 2034. This is the single largest corporate nuclear PPA in US history, converting merchant nuclear exposure into investment-grade contracted cash flow for two decades.

2. **Cogentrix acquisition (announced January 2026)**: 5,500 MW of modern natural gas generation across PJM, ISO-NE, and ERCOT for a net purchase price of ~$4.0B (~7.3x implied EBITDA). This adds dispatchable capacity in the tightest power markets in the country at a time when interconnection queues are 5-12 years long.

3. **2026 EBITDA guidance of $6.8-7.6B**: This represents a 15-29% step-up from 2025's $5.9B actual, driven by higher power prices, improved fleet performance, and initial contributions from recent acquisitions. The market is pricing in the misses but not the guidance upgrade.

The stock has declined 21% from its 52-week high of $219.82. At $173, the market is offering a 44 GW irreplaceable power fleet -- including 6.5 GW of nuclear and 27 GW of natural gas -- at a valuation that implies the AI-driven power demand thesis has stalled. It has not.

## Business Overview

Vistra Corp operates one of the largest competitive power generation fleets in the United States:

- **Total capacity**: ~44 GW (pro forma for Lotus Infrastructure acquisition closed November 2025; ~50 GW pro forma for Cogentrix)
- **Generation mix**: 27 GW natural gas, 6.5 GW nuclear, 8.7 GW coal (retiring over time), plus solar and battery storage
- **Retail platform**: ~5 million retail electricity customers in Texas and the Northeast through TXU Energy and other brands
- **Geographic exposure**: ERCOT (Texas), PJM (Mid-Atlantic/Midwest), ISO-NE, MISO, and NYISO
- **Market position**: Second-largest competitive power generator in the US (behind Constellation post-Calpine)

The integrated generation-plus-retail model is a natural hedge: Vistra generates power and sells it to its own retail customers, reducing exposure to wholesale price volatility while capturing the full margin stack. The nuclear fleet provides zero-carbon baseload with capacity factors above 92%, while the gas fleet provides dispatchable peaking and intermediate capacity that is increasingly scarce and valuable.

## Earnings Quality & Hidden Value

**GAAP vs. Non-GAAP divergence is structural but well-understood.** Vistra's GAAP earnings are systematically depressed by mark-to-market unrealized losses on forward power hedges. In FY2025, GAAP net income was $944M versus adjusted EBITDA of $5.9B -- the $808M unrealized hedge loss is a timing difference that reverses as hedges settle, not an economic loss. This is standard for competitive power generators with large forward hedge books, and the adjusted EBITDA figure is the better measure of ongoing cash earnings power.

**FCF conversion is strong and improving.** Adjusted FCF before growth of $3.6B on $5.9B EBITDA represents ~61% conversion in 2025, and the 2026 guide implies $4.3B FCF midpoint on $7.2B EBITDA midpoint -- roughly 60% conversion. For a capital-intensive power generator, this is exceptional.

**The share buyback is creating hidden compounding.** Since November 2021, Vistra has retired roughly 30% of its shares outstanding (~$5.9B in repurchases), from approximately 485M to 339M shares. The company plans at least $1B in additional buybacks annually through 2026-2027, with $1.5B authorized. On a per-share basis, EBITDA and FCF are growing even faster than the headline numbers suggest.

**Balance sheet is conservatively leveraged for the sector.** Pro forma net leverage of 2.6x 2026 EBITDA midpoint is moderate for a diversified power generator, and the deleveraging trajectory to 2.3x by year-end 2027 provides additional equity cushion. The company expects $3B+ of excess capital after funding buybacks, dividends, and growth investments through 2027.

## Margin of Safety

The margin of safety derives from multiple independent sources:

1. **Valuation discount**: At $173, VST trades at ~10-11x forward EV/EBITDA on the midpoint of 2026 guidance. The replacement cost of 44 GW of permitted, grid-connected generation capacity -- particularly nuclear and gas in constrained markets -- far exceeds the current enterprise value.

2. **Contracted cash flow transformation**: The Meta PPA converts 2,609 MW of merchant nuclear risk into 20-year contracted cash flow from one of the most creditworthy counterparties in the world. This de-risking is not yet reflected in the multiple.

3. **Structural power demand growth**: US electricity demand is growing at the fastest rate since 2000, driven by data centers (75.8 GW by 2026, 134.4 GW by 2030), manufacturing reshoring, and EV charging. Vistra's fleet is positioned in the highest-demand markets (ERCOT, PJM).

4. **Share count shrinkage**: At current buyback rates, the share count could decline below 320M by year-end 2026, amplifying per-share economics by an additional 5-6%.

5. **Scarcity premium on dispatchable generation**: The 2,600 GW interconnection queue with 5-12 year wait times and 80% dropout rates means new dispatchable capacity is nearly impossible to build. Vistra's existing fleet is a scarce, appreciating asset.

## Bear Case Rebuttal

**"The Q3/Q4 earnings misses signal deeper operational problems."**
Rebuttal: The Q3 miss was driven by Moss Landing battery fire (a known, insured event) and unplanned nuclear outages. The Q4 miss was driven by Martin Lake coal unit downtime and continued Moss Landing impact. Neither issue is systemic. Vistra's nuclear fleet has historically operated at 90%+ capacity factors, and the gas fleet is modern and well-maintained. Management guided 2026 EBITDA 15-29% above 2025 actual -- they would not guide up if the fleet were structurally impaired.

**"Leverage is too high (3.36x debt-to-equity)."**
Rebuttal: Net debt/EBITDA of 2.6x is moderate for the sector and declining. The Cogentrix acquisition is partially funded with equity ($900M in stock at $185/share) and assumed debt, and Vistra expects to maintain investment-grade credit metrics. More importantly, the company's $3.6B+ annual FCF provides substantial debt service capacity and deleveraging optionality.

**"Coal exposure is a liability."**
Rebuttal: Vistra's 8.7 GW of coal capacity is being managed down over time. Meanwhile, these assets generate substantial near-term cash flow in ERCOT, where reserve margins are tight and coal provides essential reliability services. The transition to gas and nuclear -- accelerated by the Cogentrix and Meta PPA deals -- is already underway.

**"AI data center power demand could disappoint."**
Rebuttal: Even in a bear case where data center demand grows at half the projected rate, US electricity demand growth from reshoring, EV charging, and commercial electrification alone would tighten power markets. Vistra's fleet benefits from any demand growth, not only AI-driven demand.

## Risk Factors

1. **Natural gas price spike**: A sustained move above $5/MMBtu would compress spark spreads for the gas fleet, though the retail segment provides a partial hedge
2. **Regulatory action on coal retirements**: Accelerated EPA mandates could force earlier-than-planned coal plant closures, creating transition costs
3. **Nuclear operational risk**: Extended unplanned outages at the nuclear fleet would directly impact contracted Meta PPA economics
4. **Cogentrix integration risk**: Any delay in regulatory approvals or integration execution could defer EBITDA contribution
5. **Hyperscaler capex deceleration**: If the AI infrastructure buildout slows, power demand growth forecasts would need to be revised
6. **Iran conflict / energy price volatility**: Prolonged Strait of Hormuz disruption could create macro headwinds despite benefiting energy stocks near-term
