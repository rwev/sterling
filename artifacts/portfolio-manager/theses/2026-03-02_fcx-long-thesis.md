# Long Thesis: Freeport-McMoRan (FCX)

## Investment Summary

Freeport-McMoRan is the world's largest publicly traded copper producer, offering dual exposure to the AI/electrification copper supercycle and the currency debasement thesis through its significant gold production. At $68.08, the stock trades near its all-time high following a 61.5% rally over three months, driven by copper prices at historical highs (~$6/lb) and the approaching restart of the Grasberg Block Cave mine in Q2 2026. The Grasberg restart is the single most important catalyst in the mining sector this year -- it will restore approximately 60% of district copper and 75% of gold production in the second half, with full-run-rate production of 1.6B lbs copper and 1.3M oz gold annually by 2027-2029. FCX generated $10B in adjusted EBITDA in FY2025 despite Grasberg operating at reduced capacity; at full production and current copper/gold prices, EBITDA could reach $14-16B. The stock trades at 11.5x EV/EBITDA on depressed 2025 earnings but only 7-8x on normalized Grasberg production -- a compelling entry for the world's premier copper-gold asset.

## Business Overview

Freeport-McMoRan Inc. is a leading international mining company with headquarters in Phoenix, Arizona. The company operates large, long-lived, geographically diverse assets with significant reserves of copper, gold, and molybdenum.

**Operating Assets:**
- **Grasberg (Indonesia):** One of the world's largest copper and gold deposits. The Grasberg Block Cave underground mine suffered a mud rush incident in September 2025, halting production. Phased restart begins Q2 2026, with 85% of district production targeted for restoration in H2 2026.
- **Americas (Arizona, New Mexico, Colorado, Chile, Peru):** Multiple open-pit copper mines including Morenci (largest copper mine in North America), Cerro Verde (Peru), and El Abra (Chile).
- **Atlantic Copper (Spain):** Custom copper smelting and refining.

**FY2025 Production:**
- Copper: ~4.1B lbs consolidated (reduced from ~4.5B pre-Grasberg incident)
- Gold: ~1.5M oz consolidated (reduced from ~1.8M pre-incident)
- Molybdenum: ~80M lbs

**Revenue Mix:** Approximately 75% copper, 15% gold, 5% molybdenum, 5% other.

## Investment Case

### 1. Grasberg Block Cave Restart -- The Defining Catalyst of 2026

The September 2025 mud rush at Grasberg was a severe operational disruption, but the mine's geology and reserve base are unchanged. FCX has guided for a phased restart beginning Q2 2026, with 85% of district production restored by H2 2026 and full-rate production of 1.6B lbs copper and 1.3M oz gold annually by 2027-2029. This production restoration represents a ~60% increase in Grasberg copper output and ~45% increase in gold output relative to 2025/2026 depressed levels. At current commodity prices, this translates to billions in incremental EBITDA.

### 2. Copper Supercycle Thesis

Copper is the essential metal for electrification -- every data center, EV, solar installation, wind farm, and grid upgrade requires copper. The macro outlook identifies $690B in hyperscaler capex driving data center construction, each requiring massive copper wiring for power distribution. Meanwhile, mine supply growth is constrained at 1-2% annually while demand accelerates. Copper historically rises 18% for every 1% increase in CPI, making it both a growth play and an inflation hedge. JP Morgan projects $12,500/mt ($5.67/lb) for Q2 2026; current prices near $6/lb are at historical highs.

### 3. Copper-Gold Dual Exposure

FCX is unique among major copper miners in having world-class gold production as a byproduct. At full Grasberg production, the company produces 1.3M oz of gold annually. At $5,278/oz gold, this represents ~$6.9B in annual gold revenue -- essentially a free call option on gold within a copper mining investment. This dual exposure captures both the AI/electrification theme (copper) and the currency debasement theme (gold).

### 4. Copper Price Sensitivity Creates Earnings Leverage

FCX has disclosed that each $0.10/lb change in copper price impacts EBITDA by $425M and operating cash flow by $330M. At $6.00/lb vs. the company's ~$1.55/lb unit cash cost, the margin is enormous. If copper were to rise from $6.00 to $6.50/lb, EBITDA would increase by approximately $2.1B. Conversely, copper would need to fall below $3.00/lb for FCX to approach breakeven -- providing substantial downside protection.

### 5. Shareholder Returns Track Record

FCX distributed $5.7B in cash returns to shareholders in FY2025 through dividends and share repurchases, demonstrating commitment to capital returns even during a period of disrupted Grasberg production. At normalized production and current commodity prices, the capacity for shareholder returns would increase materially.

## Earnings Quality

- **EBITDA Quality:** FCX's adjusted EBITDA of $10B in FY2025 is primarily operating-driven. The main adjustment is for the Grasberg mud rush impact, which is a discrete event rather than a recurring charge.
- **FCF vs. EBITDA:** Estimated FCF of ~$3.5B on $10B EBITDA reflects elevated capex for Grasberg remediation and Americas mine development. As remediation capex normalizes, FCF conversion should improve to 50-60% of EBITDA.
- **Balance Sheet:** Modest net debt of ~$2B on $10B EBITDA (0.2x leverage) with $4.5B in cash. The balance sheet is conservative for a cyclical miner.
- **Commodity Price Exposure:** Revenue and EBITDA are highly leveraged to copper and gold prices. This is inherent to the business and understood -- but it means that financial analysis must be conducted at various price scenarios.
- **Indonesia Political Risk:** Grasberg is operated through PT Freeport Indonesia (PTFI), in which the Indonesian government holds a 51% interest. The export permit and operating license framework introduces sovereign risk that is not captured in financial statements.

## Risk Factors

1. **Grasberg Restart Execution:** The mud rush was a serious geological event. If restart is delayed beyond Q2 2026, or if production ramp-up is slower than guided, the stock could de-rate meaningfully. This is the single largest risk.
2. **Copper Price Volatility:** Copper at $6/lb is at historical highs. A global recession, Chinese economic slowdown, or AI capex pullback could drive copper below $5/lb, compressing margins significantly.
3. **Indonesia Sovereign Risk:** The Indonesian government's 51% stake in PTFI and the evolving regulatory framework for mining (export permits, smelter requirements, environmental regulations) introduce political risk. Any change in fiscal terms could impact FCX's economics.
4. **Gold Price Correlation:** While gold exposure is a positive, it also means FCX is vulnerable to gold price corrections. A ceasefire in Iran or hawkish Fed pivot could pressure both copper and gold simultaneously.
5. **Valuation at Current Price:** FCX has rallied 61.5% in three months. At $68 near its all-time high of $69.75, much of the Grasberg restart and copper supercycle thesis is priced in. The risk/reward is less favorable than three months ago.
6. **Environmental and Social Risk:** Mining operations carry inherent environmental risks (tailings, water contamination, land use). Regulatory tightening on environmental standards could increase costs.

## Entry Parameters

- **Entry range:** $58 - $64 (a 6-15% pullback from current $68 levels; would require either a copper price dip or negative Grasberg restart news)
- **Target:** $86 (12-month, based on 9x normalized EBITDA at $5.75/lb copper and $5,000/oz gold)
- **Stop:** $48 (25% below entry midpoint; would correspond to copper below $4.50/lb or serious Grasberg restart failure)

Given the 61.5% run in three months, patience on entry is critical. The preferred strategy is to wait for a copper price pullback (JP Morgan projects $12,500/mt or ~$5.67/lb for Q2, suggesting near-term pricing may ease from current ~$6/lb levels) or any negative headline on Grasberg restart timeline. A 10% pullback to $61-62 would offer a materially better risk/reward.
