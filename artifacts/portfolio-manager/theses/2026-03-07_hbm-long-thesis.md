# Growth Long Thesis: Hudbay Minerals (HBM)

## Investment Summary

Hudbay Minerals is a copper-focused critical minerals producer at a generational growth inflection point. The company delivered record FY2025 revenue of $2.21B (+9.4% YoY) and record adjusted EBITDA of $1.06B (+29% YoY) while simultaneously executing two transformative growth catalysts: the $600M Mitsubishi-partnered Copper World JV (closed January 2026) and the $1.48B acquisition of Arizona Sonoran (announced March 2, 2026). Together, these projects create North America's third-largest copper district and provide a visible path to doubling annual copper production from 125,000 tonnes to 250,000+ tonnes by 2030. At $22.29 with a forward P/E of 12.4x and a PEG ratio of 0.30, the stock is being offered at a steep discount to both its growth trajectory and copper peers. The March sell-off from $28.33 (Feb 27 all-time high) to $22.29 -- driven by a Q4 EPS miss and ASCU acquisition dilution concerns -- creates a textbook growth-at-a-reasonable-price entry point into a structural copper supercycle beneficiary.

## Growth Inflection Case

Hudbay is transitioning from a mid-cap copper/gold producer into a top-tier North American copper growth platform. The inflection has five interlocking drivers:

1. **Production doubling by 2030.** Current consolidated copper output of ~125,000 tonnes/year is guided to expand to 250,000+ tonnes by 2030 via Copper World (85,000 t/yr, 20-year mine life) and potentially 350,000+ tonnes including Cactus (103,000 t/yr). This is not speculative exploration -- Copper World has a completed pre-feasibility study with a ~90% levered IRR to Hudbay (post-Mitsubishi), and a definitive feasibility study is expected mid-2026 with a sanction decision thereafter.

2. **Structural copper deficit.** JPMorgan forecasts a -330,000 tonne global refined deficit in 2026 (ING: -600,000 tonnes; UBS: -400,000 tonnes). S&P Global's January 2026 "Copper in the Age of AI" study projects a 10 million metric ton supply gap by 2040. The demand side is driven by AI data centers (475,000 mt demand in 2026, growing toward 1.1M mt by 2030), EVs, grid electrification, and defense -- none of which are GDP-sensitive. The supply side is constrained by mine disruptions (Grasberg, Kamoa-Kakula), declining ore grades, and a 17-year mine development timeline.

3. **Margin inflection from gold by-product credits.** Hudbay's consolidated cash cost was negative $(0.22)/lb in FY2025 -- one of the lowest in the industry -- because gold (at $5,100+/oz) represented 38% of total 2025 revenue. This transforms the economics: every dollar of gold revenue directly reduces the effective cost of copper production. With gold at record highs, Hudbay's cost position is structurally advantaged.

4. **Section 232 tariff tailwind.** The 50% Section 232 duty on semi-finished copper has created a 27-28% US premium over global copper prices. As Hudbay expands its US production footprint (Copper World and Cactus are both in Arizona), an increasing share of output will sell at elevated US-domestic prices. The Commerce Department review on June 30, 2026 could extend tariffs to refined copper (15% in 2027, 30% in 2028), permanently widening the premium.

5. **Balance sheet transformation.** Net debt fell to $439.7M (0.4x net debt/EBITDA) at year-end 2025, down from $525.7M (0.6x) a year earlier. Post-Mitsubishi closing, cash on hand exceeded $992M with total liquidity above $1.4B. Hudbay's estimated share of remaining Copper World capital contributions is approximately $200M, deferred to 2028 at the earliest. The company has financial flexibility to fund growth without equity dilution beyond the ASCU acquisition.

## Business Overview & TAM

Hudbay Minerals Inc. is a Canadian-based copper-focused critical minerals company with three operating mines and a pipeline of copper growth projects spanning four geographies:

**Operating Mines:**
- **Constancia (Peru):** Open-pit copper-molybdenum-silver mine. FY2025 copper production of 85,155 tonnes. Cash cost $1.08/lb. Pampacancha high-grade satellite is depleting, driving 2026 copper guidance 10% lower at the midpoint.
- **Snow Lake (Manitoba, Canada):** Underground gold-copper-zinc mine complex. FY2025 gold production of 173,453 oz. The primary gold engine, with unstreamed gold production expected to increase in 2026.
- **Copper Mountain (British Columbia, Canada):** Open-pit copper-gold mine. FY2025 copper production of 23,784 tonnes. Throughput ramp ongoing, driving higher 2026 copper contribution.

**Growth Pipeline:**
- **Copper World (Arizona, US):** Flagship growth project. 85,000 t/yr copper over 20-year mine life. 70% Hudbay / 30% Mitsubishi JV. DFS expected mid-2026, sanction decision 2026. Levered IRR ~90% to Hudbay.
- **Cactus Project (Arizona, US):** Acquired via ASCU deal. PFS estimates 103,000 t/yr copper. Adjacent to Copper World, creating an integrated Arizona copper district.
- **Mason (Nevada, US):** Earlier-stage copper porphyry deposit; long-term optionality.

**TAM:** Global refined copper consumption is approximately 26 million tonnes annually, growing at 2-3% per year on structural electrification demand. S&P Global projects a 10 million metric ton cumulative supply gap by 2040. Hudbay's addressable market is vast and growing, with the company positioned to capture outsized share through its US-based growth projects that benefit from domestic supply preferences and tariff premiums.

## Business Drivers

**Driver 1: Copper Price Leverage.** At $5.90/lb copper and AISC of $1.74/lb in FY2025, Hudbay earns approximately $4.16/lb margin on copper. A 10% increase in copper prices ($6.49/lb) would add approximately $0.59/lb in pure margin on 118,000 tonnes of annual production -- roughly $155M in incremental annual EBITDA. This is the fundamental operating leverage that drives mining equity returns in a supercycle.

**Driver 2: Gold By-Product Credits.** Gold at $5,100+/oz means Hudbay's 268,000 oz of annual gold production generates approximately $1.37B in gold revenue -- 62% of total revenue at current prices. This transforms Hudbay's cost structure: consolidated cash costs were negative $(0.22)/lb in FY2025 because gold revenue more than offsets all cash costs of copper production. As long as gold remains above $3,000/oz, Hudbay's copper operations are effectively zero-cost.

**Driver 3: Copper World Development.** The Copper World project (DFS mid-2026, sanction 2026) adds 85,000 t/yr of copper production -- a 68% increase from the current 125,000 t/yr base -- with Mitsubishi funding 30% ($600M total, $420M already received). Hudbay's deferred capital contribution of ~$200M does not begin until 2028. This is a transformational asset with a 20-year mine life, located in Arizona where Section 232 tariff benefits apply.

**Driver 4: Arizona Sonoran / Cactus Acquisition.** The $1.48B all-stock acquisition of ASCU (announced March 2, 2026, expected close Q2 2026) adds the Cactus project adjacent to Copper World, with PFS-estimated production of 103,000 t/yr. Combined with Copper World, Hudbay could reach 350,000+ tonnes of annual copper by the late 2020s/early 2030s -- nearly 3x current output. BMO's Matthew Murphy noted that "Hudbay is adding copper production at a reasonable circa $24,000 per tonne."

**Driver 5: Deleveraging and Capital Returns.** Net debt of $439.7M (0.4x EBITDA) is the lowest in over a decade. Post-Mitsubishi, liquidity exceeds $1.4B. The company doubled its dividend (first increase in company history) and has capacity for further returns as FCF generation accelerates.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Hudbay's operating leverage is among the most compelling in the copper mining sector:

- **FY2024 to FY2025:** Revenue +9.4% drove EBITDA +29%. The 3:1 EBITDA-to-revenue multiplier demonstrates the fixed-cost leverage inherent in mining operations.
- **EBITDA margin path:** 41% (FY2024) to 48% (FY2025). With gold at record highs and copper in structural deficit, margins should sustain or expand further.
- **FCF conversion:** FY2025 FCF of $387.9M on $1,060.9M EBITDA represents 37% FCF conversion. As Copper World capex ramps (funded primarily by Mitsubishi and Wheaton stream), Hudbay's existing operations generate substantial free cash.
- **Production-doubling leverage:** When copper production scales from 125kt to 250kt+, fixed G&A and corporate overhead are spread across a much larger production base. If copper prices sustain $5.50+/lb, the 250kt production scenario implies EBITDA potential of $2.0B+ annually, compared to $1.06B today.

The all-in sustaining cost trajectory tells the margin story clearly: from $2.09/lb in Q3 2025 (wildfire-disrupted) to $1.43/lb in Q4 2025, with the full-year $1.74/lb well below the $5.90/lb copper price. At current prices, Hudbay earns approximately $4.16/lb margin -- and this margin widens with every dollar of copper and gold price appreciation.

## Competitive Position & Moat

1. **Lowest cash costs in peer group.** Consolidated cash cost of negative $(0.22)/lb in FY2025 is among the lowest globally, driven by the gold by-product credit structure. This means Hudbay generates positive cash flow at copper prices that would bankrupt most competitors. The gold hedge is a structural competitive advantage that has no expiry date.

2. **Multi-mine diversification.** Operations span Peru, Manitoba, and British Columbia, with growth in Arizona. Geographic diversification mitigates single-mine operational risk (as demonstrated when Q3 2025 Manitoba wildfires were offset by strong Q4 recovery).

3. **Arizona copper district.** The Copper World + Cactus combination creates North America's third-largest copper district. US-based production benefits from Section 232 tariff premiums (27-28% above global copper) and domestic supply preferences for critical minerals. This is a difficult-to-replicate jurisdictional advantage.

4. **Mitsubishi partnership validation.** A $600M strategic investment from Mitsubishi -- one of Japan's largest trading houses -- validates the Copper World asset quality. Mitsubishi's due diligence and capital commitment provide third-party confirmation of the project economics.

5. **11 consecutive years of meeting copper guidance.** Operational reliability is a competitive moat in mining, where guidance misses are endemic. Hudbay's track record reduces the execution discount that the market typically applies to mining growth stories.

## Risk Factors

1. **Copper price cyclicality.** Despite the structural supercycle thesis, copper is a cyclical commodity. A global recession (elevated probability given NFP -92K and stagflation signals) could drive copper below $4.50/lb, compressing margins significantly. HBM's 52-week range of $5.95-$28.74 demonstrates the equity beta (2.03) inherent in a copper miner. Mitigant: Gold by-product credits provide a natural hedge; consolidated cash costs go negative at current gold prices, meaning profitability is maintained at copper prices as low as $0/lb in theory (gold revenue alone covers all cash costs).

2. **Execution risk on Copper World and Cactus.** Mining project development is notoriously subject to delays, cost overruns, and permitting challenges. The DFS (mid-2026) could reveal higher capex than the PFS estimates. Environmental or regulatory delays in Arizona could push first production beyond 2030. Mitigant: Mitsubishi's $600M investment and Wheaton's precious metals stream de-risk Hudbay's capital exposure; 11-year track record of meeting guidance supports execution credibility.

3. **ASCU acquisition dilution and integration risk.** The all-stock acquisition at 0.242 HBM shares per ASCU share dilutes existing shareholders. The market reacted negatively (-6.8% on announcement day). Integration of a pre-production asset adds complexity. Mitigant: BMO analysis suggests Hudbay is adding copper production at ~$24,000/tonne, which is accretive if copper sustains $5.50+/lb; Cactus is adjacent to Copper World, enabling shared infrastructure.

4. **Operational disruption.** Q3 2025 Manitoba wildfires demonstrated vulnerability to natural disasters. Peru operations face political instability risk (Constancia). Mitigant: Geographic diversification across four jurisdictions; insurance coverage; proven ability to recover quickly (Q4 2025 record results followed Q3 disruption).

5. **Section 232 tariff reversal.** If the June 30, 2026 Commerce Department review leads to tariff reduction or removal, the 27-28% US copper premium would compress, reducing the value proposition of US-based production. Mitigant: The political environment favors domestic critical minerals production; bipartisan support for copper supply chain security.

6. **Gold price decline.** A sharp decline in gold prices (below $3,000/oz) would increase Hudbay's effective copper cash costs by eliminating the by-product credit advantage. Mitigant: Gold is in a structural bull market driven by central bank buying and de-dollarization; current $5,100+ level has strong fundamental support.
