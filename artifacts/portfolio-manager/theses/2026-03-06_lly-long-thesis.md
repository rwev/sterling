# Long Thesis: Eli Lilly (LLY)

## Investment Summary

Eli Lilly is the premier GLP-1 franchise with a defined binary catalyst: the FDA decision on orforglipron (oral GLP-1) on April 10. The company just reported 45% revenue growth in FY 2025 ($65.2B), guided FY 2026 to $80-83B (above the $77.6B consensus), and owns the world's best-selling drug (tirzepatide at $36.5B in 2025 sales). At $979, the stock trades at 28x forward earnings for a company growing revenue 40%+ with a pipeline (retatrutide, orforglipron) that could add $40B+ in incremental peak revenue. In a stagflationary macro environment where cyclical earnings are compressing, Lilly's secular growth trajectory is uniquely insulated from GDP, oil prices, and Fed policy. The orforglipron approval would transform the addressable market by eliminating the injection barrier, expanding the obesity TAM by tens of millions of patients. This is the highest-quality growth stock in healthcare, trading at a reasonable multiple to its actual growth rate.

## Investment Case

**1. Orforglipron is the most important drug catalyst in 2026.** The FDA decision date is April 10. Approval of an oral GLP-1 for obesity would be transformational: approximately 40% of potential patients cite injection aversion as the primary barrier to treatment. Phase 3 data is strong -- ATTAIN-1 showed all three doses (6mg, 12mg, 36mg) achieved superior weight reduction vs. placebo, and ATTAIN-MAINTAIN demonstrated that patients switching from injectable incretins (Wegovy, Zepbound) to orforglipron maintained their weight loss with only 0.9 kg average difference from semaglutide switchers. UBS models $2B in orforglipron revenue for 2026 alone (H2 launch), ramping to $10B+ annually at maturity. This drug is not priced into the stock at current levels.

**2. The tirzepatide franchise is the world's best-selling drug and still accelerating.** Mounjaro generated $23B in 2025 sales (+110% YoY in Q4), and Zepbound generated $13.5B (+122% YoY in Q4). Combined tirzepatide revenue of $36.5B surpassed Keytruda ($31.7B) as the world's best-selling drug franchise. The growth is not slowing: Q4 2025 U.S. Mounjaro revenue was $4.1B (+57%), U.S. Zepbound was $4.2B (+122%), and international Mounjaro was $3.3B (vs. $899M prior year). International expansion is still in early innings -- Zepbound is not yet launched in most ex-US markets.

**3. The pipeline is the deepest in the industry.** Beyond orforglipron, Lilly has retatrutide (GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist), which showed 28.7% average body weight loss at the highest dose in Phase 3 -- the most weight loss ever demonstrated by a GLP-1 drug. Regulatory submission expected 2026, potential launch 2028. Clarivate projects retatrutide peak revenue of $30B ($10B obesity + $20B diabetes). Lilly's total pipeline addressable market across orforglipron + retatrutide + existing tirzepatide expansion is conservatively $100B+ at maturity.

**4. Medicare coverage creates a structural step-function in demand.** Medicare coverage of GLP-1 drugs for weight loss begins mid-2026. This opens access to 65M+ Medicare beneficiaries, many of whom have the highest obesity prevalence. The Trump administration's deal capping monthly GLP-1 cost at $245 for eligible patients ensures volume growth, even if it constrains per-unit pricing. Lilly's CFO has confirmed launch readiness for orforglipron in Q2 2026 pending FDA approval, timing perfectly with Medicare coverage.

## Business Overview

Eli Lilly and Company ($874.7B market cap) is a global pharmaceutical company headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. The company operates primarily in three therapeutic areas:

- **Cardiometabolic (Diabetes + Obesity)**: Mounjaro (tirzepatide for type 2 diabetes) and Zepbound (tirzepatide for obesity) -- combined $36.5B in 2025 revenue, representing 56% of total sales. This franchise is growing faster than any drug franchise in pharmaceutical history.
- **Oncology**: Verzenio (breast cancer) -- a steady contributor but not a growth driver at this stage.
- **Neuroscience**: Donanemab (Kisunla, for Alzheimer's) -- FDA approved July 2024. Early commercial traction but modest revenue contribution in 2025.

The company employs approximately 44,000 people and operates manufacturing facilities across the US, Ireland, and Puerto Rico. Lilly has invested heavily in manufacturing capacity expansion to meet GLP-1 demand, including a new $4.5B facility in Lebanon, Indiana.

Key competitive advantages: (1) tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist, demonstrating superior efficacy to semaglutide (Novo Nordisk) in head-to-head studies; (2) orforglipron is a small-molecule oral formulation that does not require food restrictions, unlike Novo's oral semaglutide; (3) retatrutide is a first-in-class triple agonist with no competitor in the category.

## Earnings Quality

**GAAP vs. Adjusted divergence**: Lilly's non-GAAP adjustments primarily strip out acquired IPR&D charges, amortization of intangibles, and asset impairment charges. The gap between GAAP and adjusted EPS is well-disclosed and relates to identifiable, non-recurring items. No red flags. FY 2025 GAAP EPS of $22.95 vs. guided FY 2026 non-GAAP EPS of $33.50-35.00 shows the core business trajectory clearly.

**FCF conversion**: FCF/Net Income conversion was 44% in FY 2025, which appears low but is explained by the massive manufacturing capex program ($5B+ annually). This is growth investment, not earnings quality deterioration. As capex normalizes post-2027, FCF conversion should normalize to 70%+.

**Working capital trends**: Inventory has increased materially as Lilly builds GLP-1 product stock to meet demand. This is appropriate given the supply constraints the industry has experienced. Receivables are growing in line with revenue -- no collection issues.

**Revenue quality**: Tirzepatide revenue is driven by volume growth (prescriptions), not price increases. In fact, realized prices are declining due to government programs and PBM negotiations. Volume-driven growth is the highest-quality revenue in pharma.

## Catalysts

- **April 10: Orforglipron FDA decision** -- The defining binary event. Approval opens the oral GLP-1 market and gives Lilly first-mover advantage over Novo's oral semaglutide in the obesity indication (Novo's oral Wegovy was just approved but has dietary restrictions). This is the single most important drug approval event of 2026.
- **April 30: Q1 2026 earnings** -- First quarter with FY 2026 guidance in place. Tirzepatide quarterly revenue run-rate will provide visibility into the $80-83B full-year target. Orforglipron pre-launch commentary will be closely watched.
- **Mid-2026: Medicare GLP-1 coverage begins** -- Opens 65M+ beneficiaries to weight loss treatment. Volume step-function for both Zepbound and (if approved) orforglipron.
- **2026: Retatrutide regulatory submission** -- Filing of the triple agonist for obesity. Phase 3 data showing 28.7% weight loss will anchor the submission. Market will begin pricing retatrutide's $30B peak revenue potential.
- **Ongoing: International Zepbound expansion** -- Zepbound is in early-stage international launch. International Mounjaro revenue grew from $899M to $3.3B in Q4 alone -- Zepbound international ramp could be even faster.

## Risk Factors

1. **Orforglipron FDA rejection or Complete Response Letter (probability: 15-20%).** The Phase 3 data is strong and the clinical benefit is clear, but the FDA could request additional safety data, particularly around GI tolerability or liver signals. A CRL would send the stock to $850-900 rapidly. Position sizing must account for this binary risk.

2. **GLP-1 pricing pressure.** The Trump administration's $245/month cap, PBM negotiations, and potential Inflation Reduction Act impacts could compress margins. Lilly has managed this so far (volume growth overwhelms per-unit price declines), but a more aggressive government pricing intervention is a tail risk.

3. **Competitive landscape intensification.** Novo Nordisk's oral Wegovy is already on market. Pfizer's danuglipron/aleniglipron is advancing. Viking Therapeutics (VKTX) has early-stage competition. While Lilly's pipeline is deepest, the market is becoming crowded. A superior competitor emerging would re-rate all GLP-1 stocks downward.

4. **Manufacturing execution risk.** Lilly has invested $4.5B+ in new manufacturing capacity, but GLP-1 demand has repeatedly outstripped supply. If orforglipron is approved, Lilly needs to execute a flawless commercial launch while simultaneously maintaining Mounjaro/Zepbound supply. Manufacturing constraints would limit revenue upside.

5. **Valuation compression in a risk-off environment.** If the stagflation regime deepens and the market broadly de-rates growth multiples, Lilly's 28x forward P/E could compress to 22-25x, implying $750-875. The secular growth narrative provides some insulation, but macro-driven multiple compression is a real risk.
