The Risk Manager flagged five items in the Round 4 assessment. Each is addressed below:

The Risk Manager flagged a discrepancy between the draft's VST price of ~$149 and a market data reference of ~$173. The PM conducted an independent price verification via web search on March 1, 2026. **Conclusion:** The draft IC memo's VST price of ~$149 is correct. The stop proximity warning stands: VST is 6.4% above the $140 hard stop. This remains the most acute near-term risk for the largest portfolio position. The stop-loss contingency plan from prior ICs remains in full effect. No change to the HOLD decision at 20.0%, but the urgency of daily stop monitoring is confirmed.

### 2. Elevated VIX and Phased Entry Timing -- ACCEPTED WITH MODIFICATION

The Risk Manager noted VIX has risen 32% from 15.1 to 19.9, and recommended the PM consider reducing tranche sizes (Option B: AVGO from 10% to 7%, COHR from 4% to 3%) to maintain a 15% cash buffer post-execution.

**Decision: Accept modified tranche sizing for AVGO. Maintain COHR tranche as planned.**

- **AVGO remaining tranche: Reduced from 10.0% to 8.0%.** If AVGO beats on March 4 and confirms AI revenue trajectory, the PM will execute 8.0% accumulation over 3-5 trading days (not 10.0%). This brings the AVGO target allocation from 15.0% to 13.0%, maintaining a 11.0% cash buffer post-AVGO execution (vs. 9.0% under the original plan). The 2.0% reduction provides a meaningful cash cushion in the elevated-VIX environment without sacrificing the core position thesis.
- **COHR remaining tranche: Unchanged at 4.0%.** The OFC catalyst on March 17 is two weeks away, allowing time for VIX to normalize. If VIX remains above 22 at the time of execution, the PM will reassess tranche sizing at that point.
- **Sequential execution confirmed.** Per the Risk Manager's recommendation, AVGO tranche completes first (post-March 4), then COHR tranche executes post-OFC (March 17+). No simultaneous deployment.

**Updated target allocation:**
- AVGO: 13.0% (reduced from 15.0%)
- COHR: 12.0% (unchanged)
- Total invested (target): 89.0% (reduced from 91.0%)
- Cash (target): 11.0% (increased from 9.0%)

### 3. AVGO March 4 Compound Risk -- ACCEPTED WITH CONTINGENCY

The Risk Manager identified that AVGO earnings on March 4 coincide with tariff escalation (25% tariffs on Canada/Mexico, China tariffs doubled to 20% taking effect the same day). The compound scenario (AVGO miss + tariff selloff + VIX spike) could produce -5.5% to -7.0% NAV impact at deployed weights, consuming 65-82% of drawdown headroom.

**Decision: Accept compound risk at current deployed weights. Add contingency protocol.**

Rationale: At 5.0% deployed AVGO weight, the direct exposure to earnings is contained. The compound scenario risk of -5.5% to -7.0% is within the -10% drawdown limit. The 23% effective cash buffer at deployed weights provides substantial protection. However, the PM adds the following contingency:

**AVGO post-earnings tranche contingency (NEW):** If AVGO beats earnings BUT the S&P 500 declines more than 3% on March 4-5 due to tariff-driven selling, the AVGO tranche execution will be delayed by 5 trading days to allow tariff volatility to subside. This prevents deploying capital into a strong AVGO result during a simultaneous macro selloff. The tranche executes only when both conditions are met: (a) AVGO earnings confirmation, AND (b) S&P 500 stabilization (defined as no further decline exceeding 1% on the day of execution).

### 4. Diversification Rejection Documentation -- ACKNOWLEDGED

The Risk Manager concurred that the IC explicitly evaluated seven drone/defense diversification opportunities and rejected them on quality grounds, not oversight. The quantitative benefit of adding a low-beta defense name (RTX at 0.50 beta) was marginal -- approximately 0.10-0.15% VaR reduction -- and does not justify a position that fails the PM's quality criteria.

**For the record:** The IC evaluated seven pitches across four analyst teams covering the full drone/defense spectrum (pure-play drones, counter-drone systems, defense primes, defense AI software, small-cap drone manufacturers). All were rejected for specific, documented reasons including above-entry pricing, insufficient risk/reward ratios, negative free cash flow, extreme valuations, and structural issues (ASX listing, political risk, single-contract dependency). The 83% AI infrastructure concentration is a deliberate, thesis-driven decision.

### 5. Cash Buffer During Tranche Execution -- ADDRESSED (See Item 2)

The Risk Manager recommended staggering tranche execution to maintain a higher average cash buffer. This is addressed in Item 2 above: AVGO tranche reduced to 8.0% and executes first; COHR tranche follows post-OFC. The average cash buffer through the March catalyst window will be approximately 18% (vs. 23% currently and 11% after full execution).
