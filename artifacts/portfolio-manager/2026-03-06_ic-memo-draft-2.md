### Pitch 1: Zscaler (ZS) -- Growth Analyst

**Price**: $165.91 (+2.4%) | **Entry**: $155-175 (WITHIN) | **Target**: $250 | **Stop**: $135
**R/R at current**: $30.91 risk / $84.09 upside = **2.72:1**
**Analyst recommendation**: Entry at $155-175, 6-9 month horizon

**Thesis summary**: ZS is exhibiting a confirmed growth re-acceleration -- revenue growth inflected from 21% trough to 26% over two consecutive quarters, RPO growing 31% (leading indicator), and ARR re-accelerating to 25%. The stock is down 51% from its November 2025 high and trades at the cheapest valuation on a growth-adjusted basis since its 2018 IPO (7.5x NTM EV/Revenue vs 10.5x peer median). Three converging catalysts: (1) Iranian state-sponsored cyber escalation against US critical infrastructure with 15+ wiper malware families targeting banks and airports, (2) CISA capability degradation from DOGE workforce cuts forcing emergency private-sector security spending, and (3) the AI Security Suite launch expanding TAM. Q2 FY2026 revenue of $815.8M beat guidance by $17-19M. Non-GAAP operating margins expanding to 22.2%.

**Thesis quality assessment**: This is a high-quality growth thesis with multiple data points confirming the re-acceleration narrative. The RPO growth of 31% exceeding revenue growth of 26% is the strongest leading indicator -- it signals bookings momentum is accelerating ahead of the P&L. The Iran cyber escalation and CISA degradation catalysts are timely and well-documented (Palo Alto Unit 42 threat brief, CNBC reporting). The valuation discount to peers is genuine and quantifiable. The organic growth concern (ex-Red Canary organic net new ARR of $139M, +7% YoY) is the legitimate bear case, but the 31% RPO growth argues against fundamental demand deceleration.

**Displacement Analysis**: ZS at 2.72:1 R/R exceeds VRT (1.7:1), CEG (1.8:1), INTC (1.9:1), and AVGO (1.9:1). The strongest displacement candidate is VRT at 13% allocation and 1.7:1 R/R -- a 60% R/R disadvantage to ZS. However:

- VRT is at the bottom of its entry range ($246.93 vs $240-260) during a VIX spike. Selling here crystallizes losses at the worst possible time.
- VRT's data center super-cycle thesis is intact, with J.P. Morgan Industrials Conference March 18 as the next catalyst.
- ZS, while compelling, is a new pitch without a confirmed catalyst within the next 30 days -- the Q3 FY2026 earnings are not until late May.
- The current VIX environment (25-28) makes adding risk inappropriate. The 20% cash position is strategic.

**Verdict**: The R/R case for ZS over VRT is clear on the numbers, but the execution timing is wrong. Displacing VRT at the bottom of its range during a VIX spike to deploy into a new position is selling low and adding risk when risk is already elevated. ZS deserves capital when conditions normalize.

**Decision: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL** -- Priority 2 in the conditional pipeline (behind PANW as VST contingency, but ahead of existing non-contingency conditionals). ZS replaces PANW as the preferred cybersecurity deployment if a slot opens independent of VST stop-out. Entry conditions: (1) VIX below 22 for 2+ sessions, AND (2) slot availability through portfolio exit or FRO stop-out, AND (3) ZS price within $155-175 entry range. Target allocation: 7-8% of NAV.

### Pitch 2: Eli Lilly (LLY) -- Long Analyst

**Price**: $979.16 (-0.4%) | **Entry**: $950-990 (WITHIN) | **Target**: $1,175 | **Stop**: $870
**R/R at current**: $109.16 risk / $195.84 upside = **1.79:1**
**Analyst recommendation**: Entry at $950-990, 3-5% allocation, phased entry around FDA

**Thesis summary**: LLY is the premier GLP-1 franchise with orforglipron (oral GLP-1) FDA decision on April 10. FY 2025 revenue grew 45% to $65.2B, FY 2026 guided $80-83B (above consensus). Tirzepatide at $36.5B in 2025 sales is the world's best-selling drug franchise. Forward P/E of 28.3x on $34.25 midpoint EPS guidance with 40%+ revenue growth implies PEG of ~0.6x. The orforglipron approval would eliminate the injection barrier for GLP-1 therapy, expanding TAM by tens of millions of patients. Medicare GLP-1 coverage begins mid-2026. Pipeline includes retatrutide ($30B peak revenue potential). Lilly's growth is entirely independent of GDP, oil prices, and Fed policy -- a genuine stagflation haven.

**Thesis quality assessment**: Excellent fundamental analysis. The GLP-1 secular growth story is among the strongest in global equities. The orforglipron binary event is both the opportunity and the primary risk: 15-20% probability of FDA rejection could send the stock to $850-900. The thesis correctly identifies this as the key risk. The analyst's recommended 3-5% sizing with phased entry around the FDA decision reflects appropriate caution. LLY's complete uncorrelation with our portfolio themes (AI, energy, defense, gold) is a significant portfolio construction benefit.

**Displacement Analysis**: LLY at 1.79:1 R/R exceeds VRT (1.7:1) but only marginally. The displacement case against VRT is weak -- a 5% R/R advantage does not justify the transaction costs and disruption of exiting a 13% position. Against CEG (1.8:1), LLY is equivalent. The displacement case is not compelling on R/R alone.

However, on a portfolio construction basis, LLY adds genuinely uncorrelated exposure (healthcare/pharma vs AI/energy/defense). The binary catalyst on April 10 creates a known information event that could transform the thesis. The optimal entry strategy is post-FDA-approval, when binary risk is removed and the stock likely trades at $1,050-1,100 with a compressed but still attractive R/R.

**Decision: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL** -- Entry conditions: (1) Orforglipron FDA approval on April 10 confirmed, AND (2) slot availability through portfolio exit, AND (3) post-approval price assessment yields R/R above 1.5:1. Pre-FDA entry is inadvisable given the 15-20% rejection probability and current VIX environment. Target allocation: 5% of NAV.

### Pitch 3: Barrick Mining (B) -- Contrarian Analyst

**Price**: $45.17 (-0.4%) | **Entry**: $43-47 (WITHIN) | **Target**: $62 | **Stop**: $37
**R/R at current**: $8.17 risk / $16.83 upside = **2.06:1**
**Analyst recommendation**: Entry at $43-47, ~5% allocation

**Thesis summary**: Barrick trades at a 22% discount to gold miner peers on EV/EBITDA despite generating $3,800+/oz margins at $5,400 gold, sitting in a $2.0B net cash position, and holding the world-class Fourmile discovery (15.6M oz at 15-17 g/t). The market is punishing Barrick for a minor production guidance reduction (2.90-3.25M oz vs 3.26M oz in FY2025) and the Newmont JV dispute. GAAP/Non-GAAP EPS convergence ($2.93/$2.93 in FY2025) confirms clean earnings quality. North American IPO planned for late 2026 could unlock conglomerate discount.

**Thesis quality assessment**: Strong contrarian analysis with a well-documented variant perception. The GAAP/Non-GAAP convergence is a genuine quality signal. The Fourmile asset is legitimately world-class and undervalued. However, the thesis is built on $5,400 gold -- and gold is currently at $5,081-5,097, approximately 6% below the thesis assumption. The Newmont JV dispute is a real near-term overhang, and the production guidance decline, while immaterial at high gold prices, signals operational headwinds.

**Critical concern: Gold correlation with existing AEM position.** We already hold AEM at 5% allocation. Adding B would give us 8-10% gold mining exposure. AEM at 2.5:1 R/R is already a stronger gold play than B at 2.06:1, with better operational execution (record 3.45M oz production, $979/oz cash costs) and no JV disputes.

**Displacement Analysis**: B at 2.06:1 does not exceed any existing position's R/R sufficiently to warrant displacement. VRT (1.7:1) is the weakest, but B's correlation with AEM (already held) means adding B does not diversify the portfolio -- it concentrates gold mining exposure.

**Decision: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL** -- Low priority. Entry conditions: (1) Gold sustains above $5,300 for 5+ sessions, AND (2) AEM position review determines whether to swap AEM for B (or add B alongside AEM), AND (3) Newmont JV dispute resolution or clear path to resolution, AND (4) slot availability. The case for B over AEM is not compelling at current prices. If gold rallies to $5,500+ and the AEM resize trigger activates, B could become relevant as a second gold miner. Target allocation: 5% of NAV (only if AEM is also held, bringing gold mining to 10%).

**Decision: REJECT.**

**Rejection rationale**: R/R of 1.46:1 is below our 1.5:1 minimum threshold. PG is a fine business but wrong for a concentrated long-only portfolio seeking outsized returns. The 5-7% annual EPS growth and 2.77% dividend yield produce an 8-10% total return profile -- competitive with bonds but not competitive with our existing positions averaging 2.3:1 R/R. Our 20% cash position already provides the defensive cushion that PG would offer. XLP's +15.2% YTD performance suggests the defensive rotation is already priced. The opportunity cost of deploying 5% of NAV in PG versus holding cash (earning 3.5-3.75% at Fed Funds with full deployment optionality) does not justify the slot or capital.
