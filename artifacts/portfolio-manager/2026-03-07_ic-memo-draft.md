## New Pitch Reviews -- Copper Supercycle Batch

### Strategic Framework: Copper Exposure for Sterling

Before evaluating individual pitches, the PM establishes the strategic framework for copper exposure:

**1. Copper reinforces the core thesis.** Sterling's portfolio is built around AI/electrification infrastructure (VRT, CEG, VST, AVGO, INTC = 47% of portfolio). Copper is the physical commodity that makes electrification possible -- every data center, EV charger, and grid upgrade requires copper wiring, transformers, and switchgear. Adding a copper miner is not a thematic departure; it is a supply-chain extension of the existing thesis.

**2. Copper adds genuine diversification.** Despite the thematic connection, copper mining equities have low correlation with AI infrastructure stocks. The revenue driver is commodity pricing, not tech earnings multiples. A copper miner's stock moves with copper prices, mine production, and mining-sector sentiment -- different factors than those driving VRT, AVGO, or CEG. This creates portfolio-level diversification within thematic coherence.

**3. Portfolio capacity constraints are binding.** The portfolio is 10/10. Any active copper position requires either: (a) exiting an existing position, or (b) adding to the conditional pipeline and waiting for a slot to open. Given that all 10 existing positions are HOLD with R/R above 1.5:1, and the weakest R/R is LHX at 1.79:1, forced displacement is a high bar. The conditional pipeline (now 9/10 after LEU drop) has one open slot.

**4. How much copper exposure is appropriate?** Sterling currently has zero direct commodity exposure (AEM is a gold miner at 5%, but that serves a different function). Adding 3-5% in copper mining would be a meaningful but not excessive new sector allocation. The PM would not allocate more than 10% total to metals mining (AEM 5% + copper 5% = 10%), and the metals mining combined cap should be monitored.

**5. Selection criteria for the copper slot.** Given that at most 1-2 copper names can enter the conditional pipeline (one slot open, possibly a second if the PM identifies a weak existing conditional to drop), the PM must rank the five pitches and select the highest-conviction name(s). The evaluation criteria are: R/R ratio, thesis quality, entry timing (is the stock at or near the entry range?), beta/volatility appropriateness for Sterling, and operational quality (clean financials, positive FCF, manageable leverage).

**Price:** $55.90 | **Entry:** $46-52 | **Target:** $57 | **Stop:** $39
**R/R:** 0.08:1 at current price ($1.10 upside / $16.90 downside) | **Analyst:** Long

**Rejection rationale:**

For a single copper position, FCX is superior." The analyst is telling us this is the second-best option. Sterling does not invest in the second-best option within a single sector.

3. **Negative free cash flow.** FCF was -$583M in FY2025 on $2.06B in capex. The company is in an investment phase where cash generation is negative. Trailing ROIC of ~4% is well below the estimated 13.5% WACC -- the company is currently destroying economic value.
