## New Pitch Reviews

### Context: The Slot Constraint

The portfolio holds 10 positions (the maximum). Cash is 9% of NAV. Approving any new pitch requires either (a) closing an existing position or (b) reducing one or more existing positions to fund and make room for the new name. The drone/defense theme is directionally interesting, but every pitch must clear the bar: is it worth displacing or reducing a holding in our AI infrastructure portfolio?

Additionally, the current portfolio has a coherent thematic thesis: AI infrastructure supercycle. Introducing defense/drone names creates thematic dilution, factor drift, and potentially reduces the Sharpe ratio by adding uncorrelated-but-lower-conviction positions.

### Assessment of the Autonomous Drones Theme

The macro outlook is well-constructed. The structural case for autonomous drones is legitimate: $13.4B Pentagon FY2026 autonomy budget, doctrinal shift confirmed by Ukraine/Russia, Iran crisis accelerating timelines, bipartisan support. The 13.8% military drone CAGR and 22% counter-drone CAGR are real secular growth rates.

However, the PM notes several portfolio-level concerns:

1. **Valuation discipline.** AVAV at 72x adjusted EPS, KTOS at 647x GAAP P/E (132x adjusted), PLTR at 46x forward revenue, RCAT at 9x FY2026E revenue with near-zero gross margins, UMAC at 60x TTM revenue. These multiples demand near-perfect execution in an uncertain procurement environment.

2. **Procurement execution risk.** The Replicator program already demonstrated the gap between Pentagon ambition and actual delivery. "1 million drones" is aspiration, not obligation.

3. **Private market competition.** Anduril ($30.5B), Shield AI, and Helsing capture disproportionate share of autonomous systems innovation. Public-market pure-plays may be the second-best horse.

4. **Opportunity cost.** Every dollar in a defense name is a dollar not in AVGO, NVDA, or COHR -- names with binding backlogs, proven unit economics, and active catalyst paths in the next 30 days (AVGO earnings March 4, OFC March 17).

5. **Portfolio is full.** No slots available without displacement. The AI infrastructure thesis is working -- disturbing it requires a very high bar.

## IC Decision: No Changes to Portfolio

The PM has reviewed all eight upstream documents (one macro outlook, seven theses) and determined that none of the drone/defense pitches justify displacing an existing AI infrastructure holding. The rationale is threefold:

1. **The AI infrastructure thesis is working.** AVGO earnings March 4, OFC March 17, and the hyperscaler earnings season (April-May) provide near-term catalysts that validate the existing portfolio. The appropriate action is to execute the phased entry plans already in motion (AVGO 5% to 15%, COHR 8% to 12%), not to disrupt the portfolio with a thematic pivot.

2. **The drone pitches do not clear the quality bar.** No pitched name offers a risk/reward ratio above 2.1:1. The best R/R in the drone cohort (KTOS at 2.1:1) comes with negative FCF, 647x GAAP P/E, and active dilution. Meanwhile, existing positions offer AVGO (4.3:1), VRT (1.5:1), NVDA (2.0:1), and MRVL (2.0:1).

3. **The slot constraint is binding.** All 10 slots are filled with conviction positions. The weakest position by R/R (EME at 1.2:1) still has record earnings, $13.3B backlog, and direct AI infrastructure alignment. There is no position weak enough to justify displacement by a drone name.

The PM acknowledges the structural merit of the autonomous drones theme and directs analysts to maintain monitoring for re-pitch opportunities. If the portfolio generates a slot opening (VST stop triggered, EME target reached, or any other close), the drone theme should be revisited -- with priority given to KTOS (best contrarian setup if FCF inflects) and AVAV (best pure-play if price retraces to entry range).
