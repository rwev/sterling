## Risk Feedback Integration

This final memo incorporates the Risk Manager's Cycle III assessment dated 2026-03-02. Seven recommendations were made; each is addressed below. Two allocation adjustments are made in response to risk flags. No positions are reversed.

### Risk Flag Responses

**1. AI Electrification Chain at 38-41% of NAV (vs. 25% Theme Guideline)**

**Decision: Accepted with mitigation.** The chain is reduced from 38-41% to 36-39% via a VST allocation cut (10% to 8%). This remains above the 25% guideline, and the IC formally acknowledges this as a deliberate, thesis-driven concentration decision.

Rationale for acceptance:
- The Risk Manager classifies this as Advisory, not Critical, and explicitly states the sub-theme diversification is real -- the effective independent bet equivalent within the cluster is approximately 2.0-2.5 positions, implying the true "single theme" exposure is 18-20% of NAV, below the 25% guideline.
- The four names occupy genuinely different value chain positions: baseload generation (CEG), diversified generation (VST), thermal/power equipment (VRT), and custom silicon (AVGO). A semiconductor inventory correction does not hit power generators; a nuclear regulatory event does not hit VRT or AVGO.
- Aggregate stop-out loss for the cluster is 5.8-6.7% of NAV (reduced from 6.5-7.4% after VST trim). This is within tolerance.
- The demand driver ($690B hyperscaler capex for 2026) has been independently confirmed by Microsoft, Amazon, Google, and Meta corporate announcements. This is not speculative extrapolation.

**Ceiling policy enacted**: No additional AI electrification chain names (ETN, GEV, PWR) may be added without a corresponding reduction within the cluster. The current 36-39% is the maximum. Promoting ETN would require reducing VRT or one of the power generators.

**2. VaR 95% at 2.24% Exceeds 2.0% Guideline (First Exceedance)**

**Decision: Accepted with partial mitigation.** The VST reduction from 10% to 8% reduces estimated VaR 95% from 2.24% to approximately 2.12-2.15%. This remains modestly above the 2.0% guideline.

Rationale for acceptance:
- The Risk Manager notes that VIX at 22.40 (vs. typical 15-18) is inflating the parametric VaR calculation. At a normalized VIX of 18, portfolio VaR 95% would be approximately 1.85-1.90%, within guideline.
- The exceedance is 6-8% above guideline (after VST trim), which is modest.
- Reducing further to bring VaR mechanically below 2.0% would require cutting high-conviction positions with strong R/R profiles -- this is an unacceptable tradeoff. VaR is an input to decision-making, not a hard constraint that overrides thesis quality.
- VaR will be tracked weekly. If it persists above 2.2% for two consecutive IC cycles without elevated VIX as explanation, allocation reductions will be considered.

**3. Effective Diversification Declined from ~5.5-6.0 to ~5.0-5.5 Independent Bets**

**Decision: Acknowledged.** The decline is driven by two factors: (a) removal of BABA (uncorrelated diversifier) per the ADR prohibition directive, and (b) addition of correlated power generation names. The VST trim partially addresses this. The portfolio retains 17% in genuinely uncorrelated positions (LHX 12%, AEM 5%) plus 7% in low-correlation positions (IPGP). This provides real risk offset in stress scenarios -- the Iran escalation stress test shows the portfolio approximately flat, which is a meaningful structural strength.

**4. VST Contributes the Most Correlated Risk of Any New Position**

**Decision: Reduce VST from 10% to 8%.** VST has the highest marginal risk contribution (0.35-0.40% of NAV) of any new position, driven by its elevated beta (1.85) and high correlation with existing holdings. At 8%, the marginal risk contribution declines to approximately 0.28-0.32%, and the AI electrification chain drops from 38-41% to 36-39%. The 2% freed capital moves to cash, improving the portfolio's buffer.

This reduction does not reflect reduced conviction in the VST thesis. The 2.3:1 R/R remains the most asymmetric in the portfolio. The trim is a portfolio construction decision, not a thesis call.

**5. Risk Manager Recommendation: Establish Hard Exit Criterion for VST**

**Decision: Adopted.** If Q1 2026 earnings (May 13) produce a fourth consecutive quarterly EPS miss AND 2026 EBITDA guidance is lowered below $6.8B, the VST position will be reduced to 4% or exited entirely. The contrarian thesis requires earnings normalization -- one more quarter of misses combined with lowered guidance would invalidate the thesis.

**6. Risk Manager Recommendation: AI Electrification Chain Ceiling**

**Decision: Adopted.** See Flag 1 above. The ceiling is formally set at the current 36-39%. Any conditional promotion from the electrification pipeline (ETN, GEV, PWR) requires a corresponding reduction within the chain.

**7. Risk Manager Recommendation: Consider Loss of International Diversification**

**Decision: Acknowledged, no immediate action.** The ADR prohibition eliminates all international options from the current pipeline. The portfolio is 100% US-listed and US-revenue-dominant. This is a known geographic concentration risk. Future analyst pitches for non-ADR international direct listings (e.g., companies with dual primary listings) would receive favorable consideration for diversification value.
