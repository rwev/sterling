## Risk Manager Response

The Risk Manager's inaugural report (2026-03-02 22:45 UTC) flagged five position-specific items and two portfolio-level concerns. Each is addressed below.

### Flag 1: VRT at 15% — Single-Name Concentration Limit with Weakest R/R (1.2:1)
**Risk recommendation**: Reduce VRT from 15.0% to 12-13%.
**Decision: ACCEPTED. VRT reduced to 13.0%.**

The Risk Manager is right. A 15% allocation demands either the best R/R in the portfolio or an overwhelmingly superior thesis quality to justify sitting at the concentration limit. VRT has the thesis quality (252% order growth, $15B backlog) but the 1.2:1 R/R is objectively the weakest approved position. Sizing at 13% still expresses strong conviction while creating 2% of dry powder that can be deployed if VRT pulls back to improve R/R, or allocated to a diversifying name in the next cycle. This is not a thesis downgrade — it is proper risk-sizing.

### Flag 2: AI Infrastructure Theme at 28% Exceeds 25% Guideline
**Risk recommendation**: Advisory; will become Critical if GEV deploys (40-43%).
**Decision: ACCEPTED with modification. VRT trim reduces AI Infra to 26%. Accept 1% breach as Advisory.**

With VRT at 13% and AVGO at 13%, AI Infrastructure stands at 26% — 1 percentage point above the 25% guideline. This marginal breach is accepted for the following documented reasons: (1) hyperscaler capex at $690B (+60% y/y) is the most significant private infrastructure buildout in history; (2) VRT and AVGO occupy distinct value chain positions (thermal/power management vs. custom silicon); (3) the 49% cash position limits portfolio-level tail risk.

**Regarding conditional GEV deployment**: If GEV reaches entry range and is promoted, the PM commits to one of: (a) reducing VRT or AVGO to keep AI Infra at or below 30%, or (b) presenting explicit IC justification for exceeding 30%. The 40-43% scenario flagged by Risk will not occur without a formal IC decision.

### Flag 3: VRT-AVGO Correlation (0.70-0.80)
**Risk recommendation**: Effective diversification reduced; portfolio has ~3.5 independent bets, not 5.
**Decision: RISK ACCEPTED with documented rationale.**

The correlation is acknowledged and correctly identified. However, we are not diversifying for diversification's sake. Both positions are in the portfolio because each independently clears our R/R threshold and thesis quality bar. The VRT trim from 15% to 13% reduces the combined correlated cluster from 28% to 26%, which moderately improves the effective diversification. The 12% LHX defense position (correlation 0.15-0.25 with both VRT and AVGO) provides genuine non-correlation. The portfolio's effective independent bet count of ~3.5 is acceptable for Day 1 of a concentrated fund with 49% cash.

### Flag 4: Worst Stress Scenario — AI/Tech Rotation -20% Costs 6.8-8.2% of NAV
**Risk recommendation**: Informational; scenario documented.
**Decision: RISK ACCEPTED.**

A 6.8-8.2% drawdown in a severe AI/tech rotation is within our tolerance for a portfolio that is structurally long the AI capex super-cycle. The 49% cash position means this scenario costs less than 5% of NAV at current immediate deployment levels. If the rotation occurs, the cash provides capacity to add to positions at better prices. This is a feature of the portfolio construction, not a bug.

### Flag 5: AVGO Binary Earnings Event (March 4)
**Risk recommendation**: 5% pre-earnings sizing is appropriate; no changes needed.
**Decision: CONCUR. No changes.**

The phased 5%/8% structure is confirmed. Worst-case pre-earnings loss of 0.75-1.56% of NAV is within acceptable bounds.

### Flag 6: INTC at 8% — Turnaround Risk
**Risk recommendation**: Sizing proportionate to risk. No action needed.
**Decision: CONCUR. No changes.**

### Flag 7: INOD at 5% — Customer Concentration and Liquidity

**Decision: CONCUR. INOD capped at 5% until customer diversification improves.**
