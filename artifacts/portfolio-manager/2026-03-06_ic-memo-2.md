## Risk Response (Pipeline Round)

The Risk Manager delivered a thorough assessment of the 5 conditional approvals and 3 rejections (2026-03-06_risk-report-2.md). The PM addresses each flag below.

### Flag 1: Gold Mining Cluster (B + ARMN + AEM) -- RESOLVED

**Risk finding:** If both B and ARMN are promoted alongside AEM, gold mining exposure would reach 13-14% of NAV, exceeding the PM's stated 10% cap. Risk recommends selecting one gold conditional and deprioritizing the other. B is lower-risk (beta 0.98); ARMN is higher-risk with discovery optionality (beta 1.87).

**PM decision: ARMN is the preferred gold conditional. B is deprioritized to lowest-tier conditional with a modified entry condition requiring AEM exit.**

Rationale: The purpose of a second gold miner is to add something AEM does not already provide. AEM is a large-cap, low-beta (0.61), operationally excellent gold miner with record production and best-in-class cash costs. Adding B (beta 0.98, large-cap, similar factor exposure) would be duplicative -- both are driven by the same gold price factor with high intra-pair correlation (estimated 0.80+). ARMN, by contrast, offers genuine differentiation:

- Discovery characteristics: 4 analysts, 37% institutional ownership, NYSE uplisting February 19
- Production growth inflection: 22% growth in 2025, 300K-350K oz guidance for 2026
- Soto Norte optionality: $2.7B NPV at 35% IRR, not priced into current valuation
- Mubadala sovereign wealth backing: credibility signal for a $4.0B company

The higher beta (1.87) is managed by the smaller allocation (3-4% vs AEM's 5%), and ARMN's idiosyncratic catalysts (earnings March 11, Soto Norte development) provide return drivers beyond gold price alone. Combined AEM (5%) + ARMN (3-4%) = 8-9% gold mining, within the 10% cap.

B's modified condition: B only deploys if AEM is exited from the active portfolio, effectively making B an AEM replacement candidate rather than a portfolio addition. This eliminates gold cluster risk entirely.

### Flag 2: LLY as Portfolio Construction Best-in-Class -- ACCEPTED, PRIORITY ELEVATED

**Risk finding:** LLY at 0.43 beta would actually reduce portfolio VaR upon deployment. Near-zero correlation with existing holdings. Post-FDA deployment is the correct risk gate. LLY should be prioritized in any deployment scenario where portfolio-level risk reduction is a goal.

**PM decision: LLY elevated to Priority 1 deployment candidate post-FDA-approval (April 10). If orforglipron is approved and a slot is available, LLY deploys ahead of all other conditional names regardless of prior priority ranking.**

Rationale: Risk's stress test analysis is decisive. LLY is the only conditional name that improves the portfolio's stress profile across all bearish scenarios (GFC replay: +0.6 pp, COVID: +0.4 pp, Rates shock: +0.3 pp) with trivial upside capture cost (-0.1 pp in recovery scenarios). No other conditional name in the pipeline can make this claim. The 0.43 beta and zero correlation with AI/energy/defense themes make LLY a genuine portfolio-level risk reducer that also generates positive expected returns. This is the rarest combination in concentrated portfolio management.

The condition remains: FDA approval must be confirmed before entry. Pre-FDA entry is inadvisable given the 15-20% rejection probability. But post-approval, LLY becomes the top capital deployment priority at 5% of NAV.

**PM decision: Accept Risk's recommendation. ZS deployment conditions now include a technical stabilization requirement.**

Requiring technical stabilization ensures we are not catching a falling knife. The 50-day SMA crossover or higher-low pattern provides objective evidence that sellers have exhausted and the re-acceleration narrative is gaining market acceptance. This condition does not change the thesis assessment -- it adds execution discipline.
