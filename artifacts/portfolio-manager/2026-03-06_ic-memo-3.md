### Risk Flag 4: IOT Beta and Pro-Cyclical Price Behavior -- ACCEPTED, CLASSIFICATION CORRECTED

**Risk finding:** IOT's beta of 1.60 means the stock behaves as a growth stock in selloffs, regardless of the revenue counter-cyclicality. The stock declined 27% from its 52-week high during a period when its fundamental value proposition was strengthening. IOT is not a portfolio hedge -- its demand is counter-cyclical but its stock price is pro-cyclical.

**PM decision: Accept Risk's distinction. Reclassify IOT's portfolio construction value.**

This is an important analytical correction. The draft memo cited IOT's "counter-cyclical demand profile" as a portfolio construction argument. Risk correctly identifies that counter-cyclical demand and counter-cyclical stock performance are different things. IOT's revenue is resilient during macro stress (fuel costs, supply disruption), but the market sells IOT alongside other growth names during risk-off events because the 52x P/E and 1.60 beta dominate the price action.

IOT's portfolio construction value is therefore: (a) AI application-layer diversification (0% current exposure to fleet/IoT), (b) low end-market correlation with data center, power, defense, and gold themes, and (c) profitability inflection with durable growth. It is NOT: (d) a counter-cyclical portfolio hedge. ELV and LLY serve that function; IOT does not.

The VaR impact analysis confirms this:
- IOT at 5% increases VaR 95% by 0.25 pp at VIX 27 (from 2.77% to ~3.02%)
- At VIX 20 (deployment condition), VaR increases by 0.15 pp (from 1.68% to ~1.83%, within guideline)
- IOT's stress test profile is unfavorable in tail scenarios (GFC: -1.1 pp, COVID: -0.8 pp)

The PM's entry conditions (price pullback to $33, VIX <22) are confirmed as well-calibrated by Risk. At $33 entry, R/R improves to 2.50:1, which justifies the VaR cost. No changes to IOT conditional terms.
