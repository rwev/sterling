## Risk Feedback Incorporation

The Risk Manager's report identified 1 active breach and 8 warnings. Below is how each was addressed in this final memo.

### 1. Power Generation / Utilities Sector at 43% -- BREACH (Existing Waiver)

**Risk Flag:** VST (23%) + CEG (20%) = 43%, exceeding the 25% sector limit by 18 percentage points.

**Action:** Resize CEG from 20.0% to 18.0%. This reduces power sector concentration from 43.0% to 41.0%. The 2.0% freed is allocated to cash, partially addressing the cash buffer concern (see item 3). The full power sector concentration remains above the 25% limit under the existing waiver. Rationale for maintaining a large power overweight: the AI-driven power demand thesis is the portfolio's highest-conviction structural theme, supported by 20-year PPAs, regulatory tailwinds, and data center buildout that is still in its first inning. The waiver continues with the understanding that if the March 7 CEG target revision is not delivered, CEG will be further reduced to 15.0%, bringing power to 38.0%.

### 2. VST Stop Proximity -- WARNING (Urgent)

**Risk Flag:** VST at $149.45 is 6.3% above the $140 hard stop after a 14% intraday decline. March 4 tariff event could drive further selling.

**Action:** Hold at 23.0%. Accept the risk with documented monitoring protocol. The decline is macro-driven (tariff fear), not thesis-specific. Fundamentals remain intact: $6.8-7.6B EBITDA guidance, 20-year AWS/Meta PPAs, Wells Fargo $234 target. Risk/reward has improved to approximately 8.6:1 (54% upside to target vs. 6.3% to stop). The $145 review trigger and $140 hard stop are confirmed. A stop-loss order at $140 must be in place. If VST closes below $145 at any point March 3-7, the PM will convene an emergency review.

### 3. Cash Buffer at 12% -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** Cash reduced from 21% to 12%, below the 15-20% preferred in high-volatility environments.

**Action:** Partially addressed by CEG trim. Cash increases from 12.0% to 14.0%. While still below the 15-20% preferred range, 14.0% provides adequate headroom for any single stop-loss event (largest: VST at approximately 1.5% NAV loss from current levels to stop). Additionally, the portfolio's contingency plan for drawdown exceeding -5% from HWM is established: trim EME by 2% and DELL by 2% to raise cash to 18%, prioritizing these names because they have the lowest risk/reward ratios among non-healthcare holdings. The March 7 CEG directive could add another 3% to cash if triggered.

### 4. Enterprise Software Concentration at 22% -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** NOW (12%) + CRM (10%) = 22% during an active SaaSpocalypse selloff.

**Action:** Accept. NOW and CRM have differentiated catalysts (Autonomous Workforce platform vs. Agentforce ARR scaling), distinct revenue models, and moderate pairwise correlation (0.55). Both have confirmed their theses through recent earnings/product launches. The 22% concentration is justified by the conviction level and the fact that the SaaSpocalypse has already compressed multiples to entry-grade valuations (NOW at 25x FCF, CRM at 12-13x EV/FCF). The risk of further multiple compression is acknowledged but priced into the stops ($88 for NOW, $165 for CRM).

### 5. AI Theme Concentration at ~73% -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** Approximately 73% of invested capital is AI-linked.

**Action:** Accept as structural and intentional. The portfolio's core thesis is that AI infrastructure buildout is the dominant secular trend of this decade. The progression from 100% AI-linked to 73% via healthcare additions demonstrates active diversification effort. The 9% healthcare allocation provides genuine defensive offset (near-zero correlation with AI cluster). Further dilution of the AI theme would compromise the portfolio's identity and conviction advantage. The "AI demand disappointment" stress test at -25.5% is acknowledged as the portfolio's tail scenario; the probability of a 25% hyperscaler capex cut is assessed as low given confirmed multi-year commitments from AWS, Meta, Microsoft, and Google.

### 6. CEG Approaching Target ($330 vs. $350) -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** CEG at $330 is within 6% of target, offering 0.2:1 risk/reward at original 20% allocation.

**Action:** Resize CEG from 20.0% to 18.0% (see item 1). The 2% trim partially de-risks the approaching-target position. The March 7 deadline for a revised target above $370 remains the primary decision point. If the Long Analyst delivers a credible revised target supported by Calpine synergies and the March 31 Business and Earnings Outlook, CEG may be resized upward. If no revision is delivered, CEG will be reduced to 15.0% at the March 7 IC.

### 7. ARGX PDUFA Binary Risk -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** May 10, 2026 PDUFA for Vyvgart in seronegative gMG. CRL could cause 15-25% position-level decline (0.6-1.0% NAV).

**Action:** Accept at 4.0% allocation. The maximum adverse NAV impact of 1.0% is well within tolerance. The probability-weighted expected value is positive given the Phase 3 data in hand. Per Risk's recommendation, a pre-PDUFA position review is established for no later than May 1, 2026 to assess: (a) whether to reduce ahead of the binary event, (b) updated probability assessment based on any FDA signals, and (c) portfolio risk profile at that time.

### 8. Stress Test Failures -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** All three historical scenarios (GFC -43.7%, COVID -32.7%, Rates Shock -26.7%) and three hypothetical scenarios breach the -10% drawdown limit.

**Action:** Accept as structural to the long-only concentrated mandate. A 9-position long-only portfolio at 86% invested will fail historical tail-risk stress tests by construction. The -10% drawdown limit is an operational risk limit governing actual portfolio management decisions (stop-losses, position reviews, emergency IC convocations), not a requirement that the portfolio survive a GFC-magnitude event without loss. Every concentrated long-only fund in history would fail these scenarios. The relevant risk management tools are: (a) hard stop-losses on every position, (b) the drawdown contingency plan (see item 3), and (c) the 14% cash buffer.

### 9. Effective Independent Bets at 3.4 -- WARNING

**Risk Flag:** 3.4 effective independent bets, below the theoretical maximum of 5.5-6.5 for a 9-position portfolio.

**Action:** Accept. Improved from 2.8 in the prior cycle. The gap between actual (3.4) and theoretical maximum (5.5-6.5) is driven by the intentional VST-CEG concentration (0.80 correlation) and NOW-CRM pair (0.55 correlation). These are deliberate conviction bets, not accidental correlation. Adding positions purely for diversification benefit (e.g., consumer staples, REITs) would dilute the portfolio's alpha-generating thesis without proportionate return improvement. The trajectory is positive -- further improvement will come from the CEG trim executed today and the potential further reduction if the March 7 target is not revised.
