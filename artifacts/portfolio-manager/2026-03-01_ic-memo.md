## Risk Response

The Risk Manager flagged 4 limit breaches and 7 warnings. Each is addressed below. The fund directive -- full AI upside exposure -- constrains the available remediation options. Where a breach cannot be remedied without contradicting the directive, it is accepted with documented rationale and compensating controls.

### Breach 1: Portfolio Beta at 1.35 (Limit 1.0)

**Decision: ACCEPT WITH WAIVER.**

The beta breach is a structural consequence of rotating out of all low-beta ballast (LMT 0.22, MRK 0.30, ARGX 0.21) and into high-beta AI infrastructure names. Restoring beta to 1.0 would require approximately 25% of NAV in cash, which directly contradicts the fund directive to maximize AI upside exposure.

Compensating controls:
- The MRVL reduction from 4% to 2% removes 0.04 from portfolio beta (1.97 beta x 2% weight), bringing portfolio beta from 1.354 to approximately 1.31.
- The phased entry on COHR (8% initial vs. 12% target) temporarily reduces beta contribution by 0.07 (1.68 x 4%), bringing effective near-term beta to approximately 1.24 during the accumulation phase.
- The drawdown contingency (trim VRT and MRVL if drawdown exceeds -5%) provides a pre-defined de-risking mechanism.
- VST stop-loss contingency (see below) would reduce beta by 0.33 if triggered, bringing residual portfolio beta to approximately 1.0.

The IC formally accepts portfolio beta above 1.0 for the duration of the AI supercycle rotation. This waiver will be reviewed at each subsequent IC meeting. Conditions for returning below 1.0: (a) the AI infrastructure thesis is invalidated, (b) hyperscaler capex guidance is materially reduced, or (c) the portfolio drawdown exceeds -7.5% from HWM.

### Breach 2: VaR 95% at 3.41% (Limit 3.0%)

**Decision: ACCEPT AS STRUCTURAL.**

The VaR breach is driven by two factors: (a) higher individual position volatilities (new positions average 50% annualized vol vs. 30% for closed positions) and (b) elevated cross-correlations (average pairwise rho approximately 0.45 vs. 0.15 in the prior portfolio). The covariance terms now exceed the variance terms in the VaR computation, meaning correlation risk dominates.

The MRVL reduction from 4% to 2% and COHR phased entry reduce near-term VaR modestly. However, the breach cannot be remedied without reducing AI exposure by 15-20% of NAV, which contradicts the fund directive.

The IC accepts that 1-day losses exceeding 3.4% of NAV are expected approximately once per month at current volatility levels. The drawdown contingency at -5% serves as the compensating control -- it triggers automatic position trimming before the -10% drawdown limit is reached.

### Breach 3: Semiconductor Sector at 29% (Limit 25%)

**Decision: PARTIALLY REMEDIATE -- reduce MRVL from 4% to 2%.**

MRVL is the optimal candidate for reduction:
- Highest beta in the portfolio at 1.97 -- reducing it improves both sector concentration and portfolio beta.
- Current price ($82) is above the analyst's $72-$80 entry range -- we are chasing at current levels.
- At 2%, MRVL remains a meaningful tracking position that can be scaled to 4-5% if Celestial AI integration milestones are achieved and the price retraces toward the entry range.

Post-adjustment semiconductor sector: AVGO 15% + NVDA 10% + MRVL 2% = 27%. This is 2% above the 25% limit. The IC accepts the residual 2% breach for the following reasons:
- AVGO (custom ASICs) and NVDA (GPUs) serve fundamentally different market segments with different customer concentration -- AVGO's largest customers are Google, Meta, and ByteDance for custom silicon, while NVDA sells standardized GPUs across all hyperscalers.
- Reducing NVDA below 10% would underweight the single most important company in the AI ecosystem, contradicting the fund directive.
- The 2% residual breach is within a reasonable tolerance band and will be resolved automatically if MRVL is trimmed further under the drawdown contingency.

### Breach 4: Effective Independent Bets at 2.5

**Decision: ACCEPT AS INHERENT TO DIRECTIVE.**

A concentrated AI infrastructure portfolio with 83% thematic exposure cannot achieve high diversification. The 2.5 effective independent bets reflect the deliberate construction of a conviction portfolio around a single macro thesis (AI supercycle). The IC notes this represents the lowest effective diversification in portfolio history and accepts the associated tail risk.

The compensating control is the drawdown contingency and the portfolio's 100% same-day liquidity profile. If the thesis is wrong, the portfolio can be fully liquidated within one trading day.
