## Risk Manager Response

The Risk Manager's Cycle II report flagged five items. Each is addressed below with the PM's final disposition.

### Flag 1: AVGO Binary Earnings Event March 4 (Advisory)

**Risk Manager assessment**: At 5% NAV, worst-case earnings miss driving 15% decline costs 0.75% of NAV. Stop-out at $270 costs 1.56% of NAV. Phased entry structure is the correct approach.

**PM response: ACCEPTED.** No adjustment required. The 5%/8% phased structure was designed for exactly this scenario. If earnings disappoint, the 5% loss is contained and Phase 2 does not deploy. If earnings confirm, the additional 3% deploys into a de-risked setup. This is unchanged from Cycle I and remains the appropriate risk management framework.

### Flag 2: BABA Geopolitical Tail Risk (Advisory)

**Risk Manager assessment**: Pentagon entity list risk, PCAOB audit access, Chinese regulatory actions, and US-China decoupling can each gap the stock 10-15%. At 7% with $110 stop, even a 30% gap through stop costs approximately 2.1% of NAV -- painful but not portfolio-threatening.

**PM response: ACCEPTED with monitoring protocol.** The 7% sizing and $110 stop provide adequate structural protection. Per the Risk Manager's recommendation, the PM hereby establishes a formal BABA monitoring protocol:

- **Pentagon entity list**: Any re-inclusion triggers immediate review; if confirmed (not withdrawn within 24 hours), reduce to 3.5% or exit
- **PCAOB audit access**: Any formal threat of revocation triggers immediate exit
- **Chinese regulatory escalation**: Material new regulatory action against BABA core businesses triggers review and potential stop tightening to $120
- **US-China diplomatic breakdown**: Significant escalation (new broad tariffs, tech export controls expansion) triggers review of position sizing

These are pre-committed responses, not ad hoc decisions. The monitoring protocol is effective immediately.

### Flag 3: IPGP Short Interest Discrepancy (Advisory)

**Risk Manager assessment**: Analyst thesis cites 27% short interest vs. Nasdaq data showing 5.18%. A 5x discrepancy that materially affects the squeeze catalyst.

**PM response: RESOLVED.** The discrepancy is definitional, not factual. The 27.11% figure (sourced from Finviz, confirmed via Benzinga reporting) represents short interest as a percentage of free float. The 5.18% Nasdaq figure represents short interest as a percentage of total shares outstanding. Both are correct; they measure different denominators. For trading purposes, the float-based figure (27%) is the operationally relevant metric because it reflects the actual borrowing pressure relative to tradeable supply. The short squeeze catalyst remains intact at 27% of float short. No adjustment to IPGP sizing or thesis required.

### Flag 4: AEM Below-Threshold R/R on Base Target (Informational)

**Risk Manager assessment**: R/R on stated parameters ($290 target / $195 stop) is 0.68:1 -- weakest in portfolio. Recommends formally adopting $320 bull target and documenting conditions.

**PM response: ACCEPTED.** The PM formally adopts a dual-target framework for AEM:

- **Base target**: $290 (original thesis, conservative)
- **Bull target**: $320 (primary target, operative for position management)
- **Conditions for bull target activation**: Gold sustains above $5,500/oz for 2+ consecutive weeks, OR AEM reports FY2026 Q1 production/earnings above consensus
- **R/R at bull target**: ($320 - $251.60) / ($251.60 - $195) = 1.21:1

At current gold ($5,408), AEM is earning approximately $4,069/oz margin on $1,339 AISC -- the bull target of $320 is well-supported by current commodity fundamentals. The base target of $290 assumed gold at $5,200, which gold has already surpassed. The PM considers the bull target the more realistic scenario and will manage the position accordingly.

The scaling approach (5% immediate, 3% on pullback to $240 or gold strength confirmation) remains unchanged and appropriate.

### Flag 5: Conditional Book Photonics Concentration (Informational)

**Risk Manager assessment**: Three of six conditional theses (CIEN, LITE, COHR) are photonics names. Recommends formalizing a maximum-one-promotion rule per IC cycle.

**PM response: ACCEPTED.** The PM hereby establishes the following rule:

**Photonics Conditional Promotion Rule**: A maximum of one photonics name (CIEN, LITE, or COHR) may be promoted from conditional to active status per IC cycle. If multiple photonics conditionals reach their entry triggers simultaneously, the PM will select the one with the best R/R at that time. This prevents inadvertent concentration in a single sub-theme.

This is consistent with the PM's draft memo statement that "one photonics position is sufficient for thematic exposure" and the conditional book should be viewed as an option menu, not a deployment queue.
