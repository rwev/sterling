### 8. FTAI Infrastructure (FIP) — Small-Cap Analyst

**Recommendation: REJECTED**

**Current Price**: ~$5.49-$5.95
**Analyst Entry Range**: $5.50-$6.50
**Target**: $11.00 | **Stop**: $4.25

**Thesis Summary**: $721M market cap infrastructure holding company trading at severe SOTP discount. $232M adjusted EBITDA in FY2025 (+82% y/y). Long Ridge power plant monetization (H1 2026) as near-term catalyst. CEO bought $2.6M at $5.22.

**Assessment**: The sum-of-parts story is intellectually interesting, but this does not meet our standards for portfolio inclusion.

**Rejection rationale**:
1. **Leverage is disqualifying at this stage.** $3.7B total debt against $721M equity market cap. Even accepting $2.5B as non-recourse, parent-level debt of $1.2B at 9.75% interest against $321M run-rate EBITDA is 3.7x — in a rising-rate environment with no clear path to free cash flow.
2. **Negative free cash flow.** The company is not generating FCF. In a portfolio construction context, every other approved position generates positive and growing FCF. FIP would be the lone exception, dragging portfolio quality.
3. **Complexity without clarity.** Four distinct infrastructure verticals (rail, power, terminal, port) make this impossible to monitor efficiently. Thesis validation depends on multiple independent catalysts (Long Ridge sale, Repauno Phase 2, rail cost synergies, debt refinancing) — any one of which could fail.
4. **Liquidity.** Daily dollar volume of $6-9M creates unacceptable exit risk for a fund our size. The analyst's own max allocation recommendation is 0.75%.
5. **Opportunity cost.** Every dollar in FIP is a dollar not in VRT, AVGO, or LHX, which offer superior risk-adjusted returns with dramatically better liquidity and earnings quality.

The CEO buying at $5.22 is a positive signal, but insider buying does not offset leverage risk and negative FCF in a concentrated portfolio.
