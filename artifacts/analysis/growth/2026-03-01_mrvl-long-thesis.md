# Growth Long Thesis: Marvell Technology (MRVL)

## Investment Summary

Marvell is transforming from a diversified semiconductor company into the optical interconnect platform company for AI infrastructure. The $3.25-5.5B acquisition of Celestial AI (completed February 2026) is the strategic catalyst: Celestial's Photonic Fabric chiplet technology delivers 16 Tbps bandwidth -- 10x the state-of-the-art 1.6T standard. With Q3 FY2026 revenue at a record $2.08B (up 37% YoY), data center revenue at $1.5B (up 38% YoY, 73% of total revenue), and Q4 guidance of $2.2B (+40%+ FY growth), Marvell is already inflecting on its core data center business. The Celestial AI acquisition adds a second growth vector that could contribute $500M annualized by FY2028 and $1B by FY2029. At $76.55 with a consensus target of $115-131, the stock offers 50-70% upside as the market begins to price in the combined platform thesis.

## Growth Inflection Case

Marvell's inflection is a two-stage story:

1. **Near-term inflection (now)**: The core data center business is accelerating. Revenue grew from $1.37B in Q4 FY2025 to $1.5B in Q3 FY2026, with data center now representing 73-76% of total revenue versus approximately 50% two years ago. The company has raised its FY2027 data center revenue growth forecast to over 25%, citing stronger cloud capex trends. Custom ASIC and electro-optics revenue is the primary driver.

2. **Medium-term inflection (FY2028-FY2029)**: Celestial AI's Photonic Fabric technology is next-generation optical interconnect. At 16 Tbps per chiplet -- 10x the bandwidth of current 1.6T transceivers -- this is not an incremental improvement but an architectural leap. Revenue is expected to ramp to $500M annualized by Q4 FY2028 and $1B by Q4 FY2029. If Photonic Fabric becomes standard interconnect plumbing for AI clusters, Marvell transitions from a chip company to a platform company with a structural moat in optical data movement.

3. **Portfolio rationalization**: The non-data-center segments (Enterprise Networking, Carrier, Consumer, Auto/Industrial) represent declining-to-stable revenue that is becoming a smaller share of the total. As data center grows to 80%+ of revenue, the blended growth rate accelerates even as these segments shrink, creating a natural mix-shift inflection.

## Business Overview & TAM

Marvell designs semiconductors for data infrastructure, with five end markets:

- **Data Center** (73% of Q3 FY26 revenue): Custom ASICs for hyperscalers (Google TPU interconnects, Amazon Trainium/Inferentia networking), electro-optics (PAM4 DSPs for optical transceivers), DPUs/SmartNICs, and now Celestial AI's photonic fabric
- **Enterprise Networking** (~8%): Ethernet switches and PHYs
- **Carrier Infrastructure** (~5%): 5G and wireline equipment
- **Consumer** (~4%): Storage controllers, gaming
- **Automotive/Industrial** (~4%): ADAS and industrial Ethernet

**TAM trajectory**: Marvell's data center TAM is expanding on multiple vectors:
- Custom ASIC market growing as hyperscalers diversify beyond NVIDIA GPUs
- Electro-optics/PAM4 DSP market scaling with the 800G-to-1.6T transition
- Photonic Fabric opens an entirely new TAM in optical computing interconnects -- a market projected to grow from $2.86B (2025) to $28.75B (2034)
- DPU/SmartNIC market expanding as cloud-native architectures require more network offload

## Business Drivers

1. **Custom ASIC momentum**: Marvell designs custom AI accelerator interconnects for hyperscalers including Google (TPU networking) and Amazon (Trainium/Inferentia). As hyperscalers diversify silicon suppliers away from sole NVIDIA dependence, custom ASIC demand is structurally growing. Broadcom has a $73B ASIC backlog -- Marvell is capturing its share of this expanding market.

2. **Electro-optics / PAM4 DSP leadership**: Marvell's PAM4 DSP chips are critical components in 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers. Every transceiver shipped by Coherent, Lumentum, or Fabrinet likely contains a Marvell or competitor DSP. The 2.6x growth in 800G+ transceiver shipments (24M to 63M units) in 2026 directly drives Marvell's electro-optics revenue.

3. **Celestial AI Photonic Fabric**: This is the strategic differentiator. At 16 Tbps per chiplet, Photonic Fabric is architecturally ahead of the 1.6T standard by a full generation. If hyperscalers adopt it for disaggregated compute-memory architectures, Marvell becomes the platform provider for optical data movement inside AI clusters. The $500M FY2028 and $1B FY2029 revenue targets provide concrete milestones to monitor.

4. **NVIDIA ecosystem**: Marvell supplies interconnect silicon for NVIDIA's networking platforms. As NVIDIA launches Rubin and Spectrum-X (1.6T ports), Marvell's content per NVIDIA system increases.

5. **Data center revenue concentration**: As data center grows from 73% to 80%+ of revenue, Marvell's growth rate profile improves mechanically. The non-data-center businesses are stable-to-declining, so the blended growth rate understates the core business momentum.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Marvell's margin trajectory is positive but less dramatic than LITE or VRT:

- **Non-GAAP gross margin**: Stable at approximately 60%, reflecting the fabless model
- **Non-GAAP operating margin**: Expanding from ~30% in Q4 FY25 to 36.3% in Q3 FY26 -- a 630 bps improvement in three quarters
- **Celestial AI integration**: The Celestial AI business will initially be dilutive to margins as it ramps from pre-revenue to $500M. However, photonic chiplets should eventually carry gross margins above 60% given their high value-add and limited competition.

The path to sustained 38-40% non-GAAP operating margins by FY2028 is credible, driven by revenue scale, mix shift toward higher-margin data center products, and operating leverage on R&D (which is front-loaded for Celestial AI integration).

## Competitive Position & Moat

- **Custom ASIC design wins**: Multi-year engagements with hyperscalers create significant switching costs. Once a custom ASIC is designed into a platform, the customer is locked in for the product's life cycle (3-5 years typically).
- **Electro-optics breadth**: Marvell is one of only two major PAM4 DSP suppliers (alongside Broadcom). This duopoly position provides pricing stability and design-win visibility.
- **Celestial AI technology moat**: Photonic Fabric is protected by significant IP and manufacturing complexity. No other semiconductor company has an equivalent product in production. The acquisition preempts competitors from accessing this technology.
- **Competitors**: Broadcom (AVGO) is the primary competitor in custom ASICs and PAM4 DSPs. Broadcom is larger and has deeper customer relationships, but the market is large enough for both to grow. In photonic fabric, there is no direct competitor at scale.

## Risk Factors

1. **Celestial AI execution risk**: The $3.25-5.5B acquisition is a significant bet on next-generation technology. If Photonic Fabric adoption is slower than expected, the capital deployed may not generate adequate returns. Revenue milestones ($500M by FY2028, $1B by FY2029) must be tracked closely.
2. **Integration risk**: Merging Celestial AI's team and technology into Marvell's product roadmap is complex. Key personnel departures or technology integration delays could impair the thesis.
3. **Broadcom competition**: Broadcom is the dominant player in custom ASICs with a $73B backlog. If Broadcom captures a disproportionate share of hyperscaler design wins, Marvell's custom ASIC growth could disappoint.
4. **Non-data-center drag**: Enterprise Networking, Carrier, and Consumer segments are flat-to-declining. If the decline accelerates, it offsets data center growth at the consolidated level.
5. **Tariff exposure**: Marvell is fabless (TSMC manufactures its chips), exposing the company to tariffs on semiconductor imports from Taiwan and packaging from Southeast Asia.
6. **Customer concentration**: Heavy dependence on a few hyperscaler customers for data center revenue. Loss of a design win at Google or Amazon would be material.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause an exit regardless of valuation:

- **Data center revenue growth falling below 20% YoY** for two consecutive quarters
- **Data center revenue declining as a percentage of total revenue** (indicating share loss, not mix shift)
- **Celestial AI failing to achieve $250M annualized revenue by mid-FY2028** (halfway to the $500M target)
- **Non-GAAP operating margin contracting below 33%** for two consecutive quarters
- **Loss of a top-3 custom ASIC design win** (Google, Amazon, or another major hyperscaler)
- **FY2027 data center revenue growth guidance reduced below 20%**
- **Any hyperscaler announcing a material reduction in 2026/2027 capex guidance**
