# Growth Long Thesis: Vertiv Holdings (VRT)

## Investment Summary

Vertiv is experiencing the most dramatic order acceleration in the data center infrastructure sector, with Q4 2025 organic orders surging 252% year-over-year and backlog doubling to $15 billion. The company guided 2026 revenue of $13.25-$13.75 billion, beating consensus by over $1 billion and representing 27-29% organic growth. This is a textbook growth inflection driven by the structural shift to liquid cooling in AI data centers, where Vertiv holds a dominant market position. At a forward P/E of ~40x on $6.20 adjusted EPS guidance, the stock is expensive in absolute terms but reasonably valued given the acceleration trajectory, 2.9x book-to-bill, and multi-year visibility from the $15 billion backlog.

## Growth Inflection Case

Vertiv is at a clear inflection point driven by three converging forces:

1. **Order acceleration is non-linear.** Organic orders grew 37% in Q1 2025, 46% in Q2, 60% in Q3, and 252% in Q4. This is not steady-state growth -- it is exponential demand pull from hyperscaler AI infrastructure buildout.

2. **Liquid cooling is becoming the industry standard.** AI GPU clusters (NVIDIA Blackwell, AMD MI300X) generate heat densities that air cooling cannot handle. Vertiv's liquid cooling solutions -- including direct-to-chip, rear-door heat exchangers, and the new CoolCenter Immersion system -- position the company at the center of this technology transition.

3. **Revenue is lagging orders by 12-18 months.** The 2.9x book-to-bill in Q4 2025 means revenue acceleration through 2027 is essentially locked in. The backlog provides rare forward visibility in a cyclical industrial business.

## Business Overview & TAM

Vertiv designs, manufactures, and services critical digital infrastructure for data centers, communication networks, and commercial/industrial environments. Core product lines include:

- **Thermal management and cooling** (liquid cooling, precision air cooling)
- **Power management** (UPS systems, switchgear, power distribution)
- **IT management and monitoring software**
- **Services** (maintenance, monitoring, professional services)

The company operates globally with particular strength in the Americas, which drove the majority of Q4 2025 order growth. Key customers include all major hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta) as well as colocation providers and enterprise data center operators.

**TAM expansion:** The data center cooling market is projected to grow at an 11.8% CAGR through 2032 (MarketsandMarkets). However, this understates the opportunity: the liquid cooling segment specifically is growing at 25-30%+ annually as AI workloads force the transition from air to liquid. Hyperscaler capex of ~$690 billion in 2026 (+60% YoY) provides the funding engine.

## Business Drivers

**1. Hyperscaler AI data center buildout.** Combined hyperscaler capex of ~$690 billion in 2026 (+60% YoY) flows directly to Vertiv through power and cooling infrastructure orders. Amazon ($200B), Alphabet ($175-185B), Meta ($115-135B), and Microsoft ($120B+) are all engaged in an arms race where under-building is an existential competitive risk.

**2. Liquid cooling technology transition.** NVIDIA's Blackwell GPU architecture and subsequent generations require liquid cooling. This is not optional -- air cooling physically cannot remove sufficient heat from 1,000W+ GPU clusters at scale. Vertiv's product portfolio spans the full liquid cooling stack: direct-to-chip, rear-door heat exchangers, CoolCenter Immersion (launched November 2025), and hybrid solutions. The company identified "adaptive liquid cooling" as a defining trend for 2026.

**3. Geographic breadth and customer diversification.** While Americas hyperscale drove Q4 order growth, Vertiv serves all major regions and customer types. APAC grew 20% in Q3 2025. Colocation providers and enterprise customers provide diversification beyond the hyperscale segment.

**4. Pricing power from supply constraints.** High-voltage transformer and switchgear lead times remain at 3-4 years. Vertiv benefits from the same supply-demand imbalance across its power management product lines, enabling price increases that flow to margin expansion.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Vertiv is demonstrating clear operating leverage:

- Adjusted operating margin expanded from 18.5% in Q2 2025 to 23.2% in Q4 2025 -- a 470 bps improvement in two quarters
- FY2026 guided adjusted operating margin of 22.5% represents ~210 bps expansion vs. FY2025
- Adjusted free cash flow guided at $2.2 billion for FY2026

The margin trajectory is driven by:
- Volume leverage on fixed manufacturing costs as revenue scales from $10.2B to $13.5B
- Manufacturing and procurement productivity improvements
- Favorable price-cost dynamics (pricing power exceeding tariff-driven input cost increases)
- Partially offset by tariff impacts on steel, aluminum, and imported components

Management guided adjusted operating profit of $3.04 billion for FY2026, implying continued margin expansion through the year as the revenue ramp accelerates.

## Competitive Position & Moat

**Market position:** Vertiv and Schneider Electric are in a near-dead heat for global data center physical infrastructure leadership. In liquid cooling specifically, Vertiv is rated as a "Star" with leading market share and the broadest product portfolio.

**Key competitors:**
- **Schneider Electric** -- Closest rival globally; strong in power distribution but less focused on liquid cooling
- **Modine Manufacturing** -- Data center revenue $644M in FY2025 (+119% YoY), but significantly smaller scale
- **Eaton** -- Acquired Boyd for liquid cooling capabilities at a $9.5B premium valuation
- **nVent** -- Growing NVIDIA partnership, ~30% DC exposure, but much smaller

**Moat characteristics:**
- Mission-critical infrastructure with high switching costs (downtime is catastrophic for hyperscalers)
- Engineering relationships with hyperscalers on next-generation cooling architectures
- Global manufacturing and service footprint
- Installed base drives recurring service revenue
- First-mover advantage in liquid cooling at scale

## Risk Factors

1. **Hyperscaler capex deceleration.** Any signal that the $690B 2026 spend is being pulled back would directly impact Vertiv's order trajectory. Amazon and Meta face negative FCF from their capex plans; any financial stress could trigger deferrals.

2. **Tariff-driven margin compression.** ISM Prices Paid at 70.5 (highest since June 2022) signals rising input costs. Vertiv's Q2 2025 margin dip of 110 bps was attributed to tariff impacts. If tariffs escalate further, pricing power may not fully offset cost increases.

3. **Backlog cancellation risk.** A $15B backlog is only valuable if customers take delivery. In a scenario where AI demand disappoints or hyperscalers reassess spending, cancellations could compress both revenue and orders.

4. **Execution risk at scale.** Scaling production 28% in a single year while maintaining quality and margins is operationally challenging. Supply chain bottlenecks (transformers, specialized components) could constrain delivery.

5. **Valuation compression.** At ~40x forward P/E, any miss on guidance or slowdown in order growth would trigger a sharp derating given the premium multiple.

6. **Iran conflict / energy cost impact.** Prolonged Strait of Hormuz disruption could raise energy and transportation costs, pressuring margins and potentially slowing data center construction timelines.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause reassessment or exit -- these are growth-rate-based, not valuation-based:

1. **Organic order growth decelerates below +50% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** The 252% Q4 print set an extraordinary bar, but sustained order growth above 50% is necessary to justify the backlog thesis.

2. **Book-to-bill falls below 1.5x.** A declining book-to-bill signals demand normalization and weakens the multi-year revenue visibility argument.

3. **Any hyperscaler publicly defers or reduces capex guidance.** This is the single most important leading indicator for Vertiv's demand trajectory.

4. **Adjusted operating margin contracts below 20% for two consecutive quarters.** Margin expansion is a key part of the thesis; sustained contraction would indicate pricing power erosion.

5. **Backlog growth decelerates to single digits YoY.** The backlog is the foundation of the forward revenue visibility argument.
