# Growth Long Thesis: Broadcom Inc. (AVGO)

## Investment Summary

Broadcom is the dominant custom AI silicon designer, commanding ~70% of the custom ASIC market with hyperscaler partnerships spanning Google (TPU v7), Meta (MTIA), OpenAI (Titan), and Anthropic. The company reports Q1 FY2026 earnings on March 4, 2026, with management guiding $19.1 billion in revenue (+28% YoY) and AI semiconductor revenue of $8.2 billion (approximately doubling YoY). Total AI backlog stands at $73 billion with an 18-month recognition window. At a forward P/E of ~31x on FY2026 consensus EPS of ~$5.00 and $319.55 current price, the stock has de-rated 27% from its 12-month average P/E of 96x while the fundamental growth trajectory has accelerated. This creates a favorable risk-reward setup for a high-conviction position, with the caveat that March 4 earnings represent a binary event.

## Growth Inflection Case

Broadcom is at the center of a structural shift in how AI compute is designed and deployed. The inflection has three dimensions:

1. **Custom silicon is displacing merchant GPUs at the margin.** Hyperscalers are increasingly designing their own chips rather than relying solely on NVIDIA. Google TPU v7, Meta MTIA, and now OpenAI Titan represent a fundamental architectural choice -- and Broadcom is the design partner of record for the majority of these programs. This is a new, additive revenue stream that did not exist at scale two years ago.

2. **AI revenue is doubling.** From $4.1B in Q1 FY2025 to a guided $8.2B in Q1 FY2026, AI semiconductor revenue is on a near-vertical trajectory. For full FY2026, AI-related revenue is projected to surge 134%. This is not extrapolation from early innings -- it is confirmed by a $73B AI-specific backlog.

3. **The VMware software business provides a high-margin anchor.** Broadcom's infrastructure software segment (primarily VMware) generates ~$6.5B+ per quarter at very high margins, providing earnings stability and cash flow to fund R&D investment in custom silicon. The VMware integration is well past the disruptive phase and is now a recurring-revenue engine.

## Business Overview & TAM

Broadcom operates two segments:

**Semiconductor Solutions (~65% of revenue):**
- Custom AI accelerators (XPUs) for hyperscalers
- AI networking (Ethernet switches, routers for AI clusters)
- Broadband, server storage connectivity, industrial

**Infrastructure Software (~35% of revenue):**
- VMware (virtualization, cloud management)
- Mainframe software (CA Technologies)
- Cybersecurity (Symantec Enterprise)

**TAM expansion:** The AI chip market is estimated at ~$500B in 2026. Broadcom's addressable portion is growing as hyperscalers shift from merchant GPUs to custom silicon. The company's 50+ XPU design pipeline represents $75 billion in potential revenue over time. Additionally, the AI networking market (800G/1.6T Ethernet switches) is growing rapidly as AI cluster scale increases from thousands to hundreds of thousands of GPUs.

Broadcom announced the first 2nm custom compute System-on-a-Chip in February 2026, utilizing 3.5D packaging technology -- extending its technology lead in custom silicon.

## Business Drivers

**1. Custom AI accelerator (XPU) pipeline.** Broadcom designs custom chips for Google (TPU), Meta (MTIA), and now reportedly OpenAI (Titan) and Anthropic. The OpenAI Titan deal alone is estimated at $150-200B over time. This is not a one-product revenue stream -- it is a platform relationship where Broadcom designs successive generations of silicon for each customer. The 50+ XPU design pipeline worth $75B provides unprecedented forward visibility.

**2. AI networking dominance.** As AI clusters scale from thousands to hundreds of thousands of accelerators, the networking fabric becomes critical. Broadcom's Tomahawk and Jericho switch families are the industry standard for 800G and emerging 1.6T Ethernet connectivity in AI data centers. This is a second, distinct growth vector within AI infrastructure.

**3. VMware subscription transition.** The VMware business is transitioning from perpetual licenses to subscriptions, driving higher recurring revenue and improving visibility. This segment generates ~$6.5B+ quarterly at high margins, providing the cash flow engine that funds Broadcom's R&D investment in custom silicon.

**4. Technology leadership in advanced packaging.** The February 2026 announcement of the first 2nm custom SoC with 3.5D packaging technology positions Broadcom at the leading edge of semiconductor design. This technology moat is widening, not narrowing.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Broadcom's margin structure is among the highest in the semiconductor industry:

- FY2025 adjusted EBITDA margin: ~66%
- FY2025 gross margin: 67.8% (GAAP) / ~78% in Q4 FY2025 (reflecting software mix)
- FY2025 operating margin: 39.9% (GAAP), up from 26.1% prior year

The margin trajectory reflects:
- VMware cost synergies flowing through (integration largely complete)
- Scaling AI semiconductor revenue on existing R&D investment
- Software revenue growing at high incremental margins
- Offset partially by higher AI revenue mix, which carries slightly lower gross margins (guided ~100 bps sequential decline in Q1 FY2026)

The company generates substantial free cash flow, enabling aggressive capital return ($5.8B quarterly dividend) and continued R&D investment.

## Competitive Position & Moat

**Market position:** Broadcom holds approximately 70% of the custom AI ASIC market. The only meaningful competitor in this space is Marvell Technology, which holds a smaller share with its own hyperscaler design wins.

**Moat characteristics:**
- **Switching costs:** Once a hyperscaler commits to a custom chip architecture with Broadcom as the design partner, switching is extremely costly and time-consuming. Chip design cycles are 2-3 years; migrating to a different design partner means starting over.
- **Intellectual property:** Broadcom's SerDes technology, advanced packaging expertise, and networking protocol implementations represent decades of accumulated IP.
- **Ecosystem lock-in:** Broadcom's networking products (switches, routers) are designed to work optimally with its custom accelerators, creating a full-stack advantage.
- **Scale:** $64B in FY2025 revenue funds an R&D budget that smaller competitors cannot match.

**NVIDIA is not a direct competitor in custom silicon.** NVIDIA sells merchant GPUs; Broadcom designs custom chips to each hyperscaler's specifications. These are complementary, not substitutive, business models -- though at the margin, custom silicon growth does reduce NVIDIA's share of total AI accelerator spend.

## Risk Factors

1. **March 4 earnings binary risk.** The Q1 FY2026 report on March 4 is the most important near-term catalyst. A miss on AI revenue guidance, margin disappointment, or soft forward guidance would trigger significant downside given the setup. This is the primary near-term risk.

2. **Gross margin dilution from AI mix.** Management guided Q1 FY2026 gross margin down ~100 bps sequentially as AI semiconductor revenue (which carries lower margins) grows faster than software. If this trend accelerates, it could pressure overall profitability.

3. **Customer concentration risk.** Google, Meta, and OpenAI likely represent a very large share of AI semiconductor revenue. Loss of any single hyperscaler relationship would be material.

4. **Hyperscaler capex deceleration.** The $690B hyperscaler capex plan for 2026 is the engine funding Broadcom's growth. Any pullback -- driven by AI monetization doubts, financial stress (Amazon/Meta negative FCF), or macro deterioration -- directly impacts Broadcom's pipeline.

5. **TSMC supply chain dependency.** Broadcom designs chips but relies on TSMC for fabrication. Any TSMC capacity constraints, pricing increases, or geopolitical disruption (Taiwan/China) would impact Broadcom's ability to fulfill its backlog.

6. **Valuation risk on deceleration.** While the current PEG is attractive, any deceleration in AI revenue growth from the ~100% YoY pace would likely trigger multiple compression, as the market has priced in sustained hyper-growth.

7. **Iran conflict / macro disruption.** The US-Iran conflict introduces broad market volatility. While Broadcom's business is not directly exposed, a sustained oil shock that reintroduces inflation and constrains Fed easing could pressure tech multiples broadly.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause reassessment or exit:

1. **AI semiconductor revenue growth decelerates below +50% YoY.** The thesis is built on AI revenue doubling; a meaningful slowdown would break the rate-of-change narrative.

2. **Loss of a major hyperscaler design win.** If Google, Meta, or OpenAI shifts custom silicon design work to Marvell or in-house, it would indicate competitive position erosion.

3. **AI backlog declines sequentially.** The $73B AI backlog is the core of the forward visibility argument; any decline signals demand normalization.

4. **Total revenue growth decelerates below +20% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** This would indicate the AI acceleration is insufficient to offset any softness in legacy semiconductor or software segments.

5. **Adjusted EBITDA margin falls below 62%.** The high-margin business model is a key part of the thesis; sustained margin contraction beyond the guided ~100 bps would signal structural issues.

6. **Management signals slowing custom ASIC pipeline.** Any indication that the 50+ XPU design pipeline is not converting to orders at the expected rate would undermine the long-term growth thesis.
