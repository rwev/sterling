- **Full-year 2025 product net sales**: $4.15B, up 90% YoY from $2.19B in 2024
- **Q4 2025 product net sales**: $1.30B, up 74% YoY
- **2026 consensus revenue**: $4.96-5.93B, implying 20-43% growth depending on estimate source

- **Rest of world**: $110M in Q4, up 108% YoY

The critical rate-of-change signal: international markets are growing faster than the U.S., meaning geographic expansion is adding a second growth vector on top of the core U.S. franchise. This is the hallmark of a platform company entering its scaling phase -- the U.S. franchise generates cash and credibility, and international launches layer on incremental high-margin revenue.

## The Business Drivers

### Vyvgart: Multi-Indication Platform Expanding Addressable Market

Vyvgart (efgartigimod) is an FcRn antagonist that reduces circulating IgG antibodies -- a mechanism applicable across dozens of IgG-mediated autoimmune diseases. This is not a one-drug company; it is a platform with a validated mechanism expanding across indications:

**Currently Approved:**
- Generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG) -- the anchor franchise
- Chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy (CIDP)

**Near-Term Label Expansions:**
- **Seronegative gMG**: PDUFA target action date May 10, 2026. Positive ADAPT-SERON Phase 3 data. Would expand the MG addressable market 15-20%.
- **Ocular myasthenia gravis**: Positive ADAPT-OCULUS Phase 3 data (p=0.012). First registrational study in this patient population.

**Pipeline Depth -- Four Phase 3 Molecules by End of 2026:**
1. **Efgartigimod (Vyvgart)**: Expanding into autoimmune inflammatory myopathies (AIM/myositis) -- ALKIVIA topline data expected Q3 2026. Graves' disease registrational study initiating in 2026.
2. **Empasiprubart (ARGX-117)**: Anti-C2 complement inhibitor. Phase 3 for multifocal motor neuropathy (MMN) -- topline data Q4 2026. Also Phase 3 for CIDP.
3. **Adimanebart (ARGX-119)**: MuSK agonist entering Phase 3 for congenital myasthenic syndromes (CMS).
4. **ARGX-121**: Anti-IgA antibody entering patient studies in 2026.
5. **ARGX-109**: Anti-IL-6 entering patient studies in 2026.

The pipeline creates a rolling catalyst calendar that sustains investor interest and provides multiple shots on goal for revenue diversification beyond the core Vyvgart franchise.

### Vyvgart Hytrulo (Subcutaneous Formulation) Driving Penetration

The subcutaneous formulation of Vyvgart now represents 60%+ of new gMG treatment starts. This is a lifecycle management success that makes the therapy more convenient and expands the treatable population (patients who prefer self-administration over infusion). The SC formulation also supports international market penetration where infusion infrastructure may be less developed.

### 19,000 Patients Globally -- But Penetration Remains Early

Argenx has treated 19,000 patients globally with Vyvgart. The total addressable patient population across approved and near-term indications numbers in the hundreds of thousands. Penetration is still in early stages, meaning the growth runway is long even before accounting for new indications.

## Key Risks

- **Asset concentration**: Vyvgart/efgartigimod generates the vast majority of revenue. Any safety signal or competitive displacement in the FcRn class would be material.
- **Competition**: Johnson & Johnson's nipocalimab and Immunovant's IMVT-1402 are advancing in overlapping indications. If clinical data proves superior, Vyvgart market share could erode.
- **Pharma tariff threat**: The administration has signaled up to 200% tariffs on pharmaceuticals. While ARGX has significant U.S.-based commercial operations, manufacturing exposure could create risk if tariffs are applied broadly.
- **PDUFA risk**: A Complete Response Letter on the May 10 seronegative gMG decision would remove the near-term catalyst and pressure the stock.
- **Valuation sensitivity**: At 30x forward earnings, the stock requires sustained growth to justify its multiple. Any deceleration in Vyvgart uptake or pipeline setback would compress the multiple.
- **International execution**: Japan and rest-of-world are growing rapidly but from small bases. Execution risk in new geographies could slow the international growth vector.
