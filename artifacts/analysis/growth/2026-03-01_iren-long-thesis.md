# Growth Long Thesis: IREN Limited (IREN)

## Investment Summary

IREN is executing one of the most dramatic business model pivots in recent market history -- from bitcoin miner to AI cloud infrastructure provider -- anchored by a $9.7B five-year Microsoft contract that transforms the company's revenue and margin profile. The company reported Q1 FY2026 (Sep quarter) revenue of $240.3M (+355% YoY, +28% QoQ), with EBITDA margins of 38%, and targets $3.4B annualized AI cloud revenue by end of calendar 2026. At $40.95 with a $14.7B market cap, IREN trades at a fraction of the analyst consensus PT of $79. The growth inflection is simple: IREN has power infrastructure and sites from its mining operations that are now worth multiples more as AI compute capacity, and the Microsoft contract converts that optionality into contracted, high-margin recurring revenue. This is a high-conviction growth long entering the steepest part of its revenue S-curve.

## Growth Inflection Case

IREN sits at the precise inflection where AI cloud revenue begins to dominate the revenue mix, replacing volatile bitcoin mining income with contracted, high-margin recurring revenue. The inflection signals are unambiguous:

1. **Revenue model transformation in progress.** FY2025 was 97% bitcoin mining revenue ($484.6M of $501M). By end of 2026, AI cloud is targeted at $3.4B ARR -- the business is inverting its revenue composition in 18 months.

2. **Q1 FY2026 revenue of $240.3M (+355% YoY)** demonstrates the mining business alone is generating substantial cash flow to fund the pivot, while AI cloud revenue begins ramping from a small base.

3. **$9.7B Microsoft contract signed November 2025** -- this single contract delivers $1.9B in annual recurring revenue at 85% EBITDA margins once fully deployed. This is one of the highest-quality revenue contracts in the AI infrastructure space.

4. **GPU fleet scaling from 10,900 (FY2025 end) to 140,000 by end of 2026** -- a 13x increase in compute capacity that directly translates to revenue.

5. **$3.6B GPU financing secured at below 6% interest** -- the capital structure for the buildout is in place, reducing equity dilution risk.

6. **EBITDA margins already at 38% (Q1 FY26)** with the high-margin AI cloud contracts still ramping -- margins are set to expand further as the 85% EBITDA margin Microsoft contract scales.

## Business Overview & TAM

IREN Limited (formerly Iris Energy) is an Australian-listed, NASDAQ-traded company that operates data centers optimized for high-performance computing. The company began as a Bitcoin miner leveraging renewable energy sources, building out significant power infrastructure and data center capacity. In 2025, management initiated a strategic pivot to AI cloud infrastructure, leveraging existing power assets and sites.

**Total Addressable Market:** The AI cloud infrastructure market is projected at $60B in 2025, growing to $499B by 2034. IREN is positioned in the "neocloud" segment -- purpose-built AI infrastructure providers that fill the capacity gap between hyperscaler demand and self-build timelines.

**Key Assets:**
- 4.5GW+ secured power capacity across multiple sites
- 750MW Childress, Texas campus (primary Microsoft deployment site)
- Mining operations at ~50 EH/s (generating cash flow during transition)
- $2.8B cash and $9.2B in secured funding

## Business Drivers

The growth acceleration is traceable to four specific, identifiable drivers:

1. **Microsoft $9.7B five-year contract (signed November 2025).** IREN will provide NVIDIA GB300 GPUs at its Childress, Texas campus across Horizons 1-4 (200MW critical IT load). Microsoft is pre-paying 20% ($1.94B), providing capital for GPU procurement. Revenue recognition begins in phases through 2026 as each horizon is commissioned.

2. **Power infrastructure cost advantage from mining origins.** IREN secured long-term power contracts and permits for its mining operations at rates and locations that are now exceptionally valuable for AI workloads. Converting a mining site to an AI data center avoids 2-3 years of greenfield permitting and construction. This is a structural moat that new entrants cannot replicate.

3. **Dell GPU procurement agreement ($5.8B).** IREN has locked in NVIDIA GB300 supply through Dell, de-risking the GPU supply chain. Many competitors are scrambling for NVIDIA allocation; IREN has contractual certainty.

4. **Bitcoin mining cash flow funds the pivot.** At 50 EH/s and current BTC prices, the mining business generates substantial free cash flow that funds operations during the AI buildout period, reducing reliance on external capital. This dual-engine model is unique among AI infrastructure peers.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

IREN's margin trajectory is set to expand dramatically as AI cloud revenue scales:

- **Current blended EBITDA margin:** 38% (Q1 FY26) -- dominated by mining economics
- **Microsoft contract project-level EBITDA margin:** 85%
- **Target blended EBITDA margin at scale:** 60-70% (as AI cloud becomes dominant revenue source)

The operating leverage story is straightforward: IREN has already built the power infrastructure and site improvements. The incremental cost of deploying GPUs in existing facilities is primarily the GPU hardware (financed at <6%) and electricity. Once deployed, each GPU generates high-margin recurring revenue with minimal incremental operating cost. As AI cloud rises from <5% to >70% of revenue mix, blended margins expand toward the 85% contract-level economics.

## Competitive Position & Moat

**Competitive landscape:**
- **CoreWeave (CRWV):** Largest neocloud, more mature, but building greenfield capacity (slower, more expensive).
- **Nebius (NBIS):** Similar hyperscaler-backed model, but earlier in revenue recognition.
- **Applied Digital (APLD):** Similar mining-to-AI pivot, but smaller scale and less advanced.

**IREN's differentiation:**
1. **Pre-built power infrastructure.** 4.5GW+ of secured power capacity across operational sites. Power permitting is the bottleneck for AI data center expansion; IREN bypasses it entirely.
2. **Single-customer anchor contract.** The $9.7B Microsoft deal provides revenue certainty that most competitors lack. Microsoft's 20% prepayment further de-risks execution.
3. **Dual revenue engines.** Bitcoin mining provides ongoing cash flow during the AI transition, reducing dilution risk and providing a floor on revenue.
4. **Renewable energy focus.** IREN's mining operations were built on renewable energy, providing ESG alignment for hyperscaler customers increasingly focused on sustainability.

**Moat assessment:** Moderate and deepening. The power infrastructure moat is real and durable -- securing 4.5GW of power capacity with permits and interconnection agreements took years and is not easily replicable. The customer relationship moat with Microsoft will deepen as operations scale.

## Risk Factors

1. **Execution risk on GPU deployment.** Deploying 140,000 GPUs in 12 months is an extraordinary operational challenge. Any delays in Dell deliveries, facility readiness, or NVIDIA supply would directly impair the revenue ramp.
2. **Customer concentration.** Microsoft represents the vast majority of contracted AI cloud revenue. Any contract modification or delay would be material.
3. **Bitcoin price risk.** The mining business provides transition cash flow; a significant BTC price decline would reduce this buffer.
4. **Dilution risk.** Despite financing arrangements, the capital needs are enormous. Additional equity raises are possible and would dilute existing shareholders.
5. **Technology obsolescence.** AI hardware cycles are accelerating; NVIDIA GB300 GPUs deployed in 2026 may face shorter useful lives than modeled if next-gen hardware demands earlier refresh cycles.
6. **Geographic concentration.** Heavy reliance on the Childress, Texas campus for the Microsoft contract creates single-site risk.
7. **Regulatory/permitting.** While existing sites are permitted, expansion to full 4.5GW capacity requires additional approvals.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause immediate thesis re-evaluation and likely exit:

1. **AI cloud revenue fails to reach $500M+ annualized run-rate by Q2 FY2026 (December 2025 quarter)** -- this was management's stated interim target and a miss would indicate deployment delays.
2. **Microsoft contract tranches delayed beyond stated timelines by more than one quarter** -- each horizon deployment has a target date; persistent delays signal systemic execution issues.
3. **GPU installation rate falls below 10,000 units per month** -- the path to 140,000 by year-end requires sustained high-velocity deployment.
4. **Bitcoin mining revenue declines sharply without offsetting AI cloud revenue growth** -- if the transition bridge collapses before the destination is reached.
5. **Management lowers the $3.4B ARR target or signals capacity constraints** -- any guidance reduction on the AI cloud ramp.
6. **Dilutive equity raise at a significant discount to market** -- would signal capital stress and erode shareholder value.
