# Growth Long Thesis: Venture Global (VG)

## Investment Summary

Venture Global is at the most powerful growth inflection point in the US energy sector. The company is the second-largest US LNG exporter and is in the steepest phase of its capacity ramp: FY2025 revenue surged 177% to $13.8 billion, Q4 2025 revenue hit $4.45 billion (+192% YoY), and cargo volumes quadrupled from 33 to 128 per quarter in Q4 alone. This is not a mature energy company trading on commodity price -- this is an infrastructure platform in hyper-growth mode, ramping Plaquemines LNG (28 MTPA capacity) from commissioning to commercial operations while simultaneously constructing CP2 (28 MTPA), its third major facility.

The growth inflection is being turbo-charged by the Strait of Hormuz closure. Qatar's LNG production halt has removed approximately 20% of global LNG supply from the market. The US Senate's rejection of the war powers resolution on March 4 (47-53 vote) extends the expected conflict duration to weeks or months. Venture Global CEO Mike Sabel stated the company has "probably the largest number of available cargos in the market" now that Qatar is offline. The Henry Hub ($2.92/MMBtu) vs. European TTF (EUR 52.88/MWh) spread is at 5-6x normal levels, flowing directly into margin expansion for every incremental cargo exported.

At $11.16 per share, VG trades at 12-13x TTM earnings and roughly 2.0x EV/Revenue -- a fraction of the valuation granted to comparable infrastructure-growth platforms. Goldman Sachs maintains a Buy rating with a $15 price target. The stock is trading near its 52-week low of $5.72, well below its $19.50 high, creating an asymmetric entry point where the capacity growth trajectory and structural LNG demand shift have not been priced.

## Growth Inflection Case

Venture Global's growth inflection is defined by three simultaneous rate-of-change accelerations:

1. **Volume ramp inflection**: The company exported 380 cargoes in FY2025 and guides for 486-527 cargoes in FY2026, a 28-39% increase. Plaquemines Phase 1 is producing more than one commissioning cargo per day, with all 36 liquefaction trains having undergone initial startup. Phase 1 targets commercial operations in Q4 2026, Phase 2 in mid-2027. This is not yet mature production -- each quarter in 2026 should show sequential volume acceleration as trains reach full capacity.

2. **Revenue and earnings acceleration**: Q4 2025 revenue of $4.45 billion represented sequential growth of 33.5% from Q3 ($3.33B) and YoY growth of 192%. Full-year 2025 EBITDA of $6.3 billion was up 198% from FY2024's $2.1 billion. The operating margin expanded from ~30% to nearly 39% in Q4 2025.

3. **Structural demand shift**: The Hormuz closure has created a step-function increase in demand for US LNG. Qatar's offline production removes ~80 MTPA from the global market. US LNG exporters, with their destination-flexible contracts, are the primary beneficiaries. The war powers vote failure makes this a durable multi-month dynamic, not a one-week blip.

## Business Overview & TAM

Venture Global develops, constructs, and operates LNG liquefaction and export facilities on the US Gulf Coast in Louisiana. The company was founded in 2013, went public in January 2025, and has built three major projects:

- **Calcasieu Pass** (operational): 12.4 MTPA peak capacity; ~150 cargoes expected in 2026
- **Plaquemines LNG** (commissioning/construction): 28 MTPA peak capacity across Phase 1 and Phase 2; ~355 cargoes expected in 2026; Phase 1 COD in Q4 2026, Phase 2 COD in mid-2027
- **CP2 LNG** (under construction): 28 MTPA capacity; $15.1 billion project financing closed; first exports targeted Q3 2027; Phase 1 construction "on schedule and on budget"
- **Plaquemines Expansion** (planned): ~25 MTPA additional capacity; FERC application filed

Total planned capacity: 68+ MTPA run-rate near-term, growing to 102+ MTPA by 2030 if all projects execute. This would make Venture Global the largest LNG exporter in the world, surpassing Cheniere Energy (~75 MTPA target by 2030) and Qatar (currently offline).

**Total Addressable Market**: The global LNG market is approximately 400 MTPA and growing at 4-5% annually, driven by the energy transition away from coal in Asia and Europe's pivot from Russian/Middle Eastern pipeline gas. The Hormuz crisis is accelerating the structural rerouting of global gas supply toward US exporters, expanding the addressable market for Gulf Coast LNG infrastructure.

**Contract base**: 72% of the 68+ MTPA run-rate capacity is contracted long-term. The company has $134 billion in total contracted third-party revenue, with an average remaining contract duration of 19 years. Recent deals include a 1.5 MTPA 20-year agreement with Hanwha Aerospace (Korea) and a 0.5 MTPA 5-year agreement with Trafigura.

## Business Drivers

**1. Plaquemines LNG capacity ramp (near-term driver)**: All 36 liquefaction trains at Plaquemines have undergone initial startup using temporary power. The facility is producing more than one commissioning cargo per day. Phase 1 will transition to permanent power plant configuration in Q2 2026, with commercial operations targeted in Q4 2026. Phase 2 COD is mid-2027. This is the primary driver of volume growth in 2026: the 341-371 cargoes expected from Plaquemines alone represent a massive step-up from 2025 commissioning volumes.

**2. Hormuz closure / Qatar offline (structural catalyst)**: Qatar's two main LNG facilities ceased production after the Iran-US conflict erupted. This removes approximately 80 MTPA (20% of global LNG supply) from the market. US LNG exporters are the primary short-term beneficiaries because their contracts lack fixed destinations, allowing them to reroute cargoes to premium-priced markets. The Henry Hub/TTF spread at 5-6x normal levels (vs. typical 2-3x) means every incremental cargo earns outsized margins. The Senate's 47-53 rejection of the war powers resolution on March 4 extends the probable conflict duration, making this a durable tailwind rather than a transient spike.

**3. CP2 construction progress (medium-term driver)**: CP2 Phase 1 ($15.1B, 14 MTPA) construction began in late July 2025 and is proceeding on schedule and on budget. First LNG exports are targeted for Q3 2027. This project nearly doubles Venture Global's capacity and represents the next leg of the growth trajectory beyond Plaquemines ramp.

**4. Long-term contract momentum (visibility driver)**: Recent signings with Hanwha Aerospace (1.5 MTPA, 20-year) and Trafigura (0.5 MTPA, 5-year) demonstrate continued customer demand for US Gulf Coast LNG supply. 69% of 2026 production is already contracted, with the remaining 31% exposed to spot pricing -- which is currently at elevated levels due to the Hormuz crisis.

**5. Fleet ownership advantage**: Venture Global operates a fleet of 9 ships (expanding to 11 in 2026), giving it independent transportation capability that most competitors lack. CEO Sabel highlighted this as a "big advantage in a disrupted market" where chartering LNG carriers has become difficult and expensive.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Venture Global is in the early stages of its operating leverage curve:

- **Gross margins**: Averaging 42-46% through 2025, with the variability driven by commissioning vs. commercial cargo mix and commodity pricing. As Plaquemines transitions from commissioning to commercial operations, the margin profile should stabilize and improve as fixed costs are amortized over higher volumes.

- **EBITDA trajectory**: FY2025 consolidated adjusted EBITDA of $6.3B (198% growth). FY2026 guidance of $5.2-5.8B reflects the transition from higher-margin commissioning cargo sales (which are recognized at full value during the commissioning phase) to a more normalized pricing structure at Plaquemines, partially offset by the Hormuz-driven premium in spot pricing. The guidance range was described as "conservative" and does not fully reflect elevated spot LNG prices.

- **Path to peak earnings**: Once Plaquemines is fully operational (mid-2027) and CP2 Phase 1 begins exporting (late 2027), Venture Global will have 54+ MTPA of capacity generating revenue against a largely fixed cost base. The incremental EBITDA margin on each new MTPA is extremely high given the infrastructure-heavy, operationally lean business model.

- **Capital allocation**: The company declared a $0.02 quarterly dividend (0.6% yield) and has capacity for buybacks once the construction phase winds down. The $30.1B debt load is project-financed and non-recourse, secured against specific facilities, which limits corporate-level credit risk.

## Competitive Position & Moat

1. **Scale and speed of capacity buildout**: Venture Global is building capacity faster than any competitor. From zero exports in 2022 to 380 cargoes in 2025, with a path to 102+ MTPA by 2030. Cheniere, the industry leader, targets ~75 MTPA by 2030. Venture Global's modular, mid-scale liquefaction train design (Baker Hughes) enables faster construction timelines than conventional large-scale facilities.

2. **Low-cost feedstock advantage**: Gulf Coast location provides access to Henry Hub-priced natural gas at $2.92/MMBtu -- among the cheapest feedstock globally. International competitors (including Qatar when operational) face structurally higher input costs. This cost advantage is durable and independent of commodity cycles.

3. **Destination-flexible contracts**: Unlike many international LNG producers with destination-restricted supply agreements, Venture Global's contracts allow cargo rerouting to the highest-priced markets. This flexibility is especially valuable during supply disruptions like the current Hormuz crisis.

4. **Project finance structure**: Each facility is project-financed with non-recourse debt, limiting contagion risk between projects. The $15.1B CP2 financing was the largest LNG project financing in history, demonstrating capital markets confidence in the platform.

5. **Competitors**: Cheniere Energy (LNG, $52B market cap) is the primary competitor with ~45 MTPA operational capacity. New Fortress Energy (NFE) is smaller and focused on downstream distribution. International competitors (Qatar, Australia, Mozambique) face geopolitical, cost, or capacity constraints. Cheniere is a more mature, lower-growth platform; Venture Global is the higher-growth challenger.

## Risk Factors

1. **Hormuz reopening / ceasefire**: A rapid de-escalation of the Iran-US conflict would normalize LNG pricing and compress the Henry Hub/TTF spread back to 2-3x. This would reduce the margin windfall on spot and short-term cargoes. However, the war powers vote failure and Iran's stated intention to maintain disruption "for months" reduce the probability of a near-term ceasefire. Even without the crisis, the underlying volume growth story remains intact.

2. **Plaquemines commissioning delays**: The company has cautioned that commissioning and remediation processes may cause "brief periods of interruption," creating a wider-than-normal production range for 2026. Delays in reaching commercial operations (currently targeted Q4 2026 for Phase 1) would push back the transition from commissioning to commercial margins.

3. **Debt load and financing risk**: Total debt of $30.1B results in a 288.8% debt-to-equity ratio. While this debt is project-financed and non-recourse, the scale creates sensitivity to interest rates, construction delays, and revenue shortfalls at the project level. Any impairment at a single facility could impact investor confidence in the broader platform.

4. **Shell litigation overhang**: Shell challenged an arbitration ruling regarding LNG supply contracts. While a New York court ruled in Venture Global's favor on March 3, 2026, further legal challenges remain possible. This dispute has weighed on investor sentiment since the IPO.

5. **IPO lockup and insider selling**: As a January 2025 IPO, the stock has experienced selling pressure from pre-IPO investors. The 6.5% decline on March 4 may reflect ongoing supply from secondary offerings or lockup expirations.

6. **Customer concentration**: Long-term contracts are concentrated among a limited number of counterparties. Counterparty credit risk, while mitigated by the blue-chip nature of customers (Hanwha, Trafigura, major utilities), is inherent in 20-year agreements.

7. **2026 EBITDA guidance below 2025**: FY2026 EBITDA guidance of $5.2-5.8B is below FY2025's $6.3B because 2025 benefited from commissioning cargo sales recognized at full market value. This optics issue may confuse investors focused on year-over-year growth rates rather than the underlying volume trajectory.

8. **Elevated put options activity**: On March 3, put option volume surged 284% above average (67,294 contracts vs. 17,504 average), signaling institutional hedging or near-term bearish positioning around earnings. This reflects uncertainty, not necessarily directional conviction, but warrants monitoring.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause an exit -- these are growth-rate based, not valuation based:

1. **Cargo volume deceleration below guided range**: If 2026 quarterly cargo counts trend below the annualized pace needed to reach 486 cargoes (low end of guidance), it signals Plaquemines commissioning problems that would impair the entire capacity ramp thesis. Two consecutive quarters below guidance pace triggers exit.

2. **Henry Hub/TTF spread compression below 2.5x**: A sustained normalization of the spread below 2.5x (from current 5-6x) would indicate either Hormuz reopening, Qatar restart, or demand destruction. This would eliminate the margin windfall component of the thesis, though the volume growth story would remain intact at a lower margin profile.

3. **CP2 construction delays beyond Q1 2028 for first exports**: The medium-term growth story depends on CP2 reaching first exports in 2027. Material delays would push back the capacity doubling and compress the growth trajectory.

4. **Long-term contract cancellations or failure to convert pipeline**: The $134B contracted revenue base provides forward visibility. Any material cancellations or a stall in new contract signings would signal competitive displacement or customer credit concerns.

5. **EBITDA margins declining below 30% for two consecutive quarters** (excluding one-time items). The current 35-40% operating margin reflects the infrastructure-heavy, operationally lean model. Sustained margin compression would indicate cost overruns, pricing pressure, or efficiency problems.

6. **Debt covenant breaches or credit rating downgrades at the project level**: Given the $30.1B debt load, any stress at the project-finance level would immediately reprice the equity risk.
