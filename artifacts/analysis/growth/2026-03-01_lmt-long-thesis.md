# Growth Long Thesis: Lockheed Martin (LMT)

**Analyst**: Growth/Momentum Desk
**Sector**: Aerospace & Defense
**Market Cap**: ~$155B
**Price (Feb 27 close)**: $658.08

---

## Thesis Summary

Lockheed Martin is at a multi-year growth inflection point driven by the convergence of three identifiable business drivers: (1) accelerating Missiles & Fire Control revenue growing at 14% with production capacity tripling on PAC-3 MSE, (2) a record $194 billion backlog providing 2.5x sales coverage and multi-year revenue visibility, and (3) the February 28 US-Israeli strikes on Iran creating a structural increase in allied defense procurement that is not yet reflected in consensus estimates. This is not a one-day geopolitical trade -- the underlying revenue acceleration was already in place before the crisis, and the crisis extends and deepens the growth trajectory.

---

## Growth Inflection Signals

### 1. Revenue Acceleration Already Underway

Full-year 2025 sales grew 6% to $75 billion, but the acceleration is concentrated in the highest-margin, highest-growth segment. Missiles & Fire Control (MFC) grew 14% to $14.5 billion -- the segment most directly relevant to current geopolitical demand. Q4 2025 sales of $20.3 billion (up 9% YoY from $18.6B) demonstrate accelerating quarterly momentum.

Management guided 2026 sales of $77.5-80.0 billion (midpoint ~5% growth), but this guidance was issued on January 29 -- a full month before the Iran strikes. The guidance is almost certainly conservative given:
- PAC-3 MSE production capacity expanding from ~600 to 2,000 units per year
- MFC management projecting "at least double-digit" CAGR through the end of the decade
- Segment operating profit guided up more than 25% YoY to $8.425-8.675 billion (10.9% margin at midpoint)

### 2. Record Backlog Provides Revenue Visibility

The $194 billion backlog -- up $17.3 billion or 17% YoY -- represents 2.5 times annual sales and marks the fourth consecutive year of backlog growth. This is not a company dependent on new order flow to sustain growth; the revenue is already contracted. The backlog-to-revenue ratio at 2.5x is the highest in at least a decade, signaling accelerating order intake relative to delivery capacity.

### 3. Operating Leverage Inflection

Segment operating profit is guided up 25%+ on 5% revenue growth -- a clear operating leverage inflection. The margin expansion from increased MFC production volume, F-35 lot maturation, and supply chain normalization is the type of rate-of-change signal that precedes sustained earnings beats. The midpoint 10.9% segment operating margin represents meaningful expansion from 2025 levels.

### 4. Iran Crisis as Structural Growth Catalyst

The February 28 US-Israeli "Operation Epic Fury" strikes on Iran are not a temporary event -- they represent a regime change in defense spending assumptions:

- **Missile inventory depletion**: US, Israeli, and allied missile inventories are being drawn down. Replenishment cycles are 2-4 years for complex systems. LMT's PAC-3 MSE, JASSM, LRASM, and Javelin systems are the primary beneficiaries.
- **Allied spending acceleration**: European NATO nations were already moving toward 2%+ GDP defense spending. A direct US military engagement in the Middle East accelerates this timeline. Germany, Japan, South Korea, and Australia are all likely to announce supplemental defense appropriations.
- **Supplemental appropriations**: Congressional supplemental defense spending bills have historically passed within 60-90 days of major military engagements. This would add to LMT's already-record backlog.
- **Multi-year demand cycle**: Even a rapid de-escalation leaves a years-long restocking and modernization cycle that benefits LMT's production ramp.

### 5. Guidance Revision Cadence

The January 29 guidance predates the Iran crisis. The next earnings report (Q1 2026, expected late April) is the first opportunity for management to formally revise guidance upward. The gap between current consensus estimates (~$30.06 EPS for 2026) and the likely revised trajectory creates an asymmetric setup. Analysts have historically been slow to revise defense estimates upward during the early stages of a conflict cycle.

## Valuation

At $658, LMT trades at approximately 22x the current consensus 2026 EPS estimate of $30.06. This is above the historical mid-cycle multiple of ~18-19x but justified by:

1. Revenue growth accelerating from the low-single-digit historical norm to mid-to-high single digits
2. Segment operating profit growing 25%+ -- the fastest margin expansion in years
3. A structural demand increase from the Iran conflict that is not yet in estimates
4. A record backlog providing 2.5x revenue coverage -- the strongest visibility in the company's history

If 2026 EPS revises to $32-34 (reflecting supplemental orders and MFC acceleration), the forward multiple drops to 19-21x -- in line with historical averages on a structurally higher growth trajectory.

Free cash flow of $6.5-6.8 billion (guided) supports continued capital return. LMT returned $8.2 billion to shareholders in 2025 through dividends and buybacks.

## Risk Factors

- **De-escalation risk**: A rapid ceasefire or diplomatic resolution with Iran could reverse the defense sentiment trade and compress the premium multiple. However, even in this scenario, missile inventory replenishment remains a multi-year demand driver.
- **Production ramp execution**: Tripling PAC-3 MSE capacity is operationally ambitious. Supply chain constraints or quality issues could delay revenue recognition.
- **Budget sequestration risk**: A change in political dynamics could lead to defense spending caps, though this is unlikely during an active military engagement.
- **Valuation premium**: At 22x forward earnings, the stock is not cheap on current estimates. The thesis requires earnings estimate revisions to justify the entry.
- **Capital intensity**: LMT is guiding capex + IR&D approaching $5 billion in 2026, up ~35% YoY. This investment is necessary for the production ramp but compresses near-term free cash flow.
