# Growth Long Thesis: Coherent Corp (COHR)

## Investment Summary

Coherent Corp is a diversified photonics and optoelectronics platform experiencing a growth inflection driven by AI datacenter demand. The Datacenter & Communications segment now represents 72% of revenue and grew 33.5% YoY in Q2 FY2026, pulling overall company revenue to $1.69B (+17.5% YoY). NVIDIA announced a $2 billion strategic investment in Coherent on March 2, 2026, along with multibillion-dollar purchase commitments for advanced laser and optical networking products. The company's book-to-bill ratio in the datacenter segment exceeds 4x, with visibility extending into 2028. While the growth rate is more moderate than Lumentum's due to Coherent's larger revenue base and declining industrial segment, the margin expansion trajectory and NVIDIA validation provide a compelling growth-at-reasonable-price setup. Coherent trades at a lower multiple than LITE with stronger near-term earnings visibility through its diversified platform.

## Growth Inflection Case

Coherent's inflection rests on a portfolio transformation and external validation:

1. **Datacenter mix shift is accelerating.** The Datacenter & Communications segment has grown from roughly 55% of revenue two years ago to 72% in Q2 FY2026. This segment grew 33.5% YoY in Q2, while Industrial declined 9.9%. As the high-growth segment becomes a larger share of total revenue, overall company growth will accelerate toward the segment growth rate -- a classic mix-driven inflection.

2. **Book-to-bill above 4x with visibility to 2028.** This is an extraordinary level of demand backlog. A 4x book-to-bill ratio means Coherent is booking orders at four times its current shipment rate in the datacenter segment. Customers have committed to multi-year purchase agreements, providing revenue visibility rare in the photonics industry.

3. **NVIDIA $2B investment validates technology position.** NVIDIA issued 7.788M shares at $256.80 to raise $2B for Coherent, plus multibillion-dollar purchase commitments. This investment funds U.S.-based manufacturing expansion and locks in supply access for NVIDIA's next-generation AI networking platforms.

4. **Margin expansion from revenue mix and operating leverage.** Non-GAAP gross margin has expanded from 37.9% (FY2025 full year) to 39.0% (Q2 FY2026), with Q3 guidance of 38.5%-40.5%. The datacenter segment carries higher margins than the industrial segment, so the ongoing mix shift provides a structural margin tailwind.

## Business Overview & TAM

Coherent Corp (formerly II-VI and Finisar) is the world's largest vertically integrated photonics company, manufacturing optical transceivers, laser components, silicon carbide substrates, and engineered materials. The company operates across two segments:

- **Datacenter & Communications** (~72% of Q2 FY2026 revenue, ~$1.2B): Optical transceivers (800G and 1.6T), laser components, and networking solutions for hyperscale data centers and telecom. This is the growth engine.
- **Industrial** (~28% of revenue, ~$478M): Materials processing, semiconductor equipment, life sciences, and defense. Currently declining (-9.9% YoY) but expected to stabilize as industrial PMI improves (ISM Manufacturing at 52.4).

**TAM Expansion:**
- Silicon photonics market growing at 29.25% CAGR to $28.75B by 2034.
- The 800G to 1.6T transition drives a multi-year replacement cycle. Coherent is a leading supplier of 800G transceivers and is qualifying 1.6T products.
- NVIDIA's purchase commitment opens a new direct revenue stream from the largest customer in the AI infrastructure ecosystem.
- The Industrial segment, while currently a drag, positions Coherent for upside when the manufacturing cycle turns. ISM Manufacturing at 52.4 suggests the bottom may already be in.

## Business Drivers

1. **AI Datacenter Optical Transceiver Demand:** Coherent is a top-two supplier of 800G optical transceivers globally (alongside Lumentum). Hyperscaler demand for high-bandwidth interconnects is growing faster than overall data center capex as AI training clusters require exponentially more data movement between GPUs.

2. **1.6T Product Transition:** Coherent is qualifying 1.6T transceivers that will begin shipping in volume in CY2026-2027. Each generation transition typically comes with higher ASPs and improved margins as new products carry premium pricing before competition catches up.

3. **NVIDIA Strategic Partnership:** The $2B investment and multibillion-dollar purchase commitment provide both capital for manufacturing expansion and a guaranteed revenue stream. NVIDIA's platforms will be designed around Coherent's optical components, creating deep integration moats.

4. **Silicon Carbide (SiC) Optionality:** Coherent is one of the world's largest manufacturers of silicon carbide substrates for EV power electronics. While the EV market has slowed, SiC is a potential second growth engine as adoption accelerates in the 2027-2030 timeframe.

5. **Industrial Recovery:** The ISM Manufacturing PMI at 52.4 signals expansion for the first time in an extended period. If the industrial segment stabilizes and returns to growth, it would remove a drag that has masked the underlying strength of the datacenter business.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Coherent's margin story is less dramatic than Lumentum's but more broadly based:

- **Gross margin:** Non-GAAP gross margin has expanded from 37.9% (FY2025) to 39.0% (Q2 FY2026). Q3 guidance of 38.5%-40.5% suggests the upper end of the range is achievable. The structural mix shift toward higher-margin datacenter products should push gross margins toward 41-42% over the next 4-6 quarters.
- **Operating margin:** With operating expenses guided at $320-340M for Q3 on revenue of $1.7-1.84B, implied non-GAAP operating margin is approximately 20-22%. This compares to approximately 18% in FY2025, indicating steady expansion.
- **Debt reduction:** Coherent carries ~$3B in debt from the Finisar and II-VI acquisitions. The $2B NVIDIA investment strengthens the balance sheet and reduces the debt overhang, potentially accelerating free cash flow generation.
- **Rule of 40:** With ~22% pro forma revenue growth and ~20% operating margins, Coherent's Rule of 40 score is approximately 42 -- crossing the threshold that typically signals healthy growth-profitability balance.

## Competitive Position & Moat

- **Vertical integration:** Coherent is the most vertically integrated photonics company globally, manufacturing from raw materials (indium phosphide wafers, SiC substrates) through to finished optical transceivers. This provides cost advantages and supply security that fabless competitors cannot match.
- **Scale:** At ~$6.5-7.0B in annual revenue, Coherent is the largest dedicated photonics company in the world. Scale provides R&D spending capacity, manufacturing efficiency, and customer relationships that smaller competitors struggle to replicate.
- **NVIDIA lock-in:** Like Lumentum, the NVIDIA investment creates structural switching costs. Dual-sourcing between Coherent and Lumentum for NVIDIA platforms ensures both companies benefit from the AI buildout.
- **Diversification:** Unlike Lumentum, Coherent has meaningful exposure to industrial, defense, and life sciences markets. While currently a drag, this diversification provides downside protection and optionality on multiple end-market recoveries.
- **SiC substrate position:** Coherent's silicon carbide business gives it optionality on the EV/power electronics market without the binary risk profile of a pure-play SiC company.

## Risk Factors

1. **Industrial segment drag:** If the industrial segment continues declining, it will offset datacenter growth and keep overall company growth in the mid-teens rather than the 20%+ range.
2. **Debt burden:** ~$3B in legacy acquisition debt constrains financial flexibility and creates interest expense headwinds. The NVIDIA investment helps but does not eliminate this overhang.
3. **Integration complexity:** Coherent is the product of three major acquisitions (II-VI, Finisar, Coherent). Integration challenges, cultural friction, and operational complexity remain ongoing risks.
4. **Share dilution:** The 7.788M shares issued to NVIDIA at $256.80 add ~4% dilution. Combined with existing dilution from acquisition-related shares, total dilution over the past several years is significant.
5. **Customer concentration in datacenter:** Three hyperscalers likely represent the majority of datacenter segment revenue. Loss of a key customer would materially impact the growth thesis.
6. **Execution on 1.6T transition:** If Coherent's 1.6T products face yield or qualification issues, competitors (Lumentum, Broadcom, Cisco) could capture share during the critical transition window.
7. **SiC market uncertainty:** The silicon carbide business is currently a lower-margin contributor. If the EV market recovery stalls, SiC could become an ongoing drag rather than an upside catalyst.

## Deceleration Triggers

These signals would cause a thesis reassessment or exit:

1. **Datacenter & Communications segment YoY growth drops below 20% for two consecutive quarters** -- indicating market share loss or demand saturation in AI optical networking.
2. **Book-to-bill ratio in the datacenter segment falls below 1.5x** -- indicating order momentum is fading and the massive backlog is not being replenished.
3. **Non-GAAP gross margin fails to sustain above 38.5%** -- indicating pricing pressure from competition or unfavorable mix shift.
4. **Overall company revenue growth drops below 10% YoY** -- indicating the industrial drag is overwhelming datacenter growth and the portfolio is not inflecting.
5. **NVIDIA purchase commitment conversion materially trails expectations** -- if the partnership revenue does not begin materializing within 3-4 quarters.
6. **Debt-to-EBITDA remains above 2.5x through FY2027** -- indicating the company is not deleveraging despite the NVIDIA capital infusion.
