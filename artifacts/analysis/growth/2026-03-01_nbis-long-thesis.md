# Growth Long Thesis: Nebius Group N.V. (NBIS)

## Investment Summary

Nebius is a hyper-growth AI infrastructure ("neocloud") provider at a revenue inflection point, transitioning from early-stage capacity buildout to hyperscaler-contracted revenue recognition. The company exited 2025 with $1.2B ARR, growing Q4 revenue 547% YoY and 56% QoQ, and has reaffirmed a $7-9B ARR target by end of 2026. This trajectory is underwritten by signed multi-year contracts with Microsoft ($19B) and Meta ($3B), not speculative demand forecasts. At $90.87 with a $23B market cap, NBIS trades at roughly 8x forward 2026E revenue ($3.0-3.4B guided), a compression from 65x trailing -- reflecting the market's uncertainty about execution. If the ramp tracks, this multiple reprices dramatically. This is a conviction growth long at a rare inflection where contract-backed demand vastly exceeds current recognized revenue.

## Growth Inflection Case

Nebius sits at the inflection between "signed contracts" and "recognized revenue." The company has $22B+ in committed multi-year hyperscaler contracts but reported only $534M in total 2025 revenue. The gap between contracted demand and recognized revenue is the opportunity -- as GPU capacity comes online and begins generating ARR through 2026, revenue recognition will accelerate nonlinearly. Key inflection signals:

1. **Q4 2025 revenue reaccelerated sequentially** -- 56% QoQ growth vs. 39% in Q3, indicating capacity deployment is accelerating, not decelerating.
2. **ARR of $1.2B at year-end 2025 exceeded guidance**, demonstrating demand pull-forward.
3. **Adjusted EBITDA turned positive in Q4** -- the margin inflection from loss-making to profitable operations changes the investment profile from "faith-based" to "cash-flow trajectory."
4. **$1.57B deferred revenue on balance sheet** -- revenue already contracted but not yet recognized, providing exceptional forward visibility.
5. **2026 EBITDA margin guided to ~40%**, up from 24% adjusted EBITDA margin in Q4 core business -- operating leverage is kicking in as fixed infrastructure costs are amortized over growing revenue.

## Business Overview & TAM

Nebius Group (spun off from Yandex in 2024) operates as an AI-first cloud infrastructure provider, offering GPU-as-a-service and full-stack AI training/inference platforms. The company builds and operates data centers optimized for AI workloads, primarily serving hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta) and enterprise AI customers.

**Total Addressable Market:** The global AI infrastructure market is valued at $60B in 2025 and projected to reach $499B by 2034 (26.6% CAGR). The neocloud segment -- purpose-built AI cloud providers as distinct from general hyperscalers -- is the fastest-growing subsegment, driven by demand that exceeds hyperscaler self-build capacity.

**Capacity Buildout:** Nebius targets 800MW-1GW connected power by end of 2026, with contracted power reaching 2.5GW. The Vineland, NJ 300MW facility is the near-term execution milestone. Operations span the US, Europe (Finland), and the Middle East.

## Business Drivers

The acceleration in Nebius's revenue is traceable to four specific drivers:

1. **Microsoft $19B multi-year contract (signed Q3-Q4 2025):** This single contract represents nearly the entire 2026 ARR target. Microsoft is paying Nebius to provide overflow GPU capacity for Azure AI workloads -- Microsoft's own data center buildout cannot keep pace with demand. Revenue recognition began in Q4 2025 and will ramp through 2026 as capacity is deployed.

2. **Meta $3B five-year contract:** Meta is using Nebius infrastructure for Llama model training. This diversifies customer concentration beyond Microsoft and validates the platform for training workloads (not just inference).

3. **Capacity deployment acceleration:** Nebius brought more MW online in Q4 than any prior quarter, enabling faster revenue recognition. The Vineland, NJ facility (300MW) and European expansions are the near-term capacity adds.

4. **Full-stack AI platform differentiation:** Unlike pure colocation providers, Nebius offers software tooling (Nebius AI Studio) that increases switching costs and enables premium pricing. This is the path from "commodity GPU rental" to "platform with moat."

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Nebius is at the earliest stage of operating leverage. The business model has massive fixed costs (data centers, power contracts, GPU procurement) that are amortized over growing revenue:

- **Q4 2025 Adj. EBITDA margin (core):** 24%, up from 19% in Q3
- **2026 guided Adj. EBITDA margin:** ~40%
- **Medium-term EBIT target:** 20-30%

The margin expansion from 24% to 40% EBITDA in one year reflects the economics of GPU-as-a-service: once a data center is built and GPUs are installed, incremental revenue has very high contribution margins. The key variable is utilization rate -- if Nebius can maintain 90%+ GPU utilization (which hyperscaler contracts essentially guarantee), margins expand rapidly.

The path to 20-30% EBIT margins depends on depreciation schedules for GPU hardware (typically 3-5 year useful life for AI accelerators) and the cadence of next-generation GPU procurement.

## Competitive Position & Moat

**Neocloud landscape:**
- **CoreWeave (CRWV):** The largest neocloud, guiding $5B+ 2025 revenue and $12B 2026E. Nebius's primary competitor in the US market.
- **Lambda:** Competing on simplicity and price, smaller scale.
- **IREN/APLD:** Transitioning from mining/other to AI infrastructure, less mature platforms.

**Nebius's differentiation:**
1. **European stronghold:** Dominant position in European AI infrastructure (Finland data centers), where US neoclouds have minimal presence. European AI sovereignty regulations favor local/non-US providers.
2. **Full-stack software:** Nebius AI Studio provides tooling that pure hardware providers lack, creating switching costs.
3. **Yandex engineering heritage:** Deep ML/AI engineering talent from the Yandex era provides proprietary optimization of GPU clusters.
4. **$3.7B cash position:** Ample liquidity to fund buildout without dilutive equity raises in the near term.

**Moat assessment:** Emerging but not yet durable. The hyperscaler contracts provide demand visibility, but GPU cloud is a capital-intensive business with low structural barriers. The moat will deepen if the software platform gains adoption and switching costs increase.

## Risk Factors

1. **Execution risk on capacity buildout.** The $7-9B ARR target requires bringing 500-700MW of new capacity online in 2026. Any construction delays, permitting issues, or GPU supply constraints would directly impair revenue ramp.
2. **Customer concentration.** Microsoft and Meta represent the vast majority of contracted revenue. Loss or renegotiation of either contract would be catastrophic.
3. **GPU supply dependency.** Nebius relies on NVIDIA for GPU allocation. Any supply chain disruption or reallocation by NVIDIA could delay deployment.
4. **Capital intensity.** The buildout requires billions in capex. While $3.7B cash provides runway, sustained growth beyond 2026 may require additional capital raises.
5. **Yandex legacy risk.** Residual exposure to Yandex-related assets, liabilities, or sanctions risk from the corporate separation.
6. **Competition from hyperscalers.** If Microsoft/Meta build internal capacity faster than expected, they may reduce reliance on neoclouds, pressuring contract renewals.
7. **Macro/tariff risk.** AI infrastructure capex is sensitive to broader corporate spending. A significant economic downturn could slow enterprise AI adoption.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause immediate thesis re-evaluation and likely exit:

1. **Sequential QoQ revenue growth falls below 30% for two consecutive quarters** -- this would indicate the ramp is slowing and the $7-9B ARR target is unreachable.
2. **ARR growth flatlines or declines quarter-over-quarter** -- any stall in the ARR ramp would signal demand or capacity deployment issues.
3. **Microsoft or Meta contract modification, deferral, or cancellation** -- any indication that hyperscaler demand is softening.
4. **Vineland facility commissioning delayed beyond Q3 2026** -- this is the critical near-term capacity milestone.
5. **Adjusted EBITDA margin contracts below 20%** -- margin compression during a ramp phase would signal structural cost issues, not just growth investment.
6. **GPU utilization rates disclosed below 85%** -- would indicate demand is not matching capacity, undermining the entire infrastructure thesis.
