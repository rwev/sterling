# Growth Long Thesis: Zscaler, Inc. (ZS)

## Investment Summary

Zscaler is exhibiting a textbook growth re-acceleration at a historically discounted valuation. Revenue growth has inflected from a 21% trough in Q4 FY2025 to 26% in the two most recent quarters, ARR is growing 25% to $3.36B, and remaining performance obligations are growing 31% to $6.1B -- the strongest leading indicator in the model. The stock trades at $165.91, down 51% from its November 2025 high of $337 and sitting at a forward P/S of approximately 8x on FY2026 revenue guidance of $3.31-3.32B. This is the cheapest ZS has been on a growth-adjusted basis since its 2018 IPO. The convergence of three catalysts -- Iranian state-sponsored cyber escalation against US critical infrastructure, CISA capability degradation from DOGE workforce cuts forcing emergency private-sector security spending, and Zscaler's AI Security Suite launch expanding its addressable market -- creates a setup where the growth re-acceleration is likely to sustain or intensify through FY2027. The market is pricing ZS for growth deceleration. The data says the opposite.

## Growth Inflection Case

The inflection rests on three converging forces:

1. **Revenue re-acceleration is confirmed across multiple quarters.** After bottoming at 21% YoY growth in Q4 FY2025 ($719.2M), revenue growth has jumped to 26% in both Q1 and Q2 FY2026 ($788.1M and $815.8M respectively). Q2 FY2026 revenue of $815.8M beat management guidance of $797-799M by $17-19M -- a meaningful upside surprise for a company of this scale. The company raised full-year FY2026 revenue guidance to $3.309-3.322B. Q3 FY2026 guidance of $834-836M implies continued 23%+ growth and another potential beat given the pattern of conservative guidance.

2. **RPO growth of 31% significantly outpaces revenue growth.** Remaining performance obligations of $6.1B growing 31% YoY represent contracted but not yet recognized revenue. When RPO growth exceeds revenue growth by 500+ basis points, it signals that bookings momentum is accelerating ahead of the P&L -- a classic leading indicator of sustained revenue acceleration. The company also raised its FY2026 ARR target to $3.745B, implying 24% growth.

3. **Iran cyber escalation creates a step-function demand catalyst the market has not priced.** Palo Alto Networks' Unit 42 published a threat brief on March 3 documenting active wiper malware campaigns by Iranian groups Seedworm, MuddyWater, and the FAD Team (Fatimiyoun Cyber Team) against US banks, airports, and software suppliers. Iran's wiper arsenal now includes 15+ malware families. Critically, CISA -- the lead US federal cyber agency -- has canceled cybersecurity assessments and stopped updating its website since February 17 due to federal funding lapses from DOGE cuts. The degradation of federal cyber defense capabilities forces enterprises to accelerate private-sector security spending. Zscaler's Zero Trust Exchange, which inspects all traffic in-line regardless of source or destination, is the architectural answer to wiper-style attacks that bypass perimeter defenses.

## Business Overview & TAM

Zscaler operates the world's largest inline cloud security platform, the Zscaler Zero Trust Exchange. Unlike legacy firewall architectures that create a perimeter around the corporate network, Zscaler's platform sits between users and the internet/applications, inspecting all traffic bidirectionally. The platform serves over 8,600 customers globally, including 40% of the Fortune 500, with 620 customers generating over $1M in ARR (+18% YoY).

The company addresses three core markets:

- **Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA)**: Replacing VPNs and firewalls with identity-based access controls. Gartner projects 80% of enterprises will adopt ZTNA by 2027, up from approximately 20% today.
- **Cloud Security / SSE (Security Service Edge)**: Zscaler holds the number one position in Gartner's SSE Magic Quadrant with a 4.7-star rating on 1,123 peer reviews, outscoring Palo Alto Networks (4.5 on 543 reviews).
- **AI Security**: Launched in January 2026, the Zscaler AI Security Suite addresses the emerging market for securing AI/ML model usage, data flows, and shadow AI within enterprises. Zscaler processed nearly 1 trillion AI transactions in calendar 2025 -- a 91% YoY surge in AI-related traffic through its platform. The combined emerging product pillar (AI security, zero trust segmentation, data security) crossed $1B ARR in Q1 FY2026.

Total addressable market for cloud security is estimated at $96B by 2028 (Gartner), with the AI-driven cybersecurity segment projected to grow from $31.5B in 2025 to $93.8B by 2030. Zscaler's current ~$3.4B ARR represents low single-digit penetration of its addressable market.

## Business Drivers

**Zero Trust Replacement Cycle**: The global shift from perimeter-based security (firewalls, VPNs) to zero-trust architecture is a multi-year replacement cycle. Gartner estimates only 20% of enterprises have completed ZTNA adoption. Zscaler's cloud-native architecture positions it as the default replacement for legacy Cisco/Juniper VPN infrastructure. Each enterprise migration is a multi-year, multi-million-dollar commitment that drives both new logo acquisition and expansion within existing accounts.

**Iranian Cyber Escalation and CISA Degradation**: The convergence of two unprecedented events creates emergency spending conditions. First, Iranian state-sponsored groups have launched active wiper malware campaigns against US critical infrastructure since March 3, 2026, targeting banks, airports, and software supply chains. Second, CISA -- the federal agency responsible for protecting civilian infrastructure from cyber threats -- has been functionally degraded by DOGE workforce cuts, with its website not updated since February 17 and cybersecurity assessments canceled. This forces enterprises and state/local governments to accelerate private-sector security procurement. Zscaler's inline inspection architecture is specifically designed to detect and block wiper-style attacks that bypass traditional perimeter defenses.

**AI Security as TAM Expansion**: Zscaler processed nearly 1 trillion AI transactions in calendar 2025 (91% YoY growth in AI traffic). The January 2026 launch of the AI Security Suite -- including AI asset inventory, dependency mapping, zero-trust controls for AI workflows, and automated red teaming -- creates an entirely new product pillar. The emerging products pillar (AI security + data protection + zero-trust segmentation) crossed $1B combined ARR in Q1 FY2026. This is a new growth vector that did not exist 12 months ago.

**Platform Consolidation with CrowdStrike**: The strategic partnership between Zscaler (network/cloud security) and CrowdStrike (endpoint security) creates a unified zero-trust platform that outperforms legacy vendors like Palo Alto Networks and Microsoft in multi-cloud environments. This partnership drives joint go-to-market deals and reduces customer acquisition friction. The "Zero Trust Everywhere" enterprise cohort has grown to over 550 customers.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Zscaler is at an inflection point in its profitability trajectory:

- **Non-GAAP operating margin** has expanded from approximately 19.6% in Q2 FY2025 to 22.2% in Q2 FY2026, a 260 bps improvement in four quarters. Management guided FY2026 non-GAAP EPS of $3.99-$4.02, implying continued margin expansion.
- **Gross margin** is stable at 76-77%, reflecting the inherent leverage in a cloud platform model where incremental revenue carries minimal variable cost.
- **FCF generation** is robust: FY2025 FCF was $726.7M on $2.67B revenue (27% FCF margin). Q1 FY2026 alone generated $413.3M FCF (52% of revenue). Q2 FCF of $169.1M was seasonally lower due to cash collection timing, not structural deterioration.
- **Path to 25%+ non-GAAP operating margins** by FY2028: As revenue scales toward $4-5B, R&D and S&M spending growth should moderate relative to revenue, driving 200-300 bps of additional margin expansion. Consensus estimates FY2027 non-GAAP EPS of $4.64 (18% growth) and FY2028 of $5.62 (21% growth).

The combination of 22-26% revenue growth and expanding margins creates a compounding earnings trajectory that the market is not pricing at current levels.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Zscaler's competitive moat rests on four pillars:

1. **Largest inline security cloud**: Zscaler's Zero Trust Exchange processes over 500 billion transactions daily across 150+ data centers globally. This scale creates a network effect: more traffic means better threat intelligence, which means better detection, which attracts more customers. No competitor matches this scale of inline inspection.

2. **Architectural advantage**: Unlike Palo Alto Networks and Fortinet, which adapted legacy firewall architectures for the cloud, Zscaler was built cloud-native from inception. This architectural purity means no performance trade-offs from hardware emulation and no technical debt from legacy code bases. In the SSE market specifically, Gartner ranks Zscaler number one.

3. **Switching costs and embedded base**: With 620 customers generating $1M+ ARR and deep integration into enterprise networking stacks, customer switching costs are extremely high. The discontinued net retention metric was 115% (trailing twelve months) before Zscaler stopped reporting it in Q2 FY2026, reflecting strong expansion within the installed base.

4. **CrowdStrike partnership as force multiplier**: The joint ZS+CRWD zero-trust platform creates a two-vendor standard that is increasingly difficult for competitors to displace. Enterprises committing to this stack face prohibitive switching costs across both network and endpoint security simultaneously.

## Risk Factors

1. **Organic growth deceleration proves real.** If ex-acquisition net new ARR growth remains in the single digits for Q3-Q4 FY2026, the bull thesis weakens materially. The 31% RPO growth argues against this but does not eliminate the risk.

2. **AI disruption to seat-based security licensing.** The market's fear that frontier AI models could cannibalize specialized security software is overstated for Zscaler -- its value proposition is inline traffic inspection at cloud scale, not a software feature that an LLM can replicate -- but the narrative has compressed multiples sector-wide.

3. **Macro deterioration impacts IT budgets.** The NFP miss of -92,000 signals potential economic weakness. If enterprises enter a broad spending freeze, cybersecurity may not be entirely immune despite its essential nature. Counter: cybersecurity consistently outperforms IT spending in downturns (14% structural CAGR vs 5% total IT).

4. **Competitive pressure from Microsoft.** Microsoft Entra and Microsoft Defender are expanding into ZTNA territory, leveraging the existing Microsoft 365 installed base. While Zscaler's architectural superiority is clear, Microsoft's bundling strategy can slow deal velocity at budget-constrained enterprises.

5. **Iran ceasefire risk.** A sudden diplomatic resolution or Iranian capitulation would remove the cyber escalation catalyst. Probability assessed at 10-15% over two weeks given Trump's unconditional surrender demand and Iran's refusal to negotiate. However, even in a ceasefire scenario, enterprise cyber budgets that have been accelerated are rarely reversed -- security spending is asymmetrically sticky upward.
