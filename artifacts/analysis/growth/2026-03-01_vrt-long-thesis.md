# Growth Long Thesis: Vertiv Holdings (VRT)

## Investment Summary

Vertiv is the dominant pure-play data center power and cooling infrastructure company, sitting at the inflection point of the most consequential capital expenditure cycle in modern history. The company's Q4 2025 results revealed a step-function change in demand: organic orders surged 252% year-over-year, backlog doubled to a record $15B, and book-to-bill reached 2.9x. The 2026 revenue guidance of $13.25-13.75B beat consensus by over $1B. This is not a company growing into a stable demand curve -- it is experiencing demand acceleration that is still being underestimated by the Street. With adjusted operating margin guided to 22.5% (up 210 bps), adjusted EPS growing 43% to $6.20, and a $15B backlog providing multi-year revenue visibility, Vertiv is a compounding growth story with unusually high earnings visibility.

## Growth Inflection Case

Vertiv's inflection is defined by three converging forces:

1. **Order acceleration**: Organic orders went from +60% YoY in Q3 2025 to +252% YoY in Q4 2025. This is not a gradual trend -- it is a hockey stick. The book-to-bill ratio of 2.9x means orders are coming in nearly three times faster than revenue is being recognized, ensuring sustained growth well into 2027.

2. **Liquid cooling tipping point**: Industry liquid cooling penetration is inflecting from approximately 15% to a projected 50%+ of all new data center builds in 2026. Vertiv's deep partnership with NVIDIA (co-developed reference architectures for GB200 NVL72 systems) positions it as the default cooling solution for next-gen AI infrastructure. The PerchRight acquisition strengthens fluid management and direct liquid cooling capabilities.

3. **Revenue guidance beat**: The 2026 guide of $13.5B (midpoint) exceeded consensus of $12.39B by $1.1B, or 9%. This is a massive guidance beat that signals management confidence in backlog conversion. At 28% organic growth, the revenue acceleration from 2025's 27.7% growth suggests the ramp is still building, not plateauing.

## Business Overview & TAM

Vertiv designs and manufactures critical digital infrastructure for data centers, including:

- **Power management**: UPS systems, switchgear, power distribution, busway
- **Thermal management**: Precision cooling (air and liquid), containment systems
- **IT management**: Rack systems, monitoring software, service

The company operates globally across three segments: Americas, APAC, and EMEA. Americas is the largest and fastest-growing segment (43% organic growth in Q3 2025), driven by hyperscaler build-outs concentrated in the US.

**TAM trajectory**: The global data center infrastructure market is expanding rapidly. AI racks draw 120-150 kW each versus 10-15 kW for traditional workloads -- a 10x increase in power and cooling content per rack. As AI penetration of data center capacity grows from approximately 20% today to 50%+ over the next 3-5 years, Vertiv's addressable content per data center expands proportionally. The liquid cooling market alone is projected to grow from a few billion dollars today to $15-20B by 2030.

## Business Drivers

1. **NVIDIA partnership depth**: Vertiv co-developed the reference cooling architecture for NVIDIA's GB200 NVL72 systems. As NVIDIA ships Blackwell and Rubin platforms, every rack requires Vertiv-designed thermal solutions. This is a designed-in relationship, not a commodity supplier position.

2. **Liquid cooling adoption**: At 120-150 kW per AI rack, air cooling is physically insufficient. The industry transition from 15% to 50%+ liquid cooling penetration is a structural, multi-year demand driver. Vertiv's recent PerchRight acquisition adds fluid management capabilities, strengthening its position in direct liquid cooling.

3. **Hyperscaler capex**: Combined 2026 capex from the top four hyperscalers exceeds $625B, with 75% directed at AI infrastructure. Each dollar of AI capex requires proportional investment in power distribution and cooling -- Vertiv captures a share of every build.

4. **Power density escalation**: Each successive AI chip generation draws more power. The trend from 10 kW/rack to 150 kW/rack is not reversing. Vertiv's UPS, switchgear, and power distribution content per data center scales linearly with power density.

5. **Geographic expansion**: Americas grew 43% in Q3 2025, but APAC is accelerating as hyperscalers build international AI capacity. This provides a long runway for geographic revenue diversification.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Vertiv's margin expansion story is credible and well-supported:

- **FY2024 adjusted operating margin**: approximately 20.3%
- **FY2025 adjusted operating margin**: approximately 21.0%
- **FY2026 guided adjusted operating margin**: 22.5% (210 bps expansion)

The margin path is driven by:
- **Volume leverage**: Fixed manufacturing and SG&A costs spread over a rapidly growing revenue base
- **Pricing power**: Vertiv has demonstrated the ability to raise prices in a supply-constrained environment. AI cooling solutions carry premium margins versus commodity power equipment.
- **Mix shift**: Higher-margin liquid cooling and integrated solutions represent a growing share of revenue
- **Backlog conversion efficiency**: A $15B backlog allows optimized production scheduling and procurement

With revenue at $13.5B and operating margin at 22.5%, adjusted operating profit is guided at $3.04B -- a 48% increase from 2025. Adjusted free cash flow is guided at $2.2B.

## Competitive Position & Moat

- **NVIDIA co-design relationship**: The most significant moat. Being the reference architecture partner means Vertiv is specified into builds by default. Hyperscalers follow NVIDIA's reference designs.
- **Installed base and service**: Vertiv has the largest installed base of data center infrastructure globally, generating recurring service revenue and deep customer relationships.
- **Full-stack offering**: Power + cooling + IT infrastructure from a single vendor reduces integration risk for customers building at scale and speed.
- **Manufacturing scale**: Global manufacturing footprint with capacity being expanded to meet backlog demand.
- **Competitors**: Schneider Electric and Eaton compete in power; Schneider and smaller players compete in cooling. None match Vertiv's pure-play focus and NVIDIA integration depth.

## Risk Factors

1. **Backlog conversion risk**: A $15B backlog must be converted to revenue. Supply chain disruptions, component shortages, or execution missteps could delay conversions and disappoint growth expectations.
2. **Tariff exposure**: Vertiv has manufacturing and sourcing in Asia. The 20% China tariffs effective March 4 could compress margins if costs cannot be passed through.
3. **Concentration risk**: Heavy dependence on a small number of hyperscaler customers. A capex pause by any major customer would have an outsized impact.
4. **Valuation premium**: At 41x forward earnings, any growth miss would trigger meaningful multiple contraction.
5. **Competition from Schneider Electric**: Schneider is investing aggressively in data center cooling and power. As the market grows, competitive intensity may increase and compress Vertiv's premium pricing.
6. **Cyclicality**: Data center infrastructure spending has historically been cyclical, though the AI-driven structural demand may moderate this risk.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause an exit regardless of valuation:

- **Organic order growth decelerating below 50% YoY** for two consecutive quarters
- **Book-to-bill falling below 1.2x**, indicating order momentum is fading
- **Backlog declining sequentially** from the $15B level
- **2026 revenue guidance being reduced below $12.5B** at any point
- **Adjusted operating margin contracting below 20%**, signaling pricing pressure or cost overruns
- **Loss of NVIDIA reference architecture partnership status**
- **Any top-3 hyperscaler announcing a material reduction in 2026/2027 capex**
