# Growth Long Thesis: Celestica Inc. (CLS)

## Investment Summary

Celestica is the premier contract manufacturer for AI server and networking hardware, and its revenue growth is accelerating on a quarterly basis in a pattern that signals a durable inflection rather than a one-time spike. Quarterly YoY revenue growth moved from +20% (Q1 2025) to +21% (Q2), +28% (Q3), and +44% (Q4), a textbook sequential acceleration curve driven by hyperscaler AI infrastructure deployment volumes. Management raised 2026 revenue guidance from $16B to $17B (+37% YoY) and adjusted EPS guidance from $8.20 to $8.75 (+45% YoY), while operating margins expanded from 6.4% in FY2024 to 7.7% in Q4 2025, with 7.8% guided for FY2026. At $271 with a consensus target of $373 (38% upside), the stock offers a compelling entry into a company that functions as the volume proxy for total AI hardware deployments -- every GPU that ships needs a server to house it, and Celestica is the integrator.

## Growth Inflection Case

Celestica's inflection is driven by three converging dynamics:

1. **Revenue acceleration is sequential and steepening.** This is the single most important signal. Q4 2025 revenue of $3.65B was 44% above Q4 2024 and 14% above Q3 2025 sequentially. The growth rate is not stable -- it is accelerating quarter over quarter. Q1 2026 guidance of $3.85-4.15B (midpoint $4.0B, +51% YoY) confirms the acceleration is continuing into 2026. This is rare and powerful: companies that show sequential revenue growth acceleration over four or more quarters are typically in the early-to-middle stages of a structural demand ramp, not at the peak.

2. **Operating leverage is emerging.** Celestica has historically operated with thin margins as a contract manufacturer. The shift from 6.4% non-GAAP operating margin in FY2024 to 7.7% in Q4 2025 represents a 130 basis point expansion in four quarters. This is significant for a company in the EMS (electronics manufacturing services) sector, where every 50 basis points of margin expansion drops directly to the bottom line on a high-revenue base. The 7.8% FY2026 guidance suggests management is conservatively guiding margin, leaving room for upside.

3. **Mix shift toward higher-value AI infrastructure.** Celestica is not simply assembling more of the same product. The AI server and networking hardware it assembles for hyperscalers carries higher content value, more engineering complexity, and better margins than traditional enterprise IT hardware. As AI infrastructure becomes a larger share of total revenue, the structural margin profile improves even before scale efficiencies are considered.

## Business Overview & TAM

Celestica is a global electronics manufacturing services (EMS) company headquartered in Toronto, Canada. It designs, manufactures, and assembles complex electronic systems for OEMs and hyperscalers. The company operates through two segments:

- **Advanced Technology Solutions (ATS)**: Higher-margin segment including aerospace, defense, healthcare, and industrial applications
- **Connectivity & Cloud Solutions (CCS)**: The growth engine, encompassing servers, networking equipment, storage, and AI infrastructure for hyperscale data centers

The CCS segment is driving the current inflection. Celestica assembles GPU servers (including NVIDIA-based platforms), high-performance networking switches, and rack-scale integrated systems for the major hyperscalers. Every NVIDIA Blackwell or Rubin GPU deployment requires server integration, thermal solution assembly, and rack-level system configuration -- and Celestica is one of a small number of qualified tier-one integrators.

**TAM**: The hyperscaler capex cycle is deploying approximately $630B in 2026, of which 35-40% flows to compute hardware (roughly $160-180B for GPUs, servers, and system integration). Celestica's addressable portion of this -- server assembly, networking hardware, and rack-scale integration -- represents a TAM well in excess of $50B annually. At $17B in 2026 revenue, Celestica's current share is substantial but still has room to grow as the total deployment volume increases through 2027-2028.

## Business Drivers

The acceleration signals trace to specific, identifiable business drivers:

1. **Hyperscaler AI server volume ramp.** The Big Four hyperscalers (Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft) committed $630B in 2026 capex, up 62% from 2025. Approximately 75% is directed at AI infrastructure. Celestica is a qualified tier-one contract manufacturer for multiple hyperscalers, meaning it captures volume regardless of which hyperscaler grows fastest. The Q4 2025 revenue surge (+44% YoY) coincides with the hyperscaler capex acceleration into H1 2026 deployment cycles.

2. **NVIDIA Blackwell/Rubin platform ramp.** Each new NVIDIA GPU generation requires new server designs, new thermal solutions, and new rack configurations. Celestica's engineering capability in custom server design gives it a competitive advantage in winning new platform build slots. The Blackwell GB200 NVL72 systems are more complex and higher-value than prior generations, increasing Celestica's revenue content per system.

3. **Networking hardware buildout.** Celestica is not only assembling servers -- it also manufactures networking switches and optical infrastructure for data centers. The 400G-to-800G transition in data center networking is a parallel revenue stream that grows alongside server deployments.

4. **Manufacturing capacity expansion.** Celestica is investing approximately $1B in 2026 capex to expand manufacturing in Texas and Thailand. The Texas facility serves the onshoring trend for US hyperscaler supply chains, while Thailand provides cost-advantaged capacity for global customers. This expansion positions Celestica to capture share as total deployment volume increases in 2026-2027.

5. **Customer concentration advantage.** Celestica's top hyperscaler customers have multi-year infrastructure buildout plans. The shift from project-based orders to programmatic deployment (quarterly volume commitments) provides revenue visibility that was not available in prior cycles.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Celestica's margin expansion from 6.4% (FY2024) to 7.7% (Q4 2025) and 7.8% guided (FY2026) reflects three structural forces:

- **Scale efficiencies.** Revenue growing from $9.65B (2024) to $17B (2026) on a manufacturing footprint that is expanding but at a slower rate than revenue creates natural operating leverage. Fixed costs (facilities, engineering teams, IT systems) are spread across a larger revenue base.

- **Mix shift.** AI infrastructure carries higher engineering content and better margins than legacy enterprise hardware. As AI becomes a larger share of total revenue, consolidated margins improve structurally.

- **Pricing discipline.** The bottleneck in qualified tier-one integrators gives Celestica modest pricing power on new platform builds. Hyperscalers are prioritizing speed-to-deployment over cost minimization, which favors margin stability.

The path to 8%+ non-GAAP operating margin is visible by FY2027 if revenue continues to scale. For context, Celestica operated at 4-5% operating margins as recently as 2022. The margin trajectory is a structural improvement, not a cyclical bounce.

## Competitive Position & Moat

Celestica's competitive moat is narrower than a semiconductor company's but real:

- **Qualification barriers.** Becoming a qualified tier-one manufacturer for hyperscalers requires extensive validation, audit, and quality certification processes that take 12-18 months. This limits the number of competitors that can enter the market for specific platforms.

- **Engineering depth.** Celestica's custom server design capability -- the ability to co-develop server and rack architectures with customers -- differentiates it from lower-tier contract manufacturers. This is not commodity assembly; it is value-added engineering.

- **Geographic diversification.** Manufacturing presence in North America (Toronto, Texas), Asia (Thailand, Malaysia, Japan), and Europe provides supply chain resilience that hyperscalers value in a tariff-sensitive environment.

- **Key competitors.** Foxconn (private/Taiwanese), Quanta Computer (Taiwanese), Wistron, and Flex are the primary competitors. Celestica's advantage is its North American engineering base and its established relationships with Western hyperscalers. The tariff environment (25% on China imports) favors non-China manufacturers like Celestica.

## Risk Factors

1. **Customer concentration.** Celestica's top hyperscaler customers likely represent 50%+ of CCS segment revenue. Loss or reduction of a single large customer relationship would be material.

2. **Tariff exposure.** The March 4 tariff escalation (25% Canada/Mexico, 20% China) creates cost uncertainty. Celestica manufactures in Canada (Toronto) and Thailand, which may face future tariff actions. However, the US-focused Texas expansion partially mitigates this risk.

3. **Margin compression risk.** If hyperscalers begin cost-optimizing their supply chains as the initial buildout matures, contract manufacturers could face pricing pressure. Celestica's thin margin profile (7-8%) means even modest compression is painful.

4. **Capex execution risk.** The $1B capex in 2026 represents a significant ramp in manufacturing capacity. If demand slows before the capacity comes online, utilization rates and margins could disappoint.

5. **Cyclicality.** EMS companies are inherently cyclical. If hyperscaler capex decelerates sharply (from 62% growth to <20%), Celestica's order volume could slow faster than the revenue backlog can offset.

6. **Valuation gap to consensus.** The stock is currently below its post-earnings high of $404 (January 2026), having pulled back approximately 33%. This pullback may reflect broader tech de-risking rather than fundamental deterioration, but the volatility is real.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause an exit or significant position reduction -- these are growth-rate-based, not valuation-based:

1. **YoY revenue growth dropping below 25%.** The current trajectory is 37% for FY2026. A deceleration below 25% in any quarter would signal the demand inflection is peaking.

2. **Sequential QoQ revenue decline.** Any quarter where revenue declines sequentially from the prior quarter would break the acceleration pattern and signal a demand plateau.

3. **2026 revenue guidance reduction.** Any downward revision to the $17B FY2026 revenue target would indicate customer order deferrals or cancellations.

4. **Operating margin contraction below 7.0%.** If operating margins reverse course and compress below 7.0%, the operating leverage thesis breaks.

5. **Hyperscaler capex guidance cuts.** If any two or more of the Big Four hyperscalers reduce their 2026/2027 capex guidance, the entire demand thesis for Celestica is at risk.

6. **Loss of a top-3 customer.** Any public indication that a major hyperscaler is shifting assembly volume to a competitor would be a material negative signal.
