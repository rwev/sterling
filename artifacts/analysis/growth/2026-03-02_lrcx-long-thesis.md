# Growth Long Thesis: Lam Research (LRCX)

## Investment Summary

Lam Research is the leading provider of wafer fabrication equipment for etch, deposition, and clean processes -- the critical steps that define the physical architecture of every advanced semiconductor chip. The company is in the early stages of a multi-year revenue acceleration driven by the convergence of three structural demand waves: the CHIPS Act-fueled US fab buildout, the AI-driven expansion of advanced packaging and high-bandwidth memory, and the long-awaited NAND recovery. Revenue has grown for ten consecutive quarters, from $3.48B in Q4 FY2024 (March 2024) to $5.34B in Q2 FY2026 (December 2025) -- a 53% increase in less than two years. Management raised its 2026 wafer fabrication equipment (WFE) industry forecast to $135B, up 23% from $110B in 2025, and guided Q3 FY2026 revenue to $5.7B (midpoint), which would represent approximately 24% YoY growth. With gross margins near 50%, operating margins at 34-35%, and an advanced packaging business growing over 40% annually, Lam is the highest-quality picks-and-shovels play on the semiconductor reshoring and AI infrastructure build-out.

## Growth Inflection Case

Lam Research's inflection is defined by three rate-of-change signals converging simultaneously:

1. **Revenue re-acceleration after the 2023 trough.** Lam's revenue bottomed in FY2024 Q1 (June 2023) at approximately $3.2B during the semiconductor downcycle. Since then, revenue has inflected sharply upward for ten consecutive quarters. The YoY growth rate has re-accelerated from single digits in mid-FY2025 to 22% in Q2 FY2026, with Q3 FY2026 guidance implying 24%+ YoY growth. This is a classic cyclical recovery that is now being amplified by structural demand.

2. **Advanced packaging is a new growth vector.** AI chips require advanced packaging -- chiplet architectures, 2.5D/3D integration, CoWoS, and hybrid bonding -- that is intensely equipment-intensive. Lam's advanced packaging revenue is growing over 40% in 2026, faster than any other segment. This is incremental TAM that did not exist at this scale three years ago. Every TSMC CoWoS line, every Intel Foveros expansion, and every Samsung 3D packaging capacity addition requires Lam's etch and deposition tools.

3. **CHIPS Act demand is just beginning.** The $52.7B CHIPS Act is driving 10+ major US fabs into construction or ramp. TSMC Arizona Fab 2 begins equipment installation in Q3 2026. Samsung Taylor is installing EUV tools. Intel Fab 52 is in high-volume manufacturing. Each new fab represents hundreds of millions in equipment purchases from Lam. This demand layer is additive to the existing global capacity build-out and creates a multi-year order pipeline through 2028-2029.

## Business Overview & TAM

Lam Research designs, manufactures, and services semiconductor processing equipment used in the fabrication of integrated circuits. The company operates in three product segments:

- **Etch systems** (~45% of systems revenue): Plasma etch tools that define circuit patterns on wafers. Lam is the clear market leader with approximately 50% share in the etch market.
- **Deposition systems** (~40% of systems revenue): Chemical vapor deposition (CVD) and atomic layer deposition (ALD) tools that build up thin films on wafers. Lam holds approximately 30% share, competing with Applied Materials.
- **Clean/other** (~15% of systems revenue): Wafer cleaning and surface preparation equipment.

Additionally, Lam's **Customer Support Business Group (CSBG)** provides spare parts, upgrades, and service to the installed base, generating approximately $1.8-2.0B per quarter in recurring revenue.

**TAM**: Management projects the WFE market at $135B in calendar year 2026, up 23% from $110B in 2025. Lam's addressable portion (etch + deposition + clean + advanced packaging) represents approximately 35-40% of total WFE, or $47-54B. At approximately $21B in trailing revenue, Lam has a roughly 40% share of its addressable market with room to grow as advanced packaging expands the equipment intensity per wafer.

## Business Drivers

The acceleration signals trace to specific, identifiable drivers:

1. **CHIPS Act fab build-out.** TSMC Arizona (3 fabs, $100B committed), Intel Arizona (Fab 52 in HVM), Samsung Taylor (EUV installation March 2026), and multiple other projects are creating a US-specific demand layer for wafer fab equipment. TSMC Fab 2 equipment installation beginning Q3 2026 is a near-term catalyst for Lam's order book. The January 2026 Section 232 tariff of 25% on advanced logic chips and the threat of 100% tariffs on TSMC/Samsung are accelerating reshoring timelines.

2. **Advanced packaging expansion.** CoWoS capacity at TSMC is doubling in 2025-2026 to meet AI chip demand. Intel's Foveros and Samsung's I-Cube packaging lines require Lam's etch and deposition tools. Advanced packaging revenue is growing over 40% and management has identified this as a structural growth driver for the next 3-5 years. Every AI accelerator chip (NVIDIA H100/B200/Rubin, AMD MI-series, custom ASIC) uses advanced packaging.

3. **NAND recovery.** After a brutal downcycle that saw NAND capex cut by over 50%, the NAND market is recovering as inventory normalizes and new 200+ layer architectures require equipment-intensive transitions. NAND has historically represented 25-30% of Lam's revenue. Even a partial recovery adds billions in incremental demand.

4. **Equipment pull-forward from tariff uncertainty.** The March 4 tariff deadline is causing fab builders to front-load equipment imports before potential cost increases. This creates a near-term demand spike, though it may borrow from future quarters. The structural effect is to accelerate fab timelines, which is positive for Lam's multi-year order visibility.

5. **Installed base growth.** The Customer Support Business Group generates approximately $1.8-2.0B per quarter in recurring revenue from the expanding installed base. As more tools ship into new fabs, CSBG revenue grows with a 2-3 year lag, creating a durable earnings stream.

## Operating Leverage & Margin Path

Lam's margin profile is among the best in the semiconductor equipment industry:

- **Gross margin**: Running at 49-51%, up from approximately 46% at the downcycle trough. The expansion reflects pricing discipline (equipment lead times are extending), mix improvement (advanced packaging tools carry premium margins), and manufacturing scale. Guided at 49% for Q3 FY2026, suggesting stability at elevated levels.

- **Operating margin**: Running at 34-35%, up from approximately 28% at the trough. R&D investment is running at approximately 14% of revenue, roughly flat in percentage terms but growing in absolute dollars to fund advanced packaging and next-generation etch capabilities.

- **Path forward**: As WFE grows to $135B in 2026 and Lam's revenue scales toward $22-23B annually, operating margins should sustain at 34-36%. The CSBG recurring revenue stream provides a margin floor, as service and spare parts carry higher margins than new equipment.

The key insight is that Lam's margins are not cyclically elevated -- they are structurally higher than prior peaks because the equipment intensity per wafer is increasing (more etch and deposition steps per advanced chip) and the advanced packaging revenue stream is incremental.

## Competitive Position & Moat

- **Market leadership in etch.** Lam holds approximately 50% global market share in etch, a position built over decades of process development with leading chipmakers. Switching costs are enormous -- qualifying a new etch tool for a specific process node takes 12-18 months and billions of R&D investment by the customer.

- **Process-of-record stickiness.** Once Lam's tools are qualified for a specific manufacturing process, they become the "process of record" and are specified into every fab running that process. TSMC's N3/N2, Intel's 18A, and Samsung's advanced nodes all have Lam tools as process-of-record. This locks in revenue for the lifetime of that process node.

- **Advanced packaging first-mover.** Lam has invested heavily in developing equipment specifically for advanced packaging processes (hybrid bonding, through-silicon vias, redistribution layers). This positions it ahead of competitors in a market growing over 40% annually.

- **CSBG moat.** The installed base of over 90,000 tools globally generates recurring service revenue. Customers must use Lam's proprietary spare parts and process kits. This is a high-margin, high-visibility business that grows as the installed base expands.

- **Key competitors.** Applied Materials (AMAT) competes in deposition; Tokyo Electron (TEL) competes in etch and coater/developer. KLA competes in inspection/metrology but not directly with Lam. Lam's differentiation is its pure-play focus on etch and deposition, where process expertise is deepest.

## Risk Factors

1. **China revenue concentration.** China has represented 30-40% of Lam's revenue in recent quarters. US export controls on advanced semiconductor equipment to China could reduce this revenue stream. Further restrictions on trailing-edge tools would be a material headwind.

2. **WFE cyclicality.** The $135B WFE forecast for 2026 assumes continued strong investment. If hyperscaler capex decelerates or memory pricing weakens, WFE could come in below forecast and Lam's revenue would miss expectations.

3. **Tariff cost pass-through.** The March 4 tariff escalation raises costs for materials and components sourced from tariffed countries. Lam may face margin pressure if these costs cannot be passed through to customers.

4. **Inventory pull-forward risk.** The near-term demand spike from tariff-related front-loading could create a demand air pocket in the back half of 2026. Q3 FY2026 guidance is strong, but Q4 could be softer if customers have pulled forward orders.

5. **Valuation premium.** At 43x FY2026 earnings, any growth miss or margin compression would trigger meaningful multiple contraction. The stock declined nearly 12% from its February high, suggesting the market is sensitive to growth expectations.

6. **Competitive share shifts.** Applied Materials and Tokyo Electron are investing aggressively in etch and advanced packaging. While Lam's market position is strong, any share loss in key process steps would be a negative signal.

## Deceleration Triggers

The following signals would cause an exit or significant position reduction -- these are growth-rate-based, not valuation-based:

1. **Revenue YoY growth decelerating below 15% for two consecutive quarters.** The current trajectory is 22-24%. A sustained deceleration below 15% would signal the cyclical recovery is peaking without structural growth taking over.

2. **WFE forecast being revised below $120B for 2026.** Management's $135B outlook is a key pillar of the thesis. A downward revision of more than 10% would indicate demand weakness across the industry.

3. **Gross margin compression below 47%.** The current 49-51% range reflects pricing power and mix. A drop below 47% would signal competitive pressure or unfavorable mix shift.

4. **Advanced packaging growth decelerating below 25%.** The 40%+ growth in advanced packaging is a key differentiator. If this segment slows materially, the structural growth argument weakens.

5. **China revenue declining by more than 30% without offsetting growth elsewhere.** China is a significant revenue contributor. Export control escalation without demand replacement would compress the total growth rate.

6. **CHIPS Act disbursement delays or fab construction pauses.** If TSMC Arizona, Samsung Taylor, or Intel fab projects face significant delays, the domestic demand tailwind weakens.

7. **Any top-3 hyperscaler announcing a material reduction in 2026/2027 AI capex commitments.** Hyperscaler spending drives chip demand, which drives WFE demand. A capex pullback at the hyperscaler level would ripple through the entire chain.
