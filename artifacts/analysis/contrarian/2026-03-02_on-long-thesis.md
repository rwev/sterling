# Contrarian Long Thesis: onsemi (ON)

## Investment Summary

onsemi is a power semiconductor company that the market has left for dead as a broken automotive cyclical. The stock sits at $66, down from $80+ highs, after an 8% single-day plunge on Q4 2025 earnings when Q1 2026 guidance came in 40% below consensus EPS estimates. Revenue has declined 27% over two years from $8.25B (2023) to $6.0B (2025). Sell-side consensus is tepid, with an average price target of $64 -- below the current price.

But the forensic work tells a fundamentally different story. Beneath the ugly GAAP numbers ($0.29 full-year GAAP EPS) sits a company generating $1.4B in free cash flow at a 24% FCF margin -- a record. The balance sheet is a fortress with near-zero net debt. Management is deliberately exiting $300M in non-core, low-margin revenue to reshape the business toward higher-value SiC and GaN power semiconductors. And the NVIDIA 800V DC power delivery collaboration opens a new secular growth vector in AI data center power management that consensus estimates assign essentially zero value to. At 22x consensus 2026 EPS of $3.00, the stock appears fair. But if normalized earnings power is $4.00+ (which the margin trajectory and mix shift support), and if AI power management revenue scales from $250M toward $500M+, the stock re-rates from automotive-cyclical multiples to AI infrastructure multiples. The margin of safety comes from the balance sheet, the FCF generation, and the fact that consensus expectations are already rock-bottom.

## Why the Market Is Wrong

The market is making three interconnected errors:

1. **Pricing onsemi as a structurally impaired automotive cyclical.** The 27% revenue decline from peak ($8.25B to $6.0B) has convinced the market that onsemi's best days are behind it. But this decline is cyclical, not structural. Management stated on the Q4 call that "automotive inventory digestion is largely behind us" and industrial PMI trends point to early expansion. The customer de-stocking that crushed 2024-2025 is a one-time working capital adjustment, not a permanent demand impairment.

2. **Ignoring the GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap that understates true earnings power.** Full-year 2025 GAAP EPS was $0.29 versus non-GAAP EPS of approximately $2.35. The $2.06 gap is primarily driven by restructuring charges and asset impairments related to the deliberate exit of $300M in non-core revenue. This is not earnings manipulation -- it is the financial signature of a company actively reshaping its portfolio toward higher-margin products. The GAAP number makes the income statement look catastrophic; the cash flow statement tells the real story.

3. **Assigning zero value to AI data center power management.** onsemi cleared $250M+ in AI-related revenue in 2025, up from near zero two years ago. The NVIDIA 800V DC collaboration, the EliteSiC platform for solid-state transformers and power supply units, and the Treo platform (design funnel now exceeding $1B) are all real revenue generators that are not in sell-side models at scale. Consensus 2026 EPS of $3.00 does not reflect an AI power management business that could be $500M+ in revenue within 18 months.

## Business Overview

onsemi is a vertically integrated power semiconductor company with three reportable segments: Power Solutions Group (PSG), Advanced Solutions Group (ASG), and Intelligent Sensing Group (ISG). The company is the second-largest silicon carbide (SiC) power device maker globally by revenue (behind STMicroelectronics), with a vertically integrated SiC supply chain including wafer fabrication in Bucheon, South Korea.

End markets: automotive (~55% of revenue), industrial (~30%), and other (~15%). The company is deliberately shifting its revenue mix toward higher-value applications: SiC and GaN power semiconductors for EVs (800V battery systems), AI data center power management (800V DC distribution), and energy infrastructure.

Key competitive moat: onsemi owns its SiC substrate and epitaxial growth capabilities end-to-end, giving it cost and supply chain advantages versus fabless competitors. The EliteSiC M3e platform is designed into 50% of all new EV models showcased at the 2025 Shanghai Auto Show, including Xiaomi's YU7. Revenue from China grew 23% sequentially in Q2 2025 on new EV ramps.

## Earnings Quality & Hidden Value

**The FCF story is the real story.** onsemi generated $1.4B in free cash flow in 2025 on $6.0B in revenue -- a 24% FCF margin that management described as a record. This is extraordinary for a semiconductor company at the trough of its cycle. Cash from operations was $1.8B. The company returned 100% of annual FCF to shareholders through share repurchases and authorized a new $6B buyback program over three years.

**The GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap is legitimate and temporary.** The $2.06 per share delta in FY2025 is driven by restructuring charges, asset impairments, and costs associated with exiting $300M of non-core revenue. These are real cash costs in some cases, but they are non-recurring by nature. As the non-core exits complete (distributed through 2026 with ~$50M in Q1 and higher levels in Q2-Q3), the gap will narrow. The important signal: management is spending money now to fix the revenue mix, which will drive margin expansion in 2027 and beyond.

**Balance sheet is a fortress.** Total debt of $3.3B against cash and short-term investments of $3.0B yields net debt of approximately $300M -- a net debt to equity ratio of just 4%. For context, the $6B share repurchase authorization is larger than the company's total debt. This balance sheet provides substantial downside protection and strategic flexibility.

**Accruals are clean.** FCF of $1.4B versus non-GAAP net income of approximately $990M (est. ~$2.35 x 421M shares) implies FCF conversion well above 100%. This is the hallmark of high earnings quality -- cash flow exceeds reported earnings, meaning the income statement is conservative relative to actual cash generation.

**Hidden value: the Treo platform.** The Treo platform, launched as a "multi-market growth engine," has a design funnel exceeding $1B with the number of products sampling doubling year-over-year. This is a revenue engine that does not appear in current numbers but will begin contributing meaningfully in 2027.

## Margin of Safety

The margin of safety is constructed from three independent pillars:

1. **Balance sheet protection.** Net debt of ~$300M on $6B revenue with $1.4B FCF. Even if revenue declines another 10%, the company can service its obligations, continue buybacks, and invest in growth. There is no balance sheet risk here.

2. **Trough earnings valuation.** At $66, onsemi trades at approximately 28x trough GAAP EPS ($2.35 non-GAAP) and 22x consensus 2026 EPS of $3.00. On normalized mid-cycle earnings of $4.00-4.50 EPS (which the company achieved as recently as 2024 and which the margin mix shift supports), the stock trades at 15-17x -- cheap for a company with secular growth drivers in SiC, AI power, and energy infrastructure.

3. **Sentiment washout.** The stock plunged 8% on February 9 when Q1 2026 guidance came in dramatically below consensus. Analysts have pulled targets below the current price ($59.81 average on one survey). Short interest is elevated. This is classic capitulation -- when the last bull has sold, the incremental seller is exhausted.

## Bear Case Rebuttal

**Bear: "The automotive downcycle has further to run."**
Rebuttal: Management stated automotive inventory digestion is "largely behind us." China EV ramps are accelerating -- onsemi products are designed into 50% of new EV models at the 2025 Shanghai Auto Show. China revenue grew 23% sequentially in Q2 2025. Even if North American and European automotive remains sluggish, China is now the marginal driver of SiC demand, and China is recovering.

**Bear: "The NVIDIA 800V partnership is not exclusive -- 14+ suppliers are listed."**
Rebuttal: Correct. This is not an exclusive deal, and investors should not treat it as one. However, onsemi's competitive advantage is its vertical integration in SiC and its breadth of power management solutions (solid-state transformers, PSUs, DC distribution, and core power delivery). Most of the 14 listed partners are point-solution providers. onsemi and Infineon are the only two with end-to-end capability across the full 800V DC architecture. The revenue opportunity does not require exclusivity -- the TAM is large enough that even a 15-20% share of the AI data center power management market represents $1B+ in revenue at scale.

**Bear: "ROIC is below WACC -- the company is destroying value."**
Rebuttal: ROIC at ~8.9% is below WACC of ~14.6% on trailing numbers, but this is a trough-cycle measurement. ROIC peaked at 21.3% in 2022 and was 19.0% in 2023. The denominator (invested capital) has expanded due to the SiC capacity buildout and the ongoing vertical integration investments. As revenue recovers and the high-margin SiC/GaN/AI products scale, ROIC will inflect back above cost of capital. This is the classic pattern for a cyclical company investing through the trough.

**Bear: "Q1 2026 guidance was catastrophic -- 40% below consensus EPS."**
Rebuttal: The Q1 guidance miss was driven by the $300M non-core revenue exits (with ~$50M hitting Q1) and continued weakness in European automotive/industrial. This was a kitchen-sink quarter -- management reset expectations to a floor they can beat. The distribution of non-core exits is weighted toward Q2-Q3, meaning Q1 is not the worst quarter for this headwind. More importantly, Q1 is seasonally weak for onsemi, and the guidance miss triggered the capitulation selling that creates the entry opportunity.

## Risk Factors

1. **Automotive downcycle extends into H2 2026.** If European and North American EV demand weakens further, revenue recovery is delayed and the thesis timeline extends.

2. **AI data center power management fails to scale.** If hyperscalers adopt alternative architectures or competing suppliers capture disproportionate share, the AI re-rating does not materialize.

3. **SiC pricing pressure from Chinese competitors.** SICC, TanKeBlue, and other Chinese SiC substrate manufacturers are scaling rapidly. If pricing deteriorates faster than onsemi's cost reductions, gross margins compress.

4. **Tariff escalation.** onsemi has significant Asian manufacturing exposure (South Korea SiC fab, global supply chain). The March 4, 2026 tariff implementations could create margin pressure.

5. **Macro recession.** A broader economic downturn would extend the automotive/industrial trough and delay recovery.
