# Contrarian Long Thesis: Barrick Mining (B)

## Investment Summary

Barrick Mining trades at $45.17, down 13% from its 52-week high of $54.69 in a week where gold recovered to $5,400/oz and the macro regime shifted decisively toward stagflation. The market is punishing Barrick for a minor production guidance reduction (2.90-3.25M oz vs. 3.26M oz in FY2025) and a Newmont JV dispute over the Nevada Gold Mines spinoff, while ignoring the fact that Barrick is generating $3,800+/oz margins at current gold prices, sits in a net cash position of $2.0B, and trades at 4.3x FY27E EV/EBITDA -- a 22% discount to peers. With gold at $5,400 and the most supportive macro regime for precious metals since 2022, this is a classic case of short-term noise obscuring a structurally improving earnings trajectory.

## Why the Market Is Wrong

The market is treating Barrick as though three temporary headwinds are permanent impairments:

1. **Production guidance decline is immaterial at these gold prices.** Barrick guided 2026 production at 2.90-3.25M oz vs. 3.26M oz produced in FY2025. This 0-11% volume decline is overwhelmed by margin expansion: at $5,400 gold with AISC of $1,760-1,950/oz, Barrick generates $3,450-3,640/oz in margin vs. approximately $3,150/oz at Q4 2025 realized prices. On 3.0M oz at $3,500/oz margin, that is $10.5B in gold-level operating cash flow before copper contribution. The market is pricing a volume headwind while ignoring a margin windfall.

2. **The Newmont JV dispute is a negotiating tactic, not a structural risk.** Newmont issued a default notice in February over alleged resource diversion from Nevada Gold Mines to Fourmile. This is a contractual dispute between joint venture partners, not a threat to Barrick's operating assets. Both parties have economic incentives to resolve it: Newmont needs Barrick's operating capability, and Barrick needs Newmont's consent for the North American IPO. The dispute will end in either renegotiation or arbitration, not asset destruction.

3. **The gold price pullback this week is noise, not trend.** Gold fell from $5,400 to below $5,100 on dollar strength -- Barrick dropped 13% in sympathy. But the structural drivers of gold (stagflation, trapped Fed, central bank buying, geopolitical risk) have all strengthened this week, not weakened. The NFP miss of -92K and Trump's unconditional surrender demand on Iran both reinforce the gold bull case. The dollar strength is a short-term positioning move, not a fundamental regime change.

The consensus bear case -- that Barrick is a lower-quality miner with execution risk and JV complications -- was correct at $20 gold miner prices. It is deeply mispriced at $5,400 gold with $2.0B net cash, $3.87B in annual FCF, and a pipeline that includes one of this century's most significant gold discoveries (Fourmile).

## Business Overview

Barrick Mining is the world's second-largest gold producer and a major copper producer, operating 13 mines across 18 countries. The company produced 3.26M oz of gold and 220K tonnes of copper in FY2025. Key assets include the Nevada Gold Mines JV (61.5% Barrick / 38.5% Newmont), Pueblo Viejo (Dominican Republic), Kibali (DRC), Loulo-Gounkoto (Mali), and North Mara/Bulyanhulu (Tanzania). The wholly owned Fourmile project in Nevada is an emerging world-class deposit with 15.6M oz in indicated+inferred resources grading 15-17 g/t.

The company renamed itself from Barrick Gold to Barrick Mining in May 2025 (ticker changed from GOLD to B) to reflect its growing copper business, which contributed approximately 20% of FY2025 revenue. Copper production of 220K tonnes at $2.14/lb C1 cash costs provides meaningful diversification and additional margin at current copper prices above $5.00/lb.

CEO Mark Bristow has led a disciplined capital allocation strategy since 2019: paying down debt, selling non-core assets ($2.6B in 2025), and investing in organic growth through Fourmile and Reko Diq (Pakistan copper-gold). The North American IPO (targeting late 2026) would separate the high-quality Nevada/Pueblo Viejo assets into a standalone vehicle, potentially unlocking significant value currently trapped in the conglomerate discount.

## Earnings Quality & Hidden Value

**GAAP/Non-GAAP Convergence:** Barrick's FY2025 GAAP EPS of $2.93 equals adjusted EPS of $2.93, a rare alignment for a mining company. This convergence indicates clean earnings quality with no significant impairments, restructuring charges, or non-cash write-downs masking the underlying business. In prior years (FY2023-2024), the gap was minimal ($0.04-0.06), confirming consistent accounting quality.

**FCF Conversion:** Free cash flow of $3.87B on EBITDA of approximately $9.5B represents a conversion rate of approximately 41%. This is strong for a mining company with active growth capex (Fourmile, Reko Diq). At maintenance capex levels, FCF conversion would exceed 55%. The FY2025 FCF yield at current market cap ($75.8B) is 5.1%, rising to 7-8% at $5,400 gold with 2026 production.

**Hidden Balance Sheet Strength:** Barrick ended FY2025 with $6.71B in cash vs. $4.70B in debt, a $2.0B net cash position. This is a 65% increase in cash year-over-year. No major debt maturities until 2033. The balance sheet optionality is enormous: Barrick can fund Fourmile development, increase dividends (currently $0.85/share, 1.87% yield), and execute the North American IPO without external financing.

**Fourmile -- The Hidden Asset:** The market is not adequately valuing Fourmile, which holds 4.6M indicated oz at 17.59 g/t and 13M inferred oz at 16.9 g/t. For context, grades above 10 g/t are exceptional in modern gold mining. Barrick's PEA projects 600-750K oz/year of annual production from Fourmile, with operating cash flows 70% higher than the already world-class Goldrush project. At $5,400 gold with sub-$1,000/oz AISC (expected for this grade), Fourmile alone could generate $2.5-3.0B in annual operating cash flow once in production. This is a transformative asset that the market is giving essentially zero value to in the current stock price.

**Copper Optionality:** Barrick's copper business (220K tonnes at $2.14/lb C1) is generating substantial margins with copper above $5.00/lb. The Reko Diq project in Pakistan will add 200K+ tonnes of annual copper production when it reaches full capacity. Copper provides a structural hedge against gold price volatility and exposure to the electrification supercycle.

## Bear Case Rebuttal

**"Production is declining."** Barrick's 2026 guidance of 2.90-3.25M oz is a modest decline from 3.26M oz in FY2025, and the midpoint (3.075M oz) is within 6% of FY2025. More importantly, Fourmile (600-750K oz/year once in production) and Reko Diq copper growth will drive volume recovery in 2028+. At $5,400 gold, each ounce is worth $3,500+ in margin -- a 6% volume decline is more than offset by a 40% margin expansion.

**"The Newmont JV dispute could derail the North American IPO."** Newmont and Barrick have been JV partners in Nevada since 2019. The current dispute is over resource allocation, not fundamental asset ownership. Both parties benefit from the JV continuing to operate. The IPO may be delayed but is unlikely to be canceled -- the economic incentives for separation are too compelling. Even if the IPO never happens, Barrick retains full ownership of its JV interest and Fourmile.

**"AISC guidance of $1,760-1,950 shows cost inflation."** The 2026 AISC guidance is based on a $4,500 gold price assumption, not current $5,400 gold. Some AISC components (royalties, production taxes) scale with gold price, mechanically increasing reported AISC at higher gold prices without reflecting actual cost deterioration. Cash costs remain well-controlled at approximately $1,050-1,100/oz.

**"Gold could crash if there's a ceasefire."** A rapid ceasefire is a genuine risk (10-15% probability per our macro outlook). However, gold's structural drivers extend far beyond the Iran conflict: central bank buying (1,000+ tonnes/year for three consecutive years), de-dollarization, negative real rates in a stagflation environment, and fiscal deficit monetization. Gold was at $2,000 before any of the current geopolitical tensions. A ceasefire might take gold to $4,500-4,800, where Barrick still generates $2,800-3,200/oz margins and $2.50+ EPS.

## Risk Factors

1. **Gold price decline.** A rapid decline to $4,000 or below would compress margins and EPS, though the net cash balance sheet and low AISC provide a wide buffer. A ceasefire and dollar surge could trigger a 15-25% gold correction.

2. **Geopolitical risk to mining operations.** Barrick operates in jurisdictions including DRC, Mali, Tanzania, and Pakistan. Resource nationalism, regulatory changes, or political instability could disrupt production. The North American IPO partially addresses this risk by separating lower-risk assets.

3. **Newmont dispute escalation.** If the JV dispute deteriorates into protracted litigation, it could impair operations at Nevada Gold Mines and delay or prevent the IPO. Worst case: forced buyout of Newmont's 38.5% stake at an unfavorable price.

4. **Cost inflation.** Rising energy costs ($91 Brent) and labor costs could push AISC above the guided $1,760-1,950/oz range. However, Barrick's Nevada and Canadian operations are powered by grid electricity and natural gas, not diesel, limiting direct oil exposure.

5. **Copper price decline.** A global recession could reduce copper demand and prices, offsetting some of the gold margin expansion. Copper contributes approximately 20% of revenue.
