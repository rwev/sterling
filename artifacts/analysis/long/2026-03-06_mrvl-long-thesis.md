# Long Thesis: Marvell Technology (MRVL)

## Investment Summary

Marvell Technology reported record Q4 FY2026 revenue of $2.22 billion and full-year revenue of $8.20 billion (+42% YoY), driven by a data center segment that surpassed $6 billion and now represents 74% of quarterly revenue. The company's custom AI ASIC business has scaled from near-zero to a $1.5 billion annual run-rate in a single fiscal year, co-architecting chips for Amazon (Trainium) and Microsoft (Maia). Management guided FY2027 revenue above $11 billion (+30% YoY) and FY2028 revenue approaching $15 billion (~40% growth), with non-GAAP EPS "well over $5" in FY2028. At $89.57, the stock trades at approximately 31.5x FY2027 consensus non-GAAP EPS of $2.84 but compresses to roughly 17-18x FY2028 earnings power -- an attractive multiple for a company growing revenue 30-40% annually with expanding margins. The consensus analyst target is $116.34 (+30% upside) with J.P. Morgan at $135 and Evercore at $156. For Sterling, MRVL diversifies the existing AVGO position across a different hyperscaler customer set (Amazon/Microsoft vs. Google/Meta/OpenAI). The primary risk is customer concentration: a single customer (widely believed to be AWS) reportedly accounts for approximately 76% of data center revenue. This concentration, combined with Sterling's existing 8% AVGO and 8% INTC positions, requires careful sizing.

## Investment Case

### 1. Custom AI ASIC Business Is the Fastest-Growing Segment in Semiconductors

Marvell's custom silicon business has gone from concept to $1.5 billion annual run-rate in approximately 18 months. The company co-designs application-specific AI accelerators for hyperscalers who want alternatives to NVIDIA's general-purpose GPUs. Amazon's Trainium (generations 2 and 3) and Microsoft's Maia accelerator are the anchor programs. Marvell provides the chip architecture, silicon design, and packaging expertise; the hyperscaler provides the AI workload specifications and software stack.

The economics are compelling for both parties. Hyperscalers get chips optimized for their specific inference and training workloads at lower power consumption and cost per transaction than general-purpose GPUs. Marvell gets multi-year design wins with high switching costs -- once a hyperscaler commits to a custom ASIC architecture, it takes 2-3 years and hundreds of millions of dollars to switch designers. Each new chip generation (process node migration from 5nm to 3nm to 2nm) deepens the relationship.

Management expects FY2028 revenue approaching $15 billion, roughly $2 billion above what analysts had previously modeled. This implies the custom ASIC ramp is accelerating beyond initial expectations. The company has secured 2nm process design wins, positioning it for the next generation of AI silicon shipping in 2028-2029.

### 2. Optical Interconnect Leadership Provides a Second Growth Vector

Marvell is not purely a custom ASIC story. The company is a leader in PAM4 DSPs (digital signal processors) for high-speed optical interconnects, enabling 1.6 Tbps data transmission speeds in AI data center clusters. As AI training clusters scale from thousands to tens of thousands of GPUs/ASICs, the bandwidth requirements between chips grow exponentially. Marvell's optical interconnect products are essential infrastructure for these clusters.

This business is synergistic with the custom ASIC work: Marvell designs both the custom chip and the interconnect technology that links those chips together. The total addressable market for AI data center networking is estimated at $25-30 billion by 2028, and Marvell is positioned as a leading supplier.

### 3. Revenue Diversification Beyond Data Center Provides a Floor

While data center represents 74% of Q4 revenue, Marvell operates across four additional segments: enterprise networking, carrier infrastructure, consumer, and automotive/industrial. These segments generated approximately $570 million in Q4 FY2026 revenue. While not high-growth, they provide a revenue floor and diversification. Enterprise networking in particular is showing stabilization after inventory corrections in FY2024-2025.

### 4. Margin Expansion Ahead as Revenue Mix Shifts Toward Data Center

Non-GAAP gross margin was 59.0% in Q4 FY2026, with non-GAAP operating margin at 35.7%. As the revenue mix continues shifting toward the higher-margin data center segment (custom ASICs and optical interconnects carry strong margins), operating margins should expand further. Management's FY2028 guidance of EPS "well over $5" on approximately $15 billion in revenue implies operating margins approaching 38-40%, a meaningful step-up from current levels.

GAAP gross margin was 51.7% in Q4, reflecting stock-based compensation and amortization of acquired intangibles. The GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap is approximately 7 percentage points at the gross margin level, driven primarily by acquisition-related amortization from the Inphi and Cavium acquisitions. This is standard for the semiconductor industry and is amortizing over time.

### 5. Post-Earnings Pullback Creates Better Entry Than the March 6 Spike

The stock surged 18.35% on March 6 to $89.57, but this followed a decline from the 52-week high of $102.77. At $89.57, the stock remains 13% below its 52-week high and 23% below the consensus analyst target of $116.34. The broader market selloff (S&P 500 at 6,740, VIX at 25.26) is creating an entry environment where AI infrastructure names are available at a discount to where they would trade in a risk-on market. If the macro deterioration continues (NFP -92K, Brent $92+), there may be further pullback opportunities into the $75-85 range.

## Business Overview

Marvell Technology, Inc. is a fabless semiconductor company headquartered in Wilmington, Delaware, designing and selling integrated circuits for data infrastructure. The company operates across five reportable segments:

- **Data Center (74% of Q4 revenue):** Custom AI ASICs, electro-optics (PAM4 DSPs), networking switches, storage controllers, and security processors for hyperscale and enterprise data centers. Key customers include Amazon (Trainium), Microsoft (Maia), and major cloud providers.
- **Enterprise Networking (12%):** Ethernet switches, PHYs, and embedded processors for campus and enterprise networks.
- **Carrier Infrastructure (7%):** 5G base station processors, transport, and switching for telecom operators.
- **Consumer (4%):** Storage controllers for consumer SSDs and gaming consoles.
- **Automotive/Industrial (3%):** Ethernet and storage for automotive and industrial applications.

Marvell is fabless, outsourcing manufacturing to TSMC, Samsung, and GlobalFoundries. The company employs approximately 8,000 engineers globally. Fiscal year ends on the Saturday closest to January 31.

## Earnings Quality

- **GAAP vs. Adjusted divergence:** Non-GAAP EPS of $2.84 in FY2026 versus GAAP EPS of $3.07. Unusually, GAAP EPS exceeds non-GAAP EPS in FY2026 due to a large tax benefit and gains. The primary non-GAAP adjustments are stock-based compensation ($1.2B+ annually) and amortization of acquired intangibles (~$600M annually). SBC at approximately 15% of revenue is elevated but declining as a percentage as revenue scales. The amortization is non-cash and relates to the Inphi and Cavium acquisitions completed in 2020 and 2018 respectively.
- **FCF Conversion:** Free cash flow of $1.6B on $8.2B revenue implies a 19.5% FCF margin. This is reasonable for a fabless semiconductor company with modest capex requirements ($310M). The gap between non-GAAP net income ($2.47B) and FCF ($1.6B) is primarily SBC and working capital.
- **Accruals and Working Capital:** Inventory levels require monitoring as the company ramps production for multiple hyperscaler programs simultaneously. No red flags in current working capital trends.
- **Balance Sheet Durability:** Net debt of $2.1B on $2.9B non-GAAP EBITDA is comfortable at 0.7x leverage. No near-term refinancing risk. The company has investment-grade credit (BBB-).
- **Conservative Accounting:** Marvell uses standard semiconductor industry accounting. Revenue recognition is straightforward (product shipment). The large intangible asset base ($15B+ from acquisitions) is being amortized on schedule and does not represent a financial risk.

## Risk Factors

1. **Customer Concentration (PRIMARY RISK).** A single hyperscaler customer (widely understood to be AWS) reportedly accounts for approximately 76% of Marvell's data center revenue. If AWS delays, cancels, or shifts its next-generation Trainium design to a competitor, MRVL's revenue trajectory would be severely impaired. Benchmark analyst moved to Hold on reports that Amazon was evaluating Alchip for Trainium 3 and 4 designs. This is the single most important risk to monitor.

2. **Semiconductor Concentration in Sterling Portfolio.** AVGO (8%) + INTC (8%) = 16% semiconductor exposure. Adding MRVL at 3-4% pushes total semiconductor weight to 19-20%. The IC must evaluate whether this concentration is acceptable given the correlation risk during a tech selloff. Mitigant: MRVL's customer set (Amazon/Microsoft) is different from AVGO's (Google/Meta/OpenAI), providing some diversification within the custom ASIC theme.

3. **Hyperscaler Capex Cycle Risk.** The entire thesis depends on AI capex exceeding $430B in 2026 and growing further in 2027-2028. If the NFP -92K print is the beginning of a genuine recession, hyperscalers could moderate capex plans. History shows Google and Meta cut capex aggressively in 2022-23 before re-accelerating. A capex pause would hit MRVL's revenue trajectory.

4. **Valuation Premium to Historical Range.** At 31.5x trailing non-GAAP EPS, the stock is not cheap on current earnings. If the revenue ramp disappoints even modestly (FY2027 at $10B instead of $11B), the multiple compression could be severe. The stock has a beta of 1.99, making it highly sensitive to broad market moves.

5. **GAAP Profitability Masked by SBC.** Stock-based compensation of approximately $1.2B annually (15% of revenue) dilutes shareholders. While SBC is declining as a percentage of revenue, it remains elevated. GAAP EPS and non-GAAP EPS convergence depends on SBC growing slower than revenue.

6. **Optical Interconnect Competition.** Broadcom, Cisco, and others compete in the data center networking space. While Marvell has a technology lead in PAM4 DSPs, the competitive landscape is intensifying as AI networking becomes a larger market.
