# Long Thesis: Teck Resources (TECK)

## Investment Summary

Teck Resources offers a differentiated entry into the copper supercycle through the combination of a standalone copper-zinc producer trading at 4.5-5.5x forward EV/EBITDA and a merger-of-equals with Anglo American that creates a top-five global copper producer with 1.2 million tonnes of annual output, $800M in corporate synergies, and $1.4B in Chilean asset optimization EBITDA. At $50.54, the stock has pulled back 19% from its February 25 all-time high of $62.41, creating an entry point where downside is partially protected by the merger-implied value of approximately $55 per share while upside is leveraged to copper price appreciation and merger synergy realization. The Q4 2025 earnings beat was decisive -- adjusted EPS of $1.37 versus the $0.63 consensus estimate, with EBITDA up 81% year-over-year -- and QB production of 55,400 tonnes in Q4 was the strongest quarterly performance of 2025. However, this is not a clean quality name: Quebrada Blanca's TMF bottleneck has forced a meaningful production guidance cut for 2026 (200-235kt versus original 280-310kt guidance), trailing ROIC is below cost of capital, and free cash flow was negative in 2025. The thesis requires a longer holding period and tolerance for operational complexity that FCX does not demand. This is a copper exposure diversifier alongside the existing FCX thesis, not a replacement for it.

## Investment Case

### 1. Anglo American Merger Creates a Global Copper Champion with Defined Synergy Value

Teck and Anglo American announced their merger of equals on September 9, 2025. Shareholders of both companies voted overwhelmingly in favor (99.7% of Teck Class A, 99.17% of Anglo) on December 9, 2025. The Canadian government granted Investment Canada Act approval on December 15, 2025. Teck's CEO stated at the BMO conference in late February 2026 that only Chinese regulatory approval remains outstanding among the key jurisdictions, with nothing causing management to change their 12-18 month closing timeline.

The combined entity, Anglo Teck, will be:
- A top-five global copper producer with 1.2 million tonnes of 2026 copper output, growing to 1.35 million tonnes by 2027
- Headquartered in Vancouver with primary listing in London and secondary listings in Toronto and Johannesburg
- More than 70% exposed to copper, the highest among diversified miners
- Expected to deliver $800M in annual corporate synergies (80% realized by end of year two)
- Expected to unlock $1.4B in additional annual EBITDA from optimizing adjacent Chilean assets (Collahuasi and Quebrada Blanca are geographically proximate in northern Chile) between 2030 and 2049

The exchange ratio is 1.3301 Anglo American ordinary shares per Teck share. Anglo American will pay a $4.5B special dividend ($4.19 per share) to its existing shareholders prior to closing, creating an efficient opening balance sheet. Post-dividend, the implied TECK value through the merger is approximately $55 per share based on Anglo's March 6 closing price of 3,387p and GBP/USD of 1.34. This represents approximately 8% upside from $50.54 on the merger spread alone, with additional upside from copper price appreciation and synergy realization post-close.

Teck shareholders will own 37.6% of Anglo Teck. The combined entity's copper production of 1.2-1.35M tonnes per year, at current copper prices of $5.84/lb, implies copper revenue alone of approximately $15-17B annually.

### 2. Q4 2025 Demonstrated Earnings Power at Current Copper Prices

Teck's Q4 2025 results, reported February 19, 2026, were a step-function improvement:

- Revenue: C$3.06B (up 10% QoQ, up from C$2.79B in Q4 2024)
- Adjusted EBITDA: C$1.51B (up 81% YoY, up C$678M from Q4 2024)
- Adjusted EPS: C$1.37 (118% above the C$0.63 consensus estimate)
- Full-year 2025 adjusted EPS: C$3.10
- Operating cash flow: C$1.3B in Q4 alone
- Liquidity: C$9.3B as of February 18, 2026 (C$5.2B cash)

The beat was driven by copper averaging US$5.03/lb in Q4 (closing the year at US$5.67/lb) and QB achieving its best quarterly production of 55,400 tonnes. The full-year EBITDA margin of 37.2% reflects the operating leverage inherent in copper mining at elevated prices.

### 3. 2026 Standalone Earnings Power Supports the Valuation

Teck's 2026 guidance provides visibility into standalone earnings power:

- Copper production: 455,000-530,000 tonnes (all operations, including QB at 200-235kt and HVC, Carmen de Andacollo, and Antamina contributions)
- Copper unit costs: US$1.85-2.20/lb (improved from 2025's $2.67/lb, reflecting higher QB volumes and cost optimization)
- Zinc production: 410,000-460,000 tonnes
- Zinc unit costs: US$0.65-0.75/lb

At the midpoint of production guidance (492kt copper) and copper at $5.50/lb (a conservative assumption below current spot):
- Copper margin: $3.43/lb (at $2.07/lb midpoint costs) x 492kt x 2,204.6 lbs/tonne = approximately $3.72B copper gross profit
- Zinc contribution: approximately $1.0-1.3B gross profit
- Less: depreciation, SG&A, corporate = approximately $1.5-2.0B
- Estimated 2026 EBITDA: approximately $4.5-5.5B (USD)

With an enterprise value of approximately $24.6B (market cap $24.7B, roughly net cash), the forward EV/EBITDA is 4.5-5.5x. This is cheap relative to FCX at 7.5-8.0x forward, the metals/mining peer median of 9.6x, and Teck's own 5-year average of approximately 6-7x.

### 4. Copper Price Sensitivity Provides Asymmetric Leverage

Like FCX, Teck's earnings are highly leveraged to copper prices. At 490kt of production, each $0.25/lb increase in copper adds approximately $270M to annual EBITDA. If copper averages $6.00/lb in 2026 (current spot is $5.84):
- Copper margin: $3.93/lb x 490kt x 2,204.6 = approximately $4.25B copper gross profit
- Estimated EBITDA: $5.5-6.5B
- EV/EBITDA: 3.8-4.5x -- extremely cheap

The downside at $4.50/lb copper:
- Copper margin: $2.43/lb x 490kt x 2,204.6 = approximately $2.62B copper gross profit
- Estimated EBITDA: $3.0-3.5B
- EV/EBITDA: 7.0-8.2x -- still below peer median

The asymmetry is favorable: at $6.00 copper, TECK trades at sub-4.5x EBITDA; at $4.50 copper, it trades at 7-8x. The downside multiple is still reasonable, while the upside multiple is deeply discounted.

### 5. Highland Valley Copper Mine Life Extension Secures Long-Duration Production

In Q3 2025, Teck announced the go-ahead for the Highland Valley Copper (HVC) Mine Life Extension project in British Columbia, extending the mine's life by approximately 20 years. This secures domestic Canadian copper production through the 2040s, complementing QB's Chilean production with a politically stable jurisdiction.

HVC is a mature, well-understood asset with lower execution risk than QB. The extension adds long-duration copper reserves at a time when new mine development timelines have stretched to 17+ years globally.

## Business Overview

Teck Resources is a Canadian diversified mining company headquartered in Vancouver, BC. Following the sale of its steelmaking coal business to Glencore in 2023-2024 for approximately $9B, Teck has repositioned as a copper-focused miner.

**Copper Operations:**
- **Quebrada Blanca (Chile, 60% owned):** The cornerstone growth asset. QB2 commenced commercial production in 2024. 2025 production of approximately 180,000 tonnes was below guidance due to TMF constraints. 2026 guidance: 200,000-235,000 tonnes. The TMF bottleneck (ultra-fines drainage in tailings) is expected to be resolved by 2027.
- **Highland Valley Copper (Canada, 100%):** Long-running open-pit mine in British Columbia. Mine life extension approved, extending operations through the 2040s.
- **Carmen de Andacollo (Chile, 90%):** Smaller copper mine contributing approximately 40,000-50,000 tonnes annually.
- **Antamina (Peru, 22.5%):** Equity interest in one of the world's largest copper-zinc mines.

**Zinc Operations:**
- **Red Dog (Alaska, 100%):** One of the world's largest zinc mines. 2026 guidance: 410,000-460,000 tonnes of zinc.
- **Trail Operations (Canada, 100%):** Zinc and lead smelting and refining complex in BC.

**Revenue Mix (2025):** Approximately 60% copper, 25% zinc, 15% other metals and by-products.

## Earnings Quality

- **GAAP vs. Adjusted divergence:** Minimal. The FY2025 GAAP/Adjusted EPS gap (C$2.83 vs. C$3.10) is attributable to non-cash items and financial instrument mark-to-market, not operating adjustments. Teck does not engage in heavy addback accounting.
- **FCF Conversion:** This is the primary quality concern. FCF was negative $583M on approximately $2.9B in operating EBITDA (continuing ops basis), reflecting $2.06B in capex. The capital intensity is temporary -- QB2 growth capex and TMF remediation will decline as production normalizes toward nameplate. By 2027-2028, when QB2 is operating at 280-310kt annually and TMF capex subsides, FCF conversion should normalize to 30-40% of EBITDA. Until then, this is a capital-consuming investment phase.
- **Accruals and Working Capital:** Working capital was a modest user in 2025. No red flags in inventory or receivables trends.
- **Balance Sheet Durability:** Net cash position with C$9.3B in total liquidity. The debt/capitalization ratio is 1% versus a 60% covenant threshold. This is a fortress balance sheet that can absorb a prolonged copper downturn. Total debt of C$4.9B is well-laddered with no near-term maturities of concern.
- **Conservative Accounting:** Teck uses standard IFRS mining accounting. The coal business divestiture to Glencore was cleanly executed. QB2 is consolidated at 60% ownership, with the 40% minority interest held by Sumitomo. No off-balance-sheet concerns.

## Risk Factors

1. **Quebrada Blanca TMF Execution:** The tailings management facility bottleneck has caused repeated production guidance cuts. The 2026 guidance of 200-235kt is 28% below original estimates. If the TMF ultra-fines drainage issue proves more persistent than expected, QB may not reach nameplate capacity until 2028-2029 rather than 2027. Each 10kt shortfall at QB reduces annual EBITDA by approximately $100M at current copper prices.

2. **Chinese Regulatory Approval:** The Anglo Teck merger requires Chinese antitrust clearance. Given the combined entity's control of significant Chilean copper assets (Collahuasi, QB, Los Bronces, Quellaveco) and China's strategic interest in securing copper supply, there is non-trivial risk that Beijing demands conditions (offtake agreements, pricing commitments) or delays approval. The current US-China trade tensions add geopolitical complexity. Management states nothing causes them to change their view on the 12-18 month timeline, but Chinese regulatory processes are opaque.

3. **Copper Price Cyclicality:** TECK's earnings are highly leveraged to copper. A US recession (NFP -92K, rising VIX) could drive copper below $5/lb in the near term, compressing earnings significantly. While the structural deficit thesis supports medium-term copper prices, the cyclical risk in the next 6-12 months is real. At $4.50/lb copper, 2026 EBITDA would decline approximately $1.5B from the $5.50 base case.

4. **Merger Integration Risk:** Combining two large mining companies across 4 stock exchanges, 9 regulatory jurisdictions, and diverse geographic operations (Chile, Canada, South Africa, Peru, Brazil, Australia) is complex. The $800M synergy target requires flawless execution on corporate cost reduction, procurement consolidation, and operational optimization. History shows mining mergers frequently underdeliver on synergies.

5. **ROIC Below Cost of Capital:** Trailing ROIC of approximately 4% versus an estimated WACC of 13.5% means the company is currently destroying economic value. The thesis requires ROIC to improve materially as QB2 ramps and growth capex declines. If the production ramp stalls, the capital destruction persists.

6. **Negative Free Cash Flow:** FCF was -$583M in 2025. While this reflects investment-phase spending, it means Teck cannot self-fund dividends and buybacks from organic cash flow. The company is relying on its cash reserves and coal sale proceeds. If the QB2 ramp takes longer than expected, the cash cushion erodes.

7. **Political Risk in Chile:** QB and Carmen de Andacollo are in Chile, which has imposed higher mining royalties under the 2024 fiscal reform. While Teck has navigated Chilean politics for decades, additional fiscal changes (higher royalties, environmental restrictions) could compress margins.

## Entry Parameters

- **Entry range:** $46 - $52 | **Target:** $57 | **Stop:** $39

The entry range reflects the current pullback from the $62.41 all-time high. The lower bound of $46 represents approximately 6x 2026 EBITDA at the low end of estimates and is also near the 200-day moving average support level. The upper bound of $52 provides modest upside to the merger-implied value of $55.

The target of $57 (13% upside from midpoint) reflects a blended probability-weighted valuation incorporating merger close, synergy realization, and copper price appreciation. This is below the consensus analyst target of $76.40 (which uses standalone methodology at higher multiples) because I apply a merger discount for the special dividend extraction and integration risk.

The stop at $39 (20% below entry midpoint) corresponds to either: (a) copper falling below $4.50/lb, which would impair 2026 earnings significantly; (b) a material Grasberg-style operational incident at QB; or (c) Chinese regulatory rejection of the merger. At $39, TECK would trade at approximately 7x trough EBITDA, which is where I would reassess the entire thesis.

**Risk/Reward:** From the entry midpoint of $49, upside to $57 is $8 (16% gain) versus downside to $39 of $10 (20% loss). The R/R ratio is approximately 0.8:1. This is below my preferred 2:1 threshold, and is the primary reason this is a moderate-conviction thesis rather than a high-conviction one. The R/R improves significantly if the entry is closer to $46 (R/R of 1.6:1).

**Comparison to FCX:** FCX offers a cleaner financial profile (positive FCF, higher ROIC, simpler structure, direct Section 232 benefit), better R/R (entry $55-62, target $76, stop $45 = approximately 1.5:1), and lower complexity risk. For a single copper position, FCX is superior. TECK is additive only if the PM wants diversified copper exposure across two names or wants the merger optionality.
