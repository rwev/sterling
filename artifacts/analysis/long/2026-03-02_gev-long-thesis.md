# Long Thesis: GE Vernova (GEV)

## Investment Summary

GE Vernova is the dominant Western gas turbine manufacturer at a moment when gas-fired generation is the only technology that can bridge the widening gap between AI data center power demand and available grid capacity. The company's gas turbine production is sold out through 2029, its total backlog stands at $150 billion, and Q4 2025 orders surged 65% year-over-year to $22.2 billion. Management has raised 2026 guidance to $44-45 billion in revenue with 11-13% adjusted EBITDA margins and $5.0-5.5 billion in free cash flow -- and the 2028 outlook calls for $56 billion in revenue at 20% EBITDA margins with cumulative FCF of at least $24 billion. At $884 per share ($238 billion market cap), GEV trades at approximately 43x 2026E FCF, which is optically expensive but materially underestimates the earnings trajectory through 2028. This is a structural monopoly with multi-year pricing power in a supply-constrained market. We see a path to $1,050-1,100 over 12-18 months as the margin expansion story plays out.

## Business Overview

GE Vernova was spun out of General Electric in April 2024 as a pure-play energy technology company. It operates across three segments:

- **Power** (~$21B FY2025 revenue): Gas turbines (heavy-duty and aeroderivative), steam turbines, nuclear services, and power conversion. This is the franchise -- GE's installed base of gas turbines is the largest in the world, creating a services annuity that generates high-margin recurring revenue. FY2025 Power segment EBITDA margins expanded to approximately 15%, with 2026 guidance of 16-18%.

- **Electrification** (~$9B FY2025 revenue, growing to $13.5-14.0B in 2026 with Prolec GE): Grid solutions, power conversion, solar and storage, and now Prolec GE (transformers). The Prolec GE acquisition, expected to close February 2, 2026, adds approximately $3 billion in transformer revenue to this segment. 2026 segment EBITDA margin guided at 17-19%. This segment directly benefits from the $228 billion annual utility capex cycle.

- **Wind** (~$8B FY2025 revenue): Onshore and offshore wind turbines. This is the challenged segment -- revenue is guided down low-double digits in 2026 with approximately $400 million of EBITDA losses. Wind is the drag on consolidated margins, but losses are narrowing and management has a credible path to breakeven by 2027-2028.

The combined company employs roughly 80,000 people across 100+ countries. Headquarters moved to Cambridge, Massachusetts in 2025.

## Investment Case

### 1. Gas Turbine Monopoly in a Supply-Constrained Market

The power supply deficit is the binding constraint on the AI buildout. US data center power demand is projected to reach 74 GW by 2028 against a 49 GW shortfall (Morgan Stanley). Gas-fired generation is the only technology that can be deployed at scale within a 2-4 year timeframe -- nuclear restarts take 3-5 years, renewables are stuck in interconnection queues (2,600 GW backlog, 4-7 year wait times), and SMRs are late-decade at the earliest.

GE Vernova controls approximately 40% of the global heavy-duty gas turbine market. Its HA-class turbines are the most efficient in the world (>64% combined-cycle efficiency). Production slots are sold out through 2029. This is not a market where customers can switch suppliers -- turbine specifications are locked into engineering designs years before installation, and qualification processes for alternative suppliers take 12-18 months minimum.

The Chevron-GE Vernova partnership targeting 4 GW of behind-the-meter data center power by 2027 is a template. Hyperscalers are building their own gas plants because the grid cannot serve them fast enough, and GE Vernova is the equipment supplier they must use.

### 2. Extraordinary Backlog Visibility

- FY2025 total backlog: $150 billion (up $31.2 billion year-over-year)
- FY2025 orders: $59.3 billion (up 34% organically)
- Q4 2025 orders: $22.2 billion (up 65% YoY; equipment orders up 91%)
- Equipment margin in backlog grew by $8 billion with 6 points of accretion

This backlog represents roughly 4 years of revenue at current run rates. More importantly, the margin quality of the backlog is improving -- new orders are being booked at higher prices than the existing backlog, which means reported margins will expand mechanically as older, lower-priced orders roll off and are replaced by higher-priced new orders. Management's 2028 target of 20% consolidated EBITDA margins (versus 8.4% in FY2025) is primarily driven by this backlog price/mix improvement.

### 3. Prolec GE Transforms the Electrification Segment

The Prolec GE acquisition adds approximately $3 billion in transformer revenue to the Electrification segment. Large power transformers have lead times of 12-24 months, and demand from data center construction and grid modernization far exceeds supply. Prolec GE is one of the largest transformer manufacturers in the Americas, giving GE Vernova a vertically integrated position across turbines, grid equipment, and transformers -- the three physical layers of the power infrastructure buildout.

### 4. Margin Expansion Is the Core Earnings Driver

The financial trajectory from FY2025 to 2028 is defined by margin expansion:

| Metric | FY2025 | 2026E | 2028E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $38.1B | $44-45B | $56B |
| Adj. EBITDA Margin | 8.4% | 11-13% | 20% |
| Adj. EBITDA | $3.2B | $4.8-5.9B | $11.2B |
| Free Cash Flow | $3.7B | $5.0-5.5B | ~$8B+ |

The margin expansion is driven by: (a) higher-priced orders in the Power segment flowing through, (b) Wind segment losses narrowing toward breakeven, (c) Electrification margin accretion from Prolec GE and data center demand, and (d) operating leverage on a largely fixed cost base. This is not aspirational -- it is backlog math.

### 5. Capital Return Acceleration

GE Vernova doubled its quarterly dividend in January 2026 and increased its share buyback authorization. With $5.0-5.5 billion in 2026 free cash flow and a clean balance sheet, the company has significant capacity for capital returns. Cumulative FCF of at least $24 billion through 2028 suggests buybacks and dividend increases will compound meaningfully.

## Earnings Quality

GE Vernova's earnings quality is improving materially as the company transitions from a GE subsidiary with complex accounting into a standalone entity:

- **FCF conversion**: Free cash flow of $3.7B on $3.2B of EBITDA implies >100% FCF/EBITDA conversion, driven by favorable working capital dynamics (advance payments on long-cycle equipment orders). This is characteristic of businesses with pricing power and long lead times.
- **Backlog margin accretion**: The $8 billion increase in equipment margin within the backlog, with 6 points of accretion, provides hard evidence that future revenue will be more profitable than current revenue. This is visible, auditable, and not dependent on assumptions.
- **Wind segment losses are the primary drag**: The $400M in guided 2026 Wind EBITDA losses depress consolidated margins. As Wind moves toward breakeven, the improvement flows directly to the bottom line without requiring revenue growth.
- **Prolec GE integration**: The $3B of Prolec GE revenue at Electrification-level margins (17-19%) is accretive to consolidated margins. Integration risk exists but the business is operationally standalone.
- **Conservative guidance pattern**: Management has raised guidance at every reporting period since the spin. The 2026 guidance raise (from $41-42B to $44-45B revenue; from $4.5-5.0B to $5.0-5.5B FCF) followed the Prolec GE close but also reflected underlying demand strength.

For a company with a multi-decade installed base, sold-out production, and structural demand tailwinds, this is reasonable -- and if the margin trajectory is achieved, the stock re-rates higher as 2028 estimates are pulled forward.

Analyst consensus price target: $758 (23 analysts, Buy consensus). The consensus appears to embed more conservative margin assumptions than management guidance. Our target assumes management delivers on the guided trajectory.

## Risk Factors

1. **Wind segment losses persist or worsen.** Wind is guided for $400M in 2026 EBITDA losses. If offshore wind project execution deteriorates further, or if onshore wind demand weakens, losses could exceed guidance and delay the path to consolidated 20% margins. Mitigation: Wind is already a known problem and is being managed down; management has reduced the offshore wind order book and is focusing on selective onshore opportunities.

2. **Valuation compression in a risk-off environment.** At $884 and 43x 2026E FCF, GEV is vulnerable to multiple compression if broader equity markets sell off (tariff escalation, Iran crisis, recession fears). The stock has risen from $252 52-week low to $884 -- a 3.5x move -- and could give back 20-30% in a market correction. Mitigation: the backlog provides fundamental support; any pullback to the $700-750 range would be an aggressive buying opportunity.

3. **Gas turbine demand cyclicality.** While current demand is structurally driven by AI power needs, a sharp deceleration in hyperscaler capex (from the current 62% growth rate) could reduce urgency for new gas generation capacity. Mitigation: turbines are sold out through 2029; even if new orders slow, revenue and margin visibility extends for years.

4. **Prolec GE integration risk.** The $3B transformer business must be integrated into the Electrification segment. Execution risk exists around systems integration, customer retention, and cost synergies. Mitigation: Prolec GE was already a 50/50 JV with GE, so operational familiarity is high.

5. **Regulatory and environmental risk.** Gas-fired generation faces long-term regulatory headwinds from carbon reduction mandates. EPA methane rules and potential carbon pricing could increase operating costs for gas plant operators (GEV's customers). Mitigation: Gas is the consensus "bridge fuel" for the next decade; CCS-ready designs provide an upgrade path; nuclear and renewables services provide diversification.

6. **March 4 tariff interaction.** Tariffs on Canadian/Mexican steel and Chinese components could raise input costs for turbine and transformer manufacturing. Mitigation: GE Vernova's supply chain is heavily US-based; Prolec GE's Mexican operations may face some cost headwinds but the transformer market has pricing power to pass through.

## Monitoring Triggers

- **Q1 2026 earnings (April-May)**: Orders, backlog, and margin trends. Any sequential margin improvement in Power above 16% confirms the trajectory. Wind segment losses narrowing faster than guided would be a positive catalyst.
- **Prolec GE integration update**: First quarter as a consolidated subsidiary. Revenue run-rate and margin contribution versus guidance.
- **Hyperscaler capex guidance (Q1 2026 earnings, April-July)**: Any revision to Amazon, Google, Meta, or Microsoft capex plans directly impacts the demand environment for gas turbines and grid equipment.
- **Gas turbine order intake**: Continued order growth confirms structural demand. Any cancellations or deferrals are early warning signs.
- **Wind segment losses**: Track quarterly EBITDA losses versus the $400M annual guidance. Worse-than-expected losses delay the margin convergence story.
- **FERC interconnection rule (April 30 deadline)**: Faster interconnection processing could reduce urgency for behind-the-meter gas generation (modest negative); continued delays reinforce the scarcity thesis (positive).
- **Natural gas prices**: Sustained gas prices above $4/MMBtu support demand for new efficient gas generation; prices above $6 could slow some projects at the margin.
