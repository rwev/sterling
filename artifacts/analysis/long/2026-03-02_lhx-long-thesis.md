# Long Thesis: L3Harris Technologies (LHX)

## Investment Summary

L3Harris Technologies is the best-positioned large-cap defense prime for the autonomous warfare cycle, trading at a discount to peers on forward EV/EBITDA despite superior organic growth, margin expansion, and a transformative catalyst in the Missile Solutions IPO. The US-Iran conflict validates the autonomous systems thesis in real time — drones, counter-UAS, and precision munitions are consuming inventory and accelerating procurement. With a 1.3x book-to-bill, 7% organic revenue growth guidance for 2026, expanding margins into the low-16% range, and $3B in guided free cash flow, LHX offers a compelling combination of defense-grade earnings visibility and catalyst-driven upside. The Missile Solutions spin-off with $1B Pentagon equity investment unlocks hidden value. Entry at $365-380, target $450.

## Business Overview

L3Harris Technologies is the sixth-largest US defense contractor by revenue ($21.9B in 2025), formed from the 2019 merger of L3 Technologies and Harris Corporation, and bolstered by the 2023 acquisition of Aerojet Rocketdyne for $4.7B.

The company operates across four segments:

- **Space & Airborne Systems** (~30% of revenue): Satellites, electronic warfare, ISR platforms, classified programs. Key programs include the F-35 electronic warfare suite and classified space systems.
- **Integrated Mission Systems** (~25%): Maritime, land, and air C4ISR platforms. Includes the Global Hawk unmanned aircraft systems.
- **Communication Systems** (~20%): Tactical radios, night vision systems, broadband communications. Recently won $466M night vision contract.
- **Aerojet Rocketdyne** (~20%): Solid rocket motors, liquid propulsion, hypersonic propulsion. Being spun off as "Missile Solutions" with Pentagon backing.

L3Harris's competitive moat is its position as the integrator of sensors, communications, and autonomous systems — the connective tissue between platforms. This role becomes more valuable as warfare shifts toward networked autonomous systems.

## Investment Case

### 1. Defense Budget Tailwind With Autonomous Systems Accelerant

The FY2026 DoD budget is $839B with $13.4B directed to autonomous and unmanned systems. The Defense Innovation Unit budget jumped to $2B from $1.3B. The Iran conflict — featuring hundreds of drone attacks and counter-UAS engagements — validates the autonomous warfare thesis and will likely accelerate spending. Congressional appetite for defense is bipartisan, and the reconciliation bill includes $152B in additional Pentagon spending.

L3Harris is a primary beneficiary through its electronic warfare systems, ISR platforms, counter-UAS technology, night vision systems, and munitions propulsion (via Aerojet). The company's 1.3x book-to-bill in 2025 (orders of $27.5B vs. revenue of $21.9B) demonstrates that demand is accelerating ahead of revenue.

### 2. Missile Solutions IPO Unlocks Hidden Value

The planned Missile Solutions spin-off is a first-of-its-kind defense transaction:
- The Pentagon is investing $1B as an anchor investor — a convertible preferred security that converts to common equity at IPO (H2 2026).
- The business generated $3.6-3.8B in revenue in 2025 with ~7,000 employees.
- Proceeds will fund tripling of rocket motor production capacity by 2030.
- L3Harris retains majority ownership and control.

At a conservative 15x EV/EBITDA for the spun-off entity (defense primes trade at 17-20x), and assuming 15% EBITDA margins on $3.7B revenue ($555M EBITDA), the Missile Solutions entity could be valued at $8-10B. L3Harris's majority stake would represent $5-7B in value, or $27-37 per LHX share — value not currently reflected in the stock price.

### 3. Margin Expansion and Cost Synergies

L3Harris has executed aggressively on cost reduction since the Aerojet acquisition, raising its cost savings target to $1.2B by end of 2025 (a year ahead of schedule). Segment operating margins expanded 40bps to 15.8% in 2025, with 2026 guidance for the low-16% range. This trajectory continues:
- Aerojet integration synergies still flowing through
- LHX Next operating model driving efficiency
- Revenue growth providing operating leverage
- Mix shift toward higher-margin services and software

### 4. Valuation Discount to Peers

At a forward P/E of ~30x on 2026E EPS of $12.59 and EV/EBITDA of ~17x, L3Harris trades at a discount to the defense prime peer median of 33.6x P/E and 20-22x EV/EBITDA. This discount exists because of the higher leverage from the Aerojet acquisition (net debt/EBITDA of 3.0x, down from 4.0x). As deleveraging continues (targeting below 2.5x by 2027), the discount should narrow, providing multiple expansion on top of earnings growth.

## Earnings Quality

- **FCF conversion**: Adjusted FCF of $2.8B vs. non-GAAP net income of approximately $2.0B represents strong >100% FCF conversion, supported by advance payments on long-cycle defense contracts and disciplined working capital management.
- **Deleveraging**: Net debt/EBITDA reduced from 4.0x post-Aerojet to 3.0x in just two years, entirely from organic cash generation — no equity dilution.
- **Cost savings verification**: The $1.2B cost savings target (achieved ahead of schedule) is verifiable through margin progression, not reliant on non-recurring items.
- **Book-to-bill**: 1.3x for FY2025 means the order pipeline is growing faster than revenue, providing forward visibility and reducing earnings risk.
- **Insider activity**: One insider sold 5,528 shares at $370 in early March — modest and likely programmatic, not a conviction signal.

## Risk Factors

1. **Leverage**: Net debt/EBITDA of 3.0x is elevated for a defense prime. Rising rates increase interest expense and constrain financial flexibility.
2. **Missile Solutions IPO timing**: Market conditions or regulatory delays could push the IPO past H2 2026, delaying the catalyst.
3. **Iran conflict resolution**: A rapid de-escalation could remove the near-term defense spending urgency, though structural budget growth remains intact.
4. **Program execution**: Large classified programs carry execution risk. Any cost overruns on fixed-price contracts would compress margins.
5. **Revenue growth deceleration**: Organic growth of 5% in 2025 (2.8% reported) was below guidance expectations. If 2026 growth misses 7%, the multiple expansion thesis weakens.
6. **ROIC**: At 5.6%, ROIC remains below cost of capital for some investors. The Aerojet goodwill weighs on capital efficiency metrics.
