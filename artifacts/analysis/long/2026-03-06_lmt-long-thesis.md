# Long Thesis: Lockheed Martin (LMT)

## Investment Summary

Lockheed Martin is the world's largest defense contractor with a $194B record backlog, direct operational involvement in the Iran conflict (F-35s flying combat missions, Tomahawk and JASSM cruise missiles being expended, PAC-3 and THAAD interceptors in active use), and a multi-year structural rearmament cycle catalyzed by Trump's proposed defense budget increase to $1.5 trillion by 2027. The stock has already rallied 38% YTD to $665, but the fundamental case extends beyond the war premium: FY 2026 guidance calls for 5% revenue growth, 25%+ segment operating profit growth, and $6.5-6.8B in free cash flow, with meaningful upside from supplemental appropriations to replenish depleted munition stockpiles. At 22x forward earnings with $29.58 in FCF per share, LMT offers a rare combination of defensive cash flow characteristics and event-driven upside. The primary risk is that analysts have not yet caught up to the stock -- the consensus target of $576-660 is below the current price, reflecting pre-war estimates that are now stale.

## Investment Case

**1. The Iran conflict is consuming munitions at an extraordinary rate, creating a multi-year replacement cycle.** Operation Epic Fury has expended hundreds of Tomahawk cruise missiles ($2M each), JASSMs ($1.5M each), and precision-guided munitions since March 2. The Pentagon has already signed a $122.6M contract addition with Lockheed to ramp JASSM production. THAAD interceptor production is being scaled from 96/year to 400/year. PAC-3 MSE production set 620-unit records in 2025 and will scale further. These are not one-time orders -- they represent a structural increase in the production rate that will persist for 3-5 years as the US replenishes stockpiles while maintaining operational readiness.

**2. Supplemental defense appropriations are highly probable and would flow disproportionately to LMT.** Breaking Defense reported on March 3 that lawmakers are actively weighing supplemental defense funding to replenish expended munitions. Defense executives met at the White House the same week. Trump's proposed $1.5T defense budget by 2027 (vs. $901B in FY 2026) would represent a 76% increase, with Lockheed positioned to capture the largest share given its F-35, missile defense, and precision munitions programs. The question is not whether supplemental funding will come, but how much and how fast.

**3. The $194B backlog provides extraordinary revenue visibility.** At $75B in annual revenue, the backlog represents 2.6 years of revenue coverage -- the highest in Lockheed's history. The FY 2025 book-to-bill ratio of 1.2x means orders are still growing faster than revenue, and this was before the Iran conflict created incremental demand. Key programs in the backlog: F-35 (191 deliveries in 2025, record), PAC-3 MSE, THAAD, CH-53K (largest-ever Rotary & Mission Systems contract), and hypersonic weapons development.

**4. Defense is the ultimate stagflation hedge.** In a macro environment where NFP is -92K, oil is $91, and the Fed is trapped, defense revenue is entirely non-cyclical. LMT's customers are governments with sovereign spending authority. Defense spending increases during wars and geopolitical crises, which is precisely the environment we are in. On March 6, LMT gained 1.6% while the S&P 500 fell 1.0% and financials dropped 3%+. This negative correlation with the economic cycle is extremely valuable for portfolio construction.

## Business Overview

Lockheed Martin Corporation ($153.1B market cap) is the world's largest defense contractor by revenue ($75.0B in FY 2025). The company operates four business segments:

- **Aeronautics** (~40% of revenue): F-35 Lightning II (world's largest fighter program), F-16, C-130J, classified programs. 191 F-35s delivered in 2025, a record. Production targeting 156 deliveries in 2026 per the current sustainment plan.
- **Missiles and Fire Control** (~18% of revenue): PAC-3 MSE interceptors, THAAD, Javelin, Hellfire, JASSM, and hypersonic weapons development. This segment has the most direct near-term upside from Iran-related munition replenishment.
- **Rotary and Mission Systems** (~19% of revenue): Sikorsky helicopters (Black Hawk, CH-53K), ship combat systems (Aegis), C4ISR, and training systems. CH-53K received the largest-ever contract award for this segment.
- **Space** (~23% of revenue): Space launch, military satellites, missile defense sensors, Orion spacecraft. Structural growth from missile defense architecture and Space Force demand.

Key competitive advantages: (1) F-35 is a monopoly platform with 3,500+ aircraft in the global order book and 20+ operator nations; (2) PAC-3 and THAAD are the gold-standard missile defense systems with no viable substitutes; (3) 70%+ of revenue comes from the US government, providing maximum payment certainty; (4) high barriers to entry across all segments due to classified technology and decades of development.

## Earnings Quality

**GAAP vs. Adjusted divergence**: LMT does not heavily adjust its GAAP numbers. The primary reconciling items are pension-related adjustments and occasional contract charges. FY 2024-2025 EPS was depressed by identifiable, non-recurring F-35 TR-3 charges. The company's segment operating profit metric is a clean measure of underlying business performance and grew 11% in FY 2025.

**FCF conversion**: FCF/Net Income conversion was 138% in FY 2025, which is excellent. Defense contractors typically generate FCF above net income due to customer advances (milestone payments received before revenue recognition). This is a structural feature of the business model, not an anomaly.

**Contract accounting**: LMT uses percentage-of-completion accounting, which introduces estimation risk on long-duration contracts. The F-35 TR-3 charges in FY 2024 are a reminder that contract estimates can be wrong. However, the $194B backlog represents primarily mature, well-understood programs where estimation risk is lower.

**Capital intensity**: Capex of $1.65B in FY 2025 is modest relative to the $75B revenue base (2.2% capex intensity). Defense manufacturing is not capital-intensive compared to energy or industrials. This supports the strong FCF conversion.

The valuation is the most challenging aspect of this thesis. LMT is trading at the upper end of both its historical range and peer comparisons. The trailing P/E of 30.9x is 50-60% above its 5-year average. The stock's 38% YTD rally has priced in a significant war premium.

**However**, the forward P/E of 22.3x is more reasonable when considering: (1) FY 2026 segment operating profit growth of 25%+, (2) the probability of supplemental appropriations not yet in estimates, and (3) the structural rearmament cycle (NATO 3% GDP targets, $1.5T US defense budget proposal) that extends revenue visibility beyond what a single-year P/E captures.

**Price target methodology**: FY 2026 EPS consensus of $29.81, growing to $32.36 in FY 2027. Applying a 22-24x multiple (reflecting the elevated geopolitical risk environment and structural growth outlook) to FY 2027 EPS yields $712-776, discounted back at 8% gives $660-720. The high analyst target is $740. I set the target at $740, representing the upper end of the range achievable if supplemental appropriations materialize.

## Catalysts

- **March-April 2026: Supplemental defense appropriations.** Lawmakers are actively discussing funding to replenish expended munitions. A $20-30B supplemental bill would flow disproportionately to LMT (missiles, interceptors, fighter parts). This is the most immediate upside catalyst.
- **April 21: Q1 2026 earnings.** First quarter reflecting Iran conflict orders. Watch for backlog additions, new contract awards, and updated full-year guidance. Any guidance raise on supplemental funding visibility would be a positive catalyst.
- **H2 2026: FY 2027 defense budget negotiations.** Trump's $1.5T proposal enters Congressional debate. Even if the final number is $1.0-1.2T, it represents a massive increase from $901B and would support LMT's long-term growth outlook.
- **Ongoing: NATO rearmament cycle.** European allies increasing defense spending to 3% GDP. Germany, Poland, and others are placing large orders for F-35s, PAC-3 systems, and other LMT platforms. International revenue is the fastest-growing segment.
- **Ongoing: Munition production rate increases.** THAAD from 96 to 400/year, JASSM acceleration, PAC-3 MSE scaling. Each rate increase represents recurring, high-margin revenue for multiple years.

## Risk Factors

1. **Valuation overshoot already priced.** The stock has rallied 38% YTD and trades above the consensus analyst target. If the Iran conflict de-escalates or a ceasefire emerges, the war premium could unwind by 10-15%, taking the stock to $565-600. This is the near-term risk that must be managed through position sizing and stop discipline.

2. **Fixed-price contract risk.** The F-35 TR-3 charges in FY 2024 demonstrate that long-duration fixed-price contracts can produce negative surprises. LMT has approximately 60% of revenue on cost-plus contracts, but the remaining 40% on fixed-price contracts carries margin risk if costs inflate (supply chain disruptions, labor shortages).

3. **Defense budget uncertainty.** Trump's $1.5T proposal is aspirational and requires Congressional approval. Political dynamics (budget hawks, DOGE cost-cutting pressure, competing priorities) could result in a much smaller increase. If the FY 2027 budget increase disappoints expectations, the structural growth narrative weakens.

4. **Analyst consensus is "Hold" with downside targets.** The median analyst target of $576-660 implies that most analysts believe the stock is fairly valued or overvalued at $665. Analyst upgrades need to follow the stock higher for it to sustain this level. If the consensus does not catch up, the stock could stagnate.

5. **Sector rotation risk.** If the macro environment shifts from stagflation to recession, even defense stocks could de-rate as investors liquidate equities broadly. Defense is macro-insulated on the revenue side, but not immune to multiple compression in a severe risk-off event.
