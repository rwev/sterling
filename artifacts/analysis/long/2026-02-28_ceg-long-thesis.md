# Long Thesis: Constellation Energy (CEG)

## Investment Summary

Constellation Energy is America's largest electricity producer following its January 2026 completion of the $16.4B Calpine acquisition, which combined the nation's largest nuclear fleet with Calpine's natural gas and geothermal assets. The stock has pulled back 23% YTD from its October 2025 high of $403 to approximately $288, creating an entry opportunity into a structurally advantaged business at a discount to its long-term earnings power. The Calpine deal is immediately accretive (20%+ to adjusted EPS in 2026) and positions Constellation as the dominant counterparty for hyperscaler power purchase agreements. With 23 analysts covering the stock at a median price target of $406 and a Strong Buy consensus, we see 35-40% upside over 12-18 months as the market re-rates the combined entity.

## Business Overview

Constellation Energy operates the largest fleet of carbon-free electricity generation in the United States, anchored by approximately 21 GW of nuclear capacity across plants in Illinois, Pennsylvania, New York, Maryland, and other states. Following the Calpine acquisition, the combined company operates:

- **Nuclear**: ~21 GW -- the largest nuclear fleet in the U.S., providing baseload, zero-carbon power with 90%+ capacity factors
- **Natural gas**: ~26 GW via Calpine's fleet of efficient combined-cycle and peaking plants across California, Texas, and the Mid-Atlantic
- **Geothermal**: ~725 MW via The Geysers complex in California, the world's largest geothermal facility
- **Retail and commercial**: One of the largest competitive retail electricity suppliers in the U.S., serving C&I customers

The combined entity generates approximately 175 TWh of electricity annually, making it the single largest power producer in the country. The generation mix is approximately 70% carbon-free (nuclear + geothermal + renewables), which is a meaningful competitive advantage as corporate buyers increasingly mandate clean energy procurement.

## Investment Case

### 1. Calpine Transforms the Growth Profile

The Calpine acquisition, closed January 7, 2026, is a transformational deal:
- **Net purchase price**: $26.6B (including ~$12.7B of assumed Calpine net debt)
- **Acquisition multiple**: 7.9x 2026E EV/EBITDA -- attractive for contracted gas and geothermal assets
- **EPS accretion**: Management expects 20%+ adjusted EPS accretion in 2026, with at least $2/share in subsequent years
- **Synergy opportunity**: Operational and procurement synergies from combining two large generation fleets; cross-selling opportunities in the retail/commercial channel

The deal diversifies Constellation's fuel mix away from nuclear-only dependence, adds dispatchable gas capacity that complements baseload nuclear, and brings geothermal assets that are both carbon-free and non-intermittent.

### 2. Nuclear Fleet Is the Premier AI Power Asset

Constellation's nuclear plants are the most sought-after generation assets in America for hyperscaler power procurement:
- Nuclear provides 24/7 baseload power with zero carbon emissions -- precisely what data centers require
- The company has signed direct PPAs with technology companies, including a 380 MW deal with CyrusOne announced in early 2026
- PJM Interconnection's new "bring-your-own-generation" framework is accelerating bilateral power deals between generators and hyperscalers
- Nuclear capacity is effectively impossible to replicate on the timeline hyperscalers need power (new nuclear takes 10+ years to permit and build)

This scarcity value means Constellation can command premium pricing on PPAs, and the contracting pipeline is growing.

### 3. The 23% YTD Pullback Creates a Valuation Entry

CEG's selloff from $403 to $288 appears driven by:
- Broader rotation out of U.S. equities into international markets (macro, not company-specific)
- Sector-wide multiple compression in power/utility names after a massive 2024-2025 run-up
- Integration uncertainty around the Calpine deal
- General risk-off in the market as tariff uncertainty weighs on sentiment

None of these factors impair the fundamental thesis. The pullback has brought the stock to approximately 27x forward P/E, which is elevated versus traditional utilities but appropriate for a company with 20%+ EPS growth, contracted revenue, and a secular demand tailwind.

### 4. Operating Cash Flow Inflection

FY2025 cash from operations surged to $4.23B from a cash use of $2.46B in FY2024 (which included large working capital swings). Capital expenditures were $2.95B, implying levered FCF of approximately $1.3B in FY2025. With Calpine's cash flows now consolidated and synergies ramping, FCF should expand materially in FY2026 and beyond.

## Earnings Quality

Constellation's adjusted operating earnings exclude mark-to-market gains/losses on commodity hedges, pension adjustments, and merger-related costs. The gap between GAAP EPS ($7.40) and adjusted EPS ($9.39) in FY2025 is primarily driven by non-cash items and is consistent with prior years.

Key quality signals:
- **Cash from operations surged**: $4.2B in FY2025 vs. negative in FY2024, confirming that adjusted earnings are backed by cash
- **Nuclear PTCs**: Constellation benefits from production tax credits under the Inflation Reduction Act, which provide a floor on nuclear economics even in low-price environments. These are real cash benefits, not accounting adjustments
- **Conservative hedging**: The company typically hedges 85-95% of expected generation 12-18 months forward, providing earnings visibility
- **Calpine integration risk**: The primary earnings quality concern is the complexity of integrating a $16.4B acquisition. Execution risk is real but manageable given Constellation's operational track record

## Risk Factors

1. **Integration execution risk**: Calpine is a $16.4B acquisition that adds significant operational complexity. Integration missteps, culture clashes, or delayed synergies could weigh on near-term earnings.
2. **Leverage elevation**: Post-Calpine net debt of approximately $21B represents 3.0-3.5x EBITDA, elevated versus pre-deal levels. The company must deleverage through earnings growth and FCF, which limits capital return flexibility.
3. **Nuclear regulatory risk**: NRC oversight, license renewal timelines, and potential changes to nuclear production tax credits under future administrations.
4. **Power price risk**: While nuclear PTCs provide a floor, a sustained collapse in wholesale electricity prices would reduce merchant revenue and PPA negotiating leverage.
5. **AI demand deceleration**: If hyperscaler capex plans are revised down, the demand growth narrative weakens. However, existing PPAs are binding contracts.
6. **Tariff pass-through**: Higher equipment and maintenance costs from tariffs could increase capex, though this is a second-order effect.
7. **Share dilution**: The Calpine deal included 50 million shares of CEG stock, diluting existing shareholders by approximately 15%.
