# Long Thesis: Constellation Energy (CEG)

## Investment Summary

Constellation Energy is now the largest electricity producer in the United States following the January 2026 completion of its $16.4 billion Calpine acquisition, commanding 55 GW of generation capacity spanning nuclear, natural gas, and geothermal. The company's 33 GW nuclear fleet -- the largest in the nation -- is an irreplaceable asset in a market where 24/7 baseload power is being repriced higher by hyperscaler demand for data center electricity. The Microsoft 20-year PPA for the Crane Clean Energy Center restart, the $1 billion DOE loan guarantee, and immediate 20%+ EPS accretion from Calpine provide a multi-year earnings growth trajectory that the market has not yet fully priced. At $327 per share, CEG trades at approximately 29x forward earnings with a consensus target of $400-$408, offering 22-25% upside. We recommend initiating a position in the $310-$335 range.

## Business Overview

Constellation Energy was spun out of Exelon in February 2022 as a pure-play power generation and energy marketing company. The business operates the nation's largest fleet of nuclear power plants with approximately 33 GW of capacity across 21 reactors at 12 sites. Following the Calpine acquisition completed on January 7, 2026, the combined company added 26 GW of natural gas generation and 1.5 GW of geothermal capacity (The Geysers, the world's largest geothermal complex), creating a 55 GW diversified generation portfolio.

**Generation Mix (Post-Calpine):**
- Nuclear: ~33 GW (60% of capacity) -- 92%+ capacity factor, zero-carbon baseload
- Natural Gas: ~26 GW (47% of capacity) -- dispatchable, gas-fired fleet across key markets
- Geothermal: ~1.5 GW -- The Geysers complex, renewable baseload
- Other (hydro, solar, wind): remainder

**Key Markets:** PJM Interconnection (primary), ERCOT, CAISO, NY-ISO, ISO-NE

**Revenue Streams:** Wholesale power generation, retail electricity sales, energy marketing, capacity payments, and long-term PPAs with hyperscalers and corporates.

The company serves approximately 2 million residential, public sector, and business customers through its retail electricity business, providing a natural demand hedge against wholesale power price volatility.

## Investment Case

### 1. Irreplaceable Nuclear Fleet in a Structurally Short Power Market

The US power market faces a projected 49 GW shortfall by 2028 against data center demand alone (Morgan Stanley Research). The interconnection queue backlog stands at 2,600 GW with median wait times of 5 years and nearly 80% of projects withdrawing due to delays. In this environment, Constellation's 33 GW nuclear fleet is essentially irreplaceable -- no new large-scale nuclear reactor has been completed in the US in decades, and existing plants that were slated for retirement are instead being relicensed and restarted.

Nuclear provides 24/7 baseload power at a 92%+ capacity factor, which is exactly what hyperscaler data centers require (99.999% uptime). No other zero-carbon source can match this reliability profile. This structural advantage is being monetized through premium long-term PPAs.

### 2. Hyperscaler PPA Pipeline Creates Multi-Year Revenue Visibility

The Microsoft 20-year PPA for the Crane Clean Energy Center (Three Mile Island Unit 1) restart is the template for a new class of nuclear-backed corporate power agreements. The Crane restart project is approximately 80% staffed with 500+ employees on-site, ahead of the 2027 target restart date (accelerated from the original 2028 timeline). The $1 billion DOE loan guarantee secured in November 2025 de-risks the $1.6 billion restart cost.

Beyond Microsoft, the broader hyperscaler complex is aggressively seeking contracted nuclear and firm power. Amazon signed a landmark 1.9 GW / $18 billion 17-year PPA with Talen Energy. Meta signed a 1.2 GW agreement with Oklo. This is a secular trend -- every major cloud provider is pursuing firm, zero-carbon power, and Constellation owns the largest addressable supply pool.

### 3. Calpine Acquisition is Immediately Accretive and Strategically Transformative

The Calpine acquisition closed January 7, 2026, for an equity purchase price of $16.4 billion (50 million shares + $4.5 billion cash + assumption of $12.7 billion Calpine net debt). The deal was struck at 7.9x 2026 EV/EBITDA -- a reasonable multiple for a fleet of dispatchable gas-fired generation in a supply-constrained market.

**Financial Impact:**
- Immediate 20%+ adjusted EPS accretion in 2026
- More than $2 billion of incremental annual free cash flow
- At least $2 per share of EPS accretion in future years
- Combined TTM revenue of approximately $23.6 billion

The strategic logic is compelling: Constellation now controls both nuclear baseload AND gas-fired dispatchable generation across key US markets. This diversification reduces merchant risk, provides capacity to serve data center customers who need both baseload and peaking power, and positions the company to bid for integrated power supply contracts that neither a pure nuclear nor pure gas operator could fulfill.

### 4. Favorable Power Market Dynamics

US electricity demand is entering its strongest multi-year growth period since 2000. The EIA forecasts demand rising 1% in 2026 and 3% in 2027, with total consumption reaching 4,239 billion kWh in 2026 (surpassing the 2024 record of 4,082 billion kWh). Three demand drivers are compounding: data center/AI load (75.8 GW in 2026, rising to 134.4 GW by 2030), manufacturing reshoring (ISM Manufacturing PMI at 52.4), and general commercial electrification (2.6% annual growth).

Rising power demand with constrained supply additions (interconnection bottleneck, 80% withdrawal rate) creates a structurally favorable pricing environment for existing generators. Constellation's fleet is fully interconnected, permitted, and operational -- the ultimate incumbency advantage.

## Earnings Quality

Constellation's adjusted operating earnings framework excludes mark-to-market impacts on commodity hedges, unrealized gains/losses on nuclear decommissioning trust funds, and acquisition-related charges. This is standard for the utility/IPP sector and provides a more accurate picture of recurring earnings power.

**Positive Signals:**
- Nuclear fleet capacity factor consistently above 92%, providing stable, predictable generation volumes
- Long-term contracted revenue growing (Microsoft PPA, corporate PPAs) reducing merchant exposure
- Stand-alone adjusted operating EPS range narrowed to $9.05-$9.45 for FY2025, indicating management visibility into earnings trajectory
- Calpine's cash flow contribution ($2B+ annually) is well-established from its history as a standalone company

**Caution Items:**
- GAAP margin compression from 15.9% to 9.1% reflects acquisition accounting and needs monitoring
- Net debt increased substantially with Calpine debt assumption (~$12.7B)
- ROIC of 8% against WACC of ~14.8% is a concern on trailing numbers; however, this should improve materially as Calpine accretion flows through
- Integration execution risk on a $16.4B deal is non-trivial

Constellation trades at a structural premium to traditional utility peers, which is justified by: (1) the irreplaceable nuclear fleet, (2) hyperscaler PPA pipeline providing growth visibility absent from peers, (3) Calpine accretion creating 20%+ earnings growth in year one, and (4) the Crane Clean Energy Center restart providing a 2027 catalyst that no peer possesses.

The consensus price target of $400-$408 (range $277-$481) implies approximately 22-25% upside from current levels. **Bull Case Valuation:** Full Calpine accretion plus Crane restart in 2027 could drive 2028E EPS to $15+ range. At 25x, that implies $375-$400; at 30x (reflecting nuclear scarcity premium), $450-$480.

## Risk Factors

1. **Calpine Integration Execution:** The $16.4B acquisition adds $12.7B in assumed debt and requires operational integration of 26 GW of gas-fired capacity. Any execution stumbles, cost overruns, or synergy shortfalls would compress the accretion thesis.

2. **Power Price Decline:** A severe recession, demand destruction, or rapid supply additions could pressure wholesale power prices. The 29x forward P/E assumes continued favorable power market conditions.

3. **Nuclear Regulatory Risk:** The Crane Clean Energy Center restart requires NRC approval (additional information was submitted January 29, 2026). Regulatory delays or denials would remove a key growth catalyst.

4. **Hyperscaler Capex Deceleration:** If the $690B hyperscaler capex cycle slows, data center power demand forecasts collapse. Constellation's premium valuation is partly predicated on continued data center power procurement.

5. **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** With net debt now approximately $18.6B post-Calpine (including assumed debt), rising interest rates would increase financing costs. The 10-year Treasury at 4.04% is manageable, but a move above 4.50% would pressure valuation multiples.

6. **Natural Gas Price Volatility:** The Calpine fleet's profitability depends on gas-to-power spark spreads. A sustained gas price spike (geopolitical supply disruption) could compress gas generation margins, partially offset by higher power prices.

7. **Retail Customer Attrition:** The competitive retail electricity market could see margin compression if customer switching accelerates.
