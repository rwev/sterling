# Long Thesis: Newmont Corporation (NEM)

## Investment Summary

Newmont is the world's largest gold miner, generating record free cash flow of $7.3 billion in FY2025 on $21.5 billion in revenue. With gold at ~$5,400/oz and Newmont's all-in sustaining cost (AISC) at $1,358/oz, the company is producing cash margins of over $3,400 per ounce -- margins that rival the best technology companies. The stock trades at $116.34, down 9% on March 3 alongside a gold pullback, but remains up ~177% from its 52-week low of $41.93. At a forward P/E of ~12.5x and EV/EBITDA of ~8.5-10x, Newmont is trading at a meaningful discount to its intrinsic value given the structural gold bull market driven by central bank de-dollarization, geopolitical hedging, and fiscal deficit concerns. The company has committed to returning $7.1 billion to shareholders in 2026 through a $6 billion buyback program and $1.1 billion in dividends, while holding a near-zero net debt position.

## Business Overview

Newmont operates a globally diversified portfolio of Tier 1 gold mining assets across six continents. Following the 2023 Newcrest acquisition, Newmont is the undisputed scale leader in gold mining with:

- **Production**: 5.9 million attributable gold ounces in FY2025 (guided 5.3M oz in 2026 -- a trough year due to planned mine sequencing at Boddington and Cadia)
- **Tier 1 Portfolio**: 5.6 million ounces from Tier 1 assets (longest-life, lowest-cost mines including Boddington, Tanami, Ahafo, Cerro Negro, and the recently integrated Cadia and Lihir from the Newcrest acquisition)
- **By-product credits**: Significant copper, silver, and zinc by-product revenue reduces effective gold costs
- **Reserve base**: Largest gold reserve base in the industry, providing 20+ years of mine life across the portfolio

New CEO Natascha Viljoen took the helm on January 1, 2026, marking a leadership transition. The company has been aggressively optimizing the portfolio post-Newcrest, divesting non-core assets and directing capital to the highest-return Tier 1 mines.

## Investment Case

**1. Extraordinary cash generation at current gold prices.** At $5,400 gold and $1,358 AISC, Newmont generates approximately $3,400 in cash margin per ounce. On 5.3 million ounces of 2026 production, this implies roughly $18 billion in gross cash margin before corporate overhead, capex, and other costs. Even after sustaining capex, the company should generate well in excess of $5 billion in free cash flow in 2026 at current gold prices -- and FY2025 already demonstrated $7.3B in FCF. The magnitude of cash generation is unprecedented in Newmont's history.

**2. Structural gold bull market has multiple drivers.** Gold's move from ~$2,700 to $5,400 over the past 12 months is not speculative froth -- it is driven by:
   - Central bank buying as reserve managers diversify away from USD holdings (structural, multi-year)
   - Geopolitical premium from the Iran conflict and Middle East instability
   - US fiscal deficit concerns and long-term inflation hedging
   - Tariff uncertainty creating demand for non-correlated assets
   - Goldman Sachs targets $4,900 (conservative vs. current); Yardeni Research targets $6,000

**3. Massive capital return program.** The $6 billion share repurchase program represents approximately 5% of the current market cap (~$128B pre-pullback). Combined with the $1.1 billion annual dividend ($1.04/share annualized, ~0.9% yield at current price), total shareholder return commitment is $7.1 billion -- roughly the entire FY2025 free cash flow. The buyback is especially powerful at current gold margins: every dollar of repurchase is backed by near-record cash flow.

**4. Near-zero net debt provides resilience and optionality.** Newmont ended FY2025 with $7.6 billion in cash and $11.6 billion in total liquidity, reaching a near-zero net debt position. This balance sheet strength provides resilience if gold corrects and optionality for M&A or additional shareholder returns.

**5. Valuation is undemanding for the cash generation profile.** At a forward P/E of ~12.5x and EV/EBITDA of ~8.5-10x, the market is pricing Newmont as if current gold prices are temporary. If gold sustains above $5,000 for the next 2-3 years (supported by structural drivers), the current valuation implies significant upside.

## Earnings Quality

- **FCF conversion is exceptionally strong.** $7.3B FCF on $13.5B adjusted EBITDA represents 54% conversion. For a capital-intensive mining business, this is outstanding and reflects the benefits of Tier 1 assets with declining sustaining capex requirements post-Newcrest integration.

- **Adjusted vs. GAAP alignment.** FY2025 net income of $7.2B vs. adjusted net income of $7.6B shows minimal divergence, a significant improvement from FY2023 when large impairment charges distorted GAAP results. The cleanup post-Newcrest is largely complete.

- **Working capital contributed positively in Q4.** Management noted favorable working capital dynamics in Q4 2025, contributing to the record $2.8B quarterly FCF.

- **Dividend is well-covered.** The $1.1B annual dividend represents just 15% of FY2025 FCF, leaving substantial room for buybacks and reinvestment.

## Risk Factors

1. **Gold price correction.** Gold has doubled in 12 months. A ceasefire in the Iran conflict, hawkish Fed pivot, or easing trade tensions could trigger profit-taking. A 20% gold pullback to ~$4,300 would compress margins and FCF materially. Counter: Central bank buying is structural and provides a floor. Even at $4,000 gold, Newmont generates strong FCF given $1,358-1,680 AISC.

2. **2026 production trough.** Guided production of 5.3M oz (down from 5.9M) reduces revenue and FCF versus FY2025 at constant prices. Counter: This is planned mine sequencing (Boddington, Cadia), not operational failure. Production is expected to recover in 2027+.

3. **AISC inflation.** Guided AISC of $1,680/oz in 2026 vs. $1,358 in 2025 represents a 24% increase, driven by Australian labor costs and energy. If AISC continues rising, it compresses margins at any gold price. Counter: At $5,400 gold, the margin remains over $3,700/oz -- the AISC increase is immaterial to the cash flow story at these prices.

4. **New CEO transition risk.** Natascha Viljoen took over January 1, 2026. Strategy shifts or execution missteps are possible during leadership transitions. Counter: The board engineered a smooth succession and the capital allocation framework ($6B buyback) was announced alongside the transition, signaling continuity.

5. **Geopolitical risks to operations.** Newmont operates in multiple jurisdictions including Africa, South America, and Australia. Regulatory changes, tax increases, or nationalization threats are perennial risks. Counter: The post-Newcrest portfolio is weighted toward Tier 1 jurisdictions (Australia, Canada, Ghana) with long operating histories.

6. **Analyst downgrade.** NEM fell 8% on March 3 partly due to an analyst downgrade and gold pullback from recent highs. Counter: The downgrade appears driven by near-term gold price mean-reversion concerns rather than fundamental deterioration. The 17 analysts with Buy ratings vs. 2 Sells reflects the broader consensus.
