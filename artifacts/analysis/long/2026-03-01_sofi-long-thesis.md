# Long Thesis: SoFi Technologies (SOFI)

## Investment Summary

SoFi Technologies is a fintech compounder transitioning from a lending-dependent platform to a diversified financial superapp and banking-as-a-service infrastructure provider. The stock has declined 46% from its 52-week high of $32.73 to $17.76, creating an entry opportunity into a business delivering 30%+ revenue growth, nine consecutive quarters of GAAP profitability, and an accelerating shift toward capital-light technology platform revenue. At approximately 30x FY2026E adjusted EPS of $0.60, the valuation is demanding but justified by the growth trajectory, improving margin profile, and optionality in the Galileo/Technisys platform business. We see 55-70% upside to a $27-30 target over 12 months as the market re-rates the business model transformation.

## Business Overview

SoFi Technologies operates a diversified financial services platform structured across three segments:

**Lending (44% of FY2025 revenue):** Personal loans, student loans, and home loans. SoFi originates to a prime/super-prime borrower base (weighted average FICO ~750) and increasingly distributes through its balance sheet funded by low-cost deposits. Q4 2025 total originations reached a record $10.5B (+46% YoY). Personal loan charge-offs improved to 2.60% from 2.83%, and 90-day delinquencies held at 43 bps.

**Financial Services (growing segment):** SoFi Money (checking/savings), SoFi Invest (brokerage), SoFi Credit Card, and insurance. This segment drives member acquisition and cross-sell. The 40% cross-buy rate among existing members demonstrates genuine platform stickiness. Deposits now fund 74% of total assets, with $11.5B in deposit growth during 2025.

**Technology Platform (Galileo/Technisys):** Banking-as-a-service infrastructure powering 160M+ enabled accounts for third-party clients including Robinhood, Chime, and MoneyLion. This is the "AWS of Fintech" vision -- capital-light, recurring, and high-margin. Q3 2025 tech platform revenue was $114.6M. Management targets Galileo as a $1B revenue engine by 2026. New partnerships with Wyndham Hotels and Southwest Airlines demonstrate expansion beyond traditional fintech clients.

Capital-light revenue (Technology Platform + Financial Services) now comprises 56% of total revenue, fundamentally changing the business risk profile from a cyclical lender to a platform business with lending attached.

**Members:** 13.7M as of Q4 2025, growing 35% YoY, with a record 1M members added in Q4 alone. Total products reached 14.8M with 1.6M added in Q4.

## Investment Case

**1. Business model transformation is underappreciated.** The market still prices SoFi as a consumer lender, applying a lending multiple to a business that derives 56% of revenue from capital-light sources. As the technology platform scales toward $1B in revenue, the blended margin profile and revenue quality improve structurally. This is the core mispricing.

**2. Deposit-funded lending creates a structural cost advantage.** SoFi Bank's charter allows it to fund loans with deposits rather than warehouse lines, reducing cost of funds by 200-300 bps versus non-bank fintech peers. Deposits grew $11.5B in 2025 and now fund 74% of assets. This is a durable competitive moat that most fintech competitors cannot replicate.

**3. Member growth flywheel is self-reinforcing.** SoFi's land-and-expand strategy -- acquire members through one product (often checking), then cross-sell lending, investing, and credit products -- drives a 40% cross-buy rate. Each incremental product deepens engagement and reduces churn. At 13.7M members growing 35%, the addressable cross-sell opportunity is enormous.

**4. Guidance credibility is high.** Management has beaten or met quarterly guidance in every quarter since the bank charter was granted. FY2026 guidance of $4.655B revenue, $1.6B EBITDA (34% margin), and $0.60 adjusted EPS represents achievable 30% growth from a proven trajectory. The company has consistently under-promised and over-delivered.

**5. Pullback creates entry opportunity.** The 46% decline from the 52-week high reflects broad fintech de-rating, macro fears, and some profit-taking after a strong 2025. Fundamentals have not deteriorated -- Q4 2025 was the strongest quarter in company history. The disconnect between improving fundamentals and declining price is the opportunity.

## Earnings Quality

**GAAP vs. Adjusted divergence is narrowing.** The gap between GAAP and adjusted earnings has compressed significantly as stock-based compensation moderates relative to revenue scale. FY2025 GAAP EPS of $0.39 matched adjusted EPS of $0.39 (excluding the FY2024 deferred tax benefit), indicating improving earnings quality.

**Deposit funding improves NIM transparency.** The shift from warehouse-funded to deposit-funded lending makes the net interest margin more visible and stable. SoFi's deposit base ($11.5B added in 2025) provides a reliable, low-cost funding source that is less susceptible to capital markets disruptions.

**Credit quality is holding.** Personal loan charge-offs at 2.60% (improving from 2.83%) and 90-day delinquencies at 43 bps reflect the prime borrower base. However, the sale of $360M in delinquent personal loans in 2025 did mask some underlying credit pressure -- this warrants monitoring. The weighted average FICO of ~750 provides a buffer, but consumer credit is inherently cyclical.

**Tangible book value is compounding.** TBVPS grew from $3.35 (end-FY2023) to $7.29 (Q3 2025), with management guiding $2.5B in tangible book value growth for FY2025 alone. This is a concrete measure of intrinsic value creation that anchors downside.

The valuation framework depends critically on which peer set the market uses. Priced as a bank, SOFI is expensive. Priced as a high-growth fintech platform with 30%+ revenue growth and improving margins, it is at the low end of the comparable range. As capital-light revenue approaches 60%+ of total, we expect the market to increasingly apply platform multiples rather than lending multiples. ## Risk Factors

**1. Consumer credit deterioration.** A recession would increase charge-offs and provisions, compressing margins and potentially forcing SoFi to curtail originations. The prime borrower base provides a buffer, but personal loans are unsecured and would face stress in a severe downturn. The sale of delinquent loans to manage reported charge-offs adds opacity.

**2. Interest rate sensitivity.** SoFi benefits from lower rates (more refinancing volume, lower funding costs) and is hurt by higher rates (compressed origination demand, deposit competition). The current macro environment of sticky inflation and elevated rates pressures the lending segment.

**3. Regulatory risk.** As a bank holding company, SoFi faces evolving capital requirements, consumer protection scrutiny, and potential student loan policy changes. The bank charter is a competitive advantage but also subjects SoFi to more stringent oversight.

**4. Technology platform execution risk.** The $1B Galileo revenue target is ambitious. Losing a major client or failing to win enterprise accounts outside fintech would slow the platform transformation narrative and multiple re-rating.

**5. Valuation compression risk.** At 30x forward earnings, any deceleration in growth or miss on guidance would trigger significant multiple compression. The stock has demonstrated -46% drawdown capacity within the past year.

**6. Competitive intensity.** Traditional banks (JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America) are investing aggressively in digital banking. Fintech peers (Robinhood, Chime, Cash App) compete for the same millennial/Gen-Z customer base. SoFi's differentiation through full-stack financial services and the bank charter must be continuously defended.

## Entry Parameters

- Entry range: $16.50 - $18.50
- Target: $27.00 (12-month) / $30.00 (18-month)
- Stop: $13.50

The entry range captures the current pullback zone and allows for further weakness from macro headwinds. The $13.50 stop represents approximately 1.85x tangible book value, which we view as a hard floor absent a credit event. The $27 target implies ~35x FY2026E adj EPS and is consistent with the analyst consensus mean. The $30 target assumes further multiple expansion as the platform revenue mix shift becomes undeniable.

Position sizing should reflect the binary nature of the credit cycle risk. We recommend a starter position at the current $17.76 level with room to add at $16.50.

## Monitoring Triggers

- **Q1 2026 earnings (expected April 2026):** Member growth trajectory, credit quality metrics (charge-offs, delinquencies), technology platform revenue growth, and full-year guidance reaffirmation are all critical. Any downward revision to the $4.655B revenue or $0.60 EPS guide would be a negative signal.
- **Personal loan charge-off rate:** If charge-offs exceed 3.5% or 90-day delinquencies break above 60 bps, credit quality concerns would warrant reducing position size.
- **Galileo/Technisys client wins:** New enterprise partnerships outside traditional fintech validate the platform thesis and support multiple re-rating. Conversely, client losses would undermine it.
- **Fed rate trajectory:** Rate cuts would be a tailwind for both lending volume (refi activity) and deposit cost optimization. Prolonged higher-for-longer rates pressure origination volumes.
- **Deposit growth pace:** Deposit growth decelerating below $2B/quarter would signal competitive pressure on the funding advantage.
- **Macro consumer credit indicators:** Broader consumer credit deterioration (rising unemployment, declining consumer confidence, increasing industry-wide charge-offs) would be a leading indicator of stress for SoFi's lending book.
