## Investment Summary

MasTec offers the best risk/reward in the U.S. electrical infrastructure services sector for investors seeking exposure to the grid modernization and data center buildout super-cycle. The company has the strongest backlog momentum in the group (+33% YoY to $19.0B with a 1.6x book-to-bill in Q4), the clearest margin expansion trajectory (guiding from 8.0% to 8.5% EBITDA margin in 2026, targeting 100+ bps annually thereafter), and the most diversified end-market exposure spanning clean energy, pipeline, power delivery, and telecommunications.

At ~$298, the stock trades at ~35x forward EPS on 2026 guided earnings of $8.40. This multiple is elevated on an absolute basis but understates the earnings growth trajectory: consensus 2027 EPS of ~$11+ would bring the forward multiple to ~27x at current prices. MasTec is a margin expansion story -- the company is converting a massive backlog into revenue while simultaneously improving project execution and operating leverage. If margins reach 9-9.5% by 2027-2028 (consistent with management targets and peer levels at EMCOR), the earnings power is substantially higher than the market is pricing.

## Business Overview

MasTec is one of the largest specialty infrastructure contractors in North America, operating across five segments:

| Segment | FY2025 Revenue | % of Total | Key End Markets |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clean Energy & Infrastructure (CE&I) | ~$5.4B | ~38% | Solar, wind, battery storage, hydrogen |
| Oil & Gas Pipeline | ~$3.5B | ~24% | Natural gas pipelines, LNG facilities |
| Power Delivery | ~$2.8B | ~20% | Transmission & distribution, grid hardening |
| Communications | ~$1.8B | ~13% | Fiber, 5G, broadband (BEAD program) |
| Other / Corporate | ~$0.8B | ~5% | Data center construction (new), industrial |

This diversification is a strategic advantage. Unlike Comfort Systems (45% data center) or pure-play electrical contractors, MasTec benefits from multiple independent demand drivers. If data center capex slows, pipeline and clean energy pick up. If clean energy policy support weakens, grid modernization and communications fill the gap.

## The Fundamental Case

### 1. Backlog Momentum Is the Best in the Sector

MasTec's 18-month backlog reached a record $19.0B, up $4.7B or 33% year-over-year. The Q4 book-to-bill was 1.6x, meaning the company is booking new work 60% faster than it is burning existing backlog. Key backlog details:

- **CE&I segment**: Largest contributor to backlog growth. Solar, battery storage, and grid-scale renewable projects are extending in both size and duration.
- **Data center construction**: MasTec won its first turnkey data center construction management contract in Q4, adding nearly $1B in data center-related awards. This is a new end market for MasTec and represents a meaningful growth vector.
- **Power Delivery**: Utility grid hardening and transmission line work is producing steady, high-visibility demand.

The $19B backlog provides approximately 16-18 months of revenue visibility at guided run rates, among the highest in the sector.

### 2. Margin Expansion Is the Core of the Thesis

This is what separates MasTec from a fully-priced infrastructure story. The company is early in a multi-year margin expansion cycle:

| Metric | FY2024 | FY2025 | FY2026E | FY2027E (my est.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $12.3B | $14.3B | ~$17.0B | ~$19.0B |
| Adj. EBITDA | $1.05B | $1.15B | $1.45B | ~$1.80B |
| EBITDA Margin | 8.5% | 8.0% | 8.5% | ~9.5% |
| Adj. EPS | $4.85 | $5.60 | $8.40 | ~$11.00 |

The margin expansion is driven by:

- **Project mix shift**: Larger, longer-duration projects (data centers, utility-scale solar, transmission lines) carry higher margins than smaller telecom or maintenance work. As these projects ramp, the margin profile improves.
- **Operating leverage**: Fixed costs (equipment, supervision, overhead) are spread across a larger revenue base. MasTec has guided that CE&I segment margins should improve by at least 100 bps annually.
- **Acquisition integration**: ~$500M in revenue from recent acquisitions in 2026, with margin uplift as acquired businesses are integrated into MasTec's operating platform.
- **Labor productivity**: Investments in equipment, prefabrication, and project management systems are improving crew productivity and reducing rework.

Management has explicitly targeted a path toward peer-level margins (EMCOR operates at 9.4%). The gap between MasTec's current 8.0-8.5% and EMCOR's 9.4% represents 100-150 bps of margin upside that, applied to $17-19B in revenue, translates to $170-285M in incremental EBITDA and $2-3 in incremental EPS.

### 3. Data Center Entry Is a Catalyst

MasTec's first turnkey data center construction management win is strategically significant. The company already performs electrical, mechanical, and site work for data center projects through its existing segments. Moving up the value chain to construction management on turnkey projects:

- Increases revenue per project by 3-5x
- Improves margin capture (CM fees vs. subcontractor rates)
- Deepens customer relationships with hyperscalers
- Creates repeat business opportunities as hyperscalers standardize on trusted contractors

Nearly $1B in data center awards in Q4 alone validates that this is not aspirational -- it is happening.

### 4. Balance Sheet Is Improving

MasTec carries more leverage than EMCOR but the trajectory is favorable:

- Net debt leverage: 1.8x at YE 2024, 1.9x at Q1 2025, ~1.95x at Q3 2025
- Covenant maximum: 3.50x (significant headroom)
- Operating cash flow: $1.1B in 2024, expected to grow with earnings in 2025-2026
- New $2.5B credit facility provides strategic flexibility

As EBITDA grows from $1.15B to $1.45B+ in 2026, net debt/EBITDA should decline below 1.5x, moving toward investment-grade territory. The company is prioritizing organic investment and deleveraging over large acquisitions, which is the right capital allocation stance in this environment.

### 5. End-Market Diversification Provides Downside Protection

MasTec's exposure across clean energy, pipeline, power delivery, communications, and now data centers means that no single demand driver has to be correct for the thesis to work. Consider the scenarios:

- **AI capex slows**: Data center work decelerates but grid modernization and clean energy continue. Revenue growth moderates but does not reverse.
- **Clean energy policy shifts**: Pipeline and power delivery work remain robust. MasTec pivoted successfully through previous policy cycles.
- **Tariff disruption**: Materials cost pressure is passed through via contractual escalators (with a lag). Domestic infrastructure work is inherently protected from import competition -- you cannot offshore grid construction.
- **Recession**: Utility and infrastructure spending is counter-cyclical (utilities spend more during downturns to maintain reliability). MasTec's customer base is predominantly utilities and large industrials with committed capital programs.

## The Bear Case

1. **Margin expansion may disappoint**: MasTec has a history of over-promising on margins. The CE&I segment in particular saw margin compression in 2023-2024 before recovering. If project execution falters on the larger, more complex work entering the backlog, margins could stall at 8.0-8.5% rather than expanding toward 9.5%.

2. **Valuation demands growth delivery**: At 35x forward earnings, there is no room for a miss. If 2026 EPS comes in at $7.50 instead of $8.40, the stock could reprice to $225-250 (30-33x on the lower number).

3. **Balance sheet is less forgiving than EMCOR**: At 1.8-1.9x net debt/EBITDA, MasTec has less cushion to absorb margin setbacks or working capital swings. A bad quarter on cash conversion could raise leverage concerns.

4. **Clean energy policy risk**: The CE&I segment (38% of revenue) is partially dependent on IRA and IIJA incentives. Political shifts could slow the pace of renewable project awards. The current tariff environment adds uncertainty to solar panel costs and project economics.

5. **Customer concentration in backlog**: The composition of the $19B backlog has not been fully disclosed by customer. If a small number of hyperscalers or utilities represent a disproportionate share, cancellation or delay risk is concentrated.

My assessment: The bear risks are meaningful, which is why the upside/downside ratio is tighter than I would prefer. The thesis depends on margin expansion materializing. I am comfortable underwriting this because: (a) the backlog quality has improved (larger, longer-duration projects), (b) management has a track record of achieving operational improvements (2024-2025 was a turnaround year), and (c) the structural demand environment is the strongest in MasTec's history. But this is a higher-beta position than EMCOR and should be sized accordingly.

## Relationship to EME Thesis

MasTec and EMCOR are complementary positions in the electrical infrastructure theme, not substitutes:

- **EMCOR** is the quality compounder -- highest margins, cleanest balance sheet, most defensive earnings profile. It is the anchor position in the theme.
- **MasTec** is the higher-beta growth play -- best backlog momentum, clearest margin expansion path, more diversified end markets but higher execution risk. It is the upside kicker.

Owning both provides exposure to the structural theme while diversifying across different risk profiles and end-market mixes. If forced to choose one, EMCOR is higher conviction. But the portfolio benefits from having both.

## Conclusion

MasTec is a specialty infrastructure contractor entering the steepest portion of its earnings growth curve, powered by a record $19B backlog, a new data center construction vertical, and a multi-year margin expansion trajectory from 8% toward 9.5%. The stock is not cheap at 35x forward earnings, but the growth rate justifies the multiple and the PEG ratio below 1.0x indicates the market is not fully pricing in the earnings trajectory. The risks are real -- margin execution is the key variable -- and the position should be sized for the uncertainty. But the structural demand environment and management's operational improvements give me enough confidence to initiate.

Conviction: Moderate-to-High. Initiating long at $280-305 with a $370 twelve-month target and $240 stop loss. Half-size position relative to EMCOR.
