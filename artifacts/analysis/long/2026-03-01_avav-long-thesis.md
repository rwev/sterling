# Long Thesis: AeroVironment (AVAV)

## Investment Summary

AeroVironment is the premier pure-play autonomous drone company in the public markets, positioned at the center of a structural reallocation of U.S. defense spending toward unmanned systems. The Pentagon's FY2026 budget commits $13.4 billion to autonomous systems -- the largest single-year allocation in history -- and AVAV's battle-proven Switchblade loitering munitions and Puma tactical UAS are embedded in U.S. military doctrine. The BlueHalo acquisition (closed May 2025) doubles the revenue base to ~$2 billion and extends capabilities into space, cyber, and directed energy. With 93% FY2026 revenue visibility, record bookings of $1.4 billion in Q2 alone (2.9x book-to-bill), and Switchblade production scaling from 40 to 1,200+ units per month, AVAV offers direct, high-beta exposure to the most consequential shift in military procurement since precision-guided munitions. The stock is not cheap at ~72x FY2026 adjusted EPS, but the backlog inflection and production ramp justify a premium. Near-term catalyst: Q3 FY2026 earnings on March 10, 2026.

## Business Overview

AeroVironment designs, develops, produces, and supports unmanned aircraft systems (UAS), loitering munition systems, and related sensor and software solutions for defense and commercial customers. Following the BlueHalo acquisition, the combined company operates across five capability areas:

- **Loitering Munitions**: Switchblade 300 and Switchblade 600 -- the dominant U.S. precision loitering munition platforms, battle-proven in Ukraine and selected for the DoD's Replicator initiative
- **Small UAS (sUAS)**: Puma, Raven, and Jump 20 tactical reconnaissance drones deployed across all U.S. service branches and 55+ allied nations
- **Medium UAS**: Including the acquired BlueHalo platforms for ISR and tactical support
- **Space & Directed Energy**: BlueHalo's space sensors, laser weapon systems, and electronic warfare capabilities
- **MacCready Works**: Advanced R&D division developing next-generation autonomous systems including HAPS (High Altitude Pseudo-Satellite)

The company generates approximately 85% of revenue from defense customers (U.S. DoD and allied governments). The remaining 15% comes from commercial and infrastructure customers.

**Key competitive advantages:**
1. **Doctrinal lock-in**: Switchblade is the only loitering munition system under a five-year, $990M IDIQ contract with the U.S. Army
2. **Production scale-up**: Manufacturing capacity expanding from 40 to 1,200+ Switchblade 600 units per month -- a 30x increase
3. **Battle-proven systems**: Ukraine combat deployment has validated AVAV products and created pull-through demand from NATO allies
4. **BlueHalo synergies**: Combined platform spans small/medium UAS, loitering munitions, space, directed energy, and electronic warfare -- no other public company offers this range in autonomous defense

## Investment Case

### 1. Structural Demand Inflection -- Not Cyclical, Not Speculative

The autonomous drone procurement cycle is confirmed by binding budget commitments, not analyst forecasts. The Pentagon's FY2026 budget allocates $13.4B to autonomy. The Army plans to procure 1 million+ drones over the next 2-3 years. AVAV received a $186M delivery order for next-generation Switchblades on February 26, 2026 -- just days ago -- under the existing $990M IDIQ contract. This is a multi-year, locked-in demand cycle.

### 2. Backlog Provides Extraordinary Revenue Visibility

Q2 FY2026 bookings hit an all-time record of $1.4 billion, yielding a 2.9x book-to-bill ratio. Funded backlog stands at $1.1 billion and unfunded backlog at $1.8 billion. Management has stated that 93% of FY2026 revenue guidance ($1.95-$2.0B) is backed by firm orders. This level of visibility is rare in defense and eliminates most top-line risk for the current fiscal year.

### 3. Production Ramp Creates Operating Leverage

Switchblade 600 production is scaling from 40 units/month to 240 units/month currently, with a trajectory to 1,200+ units/month. The new Salt Lake City manufacturing facility supports this ramp. As production volumes increase, unit costs decline and fixed-cost absorption improves. Management guides adjusted gross margin recovery to the high 30s by Q4 FY2026, with full-year margins in the low 30s as BlueHalo purchase accounting amortization rolls off.

### 4. BlueHalo Transforms the Platform

The $4.1B all-stock acquisition of BlueHalo (May 2025) was dilutive to near-term margins and EPS, but strategically transformative. It doubles annual revenue, adds space and directed energy capabilities, and creates a full-spectrum autonomous defense platform. The combined entity competes for larger prime-level contracts that standalone AVAV could not access. Integration is on track, with management targeting mid-teens EBITDA margins for the combined entity by FY2028.

### 5. Imminent Catalyst: Q3 FY2026 Earnings (March 10)

The March 10 earnings report is a binary catalyst. Consensus expects continued revenue acceleration and margin recovery as BlueHalo integration matures. A beat on revenue or guidance raise would validate the production ramp thesis. The $186M Switchblade order announced February 26 should flow into the reported quarter.

## Earnings Quality

**Concerns acknowledged and contextualized:**

- GAAP net loss in H1 FY2026 is driven by $150M+ in non-cash purchase accounting charges (intangible amortization, step-up inventory, transaction costs) from the BlueHalo acquisition. This is standard for large M&A and temporary.
- Adjusted EPS guidance of $3.40-$3.55 implies a meaningful gap between GAAP and non-GAAP. Investors should monitor the bridge carefully, but the adjustments are legitimate and well-disclosed.
- FCF was weak in FY2025 ($23M) due to inventory build for production ramp. This should reverse as Switchblade deliveries accelerate in H2 FY2026.
- Working capital will be a swing factor. Inventory build is purposeful (production ramp), but if deliveries lag production, working capital could be a cash drag.
- Share count increased ~77% due to the all-stock BlueHalo deal. Dilution is real but one-time.

**Strengths:**
- 93% revenue visibility eliminates top-line risk
- 2.9x book-to-bill indicates accelerating demand
- Contract structures (IDIQ with firm delivery orders) provide cash flow predictability
- No off-balance-sheet risks or unusual accounting noted

**Valuation framework**: AVAV trades at a significant premium to defense peers on every traditional metric. This premium is justified by: (1) pure-play autonomous drone exposure in a structural growth market, (2) the highest backlog growth rate in the sector, (3) production scaling dynamics that will drive margin expansion through FY2028, and (4) scarcity value -- there is no other public company offering this combination of autonomous drone and directed energy capabilities.

The consensus analyst target of $367 (range $285-$450) reflects Wall Street's view that the growth trajectory warrants the premium. Our target of $320 assumes execution on the production ramp and margin recovery but applies a more conservative multiple to reflect integration risk.

## Risk Factors

1. **Valuation risk**: At 72x FY2026 adjusted EPS, any execution miss (revenue, margin, or guidance) will cause a sharp de-rating. The stock is priced for near-perfect execution.
2. **BlueHalo integration risk**: The $4.1B acquisition is the largest in AVAV history. ERP implementation challenges, margin compression, and cultural integration are ongoing risks.
3. **Margin recovery uncertainty**: Gross margins compressed from 39% to 22% (GAAP) post-acquisition. Management guides recovery to high 30s by Q4, but the pace depends on purchase accounting amortization schedules and operational efficiency.
4. **Competition from private players**: Anduril ($30.5B valuation), Shield AI, and Helsing are well-funded private competitors that may capture disproportionate share of autonomous systems procurement.
5. **Tariff exposure**: Drone components (semiconductors, sensors, batteries) sourced from Asia face 20% China tariffs effective March 4, 2026. Defense contracts include cost escalation provisions, but margin impact during adjustment is possible.
6. **Customer concentration**: ~85% defense revenue creates dependence on U.S. government procurement cycles and political priorities.
7. **Share dilution**: 77% share count increase from BlueHalo acquisition is a one-time event, but future M&A could further dilute.
