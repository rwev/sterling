# Small-Cap Long Thesis: NuScale Power (SMR)

## Investment Summary

NuScale Power is the only company in the world with an NRC-certified small modular reactor design (77 MW VOYGR modules, certified May 2023, uprated to 77 MW in 2025). The stock has collapsed 78% from its October 2025 all-time high of $57.42 to ~$12.80, driven primarily by technical selling pressure from Fluor Corporation's forced liquidation of its entire stake ($1.95B+ in sales across 2025-2026) and a widening perception gap between the company's pre-revenue status and the urgency of the AI power thesis. At $3.9B market cap with $1.3B in cash, the enterprise value is approximately $2.6B for what amounts to a regulatory monopoly on small modular nuclear reactor technology in the United States -- at the precise moment when hyperscalers are committing $625B+ in capex and identifying power as their binding constraint. This is a high-risk, asymmetric trade: the downside is quantifiable (cash burn against a $1.3B war chest), while the upside is a category-defining franchise if SMRs become the baseload power solution for AI data centers.

## Discovery Edge

The discovery edge here is not lack of coverage -- SMR has 9-16 analysts depending on the source. The edge is that the stock has been technically destroyed by Fluor's forced selling, creating a price dislocation that has nothing to do with the fundamental thesis:

- **Fluor liquidation**: Fluor Corporation, NuScale's original majority shareholder, has been aggressively monetizing its position. In 2025, Fluor sold $605M worth of SMR shares. In February 2026, Fluor dumped an additional 71 million shares for $1.35B. Fluor has announced plans to fully exit its position by end of Q2 2026. This is not a fundamental judgment on NuScale -- Fluor is a construction/engineering conglomerate executing a portfolio simplification under activist pressure from Starboard Value.
- **Technical selling overwhelms fundamental demand**: The sheer volume of Fluor's sales (71M shares in a single block in February 2026) has cratered the stock from $53+ to $12.80, creating forced-selling conditions that institutional investors typically exploit.
- **Analyst target gap**: The average analyst price target is $24-32 (depending on source), representing 90-150% upside from current levels. The consensus has not meaningfully downgraded targets despite the price collapse, suggesting the fundamental thesis is intact.
- **Retail capitulation**: The stock is down 63% in six months and trading near its 52-week low of $11.08. Retail sentiment is deeply negative, which is often a contrarian signal for names with legitimate long-term catalysts.

The information asymmetry is that the market is pricing Fluor's exit as a fundamental negative when it is purely a technical event. Once Fluor completes its exit (guided for Q2 2026), the overhang lifts and the stock can trade on fundamentals.

## Business Overview

NuScale Power designs and markets small modular reactors for electricity generation. The company's core product is the VOYGR power plant, available in configurations of 4, 6, or 12 modules (308 MW to 924 MW total output). Each module produces 77 MW of carbon-free baseload power.

**Key differentiators:**
- **Sole NRC certification**: NuScale received its Standard Design Approval from the US Nuclear Regulatory Commission in 2023 -- the only SMR design to achieve this milestone. The NRC certification process takes 5-10+ years, creating an enormous regulatory moat.
- **Modular construction**: Unlike traditional nuclear plants ($10B+, 10+ year build times), NuScale modules are factory-built and truck-transportable, enabling faster deployment and lower capital costs per MW.
- **AI data center fit**: Hyperscalers need 24/7, carbon-free, baseload power at scale. Solar and wind are intermittent. Natural gas has emissions. Nuclear is the only technology that meets all three requirements simultaneously. NuScale's modular approach is particularly suited to phased data center buildouts.

**Project pipeline:**
- Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA): Evaluating a potential 6 GW deployment using NuScale technology. This is the single largest potential customer and would be transformative if it proceeds.
- Multiple international projects under development (Romania, South Korea, others), though timelines are extended.
- No binding construction contracts as of the most recent reporting. The cancelled Idaho National Laboratory UAMPS project (2023) remains a historical negative but was driven by cost overruns, not technology failure.

**Revenue profile**: FY2025 revenue was $31.5M (down from $37.0M in 2024), consisting primarily of engineering services and development contracts. This is not yet a product revenue company.

## Owner-Operator Assessment

- **Insider ownership**: Fluor's remaining stake is ~13.3% (40.4M shares) and declining rapidly toward zero by Q2 2026. Post-Fluor exit, insider ownership will be minimal.
- **Management**: CEO John Hopkins has led the company since 2018. The executive team is composed of nuclear engineering professionals, not promotional management.
- **Capital allocation**: Management has been aggressive in securing financing through ATM share issuance ($750M in Q4 2025 alone). This is highly dilutive but arguably necessary -- the company needs a multi-year cash runway to survive until first reactor deployment. Total shares outstanding have grown substantially, which is a real cost to existing shareholders.
- **Alignment concern**: This is not an owner-operator story. Insider ownership is low and Fluor (the original sponsor) is exiting entirely. The investment thesis rests on the technology and regulatory moat, not management alignment.

## Catalyst Path

1. **Fluor exit completion (Q2 2026)**: The single most important near-term catalyst. Once Fluor finishes selling its remaining ~40M shares, the technical overhang lifts entirely. The stock can then trade on fundamentals rather than forced selling flow.
2. **TVA project progression**: Any binding agreement, letter of intent, or milestone announcement on the potential 6 GW TVA deployment would be transformative. This is a "when, not if" catalyst that could arrive at any time.
3. **Hyperscaler nuclear announcements**: Google (Kairos Power), Amazon (X-energy), and Microsoft (Constellation Energy) have all made nuclear commitments. If any hyperscaler announces an SMR-specific initiative with NuScale, the stock re-rates immediately.
4. **First binding construction contract**: Any confirmed, contracted reactor order -- domestic or international -- validates the commercial model and shifts the narrative from "science project" to "order book."
5. **Regulatory tailwinds**: The current US administration is broadly supportive of nuclear energy. Any policy actions that streamline SMR permitting, provide construction subsidies, or mandate clean energy for federal facilities would benefit NuScale disproportionately as the sole NRC-certified SMR provider.
6. **Sector rotation**: If AI infrastructure investors begin looking beyond semiconductors and cooling toward the power layer, NuScale is the highest-beta pure-play on AI nuclear power.

## Risk Factors

1. **Cash burn acceleration**: Q4 2025 burn of $204M was more than double the prior quarterly average. If this rate persists, the $1.3B cash position lasts only 6-7 quarters. Additional dilutive financing would be necessary.
2. **No binding orders**: Despite being NRC-certified for nearly 3 years, NuScale has zero binding construction contracts. The UAMPS project cancellation in 2023 demonstrated that interest does not equal commitment.
3. **Technology execution risk**: No NuScale reactor has ever been built or operated. Factory manufacturing, modular transportation, on-site assembly, and nuclear commissioning are all unproven at commercial scale.
4. **Regulatory and permitting delays**: Even with NRC design certification, individual site permits require years of additional review. Project timelines measured in years, not quarters.
5. **Dilution**: Shares outstanding have grown dramatically through ATM programs. Further dilution is highly likely if commercial traction does not materialize within 18-24 months.
6. **Fluor overhang (near-term)**: Fluor still holds ~40M shares and plans to exit by Q2 2026. Continued block sales create downward price pressure.
7. **Competition**: Other SMR designs (GE Hitachi BWRX-300, X-energy Xe-100, Kairos FHR) are progressing through NRC review. NuScale's first-mover advantage has a finite window.
8. **Lawsuit risk**: The company faces litigation related to its post-SPAC performance, adding legal costs and uncertainty.
