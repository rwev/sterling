## Company Overview

Willdan Group provides professional technical and consulting services to utilities, government agencies, and commercial customers. The business operates in two segments:

- **Energy:** Energy efficiency program management, distributed energy resources, grid modernization consulting, and power engineering for commercial customers including data centers and hyperscalers. This is the growth engine.
- **Engineering:** Civil engineering, building and safety, and municipal consulting services. Steady, recurring revenue base.

Founded in 1964 and headquartered in Anaheim, California. CEO Michael Bieber has led since December 2023, succeeding long-tenured CEO/Chairman Thomas Brisbin who remains Chairman.

## The Bull Case

### 1. Data Center Power Engineering Is Inflecting

Willdan acquired Alternative Power Generation (APG) in March 2025 for up to $43.5 million. APG provides electrical engineering for substations, interconnects, microgrids, and data center power systems. In 2024, APG generated ~$37 million in revenue. Management stated on the Q4 2025 earnings call that APG-related work is expected to "more than double" in 2026, with backlog building into 2027 and 2028 on long-term contracts.

Data center projects accounted for 11% of Willdan's 2025 revenue. If APG doubles and data center work continues expanding across the broader Energy segment, data center projects could reach 20% of revenue by late 2027. This is a structural demand driver -- AI data center power requirements are growing 30-40% annually according to industry estimates, and Willdan is one of the few sub-$5B market cap companies positioned as a direct beneficiary.

### 2. $1 Billion Backlog Provides Revenue Visibility

As of January 2, 2026, Willdan's total backlog stood at approximately $1.0 billion. This represents roughly 2.5x annual net revenue, providing exceptional forward visibility for a professional services company. Recent contract wins include a $97 million contract with Alameda County and a $49 million contract with Mt. SAC -- demonstrating the ability to win increasingly large engagements.

### 3. Section 179D Headwind Is Temporary and Well-Understood

The 2026 EPS guide of $4.50-$4.70 (vs. $4.89 in 2025) reflects the expiration of Section 179D tax benefits after June 30, 2026. This is a one-time headwind that is fully quantifiable and already priced into guidance. The underlying business -- net revenue of $390-405 million (+7-11% growth) and adjusted EBITDA of $85-90 million (+7-13% growth) -- is accelerating. Once the tax comparison normalizes in 2027, reported EPS growth should resume.

### 4. Balance Sheet Strength Supports Capital Allocation

- Cash and equivalents: $66 million
- Total debt: $49 million (net cash positive)
- Total available liquidity: $216 million
- Free cash flow (FY2025): $71 million
- Cash conversion ratio: 3.19x (operating cash flow to net income)

The net cash balance sheet and $216 million of available liquidity provide significant capacity for acquisitions. Management has demonstrated disciplined capital allocation through the APG, Alpha Inspections, Enica Engineering, and Compass Municipal Advisors acquisitions -- each strategically expanding capabilities or geographic reach. The acquisition pipeline remains "robust" per management commentary.

### 5. Owner-Operator Alignment

At 42.7% insider ownership, management's interests are overwhelmingly aligned with shareholders. This is the single most important quality signal in small-cap investing. When insiders own nearly half the company, capital allocation decisions are made with an owner's mentality -- no empire-building, no reckless dilution, no misaligned incentive structures.

## The Bear Case and Risks

### 1. Lumpy Contract Wins
The $97M Alameda County and $49M Mt. SAC contracts are large relative to Willdan's revenue base. If contract wins become lumpy or delayed, revenue could disappoint in any given quarter. Professional services companies are inherently subject to project timing variability.

### 2. Section 179D Legislative Risk
There is a scenario where Congress extends or reinstates Section 179D, which would be a positive surprise. But if energy efficiency mandates weaken under the current administration, Willdan's utility program management business could face headwinds.

### 3. Tariff Impact on Data Center Buildout
If tariffs on electrical equipment (transformers, switchgear, copper) escalate further, data center construction timelines could slip, delaying Willdan's power engineering revenue recognition.

### 4. Customer Concentration
Willdan derives a significant portion of revenue from a handful of large utility clients. Loss of a major utility customer relationship could materially impact results.

### 5. Valuation Not Cheap on Headline Metrics
At ~$94, WLDN trades at roughly 20x 2026 adjusted EPS ($4.60 midpoint) and ~16x adjusted EBITDA ($87.5M midpoint). For a professional services company, this is not a deep value entry. The thesis requires continued execution on data center growth and acquisitions.
