# Small-Cap Long Thesis: POET Technologies (POET)

## Investment Summary

POET Technologies is a sub-$1B market cap silicon photonics company with only 3 sell-side analysts, designing and manufacturing optical engines based on its patented Optical Interposer platform for 800G and 1.6T AI data center interconnects. The company is at a commercial inflection point -- transitioning from sample shipments to initial production orders -- directly into the 800G-to-1.6T transceiver supercycle that is the PM's highest-conviction theme. With $350M in cash post-raise, a partnership with Semtech on 1.6T receivers, production orders from multiple global customers, and a technology that integrates electronic and photonic devices on a single chip at wafer scale, POET represents the purest small-cap photonics discovery play available in public markets today.

## Discovery Edge

This is a textbook information asymmetry situation:

- **Analyst coverage**: Only 3 Wall Street analysts cover POET. The median price target is $7.30, with a high of $8.00. For context, Coherent (COHR) has 20+ analysts, Lumentum (LITE) has 15+, and Fabrinet (FN) has 18. POET is invisible to institutional allocators who require minimum coverage thresholds.
- **Institutional ownership**: The company only recently completed $250M in institutional financing (Q3/Q4 2025) with three investors at $5.00-$7.25/share. Institutional ownership is in the very early stages of building. Most large-cap photonics investors have not evaluated POET because it was too small and pre-revenue until recently.
- **Market cap screen-out**: At ~$970M, POET falls below the radar of virtually every large-cap and mid-cap technology fund. Small-cap funds with photonics expertise are rare.
- **Canadian origin**: POET was originally listed on the TSXV before its NASDAQ listing, meaning its investor base skews toward Canadian retail and small-cap specialists, not US institutional photonics investors.
- **Revenue inflection not yet visible in screens**: Quarterly revenue was $298K in Q3 2025. Quantitative screens filtering for revenue or profitability will not surface this name. The $5.6M in production orders received in H2 2025 -- with shipments beginning in 2026 -- represent the first meaningful revenue, but it will not show up in databases until Q1/Q2 2026 earnings are reported.

The market has not found this because it does not yet look like a real company by conventional screening metrics. That is the edge.

## Business Overview

POET Technologies designs, develops, and manufactures photonic integrated packaging solutions based on the POET Optical Interposer -- a patented platform that integrates electronic and photonic devices onto a single chip using wafer-level semiconductor manufacturing techniques. The company's products include:

- **POET Infinity optical engines**: Transmit and receive engines for 400G, 800G, and 1.6T applications used in optical transceiver modules for AI data center interconnects.
- **Light source products**: Laser-based components for optical communication systems.

The Optical Interposer platform is differentiated because it eliminates the need for discrete optical packaging (fiber pigtailing, active alignment, hermetic sealing) that makes traditional optical modules expensive and difficult to scale. POET's approach uses passive alignment at wafer scale, which dramatically reduces manufacturing cost and enables higher yields at volume.

**Key partnerships and customers:**
- Semtech: Collaboration to launch 1.6T optical receivers integrating POET's Interposer with Semtech's FiberEdge technology (announced September 2025).
- Three unnamed "major technology leaders" received final design samples of POET Infinity transmit engines for 400G and 800G applications (March 2025).
- $5M production order from a leading systems integrator for 800G optical engines (October 2025), with shipments expected H2 2026.
- $500K+ initial production order from a lead customer, with shipments starting early 2026 and expected to grow to high volumes.

**End market**: The optical transceiver market for AI cluster scale-out and scale-up networks is projected to double from $5B in 2024 to $10B in 2026, driven by the 800G-to-1.6T transition. POET does not need to win a large share of this market to generate meaningful revenue at its current market cap.

The bear case: If production orders stall, customers do not scale beyond initial orders, or the technology fails to meet volume manufacturing requirements, the company burns through cash over 2-3 years with minimal revenue, and the stock trades toward cash value (~$2.30/share).

## Risk Factors

1. **Technology risk**: The Optical Interposer has been demonstrated in samples and initial production, but volume manufacturing at scale has not been proven. Yield issues or performance shortfalls at volume could delay revenue ramp.
2. **Customer concentration**: With only a handful of identified customers, loss of any single customer would materially impact the revenue trajectory.
3. **Competition**: Coherent, Lumentum, and other established optical component companies have massive scale advantages. POET's differentiation is its integration platform, but incumbents could develop competing approaches.
4. **Dilution risk**: The company raised $250M at $5.00-$7.25/share. If revenue ramp is slower than expected, additional financing may be needed, further diluting shareholders. Shares outstanding have grown from ~90M to ~153M.
5. **Tariff exposure**: Manufacturing likely has Asian supply chain dependencies (POET has operations in Singapore). The March 4, 2026 tariff escalation on Chinese components could affect costs.
6. **Insider selling**: Recent insider sales, while modest, do not inspire confidence. Monitor for acceleration.
7. **Pre-revenue binary risk**: The stock could re-rate sharply in either direction based on quarterly revenue trajectory. A disappointing Q4 2025 report could send the stock back toward cash value.
