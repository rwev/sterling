# Small-Cap Long Thesis: TEN Ltd. (TEN)

## Investment Summary

TEN Ltd. (formerly Tsakos Energy Navigation) is a $1.05 billion founder-controlled tanker operator with 62 operating vessels, 26 under construction, and a $3.0+ billion contracted revenue backlog from a transformative Petrobras shuttle tanker deal -- yet it trades at 7.9x trailing earnings, 0.56x book value, and carries only 1-2 active analyst ratings. The company just reported Q4 2025 results today (March 6), beating EPS estimates by 38% ($1.70 vs. $1.23 consensus) on $222M revenue, with fleet utilization hitting 97.7%. With 22 vessels currently exposed to the spot tanker market where VLCC rates have surged to all-time records of $423,000-$481,000/day following the Hormuz crisis, TEN is capturing a freight rate windfall that the market has not priced into a stock still trading 44% below book value. Founder-CEO Nikolas Tsakos and the Tsakos Holdings Foundation together hold approximately 17% of the company, and Tsakos personally purchased $10 million worth of shares in the past 12 months at $21.05 per share -- 39% below today's price. This is an owner-operator compounder with visible, contracted earnings growth, massive spot market optionality, and no meaningful sell-side coverage to close the discount.

The market assigns zero premium for this hybrid contracted/spot business model. Additionally, TEN is taking delivery of 26 newbuilds through 2028, including 3 VLCCs, 12 shuttle tankers, 2 LNG carriers, and product tankers -- this fleet modernization will increase capacity by approximately 50% while reducing the average fleet age. No analyst model fully captures the earnings trajectory of the expanded fleet entering the strongest tanker market in history.

## Business Overview

TEN Ltd. is one of the largest independent transporters of energy in the world, founded in 1993 by Nikolas P. Tsakos from a traditional Chian shipping family in Greece. The company is publicly listed on the NYSE and headquartered in Athens. It provides seaborne crude oil, petroleum product, and LNG transportation services to national oil companies, major integrated oil companies, independent refiners, and trading houses under long-term, medium-term, and spot charters.

**Fleet Composition (as of December 31, 2025)**:

The operating fleet of 62 double-hull vessels (approximately 11 million deadweight tons) with an average age of 10.1 years consists of:

- 3 VLCCs (Very Large Crude Carriers)
- 16 Suezmaxes (including 4 DP2 shuttle tankers)
- 26 Aframaxes
- 2 Aframax LR2 product tankers
- 9 Panamax LR1 product tankers
- 4 Handysize tankers
- 2 LNG carriers

**Newbuilding Program (26 vessels under construction)**:

- 12 DP2 Suezmax shuttle tankers (Samsung Heavy Industries, South Korea)
- 3 VLCCs
- 2 MR product tankers
- 2 Suezmax tankers
- 5 LR1 product tankers
- 2 LNG carriers (Hyundai Heavy Industries, delivery Q3 2028)

Deliveries are staggered from Q1 2026 through Q4 2028. Upon completion of the newbuilding program, TEN's operating fleet will approach 80+ vessels.

**Revenue Segments**:

TEN generates revenue through three chartering modes: (1) spot/voyage charters capturing prevailing market rates, (2) medium-term time charters providing 1-3 year rate visibility, and (3) long-term contracts including the Petrobras shuttle tanker deal (15-year charters) and LNG carrier charters. Currently, 22 vessels operate in the spot market, providing maximum exposure to the Hormuz-driven rate supercycle, while the contracted fleet provides base-load earnings stability.

**Petrobras Shuttle Tanker Contract**: In 2025, TEN secured one of the largest contracts in shipping history -- nine Suezmax DP2 shuttle tankers built at Samsung Heavy Industries, chartered to Transpetro/Petrobras for up to 15 years, generating approximately $2.0 billion in gross revenue and contributing to a minimum revenue backlog of $3.0+ billion. This contract won the "Best Deal of the Year Award" at the 2025 Lloyd's List Greek Shipping Awards and positions TEN as one of the largest shuttle tanker operators globally.

## Owner-Operator Assessment

- **Insider ownership**: Founder-CEO Nikolas P. Tsakos personally holds 8.4% of shares outstanding. The Tsakos Holdings Foundation holds an additional 8.4%. Combined family/foundation ownership is approximately 17%, representing ~$178M at the current stock price. Total insider ownership is reported at 9.0% (individual holdings only, excluding the Foundation).
- **Founder involvement**: Nikolas Tsakos founded TEN in 1993 and has served as President and CEO continuously for 33 years. He comes from a traditional Chian (Greek island) shipping family and is the son of shipping veteran Panagiotis Tsakos. His leadership has transformed TEN from a small tanker operator to one of the world's largest independent energy transporters, divesting 17 older vessels and taking delivery of 33 modern ones in recent years -- nearly doubling the fleet, tripling its deadweight capacity, and reducing the average fleet age.
- **Capital allocation track record**: Tsakos has demonstrated disciplined capital allocation across three priorities: (1) Fleet renewal -- the 26-vessel newbuilding program represents approximately $2 billion in committed capital deployed at historically favorable yard prices before the post-2024 order surge inflated shipbuilding costs. (2) Debt management -- despite the expansion, the debt/equity ratio is 1.03x and the company maintains $298M in cash. (3) Shareholder returns -- the $1.00/share annual common dividend yields 4.6% with a sustainable 50.6% payout ratio. The Petrobras shuttle tanker deal is the capstone: a single contract that de-risks earnings for 15 years while expanding capacity in a high-margin segment.
- **Incentive alignment**: Tsakos's $10 million personal share purchase at $21.05 in the past 12 months is the strongest insider signal -- a founder putting significant personal capital to work 39% below the current price. His family's multi-generational shipping legacy and 17% ownership stake ensure alignment between management and shareholders on capital allocation, fleet composition, and dividend policy.

## Earnings Quality Assessment

TEN's earnings quality is high and improving:

1. **Contracted vs. spot mix**: The Petrobras shuttle tanker contract provides $3.0B+ in minimum revenue backlog, creating a floor under earnings. As shuttle tankers deliver (2026-2028), the contracted revenue share will increase, reducing earnings volatility while the spot-exposed conventional tankers capture upside.

2. **Fleet utilization**: 97.7% in Q4 2025 demonstrates operational excellence -- minimal downtime, efficient scheduling, and strong commercial management. This is among the highest utilization rates in the tanker sector.

3. **Depreciation and asset base**: The 10.1-year average fleet age and ongoing newbuilding program mean TEN's fleet is modern and well-maintained. Depreciation of $44.4M/quarter is consistent with the fleet's asset base. The gap between book value ($62.48/share) and market price ($34.75) reflects the market's chronic undervaluation of shipping assets, not impairment risk.

4. **Cash flow conversion**: With $416M in adjusted EBITDA and $298M in cash against $184M in scheduled principal payments, TEN generates substantial free cash flow after debt service, funding both fleet growth and dividends without dilutive equity issuance.

## Risk Factors

1. **Ceasefire/Hormuz reopening**: A rapid diplomatic resolution to the Iran conflict would collapse tanker rates from $400K+/day to $50-80K/day within days. TEN's 22 spot-exposed vessels would see dramatic revenue declines. However, the contracted shuttle tanker and time-charter book ($3.0B+ backlog) provides a floor. The Petrobras shuttle tankers are entirely independent of the Hormuz conflict. This risk is binary and must be managed through position sizing.

2. **High leverage**: Total debt of $1.92B against equity of $1.86B results in a debt/equity ratio of 1.03x. Net debt/EBITDA of ~3.9-4.3x is manageable in the current rate environment but would become strained if rates collapse and earnings normalize. The newbuilding program commits capital to yard installments regardless of market conditions. The $298M cash position and contracted backlog provide buffers.

3. **Newbuilding execution risk**: 26 vessels under construction at Samsung Heavy Industries and Hyundai Heavy Industries. Delivery delays, cost overruns, or quality issues could affect the earnings ramp. Korean yards have strong track records, but the volume of orders globally has increased yard backlogs.

4. **Greek shipping governance**: TEN is an Athens-headquartered company with the Tsakos family holding 17% effective control. Greek shipping companies have historically traded at governance discounts relative to US or Nordic-listed peers. The complex ownership structure (Tsakos Holdings Foundation, preferred shares) may deter some institutional investors.

5. **Tanker sector cyclicality**: Tanker rates are inherently volatile. The current supercycle driven by Hormuz disruption will eventually normalize. TEN's shift toward contracted shuttle tanker revenue reduces but does not eliminate cyclical exposure.

6. **Concentration risk**: The Petrobras shuttle tanker contract represents a significant portion of forward revenue. Petrobras counterparty risk, Brazilian regulatory changes, or operational issues at Brazil's pre-salt fields could affect this revenue stream.
