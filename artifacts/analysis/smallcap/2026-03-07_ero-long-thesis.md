# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Ero Copper (ERO)

## Investment Summary

Ero Copper is a $2.9 billion Brazil-focused copper and gold producer trading at 7.2x forward earnings and approximately 7x 2026E EV/EBITDA -- a significant discount to the small-cap copper peer median despite record production, near-90% year-over-year EBITDA growth, and a recently released Furnas PEA showing $2.0 billion NPV (27% IRR) on what could become one of the largest undeveloped copper deposits in the Americas. The stock trades at $27.76 after falling from its 52-week high of $39.80, providing an entry at a reasonable valuation for a company entering a multi-year production growth phase (2026 guidance of 67,500-77,500 tonnes, up 20% from 2025, growing to 80,000-90,000 tonnes by 2028). The Furnas project -- being advanced in partnership with Vale Base Metals in the Carajas district -- represents transformational optionality that is not reflected in the current share price.

## Business Overview

Ero Copper is a mid-tier copper and gold producer headquartered in Vancouver, BC with all operations in Brazil.

**Caraiba Operations (Bahia, Brazil)** -- The flagship copper mine complex in the Curaca Valley of Bahia state. Caraiba is an underground mining operation that has been producing copper since 1978. The operation is transitioning to fully mechanized mining, which is expected to improve productivity and reduce costs over 2026-2027. Caraiba produced approximately 36,035 tonnes of copper in 2025 (full-year), with production expected to stabilize in 2026 as mechanization progresses.

**Tucuma Operations (Para, Brazil)** -- A greenfield copper mine that declared commercial production on July 1, 2025. Tucuma produced a record 9,275 tonnes of copper in Q4 2025 (up 22% quarter-over-quarter), bringing the post-commercial production total to 16,854 tonnes in H2 2025. The operation is ramping toward design throughput, with additional tailings filtration equipment being manufactured to unlock further capacity (potential benefit expected in Q4 2026). Tucuma has transformed Ero from a single-mine producer into a diversified two-mine copper company.

**Xavantina Operations (Mato Grosso, Brazil)** -- An underground gold and silver mine that provides earnings diversification. Xavantina produced 52,290 ounces of gold in 2025 at Q4 C1 cash costs of $766/oz. The operation has successfully transitioned to mechanized mining. An additional value creation initiative involves monetizing a ~60,000 m3 gold-bearing concentrate stockpile (maiden inferred resource of ~29,000 oz at 37.4 g/t), with initial shipments completed in Q4 2025 and sales expected to continue through mid-2027.

**Furnas Project (Para, Brazil)** -- A greenfield copper-gold exploration project in the Carajas mineral province, approximately 50 km from Vale Base Metals' Salobo operations. Ero is advancing Furnas through an earn-in agreement with Vale (60% interest upon completion of prescribed work). The February 2026 PEA outlined a 24-year mine life producing approximately 70,000 tonnes of copper, 111,000 ounces of gold, and 532,000 ounces of silver annually over the first 15 years. Initial capex of approximately $1.3 billion at low capital intensity of $16,000 per copper-equivalent tonne. After-tax NPV(8%) of $2.0 billion and 27% IRR at base-case prices ($4.60/lb Cu, $3,300/oz Au); at spot prices ($6.10/lb Cu, $5,550/oz Au), NPV doubles to $4.7 billion with 44% IRR.

## Risk Factors

1. **Brazil jurisdiction risk**: All of Ero's operations and development assets are in Brazil. Regulatory changes (mining royalties, environmental regulations, labor laws, tax policy) could increase operating costs or delay the Furnas project. The political environment in Brazil has been volatile, and any shift toward resource nationalism would be a headwind. This is the primary structural risk and the reason ERO trades at a discount to peers.

2. **Tucuma ramp execution**: While Tucuma has progressed well (record Q4 production), the mine has not yet demonstrated sustained throughput at or above design capacity. The additional tailings filtration equipment (expected Q4 2026) is not included in guidance, creating upside optionality but also suggesting current capacity is constrained.

3. **Copper price cyclicality**: At $5.80/lb COMEX, Ero's margins are excellent. A decline to $4.00/lb would compress margins significantly at Caraiba (C1 ~$2.15-2.35/lb) and reduce the Furnas NPV by approximately 40%. The stock's 52-week range of $9.30-$39.80 illustrates the price sensitivity.

4. **Furnas development risk**: The Furnas PEA is preliminary. A $1.3B capex project in Brazil carries construction, permitting, and execution risk. The project is years from an investment decision. Any adverse exploration results or permitting delays would reduce the optionality value.

5. **Currency risk**: Ero's revenues are USD-denominated (copper sold in USD), but approximately 70% of operating costs are BRL-denominated. A strengthening real would increase costs. Conversely, real depreciation is a tailwind.

6. **Q4 2025 EPS miss and stock decline**: ERO has pulled back approximately 30% from its 52-week high of $39.80. The Q4 EPS miss ($1.04 vs. $1.11 expected) and stock dip may indicate near-term investor skepticism about the production growth trajectory or disappointment with profitability conversion.
