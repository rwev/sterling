# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Skeena Resources (SKE)

## Investment Summary

Skeena Resources is a gold-silver developer whose flagship Eskay Creek project in British Columbia's Golden Triangle is one of the highest-grade open-pit gold projects in the world at 4.5 g/t gold-equivalent. The company received its final environmental permit in February 2026, is actively in construction, and targets first production in Q2 2027. At today's gold price of $5,278/oz, the project's feasibility-study economics are extraordinary: $687/oz AISC, implying $4,591/oz operating margins -- more than double the margin embedded in the original study assumptions of $1,800/oz gold. The stock trades at $36.84 on the NYSE with a market cap of approximately $4.5B, representing roughly 0.4x the project's current-price NPV. This is a rare combination of world-class asset quality, de-risked permitting, and a gold price environment that transforms the project's economics from strong to exceptional.

## Discovery Edge

Skeena occupies an unusual position in gold equity markets: it has a Tier-1 quality asset but trades in the development-stage universe where institutional coverage is thin. The discovery gap exists because:

- **Only 10 analysts cover SKE**, predominantly Canadian mining specialists; US generalist and precious metals funds remain largely absent from the shareholder base
- **Institutional ownership at 45%** leaves significant room for accumulation, particularly by US-based gold funds that typically do not engage until construction financing is complete and first pour approaches
- **Pre-revenue status** screens out the company from most quantitative screens focused on earnings or cash flow -- yet the asset quality and gold price environment make it one of the most compelling risk-reward propositions in the gold development space
- **TSX-primary listing with recent NYSE listing** means many US funds are only now able to build positions; the NYSE listing broadens the potential buyer base substantially
- **No major gold ETF (GDX/GDXJ) inclusion yet** -- as Skeena transitions from developer to producer, inclusion in VanEck's GDXJ (Junior Gold Miners ETF) could drive significant passive buying

## Business Overview

Skeena Resources is a Canadian gold-silver developer focused on the Eskay Creek project in northwestern British Columbia. Eskay Creek was historically one of the world's richest mines (operated by Barrick from 1994-2008) and produced 3.3 million ounces of gold and 160 million ounces of silver from underground operations. Skeena is reimagining Eskay Creek as an open-pit operation, accessing lower-grade but much larger mineralized zones that were uneconomic at historical gold prices.

**Eskay Creek Project Highlights:**

- **Proven & Probable Reserves**: 39.8 million tonnes at 2.5 g/t Au and 68.7 g/t Ag (3.6 g/t AuEq)
- **Life of Mine**: 12 years of mining
- **Annual Production**: ~320,000 oz gold-equivalent
- **AISC**: $687/oz (feasibility study)
- **Initial Capex**: C$713M
- **After-tax NPV (5%, at $1,800/oz Au)**: C$2.0B
- **IRR (at $1,800/oz Au)**: 43%
- **Payback**: 1.2 years
- **Jurisdiction**: British Columbia, Canada -- Tier-1 mining jurisdiction with established permitting frameworks

At today's gold price of $5,278/oz, the project NPV is multiples of the feasibility study estimate. Even at conservative long-term gold assumptions of $3,000-3,500/oz, the economics are exceptional.

## Owner-Operator Assessment

- **Insider ownership**: 2.0% of shares held by insiders; this is typical for development-stage mining companies where project-level dilution from financing is a natural feature of the business model
- **Management team**: CEO Randy Reichert brings operational mining experience; the team has executed the permitting process on schedule, a significant achievement in British Columbia's regulatory environment
- **Board quality**: Includes mining industry veterans with track records of taking projects from feasibility through construction to production
- **Capital allocation**: The recent C$143.8M bought deal financing was completed at market terms, reflecting investor demand; management has been disciplined about raising capital in tranches rather than a single large dilutive raise
- **Insider activity**: Balanced buying and selling over the past 3 months, suggesting management views the current valuation as fair during the construction phase

## Earnings Quality

Not applicable in the traditional sense for a pre-revenue developer. However, the quality of the underlying asset economics can be assessed:

- **Grade quality**: At 4.5 g/t AuEq, Eskay Creek is among the top 5 highest-grade open-pit gold projects globally
- **Feasibility confidence**: A definitive feasibility study (November 2023) provides engineering-level cost estimates; capex and opex estimates have been tested by third-party reviewers
- **Permitting de-risked**: All major permits (Environmental Assessment Certificate, BC Mines Act Permit, Environmental Management Act Permit) have been received as of February 2026
- **Metallurgical certainty**: Eskay Creek was previously mined; metallurgical recovery rates are well-understood and proven

## Catalyst Path

1. **Construction milestones (ongoing through 2027)**: Regular construction updates demonstrating on-budget, on-schedule execution will de-risk the project and narrow the P/NAV discount
2. **Construction financing announcement (expected H1 2026)**: Securing the project debt package (likely $400-500M) would be a major de-risking event and a clear signal that lenders view the project as bankable
3. **First pour (targeted Q2 2027)**: Transition from developer to producer is the single most significant catalyst; historically, gold developers re-rate 50-100% through this transition
4. **GDXJ/GDX inclusion**: As Skeena transitions to producer status, inclusion in VanEck's gold miner ETFs would drive passive buying and broaden the shareholder base
5. **Gold price continuation**: Every $100/oz increase in gold above $5,000 adds approximately $30-40M in annual free cash flow at steady-state production, further compressing the valuation
6. **Resource expansion**: Eskay Creek has significant exploration upside beyond current reserves; any resource increase extends mine life and increases NPV
7. **Sell-side coverage expansion**: US-based precious metals analysts at major banks have not yet initiated on SKE; coverage initiations following the NYSE listing could broaden investor awareness

## Risk Factors

1. **Construction execution risk**: C$713M capex estimate is subject to cost overruns, labor shortages, and supply chain disruptions; British Columbia has experienced mining project delays in the past
2. **Financing dilution**: Additional equity raises could dilute existing shareholders by 10-20%; the terms and pricing of construction financing are not yet known
3. **Gold price risk**: At $5,278/oz, gold is at all-time highs; a significant correction (e.g., to $3,500/oz on Iran ceasefire or Fed hawkish pivot) would reduce project NPV, though Eskay Creek's economics remain robust at any gold price above $1,500/oz
4. **Permitting/regulatory risk**: Although all major permits are secured, operating conditions and compliance requirements could introduce delays or additional costs
5. **Indigenous relations**: The project is located in the territory of the Tahltan Nation; while Skeena has worked to build a constructive relationship, any deterioration in community relations could impact the project
6. **Single-asset concentration**: Skeena's entire value proposition rests on Eskay Creek; any project-specific issue (geological surprise, water management, metallurgical challenge) would have an outsized impact on the stock
