# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Viking Therapeutics (VKTX)

## Investment Summary

Viking Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing VK2735, a dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist for obesity, with both subcutaneous and oral formulations advancing through late-stage clinical trials. The stock trades at $33.34 with a $3.83B market cap, representing a rare opportunity to own a differentiated asset in the $100B+ GLP-1 obesity market at a fraction of the value the pipeline would command if commercialized or acquired. With $706M in cash, two Phase 3 trials fully or near-fully enrolled, maintenance dosing data expected Q3 2026, an oral formulation entering Phase 3 in Q3 2026, and a novel DACRA program entering human trials in Q2 2026, Viking offers a multi-catalyst 2026 with 150%+ consensus upside and persistent M&A speculation involving Pfizer, Amgen, and Roche.

## Discovery Edge

Viking sits in a unique position: too large to be ignored by biotech specialists, but too early-stage and pre-revenue to attract generalist institutional capital. The information asymmetry lies in the complexity of parsing its multi-program pipeline and the market's tendency to discount clinical-stage companies after initial Phase 2 hype fades.

- **Analyst coverage**: 14-18 analysts cover VKTX, predominantly biotech specialists. This is moderate coverage for a $3.8B biotech, but the consensus Strong Buy rating with a median target of $100.50 (200%+ upside) suggests the sell-side sees the value but the buy-side has not fully committed.
- **Institutional ownership**: ~63-68% institutional, which is respectable, but notably declined from 66.3% in September 2025 to as low as 13% by one measure in December 2025 (likely a methodological discrepancy, but the directional shift suggests institutional rotation out during the post-Phase-2-oral data volatility).
- **The market's mistake**: The stock is down from a 52-week high near $90 to $33.34 -- a 63% drawdown. The market treated the oral Phase 2 data (12.2% weight loss in 13 weeks, with a 20% discontinuation rate) as disappointing relative to elevated expectations, but objectively the data supports a competitive oral obesity drug profile. The subcutaneous program (14.7% weight loss in 13 weeks in Phase 2) is best-in-class among dual agonists. The market is pricing VKTX as if VK2735 will fail to reach commercialization, when enrollment pace and data quality suggest the opposite.

## Business Overview

Viking Therapeutics was founded in 2012 by Brian Lian, PhD, as a spin-off from Ligand Pharmaceuticals. The company is headquartered in San Diego, CA and is focused exclusively on metabolic and endocrine disorders. Viking has no commercial revenue -- all value is embedded in its clinical pipeline:

**VK2735 (Subcutaneous) -- Obesity**
- Dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist
- Phase 2 VENTURE trial: 14.7% mean body weight reduction in 13 weeks at the highest dose (vs. 0.2% placebo); 88% of patients achieved greater than or equal to 5% weight loss; 47% achieved greater than or equal to 10%
- Phase 3 VANQUISH-1: Completed enrollment of ~4,650 patients (over-enrolled ahead of schedule); randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; 78-week dosing period; primary readout expected 2027
- Phase 3 VANQUISH-2: Expected to complete enrollment Q1 2026
- Maintenance dosing study: Fully enrolled; results expected Q3 2026

**VK2735 (Oral Tablet) -- Obesity**
- Same molecule, proprietary oral formulation
- Phase 2 VENTURE-Oral: Up to 12.2% weight loss in 13 weeks; 97% achieved greater than or equal to 5% weight loss; 80% achieved greater than or equal to 10% weight loss at highest dose
- Phase 3 initiation planned for Q3 2026
- Competitive positioning: Oral formulation acts faster than Novo's oral Wegovy, achieving significant weight loss in just 13 weeks

**VK2809 -- NASH/MASH**
- Thyroid hormone receptor beta agonist
- Phase 2b VOYAGE: NASH resolution ranging from 63% to 75% (vs. 29% placebo); up to 48% achieved both NASH resolution and fibrosis improvement (vs. 20% placebo)
- Best-in-class profile; Phase 3 decision pending

**DACRA Program -- Obesity (Next-Generation)**
- Dual Amylin and Calcitonin Receptor Agonist -- non-incretin mechanism
- Preclinical data shows greater potency than VK2735 in primates
- IND filing expected Q1 2026; Phase 1 SAD/MAD study expected Q2 2026
- Differentiated asset for patients intolerant to GLP-1s

**Assessment**: Lian is a genuine owner-operator with deep domain expertise in both biotech research and financial markets. His capital allocation has been conservative relative to peers, and his compensation is meaningfully tied to stock performance. The insider selling is a yellow flag but not disqualifying given the option exercise context.

## Catalyst Path

1. **VANQUISH-2 enrollment completion (Q1 2026)**: Confirms trial execution is on track; incremental positive signal.
2. **DACRA IND filing (Q1 2026)**: Opens a new non-incretin pipeline; expands addressable patient population beyond GLP-1 tolerant patients.
3. **DACRA Phase 1 initiation (Q2 2026)**: First human data on a potentially more potent obesity compound than VK2735.
4. **Maintenance dosing study readout (Q3 2026)**: Critical data point. If VK2735 demonstrates durable weight maintenance at reduced doses, it validates the chronic treatment paradigm and differentiates from competitors with high weight regain.
5. **Oral VK2735 Phase 3 initiation (Q3 2026)**: Begins the registration path for what could be a best-in-class oral obesity drug.
6. **Medicare GLP-1 coverage launch (mid-2026)**: Dramatically expands the addressable market for all obesity drugs; validates the commercial opportunity for VK2735 even before it reaches market.
7. **M&A / partnership announcement (any time)**: Pfizer reportedly has Morgan Stanley advising on a potential acquisition. Amgen and Roche also named as potential acquirers. A takeout at $80-100/share would represent 140-200% upside.
8. **Phase 3 VANQUISH primary readout (2027)**: The definitive efficacy and safety data that will determine FDA filing and commercial viability.

## Risk Factors

1. **Clinical failure**: Phase 3 trials may not replicate Phase 2 efficacy. The 78-week VANQUISH trials test durability, not just initial weight loss. If weight loss plateaus below 15% at 78 weeks, the competitive profile weakens relative to tirzepatide (22.5% at 72 weeks).
2. **Oral formulation tolerability**: The 20% discontinuation rate in Phase 2 oral (vs. 13% placebo) is a concern. If Phase 3 oral shows higher GI adverse events than Lilly's orforglipron or Novo's oral Wegovy, the oral thesis weakens.
3. **Competitive displacement**: The GLP-1 market is crowding rapidly. Lilly's orforglipron (FDA action expected Q2 2026), Novo's oral Wegovy (already approved), Amgen's MariTide, and a dozen other dual/triple agonists are in development. Viking must demonstrate differentiation on efficacy, tolerability, or dosing convenience.
4. **Dilution risk**: With $225M annual burn and no revenue visibility until 2028+, further equity raises are likely. If the stock remains depressed, dilutive raises at $30-35 could materially impact per-share value.
5. **M&A premium compression**: If Pfizer walks away or if broader M&A appetite for obesity assets cools (due to competitive concerns), the takeout premium embedded in the stock deflates.
6. **Regulatory risk**: FDA requirements for obesity drug approval continue to evolve. If the FDA raises the efficacy bar or requires cardiovascular outcomes data (as with semaglutide's SELECT trial), Viking's timeline extends materially.
7. **Insider selling**: $12.1M in insider sales over the past three months with zero purchases. While likely option exercises, sustained insider selling without offsetting purchases is a caution signal.
