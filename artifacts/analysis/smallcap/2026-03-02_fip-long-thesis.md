# Small-Cap Long Thesis: FTAI Infrastructure Inc. (FIP)

## Investment Summary

FTAI Infrastructure is a $721M market cap infrastructure holding company trading at a severe discount to the sum of its parts. The company owns a portfolio of critical infrastructure assets -- freight railroads, a 485 MW natural gas power plant (Long Ridge), an energy terminal (Jefferson Terminal), and a deep-water port (Repauno) -- that collectively generated $232M in adjusted EBITDA in FY2025 (+82% y/y) with a current run-rate of $321M. At $6.07 per share, the stock trades at roughly 2x run-rate EV/EBITDA (using $4.36B enterprise value on $321M run-rate EBITDA, or approximately 13x on reported FY2025 EBITDA of $232M). With only 2-3 analysts covering the name, CEO Kenneth Nicholson buying $2.6M of stock at $5.22, and a pending Long Ridge power plant monetization that could unlock hundreds of millions in value, FIP offers an asymmetric risk/reward profile for patient investors willing to underwrite complexity and leverage.

The key thesis is that FIP's infrastructure assets are worth far more than the current equity market cap implies, and that a combination of EBITDA growth, deleveraging, and asset monetization will close the valuation gap over 12-18 months.

## Discovery Edge

FIP is covered by only 2-3 sell-side analysts with a consensus Buy rating and a median price target of $12.00 (98% above current price). The stock was spun off from FTAI Aviation (FTAI) in August 2022 and has been orphaned ever since -- it inherited infrastructure assets that do not fit the aviation-focused mandate of most FTAI Aviation holders. Institutional ownership is 47.7%, with 13.4% insider ownership and 38.9% retail.

The information asymmetry is substantial. FIP's asset portfolio spans four distinct infrastructure verticals (rail, power, energy terminal, deep-water port), making it difficult for generalist analysts to value. The Q4 2025 earnings miss (-$1.08 EPS vs. -$0.43 consensus) further scared away casual investors, but the miss was driven by non-cash items and one-time charges -- adjusted EBITDA of $80.2M actually beat expectations and grew 175% year-over-year. The market is pricing the headline EPS miss while ignoring the EBITDA trajectory.

Additionally, FIP's Long Ridge power plant sits at the intersection of two mega-themes from the macro outlook -- AI infrastructure power demand and energy infrastructure scarcity. A 485 MW gas-fired power plant in PJM territory, adjacent to potential data center development, is exactly the kind of asset hyperscalers are paying premium prices for. The market has not connected these dots for FIP because no one is looking at a $721M market cap infrastructure hodgepodge.

## Business Overview

FTAI Infrastructure operates through four primary segments:

**Railroad ($41.3M Q4 2025 EBITDA)** -- Owns and operates Transtar (a collection of short-line railroads serving industrial customers in the Great Lakes region) and the Wheeling & Lake Erie Railway. Combined rail EBITDA is targeted to reach $200M+ annually by end of 2026 through operational improvements, traffic growth, and cost synergies. Management has identified $20M in annual cost savings, with more than half already implemented.

**Long Ridge Energy ($36.2M Q4 2025 EBITDA)** -- A 485 MW combined-cycle natural gas power plant in Hannibal, Ohio, one of the most efficient in the US. EBITDA target of $160M annualized. The plant produces its own natural gas, with current output (~105,000 MMBtu/day) exceeding fuel requirements (~70,000 MMBtu/day), allowing excess gas sales. PJM interconnection uprate to 505 MW is in progress. Management is exploring data center co-location and behind-the-meter opportunities, with projected additional EBITDA of $50-75M annually. Management is also actively exploring monetization (sale) of Long Ridge, with a transaction potentially in H1 2026.

**Jefferson Terminal ($13.6M Q4 2025 EBITDA)** -- A multi-modal crude oil and refined products terminal near Port Arthur, Texas. Steady cash flow contributor with exposure to Gulf Coast energy infrastructure.

**Repauno ($(1.9)M Q4 2025 EBITDA)** -- A deep-water port on the Delaware River with underground storage caverns, multipurpose dock, and rail-to-ship transloading. Currently in development but potentially the most valuable long-term asset:
- Phase 2 construction in progress, expected to handle 80,000+ bbl/day of NGLs, generating ~$80M annual EBITDA. Target operational very early 2027.
- Phase 3 cavern permit received October 2025 (described as a "game changer"). Each cavern projected to generate $70-80M annual EBITDA from a ~$200M investment (3-year payback). Management securing anchor customers before committing to construction.

## Earnings Quality

Earnings quality requires careful interpretation. FIP reports negative net income due to high interest expense, depreciation, and development-stage costs at Repauno. Adjusted EBITDA is the relevant metric for infrastructure holding companies at this stage. The $232M FY2025 adjusted EBITDA is backed by real operating cash flows from rail operations, power generation, and terminal throughput, though consolidated free cash flow remains negative due to growth capex and interest payments.

The gap between EPS (-$1.08 in Q4) and adjusted EBITDA ($80.2M in Q4) is primarily driven by: high interest expense on $3.7B debt, depreciation on infrastructure assets, non-cash equity method accounting for Long Ridge investment, and development-stage losses at Repauno.

## Catalyst Path

1. **Long Ridge monetization / sale (H1 2026)** -- Management explicitly stated that a Long Ridge transaction is expected in H1 2026. At $160M target EBITDA and data center power premiums, this could unlock significant value and accelerate deleveraging. Net proceeds could reach "hundreds of millions." This is the near-term catalyst.

2. **Repauno Phase 2 completion (early 2027)** -- Phase 2 reaching operational status and generating $80M EBITDA transforms FIP's earnings profile. Initial revenue from NGL throughput could begin in late 2026 with full ramp in 2027.

3. **Repauno Phase 3 anchor customer announcement** -- Management targeting anchor customer commitments within 6 months. Each Phase 3 cavern represents $70-80M incremental EBITDA at a 3-year payback. A commercial commitment here would be a major positive catalyst.

4. **Rail EBITDA reaching $200M target** -- The rail segment is executing on cost synergies and traffic growth. Hitting the $200M target by year-end 2026 would validate the acquisition thesis and demonstrate operational leverage.

5. **Debt refinancing at lower rates** -- Management plans to refinance parent-level debt with a new bond issuance. If successful at lower rates, interest expense reduction flows directly to equity value.

6. **Sell-side initiation** -- At $721M market cap with 82% EBITDA growth and only 2-3 analysts, FIP is a candidate for coverage initiation, particularly as the Long Ridge monetization process generates dealflow interest.

## Risk Factors

1. **Leverage risk** -- $3.7B total debt against $721M equity market cap. While $2.5B is non-recourse subsidiary debt, the 9.75% interest rate on the parent-level $1.3B term loan is punitive. Any operational underperformance or capital markets dislocation could stress the capital structure.

2. **Execution risk at Repauno** -- Phase 2 is in construction with a target of early 2027. Construction delays, cost overruns, or failure to secure anchor customers for Phase 3 would impair the growth thesis.

3. **Long Ridge monetization uncertainty** -- While management is guiding for an H1 2026 transaction, the process could be delayed, and the final price may not meet expectations. If Long Ridge is not monetized or fetches a disappointing price, the deleveraging thesis weakens.

4. **Commodity exposure** -- Long Ridge's profitability is linked to natural gas prices and PJM power market dynamics. Jefferson Terminal is exposed to crude oil throughput volumes. Adverse commodity moves could pressure EBITDA.

5. **Complexity discount** -- FIP's multi-segment structure spanning four distinct infrastructure verticals makes it inherently difficult to analyze and value. This complexity may persist as a discount even as fundamentals improve.

6. **Interest rate sensitivity** -- With $3.7B in debt, rising rates would increase refinancing costs and pressure equity value. The current 9.75% rate on the term loan is already elevated.

7. **Negative free cash flow** -- The company is not generating free cash flow due to development capex and interest payments. This limits the company's ability to return capital or organically deleverage.
