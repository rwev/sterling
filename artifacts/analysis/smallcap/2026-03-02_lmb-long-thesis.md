# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Limbach Holdings (LMB)

## Investment Summary

Limbach Holdings is a $1.1 billion market cap building systems solutions company undergoing a strategic transformation from low-margin general contractor work to high-margin owner-direct relationships (ODR) -- and data centers are becoming the growth engine of that pivot. With only 4-6 analysts covering the stock, 9.7% insider ownership, a proven capital allocation track record under former CEO Charlie Bacon (now board member), and a deliberate push into hyperscale data center MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) services, Limbach is the type of quality small-cap compounder that hides below the institutional radar while compounding earnings at 30%+ rates. The stock trades at 21x forward earnings with a net cash balance sheet, accelerating ODR revenue, and a newly appointed data center-focused sales leadership team. We recommend building a position in the $85-$95 range for a target of $130 over 12-18 months.

## Discovery Edge

Limbach sits in a coverage desert. Only 4-6 sell-side analysts actively cover the name, and 3 of the 8 listed analysts rate it Hold -- meaning the buy-side signal is muted despite the fundamental inflection. Institutional ownership is 80%, but this is concentrated in small-cap and micro-cap specialists (BlackRock at 8.4%, with the remainder spread across boutique managers). The company does not screen well on traditional quantitative screens because its revenue growth was flat in 2024 (masking the ODR surge due to deliberate GCR runoff). The January 2026 appointment of Nick Angerosa as EVP of National Customer Solutions and Jay Sharp as EVP of Sales -- both explicitly focused on data center work -- has received essentially zero sell-side attention. This is a company where the thesis is in the 10-K and investor presentations, not in the consensus estimates.

## Business Overview

Limbach Holdings provides integrated building systems solutions -- design, prefabrication, installation, and maintenance of HVAC, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and building automation systems -- across the United States. Founded in 1901 and headquartered in Warrendale, Pennsylvania, the company operates through two segments:

- **Owner Direct Relationships (ODR):** Direct contracts with building owners for mission-critical MEP work, including data centers, hospitals, life sciences facilities, and industrial plants. Higher margin, recurring revenue, and deeper customer relationships. ODR represented 76.6% of Q3 2025 revenue, up from 66.6% in FY2024 and approximately 50% in FY2022.

- **General Contractor Relationships (GCR):** Traditional subcontractor work for general contractors. Lower margin, more competitive, and being deliberately wound down as a percentage of the business. GCR revenue declined 31.9% in FY2024 and continues to shrink.

The strategic pivot from GCR to ODR is the core of the investment thesis. ODR work carries gross margins of 28-32% versus GCR margins of 12-16%. As the mix shifts, consolidated margins expand even on flat or modest revenue growth. Management has guided to a long-term target of 70-80% ODR revenue mix and 35-40% gross margins.

The company completed the acquisition of Pioneer Power in July 2025 for $66.1 million, expanding into the Upper Midwest. Pioneer is expected to contribute approximately $120 million in annualized revenue and $10 million in adjusted EBITDA beginning in 2026. Additionally, Limbach acquired Consolidated Mechanical of Owensboro in late 2024, continuing its tuck-in acquisition strategy.

**Data Center Angle:** Limbach has completed confidential hyperscale data center projects and is actively positioning for the AI infrastructure buildout. The January 2026 leadership appointments specifically target data center owner-direct relationships. Data center MEP work is among the highest-value, highest-complexity work in Limbach's portfolio -- it requires precision cooling systems, redundant power distribution, and building automation at levels that most competitors cannot deliver. This is exactly the type of mission-critical work where Limbach's ODR model thrives.

## Earnings Quality

Earnings quality is high and improving:

- **ODR mix shift drives real margin expansion.** The shift from 50% ODR (2022) to 77% ODR (Q3 2025) is structural, not cyclical. Each incremental dollar of ODR revenue carries 12-16 percentage points of higher gross margin than the GCR dollar it replaces.
- **Free cash flow conversion is strong.** FCF of $52.3M on $63.7M adjusted EBITDA in FY2024 represents 82% conversion. The business is capital-light -- no factories, no heavy equipment. Capex runs at approximately 1-2% of revenue.
- **Balance sheet is clean.** Net cash of $17.7M at end of FY2024. Post-Pioneer, modest net debt of approximately $15-20M, or roughly 0.2x EBITDA. Ample capacity for additional tuck-in acquisitions.
- **Revenue quality is improving.** ODR work is stickier and more recurring than GCR. Building owners hire Limbach for ongoing maintenance, retrofit, and upgrade work after the initial installation. This creates a compounding revenue base.

## Risk Factors

1. **Data Center Concentration Risk:** If data center capex decelerates (the macro outlook warns of 62% to 25% growth rate deceleration by H2 2026), Limbach's data center pipeline could stall. Mitigant: data center is still a minority of Limbach's revenue; the company serves hospitals, life sciences, and industrial end markets that provide diversification.

2. **Pioneer Power Integration:** The $66M acquisition carries execution risk. Pioneer's margins are below Limbach core, and integration of a $120M revenue business into a $530M company is non-trivial. Mitigant: management has executed multiple integrations successfully (Consolidated Mechanical, prior acquisitions).

3. **Labor Market Tightness:** Skilled MEP technicians are in short supply. Wage inflation could compress margins. Mitigant: ODR contracts allow for cost pass-through more readily than fixed-price GCR work.

4. **Customer Concentration:** If a small number of data center customers represent a growing share of ODR revenue, the loss of a single customer could impact results. Mitigant: Limbach serves a diversified end market base.

5. **Valuation Compression:** If small-cap multiples compress in a risk-off environment (the macro outlook notes crowding risk in AI infrastructure trades), LMB could trade down regardless of fundamentals. Mitigant: net cash balance sheet and buyback program provide downside support.

6. **Regulatory Moratorium Impact:** Data center moratoriums in 11+ states could slow construction in certain geographies. Mitigant: Limbach operates nationally and can shift resources to permitting-friendly markets; moratoriums in some states benefit construction demand in others.
