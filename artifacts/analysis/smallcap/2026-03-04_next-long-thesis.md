# Small-Cap Long Thesis: NextDecade Corporation (NEXT)

## Investment Summary

NextDecade is a $1.5B market cap pre-revenue LNG developer building the Rio Grande LNG terminal at the Port of Brownsville, Texas -- the largest LNG export facility currently under construction in the United States. The stock trades at $5.90, down 51% from its 52-week high of $12.12, despite the most favorable LNG market conditions in years. The Strait of Hormuz closure and Qatar's LNG production halt have removed approximately 20% of global LNG supply, validating the structural thesis that international buyers must diversify away from Middle Eastern supply sources. Rio Grande LNG Train 1 is 65% complete, ahead of schedule, and targeting year-end 2026 commissioning with first LNG production in H1 2027.

Only 2 analysts cover the stock (consensus Hold, average target $9.00 -- representing 53% upside from current levels). Hanwha Aerospace, a 10% strategic owner, has been aggressively buying shares on the open market with zero insider selling in the past 6 months. The company has fully commercialized Trains 4 and 5 with 20-year SPAs generating approximately $1.2B in annual fixed liquefaction fees before inflation escalation. All $13.4B in project-level financing is complete. This is a pre-revenue name where the asset value is becoming increasingly visible but the equity market has not yet priced the transformation from developer to operator.

## Discovery Edge

NextDecade has only 2 sell-side analysts providing coverage -- an extraordinarily low number for a company building one of the most significant energy infrastructure projects in the hemisphere. By comparison, Cheniere Energy (the incumbent US LNG leader) has 20+ analysts. The limited coverage reflects NEXT's legacy as a speculative development-stage company, but the risk profile has fundamentally changed: all 5 trains have reached FID, all project financing is in place ($13.4B), and construction is ahead of schedule. The market is valuing NEXT based on its history as a pre-revenue developer rather than its near-term transition to a cash-generating LNG infrastructure company.

Institutional ownership is moderate, and the shareholder base skews toward energy-specialist and development-stage investors rather than the large generalist funds that would own the stock once it begins generating revenue. The Hormuz crisis creates an information asymmetry: generalist investors searching for LNG exposure find Cheniere and Venture Global first; NEXT remains overlooked despite having the largest US LNG project under construction and a stock price 51% below its 52-week high.

## Business Overview

NextDecade Corporation is developing the Rio Grande LNG terminal facility at the Port of Brownsville in southern Texas. The project is being constructed by Bechtel, the world's largest EPC contractor, under a fixed-price, date-certain contract structure that limits cost overrun risk to the developer.

**Project Status (as of Q4 2025):**

| Train | Status | Completion % | First LNG |
|---|---|---|---|
| Train 1 | Under construction (EPC: Bechtel) | ~65% | H1 2027 |
| Train 2 | Under construction | ~65% | 2027 |
| Train 3 | Under construction | ~40% | 2028 |
| Train 4 | Under construction (FID Sep 2025) | ~8% | 2029 |
| Train 5 | Under construction (FID Oct 2025) | ~3% | 2030 |
| Trains 6-8 | Pre-development / FERC pre-filing | -- | 2031+ |

**Commercialization:** In 2025, NextDecade executed five 20-year LNG sale and purchase agreements totaling 7.2 mtpa with TotalEnergies, Aramco, JERA, EQT, and ConocoPhillips. Combined with a 1.9 mtpa SPA signed with ADNOC in 2024, Trains 4 and 5 are fully commercialized with fixed liquefaction fees totaling approximately $1.2B annually before inflation escalation.

**Ultimate vision:** Double capacity from 30 mtpa (Trains 1-5) to 60 mtpa (10 trains). FERC pre-filing initiated for Train 6 in November 2025; full application expected mid-2026.

## Owner-Operator Assessment

NextDecade's management team is led by CEO Matt Schatzman, who has guided the company from concept through FID on all five trains. The management team's equity interests are aligned with shareholders through stock-based compensation.

The most significant ownership signal is from Hanwha Aerospace, a 10% strategic owner that has been aggressively buying shares on the open market. In the past 6 months, Hanwha executed 7 open-market purchases and zero sales:

- December 2025: Acquired ~2.6M shares at $5.46-$5.69 weighted average
- November 2025: Purchased 591,837 shares at $6.07
- September 2025: Purchased 551,819 shares at $6.99

Hanwha's buying accelerated as the stock declined -- a classic conviction signal from a strategic industrial partner with deep knowledge of the LNG value chain. In August 2025, Hanwha signed an MOU with Hanwha Energy and Korea Southern Power to advance an integrated LNG value chain, leveraging Hanwha Ocean's LNG carrier fleet to transport gas from Rio Grande LNG.

## Catalyst Path

1. **Strait of Hormuz disruption persistence (ongoing).** Every week of Hormuz closure validates the thesis that LNG buyers must diversify supply sources away from the Middle East. This directly benefits NEXT's ability to contract remaining uncommitted volumes and potentially negotiate better terms for future expansion trains.

2. **Train 1 commissioning (year-end 2026).** The most significant near-term catalyst. Achieving mechanical completion and beginning commissioning of Train 1 transforms NEXT from a developer into an operator and should trigger a substantial re-rating.

3. **First LNG production (H1 2027).** First cargo from Rio Grande LNG will generate the company's first revenue and demonstrate the project works as designed. This is the catalyst that typically draws generalist investor interest and new analyst coverage.

4. **FERC application for Train 6 (mid-2026).** Filing the full application for the next expansion train signals management confidence in the development pipeline and provides visibility into additional growth.

5. **New analyst initiations.** With only 2 analysts covering the stock, any additional coverage initiation (particularly from a major bank involved in the project financing) would meaningfully increase institutional awareness.

6. **Hanwha continued accumulation.** Further open-market purchases by the 10% strategic owner provide ongoing price support and signal insider conviction.

7. **Baker Hughes framework agreement execution.** Baker Hughes entered a framework agreement for LNG expansion trains, providing equipment supply visibility for future growth.

## Risk Factors

1. **Construction delay or cost overrun.** Although Bechtel is executing on a fixed-price basis and construction is ahead of schedule, LNG terminal construction is inherently complex. A delay to Train 1 commissioning would push back first revenue and potentially trigger concerns about the broader project timeline.

2. **Hormuz ceasefire collapses LNG pricing narrative.** A rapid de-escalation could reduce the urgency of international buyers to diversify LNG supply, potentially softening the pricing environment for uncommitted volumes and slowing expansion train commercialization.

3. **Sustained low LNG prices.** If global LNG supply normalizes (Qatar restart, Australian ramp, Mozambique expansion), the premium pricing environment that enhances NEXT's economics could deteriorate. The 20-year SPAs provide downside protection on contracted volumes, but expansion train economics depend on sustained favorable pricing.

4. **Project-level leverage.** $13.4B in non-recourse project debt creates significant fixed costs once trains are operational. If LNG demand or pricing disappoints, the project's ability to service debt and generate equity distributions could be impaired.

5. **Equity dilution.** As a development-stage company with limited operating cash flow, NEXT may need to raise equity or issue shares to fund corporate overhead, expansion development costs, or equity contributions for future trains.

6. **Regulatory risk.** FERC permitting for expansion trains (6-10) could face delays or opposition from environmental groups, slowing the growth trajectory.
