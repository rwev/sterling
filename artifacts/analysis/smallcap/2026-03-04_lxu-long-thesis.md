# Small-Cap Long Thesis: LSB Industries (LXU)

## Investment Summary

LSB Industries is an $850M market cap nitrogen fertilizer producer that is the direct small-cap beneficiary of the Strait of Hormuz energy dislocation. The company manufactures ammonia, UAN, and ammonium nitrate at three US plants using domestic natural gas (Henry Hub) as its primary feedstock. With European TTF gas prices surging 76% in a week due to Qatar's LNG production halt, international nitrogen fertilizer competitors face sharply higher input costs while LXU's feedstock costs remain anchored to modestly priced US gas. This is the same margin expansion dynamic benefiting US LNG exporters -- but in a name with only 3 analysts covering it, trading at 5.3x adjusted EBITDA on 2025 figures, with $244M in federal NOLs shielding future earnings from taxes.

Q4 2025 results (reported February 25) beat consensus on both revenue and EPS. Full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA grew 25% to $162M, and the company reduced net debt/EBITDA to 1.8x from 2.3x. The stock hit a 52-week high on March 1 following an analyst upgrade to strong-buy, but at $11.70 it remains well below its 2022 peak when fertilizer fundamentals last surged. We are early in the current energy dislocation, and the fertilizer pricing impact has not yet been fully reflected in forward estimates.

## Discovery Edge

LSB Industries has only 3 sell-side analysts covering the stock (Royal Bank of Canada, UBS, and Zacks). This compares to 20+ analysts covering CF Industries and 15+ covering Nutrien. The consensus price target was recently raised to $13.00 by RBC (outperform), while UBS maintains a neutral stance at $9.75 -- indicating the sell side has not yet priced in the Hormuz-driven margin expansion thesis. Institutional ownership stands at approximately 74%, but the name is absent from most large-cap fertilizer discussions. The stock's 52-week range of $4.88 to $11.94 (current price near the high) reflects the beginning of re-rating, but the fundamental case for sustained margin expansion from the international gas price dislocation is not yet widely understood.

## Business Overview

LSB Industries operates three chemical manufacturing facilities in the United States:

- **El Dorado, Arkansas** -- produces ammonia, UAN, HDAN, and nitric acid
- **Cherokee, Oklahoma (Pryor)** -- produces ammonia, UAN, HDAN, and nitric acid
- **Baytown, Texas** -- nitric acid production

The company sells nitrogen-based fertilizer products (ammonia, UAN, ammonium nitrate) to agricultural markets, as well as industrial chemicals (nitric acid, ammonium nitrate solutions) to mining and industrial end markets. The dual agricultural/industrial customer base provides diversification that pure-play fertilizer producers lack.

Natural gas is the primary raw material, comprising approximately 70-80% of variable production costs. LXU purchases gas at Henry Hub-linked prices, providing a structural cost advantage over international competitors who rely on LNG-linked or European gas-indexed pricing.

In 2025, the company achieved record nitric acid and ammonium nitrate solution production, reflecting sustained operational improvements in plant reliability and throughput.

## Owner-Operator Assessment

Insider ownership is approximately 4.3% as of the most recent reporting. The Golsen family, through Director Barry Golsen, has been a long-standing significant holder. Recent insider selling has been modest and executed under pre-planned 10b5-1 trading plans: Golsen sold approximately 186,697 shares in January 2026 (~$1.6M) and smaller amounts in February, all under plans adopted in September 2025. This pattern is consistent with routine diversification rather than loss of conviction, particularly given the stock's rise from $4.88 to the $10-12 range.

Management has demonstrated disciplined capital allocation: reducing total debt from $485M to $441M in 2025, repurchasing $39.9M of debt and 0.3M shares, and investing in the carbon capture and storage (CCS) initiative at El Dorado. The poison pill (Section 382 Rights Plan, in effect through August 2026) protects $244M in federal and $343M in state NOLs -- a genuine and rational defensive measure to preserve tax assets worth potentially $50M+ in future tax savings.

## Earnings Quality

LXU's earnings quality has improved materially. Key observations:

- **Revenue growth is price-driven and volume-supported.** Q4 2025 revenue rose 22% YoY on both higher realized prices and improved sales volumes. Record production levels in nitric acid and ammonium nitrate solutions demonstrate operational improvements, not just price tailwinds.
- **EBITDA growth outpaced revenue growth.** Full-year adjusted EBITDA grew 25% vs. 18% revenue growth, indicating operating leverage and cost discipline.
- **Cash conversion is improving.** Operating cash flow of $96M in 2025 with $53M sustaining capex yielded $44M in FCF. The company guided $75M total capex for 2026, which includes both sustaining and growth investments.
- **Deleveraging is real.** Net debt/EBITDA declined from 2.3x to 1.8x as the company repurchased $39.9M of debt.
- **NOL shield.** The $244M federal and $343M state NOLs mean the company will pay minimal cash taxes for years, enhancing after-tax FCF conversion.

The primary quality concern is cyclicality. Nitrogen fertilizer pricing is volatile and tied to both agricultural demand cycles and natural gas spreads. FY2024 showed what a trough looks like (-$19M net income, negative FCF). The current environment is inflecting favorably.

## Catalyst Path

1. **Henry Hub / international gas spread persistence (ongoing).** Every week that the Strait of Hormuz remains closed widens the cost advantage for US nitrogen fertilizer producers. European TTF gas at 60+ EUR/MWh vs. Henry Hub at $3-4/MMBtu creates a structural margin tailwind.

2. **Spring planting season (April-May 2026).** Fertilizer demand peaks during planting season. Elevated fertilizer prices combined with robust farm economics (corn and soybean prices supporting farmer purchasing power) should drive strong Q1 and early Q2 volumes.

3. **USDA Planting Intentions (March 31).** This report will provide visibility into acreage and crop mix, directly impacting fertilizer demand expectations. An increase in corn acreage (corn is nitrogen-intensive) would be bullish.

4. **Carbon capture Class VI permits (2026).** LSB expects key permits for its El Dorado CCS project in 2026. This initiative targets 400,000-500,000 metric tons of CO2 capture annually and positions the company to produce low-carbon ammonia -- a growing market for clean energy applications.

5. **Sell-side initiation risk.** With only 3 analysts covering the stock and the fertilizer sector attracting attention from the Hormuz crisis, additional coverage initiations could drive institutional awareness and multiple expansion.

6. **February CPI release (March 12).** If food and energy components show acceleration, the fertilizer pricing narrative strengthens and may attract generalist investor attention.

## Risk Factors

1. **Hormuz ceasefire / Qatar LNG restart.** A rapid resolution of the Iran-US conflict and reopening of the Strait would collapse the Henry Hub/international gas spread that underpins the margin expansion thesis. This is the primary binary risk.

2. **Plant turnaround execution (Q2-Q3 2026).** Two major turnarounds are scheduled -- El Dorado in Q2 and Pryor in Q3 -- with an estimated 60,050 tons of lost ammonia and UAN production. Turnaround delays or cost overruns could weigh on H2 2026 earnings.

3. **Natural gas price spike.** If Henry Hub gas prices rise sharply (e.g., from US LNG export demand pulling domestic supply), LXU's input costs increase and margins compress. The thesis depends on the asymmetry of low US gas / high international gas.

4. **Agricultural demand destruction.** If farm input costs (including fertilizer) rise too quickly, farmers may reduce fertilizer application rates, cutting volumes even as prices rise.

5. **Cyclicality.** Nitrogen fertilizer is a deeply cyclical business. FY2024 demonstrated trough earnings. The stock could trade back toward trough valuations if the energy dislocation proves short-lived.

6. **Insider selling pattern.** While recent sales appear routine (10b5-1 plans), continued selling by the Golsen family during a stock price rise could signal caution.
