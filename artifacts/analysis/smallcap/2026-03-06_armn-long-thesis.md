# Small-Cap Long Thesis: Aris Mining Corporation (ARMN)

## Investment Summary

Aris Mining is a $4.0 billion Colombian gold producer that just uplisted to the NYSE on February 19, 2026, with only 4 analysts covering the name, 37% institutional ownership, and a forward P/E of 11.1x -- roughly half the mid-tier gold miner peer median of 18-22x. The company operates the high-grade Segovia mine in Colombia with AISC of ~$1,482/oz, generating margins of approximately $3,900/oz at today's $5,400 gold price. Production grew 22% in 2025 to 256,503 oz, with 2026 guidance of 300,000-350,000 oz (+17-37% growth), and a long-term roadmap to 500,000+ oz with the Soto Norte project (PFS showing $2.7B NPV at 35% IRR) and Marmato expansion. With $390M in cash, minimal net debt, and the largest sovereign wealth fund (Mubadala, 9.2%) as a strategic investor, Aris is a compounding gold producer that institutional investors have not yet discovered on US exchanges.

## Business Overview

Aris Mining Corporation is a mid-tier gold producer headquartered in Vancouver, Canada, operating two underground gold mines in Colombia with additional development projects in Colombia and Guyana. The company was formerly known as GCM Mining Corp. and changed its name in September 2022 following its formation from the merger of Gran Colombia Gold and Aris Gold.

**Segovia Operations (Colombia)**: The crown jewel. A high-grade underground gold mine in Antioquia producing 227,762 oz in 2025 (2026 guidance: 265,000-300,000 oz). Segovia has been mined since the 16th century and benefits from exceptionally high grades (average ~13 g/t Au). The second ball mill commissioned in June 2025 has increased processing capacity, driving H2 2025 production 26% above H1. AISC of ~$1,482/oz on a 9-month 2025 basis. Segovia is one of the lowest-cost gold operations in the Americas.

**Marmato Complex (Colombia)**: An underground gold-silver mine in Caldas that produced 28,741 oz in 2025. The Bulk Mining Zone (BMZ) expansion is under construction with an estimated cost-to-complete of $250M. The CIP plant is expected to produce first gold in Q4 2026, with production ramping to 35,000-50,000 oz in 2026 and ultimately 150,000+ oz annually. This is the second leg of the growth story.

**Soto Norte (Colombia)**: A development-stage gold-silver project in Santander, now 100%-owned following the Q4 2025 acquisition of Mubadala's 49% interest for $60M. The February 2026 PFS outlined exceptional economics: 100% basis after-tax NPV(5%) of $2.7B, IRR of 35%, life-of-mine AISC of $534/oz, and annual production of ~215,000 oz over a long mine life. Construction decision expected in 2027-2028.

**Toroparu (Guyana)**: A gold-copper project with a PEA completed in October 2025 and PFS in progress. Provides diversification outside Colombia.

The combined pipeline positions Aris to reach 500,000+ oz of annual production by the end of the decade, which would place it firmly in the mid-tier producer category alongside names like Kinross, B2Gold, and Alamos Gold.

## Risk Factors

1. **Colombia geopolitical risk**: Colombia has a left-leaning government under President Petro that has periodically signaled hostility toward mining. However, gold mining has deep cultural roots in Antioquia (Segovia) and Caldas (Marmato), providing social license. Aris has operated in Colombia for over a decade without nationalization or expropriation. The Mubadala sovereign wealth fund relationship provides additional political insurance.

2. **Gold price decline**: A rapid ceasefire with Iran could send oil prices down $20-30, reducing inflation expectations and potentially triggering a gold correction. However, the structural gold bull market is supported by central bank buying, dollar reserve diversification, and fiscal deficit monetization -- factors independent of the Iran conflict. Aris is profitable at gold prices above ~$1,600/oz (Segovia AISC), providing a massive margin of safety.

3. **Execution risk on Marmato BMZ and Soto Norte**: Underground mine construction in Colombia carries inherent risks -- permitting delays, water management challenges, and cost overruns. The $250M Marmato cost-to-complete could increase. Soto Norte faces additional permitting hurdles given its location near the Santurban paramo (high-altitude wetland), which has been a political flashpoint.

4. **AISC inflation**: Segovia's AISC has risen from ~$1,200 to ~$1,482/oz over two years. Rising energy costs ($89 WTI) and Colombian peso appreciation could push AISC higher. However, the absolute margin per ounce continues to expand dramatically as gold price appreciation far outpaces cost inflation.

5. **Illiquidity during transition**: Daily volume of ~580K shares ($11M/day) is adequate but not deep. The NYSE uplisting will improve liquidity over time, but in the near term, large institutional orders could move the price significantly.

6. **Currency risk**: Costs are denominated in Colombian pesos while revenue is in US dollars. A strengthening peso compresses margins. The current COP/USD rate of ~3,800 is used in company guidance; a move below 3,500 would be a headwind.
