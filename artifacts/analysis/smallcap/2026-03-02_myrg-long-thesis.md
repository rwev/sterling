# Small-Cap Long Thesis: MYR Group (MYRG)

## Investment Summary

MYR Group is a $4.2 billion electrical construction company delivering record revenue, record earnings, and record free cash flow at the epicenter of two structural mega-themes: grid modernization ($228B in annual utility capex) and data center power infrastructure buildout. With only 8 sell-side analysts, the stock is meaningfully under-covered for a company of this size and quality. Gross margins have inflected from 8.6% in FY2024 to 11.6% in FY2025 as legacy low-margin contracts roll off and higher-priced data center and grid work ramps. The $2.82B record backlog provides 9+ months of revenue visibility, and the structural demand drivers -- 49 GW power shortfall by 2028, 2,600 GW interconnection queue, and $1.1T in grid investment over five years -- ensure this backlog continues to grow. MYR Group is a hidden compounder in a multi-year infrastructure super-cycle.

## Discovery Edge

MYR Group operates in the shadows of larger peers like Quanta Services ($39B backlog, widely covered) and MasTec. With only 8 analysts providing coverage and a consensus Buy rating, this is a company that institutional investors are underweight relative to the opportunity. At $4.2B market cap, MYRG sits below the threshold for most large-cap growth mandates, yet its financial quality -- 0.25x leverage, $232M FCF, 11.6% gross margins -- rivals companies twice its size. The 135% EPS surge in FY2025 barely registered in broader financial media. Institutional ownership at 89% is high, but concentrated among passive vehicles (Vanguard, BlackRock), meaning active small-cap managers are underexposed. The company does not host flashy investor days or maintain a social media presence. It simply executes.

## Business Overview

MYR Group is one of the largest electrical construction companies in the United States. It operates through two segments:

- **Transmission & Distribution (T&D)**: Builds and maintains high-voltage transmission lines, substations, and distribution networks for utilities. FY2025 T&D backlog: $1.02B.
- **Commercial & Industrial (C&I)**: Provides electrical construction services for commercial buildings, data centers, industrial facilities, and healthcare. FY2025 C&I backlog: $1.80B.

The company operates through subsidiaries including Sturgeon Electric, The L.E. Myers Co., Harlan Electric, and others, with a geographic footprint spanning the continental US and Western Canada. MYR Group's C&I segment is where the data center exposure lives -- the company has been winning increasingly large data center electrical contracts as hyperscalers build out capacity.

The business model is asset-light relative to peers: MYR Group does not own generation assets or take commodity risk. It bids on projects, executes them with skilled labor, and collects payment. The constraint -- and the moat -- is skilled electrical labor, which is in severe shortage nationwide. MYR Group's ability to recruit, train, and retain electricians and linemen is its core competitive advantage.

## Earnings Quality

Earnings quality is high for a construction company:

- **Backlog conversion is visible**: $2.82B backlog converts over 9-12 months, providing near-term revenue certainty.
- **Cash conversion is strong**: FY2025 FCF of $232M on $118M net income implies >1.9x cash conversion ratio. This is exceptional and reflects strong working capital management.
- **No acquisition distortion**: Growth is organic. No goodwill impairment risk from overpriced M&A.
- **Revenue recognition**: Percentage-of-completion method is standard for the industry. MYR Group has a clean audit history with no restatements.
- **Margin inflection is structural, not cyclical**: The shift from low-margin legacy contracts to higher-priced data center and grid work is a multi-year transition, not a one-quarter blip. Management confirmed on the Q4 2025 call that the bidding environment supports sustained margin improvement.

## Catalyst Path

1. **Sustained margin expansion in Q1-Q2 2026 earnings**: If gross margins hold above 11% and EBITDA margins approach 7%, the stock re-rates as the market recognizes this is structural, not cyclical. Next earnings report expected late April 2026.

2. **Backlog growth from grid modernization spending**: The $228B utility capex cycle in 2026 is just beginning. MYR Group's T&D segment should see accelerating order intake as transmission and distribution projects move from planning to execution. A backlog print above $3B would be a strong signal.

3. **Data center electrical contract wins**: Each major hyperscaler data center campus requires hundreds of millions in electrical construction. As MYRG wins larger C&I contracts, the revenue mix shifts toward higher-margin, higher-visibility work.

4. **Sell-side initiation**: With only 8 analysts, there is room for 2-3 additional initiations as the grid modernization theme gains broader attention. Each new initiation brings incremental institutional buying.

5. **Potential S&P MidCap 400 inclusion**: At $4.2B and growing, MYRG is approaching the threshold for MidCap 400 inclusion, which would trigger passive fund buying and increase institutional awareness.

## Risk Factors

1. **Project execution risk**: Construction companies face cost overrun risk on fixed-price contracts. A single large project gone wrong can wipe out a quarter's earnings. MYR Group has managed this well historically, but the risk is inherent.

2. **Labor scarcity**: Skilled electrical labor is the binding constraint. Wage inflation could pressure margins even as contract pricing improves. If MYR Group cannot recruit and retain enough workers, it cannot convert its backlog.

3. **Tariff impact on input costs**: The March 4 tariff escalation raises costs for electrical equipment, copper, and construction materials. MYR Group typically passes through material costs, but there can be timing lags on fixed-price contracts.

4. **Hyperscaler capex deceleration**: If AI monetization disappoints and hyperscalers slow capex, the data center portion of MYR Group's C&I backlog could stall. This would remove a key growth driver.

5. **Valuation compression**: At 27x forward PE, the stock is priced for continued execution. Any margin disappointment or backlog decline could trigger a sharp de-rating.

6. **Interest rate sensitivity**: Higher-for-longer rates increase the cost of grid infrastructure projects, potentially delaying utility capital spending decisions. MYR Group's own balance sheet is insulated (0.25x leverage), but customer spending decisions are not.
