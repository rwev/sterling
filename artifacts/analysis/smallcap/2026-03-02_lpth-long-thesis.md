# Small-Cap Long Thesis: LightPath Technologies (LPTH)

## Investment Summary

LightPath Technologies is a vertically integrated infrared optics and camera systems company undergoing a structural transformation from a niche precision optics supplier into a defense-focused infrared systems platform. The stock trades at $11.90 with a market cap of approximately $690M, yet the company holds a $103M backlog (85% defense-related), just reported 120% year-over-year revenue growth in Q2 FY2026, and sits at the intersection of two powerful secular trends: accelerating defense spending and the photonics/infrared revolution across military, security, and industrial applications. With only 7 analysts covering the name and institutional ownership at 56%, this is a classic discovery story where the company is outgrowing its coverage universe.

## Discovery Edge

LightPath is a micro-cap that has historically been ignored by institutional investors due to its sub-$50M annual revenue base and component-level business model. The company's transformation via the G5 Infrared acquisition (completed mid-2025) has fundamentally changed its revenue trajectory, backlog profile, and addressable market -- but the coverage universe has not caught up. Key discovery gaps:

- **Only 7 analysts cover the stock**, with a consensus target of just $12.10 -- barely above the current price, suggesting analysts have not fully modeled the backlog conversion
- **Institutional ownership at 56%** is moderate for a company of this size, indicating significant room for institutional accumulation as the revenue ramp becomes undeniable
- **The $103M backlog is 2.8x trailing twelve-month revenue** -- a ratio that would command significant attention if this were a larger-cap defense name
- **No major sell-side defense team covers LPTH** despite 85% of its backlog being defense-related; the stock is covered by small-cap generalists, not defense specialists
- A key early investor (North Run Strategic) has been selling into strength, creating short-term supply that may be suppressing the price even as fundamentals accelerate

## Business Overview

LightPath Technologies designs, manufactures, and distributes optical components, infrared lenses, and infrared camera systems. The company operates across four revenue streams:

- **Infrared components (31% of Q2 revenue)**: Precision molded and diamond-turned IR lenses using proprietary BlackDiamond chalcogenide glass, which replaces germanium at lower cost with superior optical properties
- **Visible components (21%)**: Precision molded glass aspheric optics for telecom, medical, and industrial applications
- **Assemblies and modules (44%)**: Integrated infrared camera systems for defense, surveillance, and public safety -- this is the fastest-growing segment post-G5 acquisition
- **Engineering services (4%)**: Custom NRE work for defense primes

The G5 Infrared acquisition (mid-2025) was transformational. G5 brought cooled infrared camera design and manufacturing capabilities, immediately giving LightPath the ability to sell complete systems rather than components. The company has redesigned G5's cameras to use LightPath's proprietary BlackDiamond glass in place of germanium, creating vertical integration from raw glass materials to finished defense systems.

In January 2026, LightPath acquired Amorphous Materials Inc. (AMI), adding complementary chalcogenide glass melting capabilities for large-diameter optics, further strengthening its vertical integration in IR materials.

## Risk Factors

1. **Pre-profitability risk**: The company remains EBITDA-negative; any delay in backlog conversion or cost overruns on camera production could extend the path to profitability
2. **Customer concentration**: Defense backlog may be concentrated among a small number of customers; loss of a key program could materially impact revenue
3. **Insider selling**: North Run Strategic has been a consistent seller ($22M+ in Jan-Feb 2026), which creates overhang; however, this appears to be a fund monetizing gains rather than a signal about fundamentals
4. **Integration risk**: Two acquisitions (G5 Infrared and AMI) within 9 months require management bandwidth and execution; integration failures could disrupt production
5. **Valuation premium**: At 11.8x LTM EV/Revenue, the stock prices in significant growth; any revenue miss could trigger a sharp correction
6. **Dilution history**: The December 2025 raise was at lower prices; further dilution is not expected given the $73.6M cash position, but cannot be ruled out if acquisition opportunities arise
