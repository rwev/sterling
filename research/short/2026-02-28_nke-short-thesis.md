# Short Thesis: NIKE, Inc. (NKE)

## Bear Case Summary

Nike is a structurally impaired franchise facing a triple headwind: $1.5 billion in annualized tariff costs compressing margins, a collapsing China business (-17% YoY with no inflection in sight), and accelerating market share losses to insurgent brands (On Running, Hoka, Anta, Li-Ning). The stock trades at 33x forward earnings for a company guiding revenue down low single digits with gross margins declining 175-225 basis points before tariff impacts. This is a turnaround story priced as though the turnaround has already begun. It has not.

## What the Market Is Missing

1. **The tariff headwind is structural, not transitory.** The new 10-15% global tariff regime adds approximately $1.5 billion in annualized product costs -- a 320 basis point gross margin headwind. Nike manufactures virtually all footwear and apparel overseas (Vietnam, Indonesia, China). There is no near-term path to reshoring. The company can either absorb the cost (margin destruction) or pass it through (volume destruction in a price-sensitive consumer environment). Neither outcome supports current multiples.

2. **China is in freefall with no floor visible.** Greater China revenue declined 17% to $1.42 billion in Q2 FY2026, the sixth consecutive quarterly decline. Footwear sales in China fell 21%. Online sales collapsed 36%. Domestic competitors (Anta, Li-Ning) are gaining share structurally, not cyclically. Nike's brand relevance with Chinese consumers is eroding, and the "Sport Offense" strategy aimed at recovery is margin-dilutive due to heavier wholesale channel reliance.

3. **Market share losses in running are accelerating.** On Running and Hoka have captured the technical running category that Nike once dominated. This is not a fashion cycle -- it is a product innovation gap. Nike's pivot back to performance from lifestyle is necessary but will take 2-3 years to show results, during which margins will remain under pressure from elevated marketing spend and wholesale-channel mix shift.

4. **Net income already declining despite revenue stabilization.** Q2 FY2026 net income fell 32% to $792 million even as revenue rose 1%. The divergence between the top line and bottom line reveals the true margin story: higher product costs, heavier markdowns, and elevated SGA to fund the turnaround.

## Accounting / Fundamental Concerns

### Inventory Management and Markdown Pressure -- Q2 FY2026 10-Q

Nike's gross margin compression in Q2 included significant markdown activity to clear excess inventory. North America gross margins declined 330 basis points despite 500+ basis points of tariff-driven product cost inflation, meaning underlying margin performance was even worse than the headline. The reliance on markdowns to maintain sell-through rates signals persistent inventory overhang and weakening brand pricing power.

### Revenue Quality -- Q2 FY2026 Earnings Call

North America revenue rose 9% in Q2, but this growth came disproportionately through wholesale channels, which carry lower margins than Nike's direct-to-consumer (DTC) business. The shift from DTC back to wholesale is a strategic retreat that flatters the top line while degrading unit economics. Revenue quality is deteriorating even as the headline number improves.

### Geographic Revenue Concentration Risk

Greater China and Converse are both declining meaningfully. Management guided Q3 performance in both to be "similar to Q2," which implies continued double-digit revenue declines. The company is increasingly dependent on North America, where tariff exposure is most acute.

## Valuation

NKE trades at approximately 33x forward earnings and approximately 37x EV/FCF for a company with declining net income, negative revenue growth guidance for Q3, and a 320 basis point structural margin headwind from tariffs. The 5-year average forward P/E of 34x was established during a period of consistent mid-to-high single digit revenue growth and expanding margins -- neither of which is present today.

Fair value under current conditions is closer to 25-28x forward earnings, reflecting the turnaround uncertainty and margin headwinds. At $2.00-$2.20 in consensus FY2026 EPS, that implies a price range of $50-$62 -- 3% to 22% below the current price of $63.91. The low-end analyst price target of $35 reflects a scenario where the turnaround fails entirely.

The stock has already declined from $81.49 (52-week high) to $63.91, but the fundamental deterioration has outpaced the price decline. Nike has lost value four consecutive years. A fifth year of losses is the base case absent a dramatic inflection in China or tariff relief.

Management guided revenue down low single digits with gross margin decline of 175-225bps (plus 315bps tariff impact). Any miss on these already-lowered expectations would be devastating. China trends and tariff commentary will dominate.
- **April 2026**: Potential tariff escalation. The administration has threatened to increase the global tariff from 10% to 15%, with further sector-specific tariffs possible. Any escalation would directly increase Nike's cost structure.
- **Q1-Q2 2026**: On Running and Hoka earnings will quantify the market share shift in running -- data that will pressure Nike's narrative further.
- **Summer 2026**: Consumer spending data. If tariff pass-through reduces purchasing power, discretionary brands like Nike face volume pressure on top of margin pressure.

## Risk Factors to the Short

- **Tariff rollback or exemption**: Any reduction in the global tariff rate or sector-specific exemption for footwear/apparel would immediately ease the $1.5 billion cost headwind and could trigger a sharp short squeeze.
- **China stabilization**: If Greater China revenue declines moderate to single digits, the narrative shifts from "structural decline" to "bottoming." Nike's brand equity in China is deep; a recovery is possible, just not yet visible in the data.
- **Turnaround execution**: New CEO Elliott Hill is implementing a strategic reset. If the "Sport Offense" strategy gains traction with consumers and new product launches resonate, the stock could re-rate before fundamentals fully confirm.
- **Takeover or activist involvement**: At sub-$100 billion market cap, Nike is not immune to activist pressure or strategic interest, though family control (Phil Knight) makes this less likely.
- **Crowded short**: Short interest is already 44.4 million shares (3.0% of float). A sharp rally could trigger a squeeze, particularly around earnings.
