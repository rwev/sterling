# ServiceNow (NOW) -- Growth Long Thesis

## Summary

ServiceNow is at a product cycle inflection point driven by its Autonomous Workforce launch, the most significant platform expansion since its ITSM-to-platform pivot. The stock trades at $108.06, down 49% from its 52-week high of $211.48, creating a rare entry into a 20%+ revenue compounder with 32% operating margins and 36% free cash flow margins. The market is punishing NOW alongside the broader software selloff and AI infrastructure fatigue, but ServiceNow sits on the opposite side of that trade: it is an AI application-layer beneficiary whose customers use AI to cut headcount and costs. Block's 40% workforce reduction and 24% stock surge on February 28 is the demand signal for exactly what ServiceNow sells.

---

## The Growth Inflection

### Revenue Acceleration Is Real, Not Narrative

ServiceNow's Q4 2025 results show acceleration across every metric that matters:

- **Subscription revenue**: $3.466B, up 21% YoY (19.5% constant currency)
- **cRPO (current remaining performance obligations)**: $12.85B, up 25% YoY -- this is the forward-looking demand indicator, and it is accelerating relative to revenue growth
- **Large customer growth**: Customers with ACV above $5M grew from 420 to 603 (+44%) over two years, with average ACV per large customer rising from $13.0M to $14.7M
- **Full-year 2025 revenue**: $13.28B with $4.58B in free cash flow

The 25% cRPO growth versus 21% subscription revenue growth signals that demand is pulling ahead of recognized revenue. This is the rate-of-change signal I look for: the pipeline is growing faster than the topline, meaning revenue growth should sustain or accelerate.

### 2026 Guidance Implies Sustained 20%+ Growth

- **FY 2026 subscription revenue guidance**: $15.53B-$15.57B (+20.5-21% YoY as reported)
- **Q1 2026 subscription revenue**: $3.65B-$3.655B (+21.5% YoY)
- **Operating margin guidance**: 32%
- **Free cash flow margin guidance**: 36%

Management is guiding for margin expansion alongside revenue acceleration. This is the operating leverage inflection -- the business is scaling AI capabilities on existing infrastructure while growing the topline at 20%+.

### AI Revenue Reaching Escape Velocity

ServiceNow expects AI-related offerings to generate over $1 billion in 2026. Agentic AI use-case adoption grew 55x between Q3 and Q4 2025. This is not incremental product enhancement -- this is a new revenue stream layered onto the existing platform.

## The Business Driver: Autonomous Workforce

On February 26, 2026, ServiceNow launched Autonomous Workforce -- a platform for deploying AI specialists that execute enterprise workflows end-to-end. The first AI specialist (Level 1 Service Desk) resolves IT cases autonomously, 99% faster than human agents. ServiceNow itself runs 90% of its own IT requests through AI.

This matters because it directly addresses the demand signal Block just validated: enterprises want to cut headcount using AI, and they need tooling to do it. ServiceNow's Autonomous Workforce is the enterprise-grade platform for exactly this use case.

The competitive moat is critical: ServiceNow has 150,000+ enterprise customers with deeply embedded ITSM, HR, and customer service workflows. AI agents need access to these workflows to function. ServiceNow is not being disrupted by AI -- it is the distribution channel for AI into the enterprise.

Key product details:
- AI specialists are embedded in Pro Plus and Enterprise Plus SKUs, driving adoption first and consumption-based monetization second
- Moveworks acquisition adds conversational AI capabilities (contributing ~100bps to 2026 growth)
- Veza acquisition strengthens identity security for autonomous AI agent governance
- Integrations with Microsoft (Agent 365), Anthropic (Claude), and OpenAI deepen model access

## Why the Market Is Wrong

The stock has fallen 49% from its July 2025 high of $211.48 to $108.06. The 52-week low was $98.00, hit just three weeks ago on February 9. This decline is driven by three forces that are either temporary or misapplied to ServiceNow:

1. **AI infrastructure fatigue**: NVIDIA's beat-and-dump, Microsoft down 20% YTD. But ServiceNow is an AI application company, not an infrastructure company. It benefits from AI capex, it does not make it.

2. **Broad software sector selloff**: Private credit stress at Blue Owl (70% of loan book in software) is creating indiscriminate selling across software names. ServiceNow has no debt dependency on private credit and generates $4.58B in annual free cash flow.

3. **Multiple compression in high-growth tech**: The rotation from mega-cap tech into tangible-economy assets has compressed all software multiples. But at a forward P/E of ~25x and EV/FCF of ~22x, ServiceNow's valuation has already absorbed significant compression from its peak of 60x+ forward earnings.

The result: a 20%+ revenue grower with 32% operating margins and 36% FCF margins, trading at 25x forward earnings after a 49% drawdown, at the exact moment its largest product cycle launch (Autonomous Workforce) enters the market alongside the strongest demand signal in enterprise AI history (Block's workforce cut).
