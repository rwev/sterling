# Macro Outlook -- February 2026

## Regime Assessment

The U.S. economy is in a late-cycle deceleration with disinflationary undertones but persistent supply-side frictions. Q4 2025 GDP printed at just 1.4% annualized, weighed down by a 43-day federal government shutdown. The consensus for 2026 full-year growth sits in the 1.8%--2.5% range (SPF: 1.8%, EY: 2.4%, Deloitte: 1.9%), with a Q1 rebound expected as government spending normalizes. Inflation is moderating -- January CPI fell to 2.4% headline, 2.5% core -- but February PPI came in hotter than expected, suggesting tariff pass-through is beginning to bite. The Fed holds the effective funds rate at 3.64%, above neutral estimates of 3.00%--3.25%. The 10-year Treasury yield broke below 4.00% to close at 3.97% on February 27, its lowest in four months, signaling the bond market is pricing slower growth and eventual further easing.

The dominant regime signal is: **growth deceleration with sticky policy uncertainty**. Tariffs remain live -- the Supreme Court struck down IEEPA-based tariffs on February 20, but the administration immediately imposed a new 10% global duty under Section 122 (with threats of 15%). This ongoing trade policy volatility is the single largest macro wildcard.

Capital is rotating aggressively. The MSCI World ex-US index has gained roughly 8% YTD versus a flat-to-slightly-down S&P 500. The DXY has fallen approximately 9% over the past 12 months, sitting near 97.65. Sector leadership has flipped: Industrials, Energy, Materials, and Utilities lead; Technology, Communication Services, and Consumer Discretionary lag. This is a textbook late-cycle rotation toward tangible assets and defensives.

## Themes for Long Analyst

1. **Power Infrastructure / Grid Modernization**: The AI buildout is creating a structural surge in electricity demand. Data center power consumption projected to grow 160%+ by 2030. 70% of the U.S. grid is approaching end-of-life. This is a multi-year capex cycle benefiting utilities, independent power producers, and grid equipment manufacturers. See Idea Brief #1 below.

Order backlogs are extending through 2027-2028. Equipment deliveries accelerating in 2026. This is a secular, not cyclical, tailwind. See Idea Brief #2 below.

3. **Weak Dollar / International Equity Re-Rating**: Non-US stocks trade at a roughly 35% P/E discount to U.S. equities even after 2025 outperformance. Dollar weakness (DXY down 9% over 12 months) provides a translation tailwind. European fiscal and monetary conditions improving. Consider broad international exposure or specific European industrials and financials.

## Themes for Short Analyst

1. **U.S. Mega-Cap Tech Under Pressure**: Microsoft down 20% YTD on AI capex fatigue and decelerating Azure growth. The market is repricing the assumption that massive AI infrastructure spending translates to near-term earnings growth. If Azure growth continues to decelerate and capex guidance remains elevated, further multiple compression is likely. See Idea Brief #3 below.

2. **Tariff-Exposed Consumer Discretionary**: Companies with heavy import reliance face margin compression as the 10% global tariff (potentially 15%) flows through supply chains. PPI already running hot. Consumer purchasing power under pressure.

## Risks to Current Positioning

- **Tariff escalation**: The administration has shown willingness to escalate rapidly (10% to threatened 15% within 24 hours). A move to 20%+ or sector-specific tariffs could trigger a broader risk-off event, hurting all equities including our overweight sectors.
- **Inflation re-acceleration**: If tariff pass-through pushes CPI back above 3%, the Fed will be forced to delay or reverse rate cuts, which would undermine the growth rebound thesis and pressure bond-sensitive sectors.
- **European political fragmentation**: Defense spending commitments depend on political consensus that could fracture, particularly in Germany post-election or if ceasefire negotiations in Ukraine accelerate.
- **AI demand disappointment**: If hyperscaler capex plans are revised down (as MSFT's experience suggests markets may fear), the power infrastructure thesis loses its primary demand catalyst.
- **Dollar reversal**: A risk-off spike could drive safe-haven flows back into USD, reversing the international outperformance trade.

Meanwhile, 70% of the U.S. grid is approaching end-of-life. This is not a speculative theme -- it is a capex cycle with signed contracts and multi-year backlogs. GE Vernova's gas turbine backlog reportedly extends through 2028 at $150 billion. Nuclear assets (Constellation, Vistra) are signing direct-power agreements with hyperscalers.

## What I Know

- Vistra (VST): Trading at ~$171, down from 52-week high of $220. 20 of 21 analysts rate it Buy, average PT $230. Nuclear and gas fleet well-positioned for baseload AI power.
- Constellation Energy (CEG): Trading at ~$292, down 23% YTD. Nuclear-pure-play; direct power purchase agreements with tech companies. Selloff may present entry opportunity.
- GE Vernova (GEV): Gas turbine order book sold out through 2028. $150B backlog. Critical infrastructure supplier.

## What Needs Verification

- Vistra's Q4 earnings were described as a "reality check" -- need to assess whether the selloff reflects fundamental concerns or sentiment-driven repricing.
- Constellation's 23% YTD decline needs explanation: is it valuation normalization, regulatory risk, or something structural?
- GEV's backlog conversion rate and margin trajectory need detailed modeling.
- Regulatory risk around nuclear power expansion and permitting timelines.

## Suggested Direction

Long. Focus on VST and CEG as potential entries on weakness. GEV as a purer infrastructure play.

## Catalyst / Time Sensitivity

Q1 2026 earnings (April) will provide updated guidance on power purchase agreement pipelines and capex plans. Hyperscaler capex announcements in April-May will either confirm or challenge the demand thesis. Not urgent, but the pullback in CEG creates a time-limited entry window.

# Idea Brief: European Defense Rearmament -- Long RNMBY (Rheinmetall), BAESY (BAE Systems)

To: Long Analyst
Priority: Normal

## The Setup (one sentence)

European NATO members are executing a generational rearmament cycle, with defense budgets rising 20%+ annually, a new 5% GDP spending target by 2035, and order backlogs extending years into the future.

## Why It's Interesting

This is the most significant shift in European defense posture since the Cold War. Germany alone has pushed defense spending past $100 billion (up from $86B in 2024). NATO allies committed to 5% of GDP on defense by 2035 at The Hague summit. Core European defense spending has doubled since 2019 and could reach EUR 800 billion annually by 2030. European defense M&A deal value rose 35% YoY in H1 2025. The STOXX Europe Aerospace & Defense Index gained 65%+ in 2025, and momentum is continuing into 2026.

## What I Know

- Rheinmetall (RNMBY): Trading at EUR 1,663.50, up 186% over the past year. 52-week range EUR 649--2,008. The premier European land systems / ammunition manufacturer. Order backlog extending through 2027+.
- BAE Systems (BAESY): Market cap $81B. UK's largest defense contractor. Diversified across land, air, maritime, and electronic systems. Benefits from both NATO and UK-specific defense investment.
- Europe is ramping to 21% of global defense spending, led by Germany.

## What Needs Verification

- Rheinmetall's valuation after a 186% run -- is the rearmament cycle already priced in, or does the multi-year spending trajectory support further upside?
- BAE Systems' order book and margin profile relative to peers.
- Currency effects: a weakening dollar benefits U.S. investors holding European defense names, but EUR/USD volatility could cut both ways.
- Execution risk: European defense procurement is historically slow and politically fraught. Can spending commitments translate to actual contract awards at the pace the market expects?

## Suggested Direction

Long. Rheinmetall as the higher-beta pure-play on European land rearmament. BAE Systems as the diversified, lower-volatility option. Consider position sizing carefully given the significant run-up already.

## Catalyst / Time Sensitivity

European Q1 defense budget votes and NATO procurement announcements through spring 2026. Rheinmetall reports Q4/FY2025 earnings in March -- will provide visibility on backlog conversion. Not urgent but the structural tailwind is strengthening, not fading.

# Idea Brief: U.S. Mega-Cap Tech Capex Fatigue -- Short Consideration on MSFT

To: Short Analyst
Priority: Normal

## The Setup (one sentence)

Microsoft is down 20% YTD as the market reprices the assumption that massive AI infrastructure investment translates to proportional near-term earnings growth, and the deceleration in Azure could have further to run.

## Why It's Interesting

Microsoft reported Q2 FY2026 adjusted EPS of $4.14 and revenue of $81.27B, but shares fell nearly 10% as Azure growth decelerated to 39% YoY from 40% the prior quarter and the More Personal Computing segment showed weakness. The stock has now declined 20% YTD. This is not an isolated event -- it reflects a broader "AI capex fatigue" narrative where investors are questioning whether the hundreds of billions being spent on AI infrastructure will generate returns on a reasonable timeline. Capital is flowing out of mega-cap tech broadly (XLK and QQQ underperforming) and into tangible-economy sectors.

## What I Know

- MSFT down ~20% YTD, underperforming the Dow by roughly 23 percentage points.
- Azure growth decelerating: 39% YoY, down from 40%, with market expecting acceleration not deceleration.
- AI capex guidance remains elevated, pressuring free cash flow.
- Broader rotation out of mega-cap tech is structural (weak dollar, valuation compression, international re-rating).
- The Magnificent Seven as a group are underperforming the equal-weight S&P 500 in 2026.

## What Needs Verification

- Is Azure deceleration a one-quarter blip or a trend? Need to assess competitive dynamics (AWS, GCP gaining share?).
- What is the actual ROI timeline on Microsoft's AI infrastructure investment? Need to model capex vs. incremental AI revenue.
- Downside target: where does valuation find support? Need to assess forward P/E relative to growth rate.
- Risk: any acceleration in AI monetization (Copilot adoption, Azure AI services) could reverse the narrative quickly.

## Suggested Direction

Short consideration. This is not a broken business -- it is a repricing of expectations. The short thesis works if Azure growth continues to decelerate and AI capex remains elevated without proportional revenue acceleration. Size accordingly; this is a high-quality company with a strong balance sheet.

## Catalyst / Time Sensitivity

Next earnings report (Q3 FY2026, expected late April) is the key catalyst. If Azure growth decelerates further to 37-38% and capex guidance holds or increases, the stock could see another leg down. The broader tech rotation provides a macro tailwind for the short. Monitor hyperscaler capex commentary across the board in April-May earnings season.
